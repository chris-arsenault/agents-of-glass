# Campaign Context

The orbital ring around Kaleidos broke a hundred and thirty years ago. The
fragments that survived are still inhabited; the spaces between them are
the Shear, full of debris and bad resonance and the kind of work that pays.
The Bloom — the reality-tearing aftermath of a Coremark experiment two
generations back — is contained but not understood, and the cordons around
it are stretched thin. Most of the system runs on inherited infrastructure
nobody fully understands, maintained by people doing their best.

You are on the inhabited fringe of all this. Not Sithari. Not the
Conclave's archives. The places where calls go out and the official channel
takes a long time to answer, or doesn't. There are still rivers under the
ground that talk in the voices of dead strangers. There are still hab
fragments drifting that nobody has heard from in years. Kite traffic has
gotten more expensive since the Conclave seizure last season; everyone
notices, nobody can do anything about it.

**The Question this campaign is asking:** *Who answers the call when the
system that was supposed to answer it can't anymore?*

**The Scarcity:** answers. There is no shortage of voices. There is a
shortage of people who can say back, with any confidence, *here is what
to do.*

**Tone:** working, grounded, hopecore-leaning. Loss is real. So is what
gets built from it.
