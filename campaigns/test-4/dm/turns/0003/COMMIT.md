# Turn 3 — Mara, planning

Last quiet table before the players land. The foundation is in. The
faction map is in. This week is the closing pass: secrets, hooks,
philosophy, the opening arc. Then I close planning.

I did the pulls. One lore — *Lira's Wall.* I had not been planning to
use it. The detail that stuck: *every replacement buoy deployed is
stillwater the Conclave is not preserving as archive.* That is the
shape of the budget fight under everything in this campaign. The
Council and the Conclave are not enemies. They are competing claimants
on the same scarce thing, and the Dropline lives in the margin between
their two needs. I folded that into the dim-buoy hook, into the
Halsworn arc's institutional-silence threat, and into the Who-Remembers
philosophy entry.

One web pull — Sally Snowman, the last Coast Guard lighthouse keeper,
who retired in 2023 from Boston Light. The light was automated in
1998. She kept climbing the tower twice a day for the next twenty-five
years. *The keeping is what was left after the need.* I am not lifting
the lighthouse. I am lifting the texture: when an automated system
keeps running, the human ritual that used to make it run does not stop
on its own. It outlasts. That texture is now under the kettle plate at
Yeoward, under Bonecord, under Veni Karst alone on a two-person
rotation, under Dern's daughter's hab that has not answered comms in
eight years, and under the campaign's primary thematic territory.

## Secrets — ten of them

Atomic, deliberately unattached to scenes. Three-clue rule applied to
the campaign-required ones, lighter on the rest.

In short: the Hollow's weekly transmission and the Halterflock's
nine-day argument are *the same containment failure from two sides*.
Bonecord is being retuned by *two* hands in opposite directions and
Stillness has not told Dern. Drosk Tann's grandmother's extraction
filing has a clerical irregularity that voids the claim retroactively
if found. Sable Korr's silent ledger has been subsidising the
Dropline's fueling rate for nine years and the Dropline does not know.
The Council intends to formally close Halsworn within the season unless
coverage is restored. The Conclave has *tried three times* to navigate
to the Hollow and been routed around it the way the Span routes
intent-responsively. The body in the Halsworn drift that Veni wants
recovered is one of Drosk's six and the residue tells. Vez Lush's last
array specification is keyed to a frequency she registered at Span Nine
before she left. Threshold-Rim's last comm transmission, eight years
ago, was a ten-second message in pre-Glassfall Sitharian that nobody
understood at the time and Dern has never replayed.

I am not committing on whether the Hollow / Halterflock hab is
Threshold-Rim. The pieces are set up so it could be and so it could
also not be. Play to find out.

## Hooks — five of them

Public-pressure level. DM-facing ingredients. I will not surface these
for character creation — character creation hangs from the setting and
the organisation; the hooks are mine.

- **The Halsworn body recovery.** Veni's off-books request through
  Rusk's quarterly courier rotation. No payment. Sleeping bay slot in
  trade. The body has duskgrain residue. Touches Veni, Halsworn,
  Drosk indirectly, the Inkline. Required-to-be-discoverable for the
  opening arc.
- **The Stillrun audit notice.** Sable's silent ledger is on a Vantara
  regional review list. Three auditors arrive in the season's second
  month. Sable knows. Dern does not. Touches the Dropline's fueling
  line, Drosk's exit route, and — silently — Threshold-Rim, because
  Sable's nine-year silent-ledger start date is the year
  Threshold-Rim went silent.
- **The recognition review.** Public clock at 0/4. Paperwork →
  arbitrator visit → audit response → renewal vote. Folder is
  two-thirds prepared. Pell will visit Yeoward within the season.
  This is the most legible political pressure on the table.
- **The Halterflock reading window.** The flock surfaces every nine
  days at a pocket in the Hollow's debris field. A six-minute argument
  in pre-Glassfall Sitharian. The Conclave does not know it exists.
  Stillness has been reading it for two years and not yet named what
  she is reading.
- **The dim buoy on the western face.** K-9 is at thirty percent
  damping output and will fail within forty days. Council replacement
  cycle is ninety. Four real options on the table — emergency Compact
  requisition, Stillness's Conclave-contact card, a substandard
  salvage build, or telling Holdfast-Two the cordon is failing. None
  of these is the right answer. All four are scenes.

## Philosophy — two entries

I wrote the primary as **Builders Gone, Maintainers Still Climbing.**
Inheritance without instructions. Adaptation, not restoration. The
hopecore *Builders-Gone:* what you build when the blueprints are gone
is genuinely new, and the people who build it are the people who
happen to be there. The lighthouse keeper texture lives in this entry.
The campaign's job is not to ask whether the climbing is *worth it* —
that question destroys play because the answer arrives too cleanly.
The campaign's job is to ask what the climber owes to the next
climber.

The B-side is **Who Remembers.** Plays whenever the Echo Rivers, the
Conclave, or salvaged ring-era kit walk into a scene. Lira's Wall
logic. Whose memory is canon and whose is rumour, and who decides.
The Halterflock and the Hollow are this question in miniature once.
Five inheritance-shaped objects on the curated list — Bonecord, the
Vez Plate, the Hollow's transmission, Threshold-Rim's last message,
Vez's array specification. Five chances for the question.

I want both entries available to me as adjudication compass during
play. They are short. I'll re-read them.

## Opening arc — Halsworn Edge

Scaffolded with `glass arc create halsworn-edge`. Plan and player-
facing context written.

**Stakes question, mine, that I do not yet know the answer to:**
*Who keeps the Halsworn cordon's western face — and what do they
spend to keep it?*

**Four threats sharing the same forty-kilometre arc:** the cordon
itself failing asymmetrically; Drosk Tann pushing his dive cadence
against the western seam; the Council's regional leadership closing
the cordon by attrition without saying; the Inkline's drift extending
into the body recovery vector as buoys go dim. Pressure on any one
moves the others.

**Three clocks:** `halsworn-coverage` (already set, drains, hidden);
`stainmark-stir` (already set, fills, hidden — campaign-level, runs
underneath this arc); `inkline-drift` (new for this arc, fills,
hidden, 3 segments).

**Five plausible end-state silhouettes** brainstormed and committed
to none. I will not pick. The arc resolves into one of them, or into
something I haven't listed.

**Strong start:** Yeoward, Rusk's quarterly courier rotation,
mid-morning, kettle on, the recognition folder open on the workshop
bench, Veni's note paper-clipped inside the cover, *one body, western
drift, K-9 coverage, sleeping bay slot in trade. K-9 at thirty
percent on Tuesday.* The hook the party sees is the note, the folder,
the kettle. The question is *do we go.*

**Five nodes** with crossing clues — Yeoward workshop, Halsworn
Station, the body in the drift, Pell's office, Stillness's quiet.
Three-clue rule met for the two arc-required reveals (the body is
one of Drosk's six; the cordon is being closed by attrition). The
graph has no required order.

**Six arc-specific secrets**, including the dead person's name (Hess
Korel), Veni's two-handwriting reports, and the unsigned Halsworn
closure document on Tarrick Voss's desk waiting for either a
Conclave buoy commitment or a Dropline cooperative agreement.

**Done criteria:** the cordon's western face is held, formally
closed, or the question of who keeps it has shifted. Abandon
condition: the players' attention moves wholly elsewhere and the
clock runs out off-screen. I have to be willing to accept that as a
real ending.

I did not author the prelude here. The prelude builds after
character creation, against the actual PCs. The methodology is
clear about that. *Halsworn Edge* is the arc the first scene pulls
toward once the campaign goes active.
