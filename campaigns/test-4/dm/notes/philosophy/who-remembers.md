# Who Remembers

The campaign's recurring B-side. Plays whenever the Echo Rivers,
the Conclave, or salvaged ring-era kit walk into a scene.

The world has a memory problem that runs underneath everything. The
Echo Rivers carry voices nobody chose to preserve. The Conclave's
stillwater archives are the official memory and they are being
spent down — every Halsworn replacement buoy is stillwater that is
not preserving history, because containment had to win that
budget fight. The Hollow transmits weekly in pre-Glassfall
Sitharian and nobody alive at the table speaks pre-Glassfall
Sitharian fluently. The Halterflock argues every nine days about a
hab failure the speakers know personally and that nobody else
knows about.

The Builders-Gone primary doesn't worry about *who decides what
gets remembered* because it is pulling on the *what gets adapted*
shape. This B-side is the question that surfaces when a specific
artifact, a specific drift voice, or a specific archive comes into
play: **whose memory is canon and whose is rumour, and who decides.**

The Conclave's answer is *we decide* — institutionally, with
stillwater and procedure. The Compact's answer is *whoever filed
first* — bureaucratically, by paperwork and pile. The Drosk Tann
answer is *the duskgrain seam knows* — pathologically, by talking
to the cell racks. The Dropline does not yet have an answer. That
is most of why this B-side matters.

The Halterflock and the Hollow's transmission are *the same event
from different sides*. If the Dropline ever puts the two together,
they have a primary-source reading nobody else in the system has,
and they have to decide what to do with it. Tell the Conclave and
trade autonomy for legitimacy. Hold it and become the only
keepers. Let it surface naturally and accept the consequences of
neither holding nor handing off.

That decision is *who-remembers* in this campaign, in miniature,
once. The B-side will surface at the table because the curated
list is engineered for it: Bonecord (a partial inheritance the
Dropline does not understand fully); the Vez Plate (a gift that
keeps heating); the Hollow's transmission (a primary source they
cannot translate); Threshold-Rim's last ten-second message (logged
but not understood); Vez Lush's last array specification (Stillness
built *Held Note* from it; Vez registered the frequency at Span
Nine before she left). Five inheritance-shaped objects. Five
chances for the question.

## What this looks like at the table

- A Tuner who reads pre-Glassfall Sitharian is *more powerful*
  than a Tuner who does not, in this campaign — not because the
  game gives them a bonus, but because the world has more to say
  to them, and they have more to decide about what to say back.
- The Conclave is not a villain. The Conclave is a *funding
  competitor* — they want stillwater for archive, the Council
  wants stillwater for buoys, and the Dropline lives in the
  margin between those two budget lines.
- An NPC's accent matters. Sable's hospitality bay smells like
  apricot tea because Sable's mother worked the Sable Crescent
  fueling line and *that* is what they served crews. That detail
  *is* the B-side. Memory through smell, through ritual, through
  who pours the tea.

## What this rules in

- **Translation as a scene.** Stillness reading the Halterflock.
  The Dropline's Tuner reading Bonecord. A player asking what
  Vez wrote on the array specification. These are scenes, not
  background.
- **Disagreement about what happened.** The Halterflock argues.
  The Hollow's transmission has minor variation in two specific
  words. The duskgrain seam disagrees with itself. *Memory is
  not a single source.* When two sources conflict at the table,
  do not resolve them administratively — play out the conflict.

## How I want to adjudicate against this

- When two memories conflict, the scene is the conflict, not the
  resolution.
- When a memory is asked to be canon, ask *whose canon, by what
  authority, paid for in what coin.* The stillwater answer.
- When a player hands something to another player and both of
  them remember the moment differently, that is *intended.* Do
  not adjudicate which one is right.
