# Shear Compact — campaign-specific layer

World-bible: `shared/lore/npcs/factions/shear-compact.md`. The Compact in
this campaign is the regulator the Dropline depends on for its
recognition, and the regulator that has *never formally rescinded* a
ninety-year-old extraction claim covering the Shear pocket where
Stainmark Holdings now operates. Both of those facts are doing work.

## Identity in this campaign

A standards body running on institutional momentum. Slow. Bureaucratic.
Rules-of-thumb-correct. The arbitrators are mostly competent, mostly
overworked, and decisively shaped by Pyre — they will never quite
believe a "just this one corner-cut" argument, because that argument
*was the corner that became Pyre.* That makes them stubborn allies of
small operators like the Dropline who clear their certifications cleanly,
and stubborn enemies of anyone who looks like Coremark in any colour.

Their weak spot is the archive. Pre-Compact mining claims, registered
before the Compact existed, were grandfathered en masse during the
founding charter and never individually audited. Most are dormant. A
few are not. The Compact does not have the staff to audit them. They
also do not have the political authority to void the lot.

## Goal — right now

Renew the Dropline's recognition cleanly, and find a way to address
Stainmark Holdings' grandfathered claim without exposing the
grandfathering loophole publicly.

## Tier

System-wide. Local: a regional arbitrator's office at Sithari that
covers the Yeoward stretch. Single arbitrator (Pell Andrik) handles
six small cooperatives plus the courier permits.

## Hold

- Certification authority across all legal Shear salvage.
- The forecasting service (frequently wrong, universally used).
- Formal mediation track for salvage claim disputes.
- The grandfathered-claims archive — **a hold that is also a liability.**

## Clock — `compact-archive-audit` (5 segments, slow fill)

If nothing opposes — i.e., if no one makes the loophole publicly
embarrassing — the Compact will continue not auditing the grandfathered
claims. If something forces the issue, the clock advances.

- 0/5 — Status quo. No audit pressure.
- 1/5 — Stainmark activity becomes flag-worthy at a Council meeting.
- 2/5 — A working journalist or a Council First Threshold makes a
  formal records request.
- 3/5 — The Compact opens a closed-door audit of the grandfathered
  archive.
- 4/5 — The archive's specific anomalies — Stainmark, three others —
  become known to senior arbitrators.
- 5/5 — Public audit. Loophole gets closed retroactively. Stainmark is
  legally exposed. Three other small operators discover their claims
  are gone too. The Compact's neutrality takes a hit.

This clock is hidden. The players can advance it through investigation
or through forcing a Council referral.

## Relationships

- **Dropline:** Rusk Talt (fixer) is the nephew of Pell Andrik (the
  Yeoward-region arbitrator). Pell is not corrupt. He is *generous on
  filing deadlines* in a way that is technically correct, in his
  nephew's direction. He'd defend any other small operator the same
  way if they had a nephew with paperwork instincts. They don't.
- **Council:** institutional courtesy, no operational integration.
- **Vantara:** Compact certifies Vantara's commercial operators. Vantara
  pays the most fines of any single operator (volume-based) and is the
  Compact's biggest revenue source.
- **Coremark / Stainmark:** Coremark proper operates outside the
  Compact entirely. **Stainmark Holdings** holds a grandfathered claim
  that the Compact has never formally rescinded. Stainmark mails its
  annual nominal-fee renewal every cycle. Pell Andrik's office files
  it without looking. That filing is what keeps Stainmark legal.

## Recognition review (public clock)

The `recognition-review` clock from `dm/notes/organization.md` is the
visible Compact-side pressure on the Dropline this season. It's already
on the public table; this faction note doesn't restate it.

## What the players will see

Pell at his small office in the Sithari hub spur, glasses on, a stack
of paperwork that is not for them, asking after Rusk before asking
about the Dropline. A forecast that is wrong about Halsworn weather
the day they need it most. A fine notice for something they did
correctly that they will have to file an appeal about. Bureaucracy as
weather.
