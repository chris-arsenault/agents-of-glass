# Vantara — campaign-specific layer

World-bible: `shared/lore/npcs/factions/vantara.md`. Vantara in this
campaign is *infrastructure with a price tag*: the fueling depots, the
microcavity racks, the route beacons. The Dropline lives within
Vantara's reach for nearly all of its working radius and is entirely
dependent on Vantara waystations for kite-cell fuel.

## Identity in this campaign

The waystation that's always there. Identical from one stop to the
next. Lights on, roof-tile clean, no questions asked at the counter.
What gets called "soulless" elsewhere is, on the Shear edge, a kind of
mercy: predictable, bureaucratic, untheatrical, never short on supplies.
The crew that limps in tells the same story they'd tell anywhere
because Vantara has trained its night-clerk personnel to ask only the
questions the company needs answered.

Vantara is not the antagonist. Vantara is the medium through which
other antagonisms move. Sable Korr's silent-fueling deal is *Vantara
infrastructure*, used by a Coremark remnant, not noticed by Vantara
itself.

## Goal — right now

Maintain the Stillrun Hab waystation as a profitable operating node.
The route Stillrun sits on has thinner traffic than it did three years
ago — Vantara's regional planning office has it on a five-year review
list. If Stillrun goes below break-even, it gets reassigned to one of
the bigger habs and downgraded to a self-serve fuel point. Sable Korr
knows this and does not want it.

## Tier

System-wide corporate. The Dropline's effective contact surface is one
waystation manager (Sable Korr) and a contracted independent rescue
clause that Vantara honors when it's convenient and bills when it's
not.

## Hold

- The Stillrun Hab waystation (microcavity racks, kite-cell refuel,
  hospitality rooms, light freight bay).
- Two route beacons covering Yeoward → Stillrun and Stillrun → the
  outer Hollow approach.
- The standing rescue clause that Vantara has never invoked but pays a
  retainer on (the Dropline gets the retainer; the clause exists so
  Vantara can say it does).
- Microcavity inventory the Dropline cannot easily replace.

## Clock — `stillrun-margin` (4 segments, draining)

What happens if nothing opposes — i.e., if Stillrun's traffic continues
its slow decline.

- 4/4 — Stillrun is profitable. Sable manages a full crew.
- 3/4 — Vantara's planning office flags Stillrun for review.
- 2/4 — Sable's silent-fueling income from Coremark becomes
  non-trivial to her balance sheet. She is dependent on it.
- 1/4 — Vantara's review concludes Stillrun is non-viable as a managed
  station.
- 0/4 — Stillrun is downgraded to a self-serve fuel point. Sable is
  reassigned. The silent-fueling arrangement either follows her,
  collapses, or is exposed.

This is hidden. Players see Sable making increasingly generous
hospitality offers to the Dropline.

## Relationships

- **Dropline:** Sable covered the Dropline through a thin year three
  winters ago. The "thin-year coverage" was Sable trading silence with
  Coremark for silence about *that* coverage. Dern knows. Rusk does
  not.
- **Compact:** licensed and certified. Pays the most fines.
- **Council:** route beacons used for Council emergency relays. No
  formal partnership.
- **Coremark / Stainmark:** Stainmark fuels at Stillrun on Sable's
  off-book ledger. Vantara corporate does not know. If exposed, Sable
  is the one who gets removed; Vantara survives the embarrassment.

## What the players will see

Stillrun's hospitality bay clean and warm; Sable Korr at the counter,
remembering everyone's name; coffee that's too good for the price; a
microcavity rack that's never out of stock when the Dropline shows up
late. A sympathetic employer-manager who does not, at any point, do
anything that looks like trouble. That's the front. The texture
underneath surfaces only if the players go looking.

## Bond to the Dropline's secret

The Vantara debt (`dm/notes/organization.md`, Secrets) lives inside
Vantara's infrastructure. Sable's silent-fueling arrangement is the
thread the players can pull on. Tugging it threatens Sable, who has
been kind to the Dropline, which is the *interesting* shape of that
hook — not betrayal-villain, not simple loyalty.
