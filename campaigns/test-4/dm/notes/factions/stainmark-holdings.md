# Stainmark Holdings

The campaign-local antagonist faction. Coremark remnant in everything but
the formal corporate genealogy: a small operation working a hidden
Shear pocket called the Stainmark Reach on a ninety-year-old extraction
claim filed before the Compact was founded. The Compact has never
formally rescinded the claim. Stainmark sends its annual nominal-fee
renewal every cycle. The renewal lands in Pell Andrik's office and
gets filed without anyone reading it. That is what keeps Stainmark
legal.

## Identity

Drosk Tann's operation. Six adults plus equipment, working out of a
hidden station built into a Shear-stable debris cluster nobody has
formally charted. Drosk's grandmother was the one who registered the
extraction claim — the family kept the paperwork because it cost
nothing to keep, and because the elder-Tann did not believe the
Compact would last. She was wrong about the Compact and right about
the paperwork.

Stainmark is not large enough to be a faction in the world-bible
sense. They are a *pocket* — local, well-resourced for their scale,
deliberately small, dependent on the Compact archive's neglect and
Vantara's incurious infrastructure. They exist because nobody is
looking for them and because nobody could legally remove them if they
did.

## Goal — right now

Three things, in order:

1. **Move the duskgrain inventory.** Two and a half racks at last
   count, slow accumulation across the season's dives. Drosk needs
   buyers that don't get the merchandise scanned by the Conclave on
   the way through.
2. **Protect the Stillrun fueling line.** Sable Korr's off-book ledger
   is the single point of failure in their supply chain. They have no
   alternative refueling option within their working radius.
3. **Keep their grandfathered claim quietly active.** Nominal-fee
   renewal, no public profile, no incidents that draw arbitrator
   attention. The claim is a legal force field; expose it and it
   collapses.

## Tier

Small. Six operators, one hidden station, two skiffs, no kite. They
piggyback on Vantara's beacon network for navigation. They are
*dangerous* out of proportion to their size because they have a
duskgrain inventory and Drosk has a duskgrain habit, and because they
will do violence to protect the legal-cover routine that keeps them
working.

## Hold

- The Stainmark Reach extraction claim (legally active, practically
  invisible).
- The hidden station — built into a debris cluster called Pin Tracery,
  occluded from Compact forecast feeds because the station's own kit
  emits a counter-pattern that reads as ambient Shear noise.
- A duskgrain inventory (~2.5 microcavity-rack-equivalents).
- Sable Korr's silent-fueling clause (functionally; she would say
  hers).
- A black-market buyer relationship somewhere in the Sable Crescent
  (unnamed, approached through couriers, possibly Conclave-adjacent).

## Clock — `stainmark-stir` (5 segments, fills with each dive cycle)

What happens if nothing opposes Stainmark — i.e., if no party
intervenes.

- 0/5 — Status quo. Drosk dives, refuels at Stillrun, ships to the
  buyer. Quiet.
- 1/5 — A Halsworn cordon-edge dive yields a duskgrain seam an order
  of magnitude richer than baseline. Drosk's habit deepens.
- 2/5 — Drosk pushes the dive cadence past safe spacing for the
  Stillrun fueling cycle. Sable's ledger gets harder to hide.
- 3/5 — A dive goes wrong: a duskgrain-laced microcavity cracks during
  recovery. One of the six is killed. Drosk pushes through the loss
  rather than pause. The body ends up in the Halsworn drift.
- 4/5 — The duskgrain inventory crosses a threshold where the Echo
  Ledger Conclave notices the resonance signature in the trade. They
  open a quiet inquiry through the buyer chain.
- 5/5 — Stainmark moves the entire inventory in a single shipment to
  outrun the Conclave. The shipment goes through Stillrun. Sable's
  cover collapses. The Halsworn cordon experiences a Bloom-edge
  expansion event correlated to Stainmark's last dive.

The clock fills on its own at the slow end. Player intervention can
accelerate it (push Drosk, threaten the buyer chain), force it
sideways (expose the claim, shut Stillrun down), or unwind it
(remove Drosk's duskgrain access).

## Relationships

- **Compact:** legally compliant on paper. Functionally invisible.
  Vulnerable to an audit they have no way to influence.
- **Council:** active threat to Halsworn cordon stability. The Council
  has flagged "irregular activity at Stainmark" twice in the last year
  and received no response.
- **Vantara:** dependent on Sable Korr's silent ledger.
- **Conclave:** unwitting buyer relationship through couriers. The
  Conclave's resonance archivists *would* recognise the duskgrain
  signature if they got close to it.
- **Dropline:** the Dropline does not know Stainmark exists yet. The
  Stainmark Reach is two days' burn from Yeoward; the cluster is
  occluded; nothing in the Dropline's ordinary work has reached it.
  This will change.

## Antagonist secrets

- Drosk's habit is well past recreational. He is dependent on a
  particular duskgrain seam that exists only in the Stainmark Reach.
- Drosk's six are *not* loyal to him; they are loyal to the work and
  to each other. If Drosk goes down, the operation might survive or
  might collapse — it depends on which of the six steps up.
- The grandmother's original claim filing contains a clerical
  irregularity that, if found, voids it retroactively. The
  irregularity is in the Sithari archive, not the Yeoward one.

## What the players will see

Initially nothing. Stainmark is the thing under the deck. The first
sign is Halsworn looking weirder than it should — a sudden Bloom-edge
flare that doesn't match seasonal patterns, a Council buoy that
fails on a clean rotation. The second sign is Sable's hospitality
becoming pointed in a way they hadn't quite registered before. The
third sign is Drosk himself, when he eventually surfaces — and by
then the players are already in a tangle they didn't know they were
in.
