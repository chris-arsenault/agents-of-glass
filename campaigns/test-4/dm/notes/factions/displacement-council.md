# Displacement Council — campaign-specific layer

The Council is the load-bearing humanitarian-and-containment faction the
Dropline has the most ambivalent relationship with. Both the world-bible
entry (`shared/lore/npcs/factions/displacement-council.md`) and this layer
apply. This file is what's true *for this campaign*.

## Identity in this campaign

The patient, understaffed neighbour who answers about half the time and
apologises the rest. Not antagonist, not ally, not patron — a peer
agency that runs out of hands faster than it runs out of mandate. The
Council respects the Dropline. It also does not call them first.

## Goal — right now

Hold the **Halsworn cordon**. Halsworn is a Bloom-edge cordon four hours'
burn from Yeoward that the Council has formally retained on the
operational map and quietly defunded across the last two budget cycles.
Personnel slots are budgeted on paper. The actual people are not there.
The cordon has slipped from full coverage to roughly a quarter of it,
and what coverage remains is rotated thin. The Council needs the
Halsworn boundary to hold for one more season while regional leadership
either restores funding or formally closes the cordon. They cannot say
either of those two things out loud yet.

## Tier

System-wide. Local presence at Halsworn is a single station and a
two-person rotation that's chronically short.

## Hold

- The Halsworn cordon (formally; weakly).
- Damping buoy supply across the system (via the Conclave's stillwater).
- Humanitarian registration — the Dropline's is filed through them.
- The Bloom Zone authentication chain — they're who you call when you
  think you've crossed into a zone and don't know which.

## Clock — `halsworn-coverage` (3 segments, draining)

What happens if nothing opposes them — except in this case "nothing
opposes" doesn't mean help; it means *no one steps in*. The cordon is
draining whether or not anyone notices.

- 3/3 — Coverage degrades to one-quarter (current state).
- 2/3 — A boundary buoy fails on a rotation no one was watching. Drift
  recorded but not investigated.
- 1/3 — Visible expansion at Halsworn's western face. Two small habs
  now within the new growth radius. Council asks Dropline informally to
  cover, off-books.
- 0/3 — Council formally closes Halsworn. Cordon authority lapses.
  Whoever's nearest is the answer.

The clock is **not** public — players will see effects, not the bar.
Players see Halsworn looking thinner than it should every time they
fly past it. The DM ticks this when they do nothing about it.

## Relationships

- **Dropline:** used them once for a Bloom-survivor extraction the
  Council couldn't justify staffing. Did not renew the contract. Veni
  Karst (cordon lead at Halsworn) is the contact they didn't call back.
- **Conclave:** dependent on Conclave stillwater for damping buoys.
  Politely strained. The Conclave wants the Hollow's transmission. The
  Council wants the Conclave's stillwater. Both pretend the other ask
  isn't on the table.
- **Compact:** institutional courtesy. The Council and Compact each
  recognise the other's certificates; that's most of the relationship.
- **Coremark remnant:** would prefer Coremark to be on someone else's
  problem list. Not equipped to handle it themselves. Has informally
  flagged "irregular activity at Stainmark" to the Compact twice in
  the last year and received no response.

## What the players will see

Veni Karst at the Halsworn station, tired, polite, doing the job. The
two-person rotation that should be six. The buoy line that has gaps.
The expression on Veni's face when she hands them the off-books request.
That's the Council in this campaign.
