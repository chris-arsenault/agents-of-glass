# Secrets and Clues

Ten atomic facts the players don't know yet, *unattached to specific
scenes*. Deploy whichever fits the moment. Three-clue rule applied to the
ones the campaign needs the players to actually reach. Lighter on the
rest. Mix of world-knows-but-hidden, DM-knows-but-saving, and play-to-
find-out blanks.

## The ten

1. **The Hollow's weekly transmission is three pre-Glassfall Sitharian
   speakers arguing about a containment failure at a hab they know
   personally.** The Dropline has fourteen years of recordings; Vez
   tagged the speakers by inflection but not by name. *World-knows-but-
   hidden.* Clues: the recordings themselves, a Tuner who reads
   pre-Glassfall Sitharian inflection, the Halterflock argument
   (which is the same containment failure from a different side).

2. **The Hollow transmission's hab and the Halterflock's hab are the
   same hab.** *Play-to-find-out — I have not committed.* If the
   players make the connection and pull on it, that's two of three
   clues; the third (the hab's identity) must come from elsewhere. If
   they never make it, the two pieces remain separately interesting
   and the connection never becomes load-bearing.

3. **Bonecord is being retuned by two different hands on different
   maintenance windows, in opposite directions.** Stillness has felt
   it for one cycle and not yet told Dern. *DM-knows-but-saving.*
   Clues: Bonecord's physical drift signature, Stillness's reading,
   maintenance log entries that don't match any Dropline crew rotation.

4. **Drosk Tann's grandmother's extraction claim has a clerical
   irregularity in the Sithari archive that voids it retroactively.**
   The irregularity is a missing co-signer mark on the original 1996
   filing. *DM-knows; required-to-be-discoverable for the antagonist
   thread to resolve cleanly.* Clues: Pell's pile-three folder cycle,
   a Sithari archivist's correspondence trail, physical evidence of
   the pre-Compact filing at Stainmark Reach.

5. **Sable Korr's Stillrun ledger has been kept off-book to
   subsidize the Dropline's fueling rate for nine years.** Sable
   knows. The Dropline does not. *DM-knows-but-saving.* Clues: the
   physical receipts at Yeoward versus the publicly registered
   Vantara fueling tariff; an off-book entry that surfaces during
   any audit; Sable's hospitality bay register. (Two of three are
   immediately near at hand. The third comes if the audit hook
   activates.)

6. **The Council intends to formally close the Halsworn cordon
   within the season unless coverage is restored.** Veni Karst
   knows. The two small habs in the cordon's expansion radius do
   not. The Council's regional leadership is not pretending — they
   are just not announcing. *DM-knows; surfaces as public-pressure
   when the players push or when the clock advances.*

7. **The Conclave wants to read the Hollow's transmission and
   cannot.** Three navigation attempts in the last decade have all
   routed around the Hollow the way they route around the Span. The
   Conclave has not asked Dern about it. *World-knows-but-the-
   Dropline-doesn't.* Clues: Conclave navigation logs (if accessed),
   a Conclave courier asking the wrong question, the routing
   pattern itself.

8. **The body Veni wants recovered from the Halsworn drift is one
   of Drosk's six. The body has duskgrain residue.** *Required-to-
   be-discoverable for the opening arc to function.* Three clues:
   the body itself; the resonance signature on the body when read
   by a Tuner; Veni's account of where the body drifted from
   (which lines up with a Stainmark dive vector if anyone cross-
   checks).

9. **Vez Lush's last array specification — the one Stillness used
   to build *Held Note* — is keyed to a frequency Vez registered
   at Span Nine before she left.** *Play-to-find-out.* The frequency
   might be a contact address. It might be a memorial. It might be
   a beacon she set so she could be found if she ever wanted to be.
   I do not know. The campaign is not *about* finding Vez and I am
   protecting that.

10. **Threshold-Rim's last comm transmission, eight years ago, was
    a ten-second message in pre-Glassfall Sitharian.** Logged by
    Yeoward at the time. Not understood. Dern has never replayed
    it. *DM-knows-but-saving.* Whether the Halterflock and the
    Hollow speak about Threshold-Rim is determined by whether this
    transmission corroborates them. Decision deferred until play
    forces it.

## Three-clue rule audit

- **Drosk's claim irregularity (4)** — three clues planted across
  Pell's office, the Sithari archive trail, and Stainmark Reach
  itself. Discoverable.
- **The body's identity (8)** — three clues on the body, the
  resonance read, and Veni's drift account. Discoverable.
- **The Hollow / Halterflock convergence (1, 2)** — two clues
  immediately, third only if the players push for it. Deliberately
  open.
- **Bonecord's other tuner (3)** — physical drift, Stillness's
  reading, log mismatch. Three clues. Discoverable.
- Items 5, 6, 7, 9, 10 are *interesting if discovered*, not
  *required to be discovered*. Two clues each is sufficient.

## What I am not writing here

- Who the second Bonecord retuner is. I have a hypothesis (Conclave-
  adjacent — they want to read the Hollow and cannot, so retuning
  the damper *the other way* is the cheapest way to surface the
  signal). I am not committing. If the players surface this thread,
  whoever they find at the other end of the retuning is whoever
  the campaign has earned by then.
- What the Sitharian speakers in the Hollow's transmission decide
  in the argument. The argument has a turn — it is not just an
  argument loop. Whether they reach a decision and what that
  decision is depends on which week the players first listen.
- Whether Vez is alive. I do not know.
