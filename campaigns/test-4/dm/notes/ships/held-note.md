# *Held Note*

The Dropline's signal-tuned skiff. Crew of two, no cargo capacity to
speak of, used for relay work, fine-resonance recording, and any job
where the *Lush Catch*'s mid-bandwidth profile is too coarse for what
the Dropline is being asked to do.

## Description

A small skiff with a long signal-array spine that runs the entire
dorsal length. The array is the skiff. The hull is functionally a
mounting bracket for the array, plus two seats and a minimum life-
support loop. *Held Note* will not survive a Shear debris event the
*Lush Catch* would shrug off; she is built for clean approaches and
clean returns.

## Provenance

Built by the Dropline themselves, six years ago, from a salvaged
Vantara courier hull and an array Vez Lush specified before she left.
The array's tuning was Vez's last contribution to the Dropline. The
skiff was assembled after she went; her name is not on the build.

## Current status

Operating. Used most often for Halterflock recording trips into the
Hollow's debris field. *Held Note* is the skiff that captures the
flock's surfacing on a nine-day cycle (whichever crew is on the
schedule).

## What it does

- Signal-band tuning fine enough to record an Echo River drifter
  surfacing cleanly.
- Rapid relay deployment for distress calls — *Held Note* can be
  airborne from Yeoward in under twenty minutes.
- Quiet enough on its own resonance signature to not attract a
  Coriolis Tap pack.

## Hook

The skiff is named for what fae folk-Tuners call the moment a
resonance hold lands cleanly — a *held note*, sustained, intentional.
The name is Stillness's contribution. Vez approved it before she
left. Stillness has used the skiff three times. She prefers, when
possible, that someone else flies it; the skiff has a tuning bias
that tracks toward whoever is on board with the strongest resonance
signature, and she finds the experience disorienting.
