# The Inkline

A Bloom-corrupted apex predator that hunts kite traffic along the
western face of the Halsworn cordon. Not a pack — a single,
territorially fixed organism that has been there since the cordon was
established. Council records list it as *anomaly H-9*. Crews call it
the Inkline because its passing leaves a black stain on resonance
filters that takes a full bandwidth-sweep to clear.

## Where encountered

The Halsworn cordon's western face. Specifically: the drift between
two failed buoys roughly four hours out from Yeoward and forty minutes
short of the Halsworn station itself. It does not leave this drift.
The Council has informally mapped its territory and informally not
told anyone, because formal recognition would require a containment
budget the Council does not have.

## Behavior

It listens for kinetic-array intake patterns. A kite under power has
a signature it can read at distance — the steady draw of a cell rack,
the harmonic of an array tuned to common bandwidths. It does not
attack the kite. It attacks the *crew's awareness of the kite* —
generates a Bloom-edge field that softens spatial relationships inside
the cabin. Crews lose where the cockpit door is. Lose which seat is
which. Lose what they were doing. Then the Inkline takes whatever
goes overboard or gets opened to vacuum during the confusion.

A crew that recognises the field early can power down the array,
drift through the territory, and re-engage on the far side. A crew
that does not, loses someone.

## Sensory

You do not see it. You see what its passage does to your filter
readouts: a black smear across the resonance display that takes
several seconds to register and several minutes to clean. You hear
nothing. You feel — Tuners report — *a held breath that isn't
yours.*

## Mechanics

- HP: high (it does not want to engage at close range; it does not
  need to).
- Attack profile: spatial-confusion field (Wis-equivalent save or
  brief disorientation; lasting effect on cabin geometry until the
  field passes).
- Resonance: produces a *black-band* signature that registers across
  the full spectrum at low amplitude and is functionally invisible
  to a kinetic array's standard filters. Reads as a *hole* in the
  ambient field, not as a peak.
- Wants: not hunger. Not territory. *Quiet.* The drift is where it
  is, and what it does to passing kites is, on its part, possibly
  defensive — a thing that does not want to be seen and removes the
  capacity to see it.
- Cannot leave the drift. Will not pursue.

## Hooks

- Halsworn cordon failure correlated with Inkline activity (the
  drift's western buoys are the ones failing).
- The Council has a closed file recording one cordon-edge crew's
  encounter. The file is not in Veni Karst's possession.
- Drosk Tann has been operating *near* the Inkline's drift but
  outside it. The duskgrain seam he is hitting may be related to the
  Inkline's territory in a way nobody has worked out.
