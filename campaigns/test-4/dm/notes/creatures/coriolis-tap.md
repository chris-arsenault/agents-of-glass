# The Coriolis Tap

A pack of Shear-anomaly fauna — small, distributed, never seen in
isolation, always in groups of nine to fifteen. They hunt by latching
onto a kinetic array's cell drain and pulling charge across a
narrow-bandwidth tap they extend into the array housing. The pack does
not engage crews directly. They drain a kite's microcavity racks and
leave the kite drifting.

The name is from the founders' generation: Coriolis Breach refit
crews encountered a small group during the Breach work and coined the
phrase *the tap is on*. The phrase predates the official pack
designation in the Compact's anomaly index by two decades.

## Where encountered

The Yeoward stretch of the Shear, principally on the inbound approach
to the Hollow. Less commonly along the Stillrun route. Compact
forecasts flag elevated Tap risk during certain resonance tides; the
flagging is right about half the time.

## Behavior

A pack moves with the resonance tide. They do not hunt against the
tide; they let the tide carry them through their feeding range. The
*Lush Catch* has crossed three packs in fourteen years and lost
fueling each time — never crew, never structural. The Dropline carries
a pack-ward kit (a counter-bandwidth emitter, kite-rack-mounted) that
deters but does not prevent the tap.

A pack that successfully drains a kite will leave it drifting and
move on. A drifting kite is then exposed to whatever else the Shear
has — debris, drift currents, occasionally the Inkline's drift if the
current carries it that way. Crews refer to the Tap as *the polite
predator*. It is not. It just does not need you.

## Sensory

You hear them as a low drone in the cabin — a hum almost below
audible, at first felt rather than heard. Cell-rack indicators begin
to read low across the board. A Tuner can locate the tap point on the
hull within thirty seconds. A non-Tuner may not realise what is
happening for several minutes.

## Mechanics

- HP per individual: low.
- Pack size: 9-15. Removing the pack means removing all of them; a
  half-cleared pack regenerates on the next tide.
- Attack profile: passive kinetic drain, narrow-bandwidth siphon.
  Damages microcavity racks (must be re-tuned after) and depletes
  cell stores; does not damage crew directly.
- Resonance: narrow-bandwidth taps in the kinetic band, attuned to
  whatever array the kite is running.
- Wants: charge. They feed on cells and disperse.
- Counter: counter-bandwidth emitter (Dropline carries one), or
  power down to ambient and let the pack pass.

## Hooks

- A crew that loses a microcavity rack to a Tap may have to refuel
  at Stillrun before completing the run. The Dropline ends up at
  Sable's counter more often than they would otherwise, which is the
  fueling-line texture.
- Drosk Tann's Pin Tracery station emits a counter-pattern that, in
  effect, deters Taps. The Stainmark Reach has no Tap activity.
  This is a clue.
