# The Halterflock

A specific named flock of Echo River drifters that surfaces near the
Hollow approximately every nine days. Two to four dozen drifters per
appearance, depending on the season. The flock has been recurring at
this surfacing point for at least eleven years; the Dropline's records
go back that far. Vez Lush named it before she left.

A *drifter*, in the Echo River sense, is a fragment of preserved voice
or signal that has surfaced through the resonance substrate and is now
loose in ambient space, repeating its content on a slow cycle until
the substrate reabsorbs it. Most drifters are isolated. The Halterflock
is unusual in that its members surface together, in formation, and
appear to *interact* — the flock's content is not a chorus of
individuals but a single conversational fragment, distributed across
many voices.

## Where encountered

A pocket of Echo River resonance substrate one hour's burn into the
Hollow's debris field. The flock surfaces on a nine-day cycle that
the Dropline's receiver tracks. Outside the surfacing window, the
pocket is silent.

## Behavior

The flock is a single conversation in pre-Glassfall Sitharian, between
three speakers, lasting approximately six minutes per surfacing.
Subject of the conversation: a *containment failure* at a hab that the
speakers know personally. The speakers are arguing about how to tell
someone — a fourth, unnamed person — that the hab cannot be saved.
The conversation does not resolve. The third speaker, the youngest
voice, ends the surfacing by saying *we have to tell her now* and the
flock disperses.

Crews who have heard the surfacing repeatedly have begun to recognise
phrases. The Conclave does not yet know the flock exists. The Dropline
has not reported it.

## Sensory

The drifters are visible as faint amber smears in the resonance
field, indistinguishable from background ringglass dust to the naked
eye but unmistakable on a Tuner's filter. The voices arrive as if
they were *in the cabin* — not over a comm. The three voices are
distinct enough that a crew that has heard the conversation more than
once can describe each speaker's character.

## Mechanics

- HP: not applicable. Drifters are not creatures in the combat
  sense; they are surfaced resonance content.
- Attack profile: none. The flock is not hostile and has no presence
  to engage with.
- Resonance: signal-band fragments. A Tuner can record the surfacing
  and study it; recording requires the Dropline's signal-tuned skiff
  *Held Note*.
- Wants: nothing. They are content. They run their cycle and
  disperse.
- Reabsorbed eventually: after another twelve to eighteen years, the
  substrate reabsorbs the flock and the surfacing ends. Vez Lush
  estimated the flock had perhaps two decades left when she named
  it. She left four years ago.

## Hooks

- The Hollow's weekly transmission (`dm/notes/organization.md`,
  Secrets) is from the same hab the Halterflock is discussing. The
  three speakers in the flock and the speaker in the transmission
  are not the same people. They are arguing about *the same event*,
  from different sides of it.
- Dern has not connected the two. She knows about the transmission.
  She does not know the flock is about the same hab. Vez did.
- The Conclave would *very much* like to know about the
  Halterflock.
