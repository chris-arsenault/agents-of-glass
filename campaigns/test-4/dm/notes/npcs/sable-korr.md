# Sable Korr

Hab-Worlder, mid-forties. Manager of the Vantara waystation at
**Stillrun Hab**. The Dropline's nearest fueling point and the place
they go when one of them wants real coffee instead of workshop coffee.
Sable owes the Dropline nothing on paper. The Dropline owes Sable a
debt nobody has spoken aloud.

## Tell

She remembers names. Not in the trained Vantara way — in a deeper way
that was already hers before Vantara hired her. When the Dropline
walks in she calls them by name without checking the manifest, asks
after the kettle, asks after Shei. The first time it happens it is
charming. The fourth time it happens the players might notice she is
asking after *every* crew that comes through, and every crew thinks
she's only this attentive to them.

## Wants

- Keep Stillrun's traffic-volume numbers out of the Vantara regional
  review's red zone. She will subsidise this from her off-book ledger
  if she has to.
- Keep the silent-fueling clause with **Stainmark Holdings** running
  smoothly. It is what props up Stillrun's books.
- Owe the Dropline less than she does. She would discharge the debt
  if she could find a way to do it that didn't expose her ledger.

## Fears

- A Compact audit of Stillrun's fuel records.
- Drosk Tann pushing his dive cadence to a point where the Stillrun
  fueling rhythm becomes visibly irregular.
- The Dropline asking her, point-blank, why the thin-year coverage
  was free.

## Bond

- Vantara, formally. She is good at this job and Vantara, who measures
  her on metrics that don't notice her ledger, considers her an asset.
- Dropline, informally. The thin-year coverage three winters ago. She
  did it because she could and because Dern Morr's daughter is from a
  hab two stops over from Sable's own. She did it again — in
  practice, by extending fuel credit through that same winter — because
  Stainmark was paying for it without knowing.
- Stainmark, secretly. Drosk's account on her ledger is encoded as a
  scheduled-bulk-customer entry. She has never spoken with him in
  person. The arrangement is courier-mediated.

## Next move

If the players never appear: Sable keeps Stillrun afloat. The Stainmark
clock advances on its own. Eventually one or the other arrangement
collapses; the other one collapses with it.

If the players engage: she is *kind*. Kindness is her surface and her
honest impulse. Underneath, she has a balance sheet she cannot let them
see. She will help them with anything that does not threaten the
ledger. She will help them generously with anything that cleans her
own books — a Dropline contract through Vantara that gives Stillrun
legitimate volume would be priceless to her, and dangerous to refuse
on her end.

## What she has not said

The thin-year coverage that saved the Dropline was Coremark money,
laundered through a Vantara fuel credit, signed off by Sable. Dern
knows. Sable knows Dern knows. Neither has named it. Rusk does not
know, and Sable has no idea whether Dern keeps that from him out of
loyalty to Sable or out of loyalty to Rusk.

## Mechanics

No combat presence. Lightly armed by Vantara policy (sidearm, never
drawn). Will not be in a fight. If trouble comes to Stillrun she
locks down the waystation and calls Vantara security, who arrive in
hours.
