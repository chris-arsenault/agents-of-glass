# Pell Andrik

Hab-Worlder, late fifties. Compact arbitrator for the Yeoward stretch
of the Shear, working out of a small office in the Sithari hub spur.
Rusk Talt's uncle on his mother's side.

## Tell

He stacks paperwork in three piles on his desk: things to read today,
things to read this week, and things he is choosing not to read yet.
The third pile is always the largest. He rotates the piles every
Friday whether he has read anything from them or not.

## Wants

- Renew the Dropline's recognition cleanly, on time, with the audit
  going well enough that nobody's regional supervisor asks questions.
- Keep his caseload off his evenings. He is six years from the Compact
  pension threshold and has, this season, started counting them down
  out loud.

## Fears

- That his nephew Rusk's filing-deadline generosity is what someone
  files an internal complaint about. Pell has been *technically*
  correct in every favour. Technically correct is not safe.
- That one of his pile-three folders becomes a regulatory crisis he
  should have caught.

## Bond

- Nephew of **Rusk Talt** (Dropline fixer).
- Files **Stainmark Holdings**' nominal-fee renewal annually without
  reading it. Has done so for nine years. Will do so again this
  season unless someone hands him a reason not to.
- Reports up to a regional supervisor in Sithari who he does not like
  and does not trust.

## Next move

If the players never appear: Pell completes the Dropline recognition
review on schedule, files the Stainmark renewal, files three other
grandfathered renewals from the same archive without reading those
either. Continues counting down to pension.

If the players engage: he is *helpful within the rules*. He will not
break them for them. He will tell them which form to file, where the
appeals window opens, and — if pressed at the right angle — which
folder the Stainmark filing sits in. He cannot be persuaded to pull a
file. He can be persuaded to *put a file on top* of his Friday pile.

## Mechanics

No combat presence. Will not be in a fight. If a fight comes to him,
he ducks behind his desk and files an incident report afterward.
