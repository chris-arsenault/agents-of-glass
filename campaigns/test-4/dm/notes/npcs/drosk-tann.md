# Drosk Tann

Hab-Worlder, mid-fifties. Operating chief of **Stainmark Holdings** — a
Coremark remnant working the Stainmark Reach on a grandfathered
extraction claim his grandmother filed before the Compact existed. Six
operators under him. One hidden station. A duskgrain habit he started
calling "calibration" eight years ago and has not called recreational
since.

## Tell

He talks to the duskgrain. Not to himself, not to a comm — directly to
the cell racks in the station's cold bay. Names the seams as if they
have their own intentions. The other five at Pin Tracery have learned
not to interrupt him in the cold bay. The sixth — the one who died
last cycle — used to.

## Wants

- Move the season's duskgrain inventory before the Conclave's resonance
  archivists notice the trade signature.
- Hit the seam that is *only* in the Stainmark Reach one more time.
- Keep his grandmother's claim quietly active.

## Fears

- Withdrawal. He has not been clean from duskgrain in seven years and
  does not believe he can survive being clean.
- His own crew turning on him after the last dive's death. They have
  not. Yet.
- A Compact archive audit.

## Bond

- Stainmark Holdings operationally — he is its chief.
- Sable Korr's silent ledger — his fueling and his exit route depend
  on Stillrun staying open.
- His grandmother's filing — a piece of paper in the Sithari archive
  he has never himself seen.
- The Halsworn cordon's western face, indirectly — the seams he is
  hitting are bleeding into the cordon and pushing it.

## Next move

If the players never appear: he dives twice more this season,
escalating. The Stainmark clock fills (`stainmark-stir`, hidden,
5 segments). The Halsworn cordon is increasingly affected. Eventually
either the Conclave or the Compact closes on him; whichever does, he
runs the inventory through Stillrun in a single shipment and burns
Sable's cover doing it.

If the players engage: he is *not* a single-fight opponent. He is the
cold-bay voice and the dive cadence behind a string of incidents. He
will not be in the room with the players until the campaign's first
arc has put pressure on Pin Tracery. When he is in the room, he is
careful, calibrated, and willing to use the duskgrain as a weapon.

## Mechanics — antagonist stat block

- HP: medium-high (a man kept upright by duskgrain calibration; he
  will not be knocked out by ordinary means).
- Attack profile: short-range duskgrain-discharge weapon (improvised,
  Stainmark-built); a sidearm; a willingness to crack a microcavity
  in close quarters as a last resort.
- Dangerous because: the duskgrain releases across the full spectrum;
  if cornered, he will discharge a microcavity in a way that
  destabilises the *room's* resonance and forces everyone to deal with
  what that does first. Tuner PCs feel this as a hammer to the chest.
- Escape: through Pin Tracery's counter-pattern emitter (the kit that
  hides the station from forecast feeds also masks his exit vector).
  He has *one* escape route. If players burn it, he is cornered.

## Antagonist clock — `stainmark-stir`

5 segments, hidden. See `dm/notes/factions/stainmark-holdings.md`. This
is Drosk's clock as much as it is the faction's. He is the one
deciding to dive again.

## What he does not know

- That his grandmother's filing has a clerical irregularity that
  voids it retroactively if found.
- That **The Held Stillness** has felt him at the Halsworn edge twice
  and is waiting for a third reading.
- That Sable Korr is on a planning-office review list and would lose
  Stillrun within a year regardless of what he does.
