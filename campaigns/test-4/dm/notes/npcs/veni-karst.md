# Veni Karst

Hab-Worlder, mid-thirties. Cordon lead at the **Halsworn cordon**, four
hours' burn from Yeoward. Bloom-survivor — pulled out of a hab at age
nineteen during a small-cordon containment failure that the Council
never publicised. Joined the Council eight years ago and has been at
Halsworn for six.

## Tell

She talks while she works. Buoy maintenance, log entries, suit checks
— all of it gets narrated out loud, partly to keep her own concentration
and partly because the cordon is short-staffed enough that talking to
herself is most of her workday. When she stops talking, she has heard
something she didn't expect.

## Wants

- One additional rotation pair for Halsworn. She has filed for this
  every quarter for three years. The slot is on the books. The bodies
  do not arrive.
- For the Dropline to take an off-books cordon-edge call she cannot
  authorise herself. Off-books because the *body* she needs them for
  is in the drift between Halsworn and an unmapped pocket Council
  hasn't been allowed to chart.

## Fears

- That Halsworn formally closes and the two small habs inside the
  current expansion radius lose their authorised containment. She has
  spent two years building informal relationships with both habs. They
  trust the cordon. The cordon is the Council. The Council is her.
- That her own hab's old containment-failure file — the one she was
  pulled out of — gets reopened in a way that names the Council. She
  was nineteen then. She has been protecting that name for sixteen
  years.

## Bond

- Council, formal — she is the Halsworn lead. Her chain of command
  goes through a regional First Threshold she sees twice a year.
- Dropline, informal — she was the Council contact on that one job
  the Council used the Dropline for. She put the Dropline up at
  Halsworn for a night when the *Lush Catch* was waiting on a
  resonance tide. She remembers Dern's name. Dern remembers hers.
- Halsworn cordon, operationally — she's there more than her partner
  is. The cordon's continued integrity is partly procedural and
  partly her stubbornness.

## Next move

If the players never appear: Veni continues to hold Halsworn at
quarter-coverage. The buoy rotation slips. The body in the drift
stays there. The cordon clock advances on its own.

If the players engage: she pays. She has very little to pay with —
a sleeping bay at Halsworn, a Council-route beacon code that opens
nominally for emergencies but practically for whoever Veni feels
like, and the names of two habs that will trust the Dropline because
she vouches for them. She will trade these for honest work on the
cordon.

## Mechanics

Tuner-trained for cordon resonance work. Lightly armed (Council issue
sidearm, Council-issue baton). Not a combatant — a containment
specialist whose only weapon is a buoy line and her radio. HP modest;
she will retreat behind cordon equipment in a fight rather than
engage.
