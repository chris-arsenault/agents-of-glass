# The Recognition Review

The Dropline's two-yearly Compact recognition review is due this
season. Public clock `recognition-review` (4 segments). The vote can
go either way. Players see this clock from turn one of play.

## Situation

The recognition review is the Compact's procedure for retaining or
revoking a cooperative response and salvage organisation's
operational legitimacy. It is also, this cycle, the most legible
political pressure the Dropline faces.

Four-step procedure:

1. **Paperwork.** The Dropline submits a response folder
   documenting the prior two years of operations: calls answered,
   fueling cycles, salvage manifests, incident reports. Rusk Talt
   handles this. Due in six weeks. The folder is roughly two-thirds
   prepared.
2. **Arbitrator visit.** Pell Andrik visits Yeoward for a
   half-day. He reviews the folder in person, walks the workshop,
   and writes a private note to himself that nobody but Pell sees.
3. **Audit response.** The Compact asks for clarification on
   exactly two things in the folder. The Dropline has fourteen days
   to respond. Skipping the audit response is the most common way
   to lose a recognition vote.
4. **Renewal vote.** The regional Compact council votes. Outcome
   binds for two years.

## Touches

- **Rusk Talt** — handles the paperwork. He is good at this.
- **Pell Andrik** — the arbitrator. Rusk's uncle. Pell's filing
  generosity is informal and deniable; he will not save the
  Dropline if the paperwork is bad.
- **Dern Morr** — the Dropline's lead. Will speak to Pell in
  person. Has, in the past, declined to do so.
- **Vantara** — Stillrun's fueling tariff is referenced in the
  fueling-cycle audit. If the audit cross-checks against Sable's
  off-book ledger, the Stillrun audit hook activates from within
  this hook.

## At stake if engaged

Survival of the Dropline as a recognised cooperative. Loss of
recognition does not shut the Dropline down — they still have
*Lush Catch*, the kettle, the workshop, and Bonecord — but it
removes their access to the Compact's emergency frequency, their
humanitarian-registered fueling rate, and their ability to legally
respond to calls in Compact territory. Within six months of a
revocation, the Dropline becomes a salvage outfit with sentimental
trappings.

Engagement here means the players doing the unglamorous parts:
helping Rusk close the folder, walking Pell through the workshop,
deciding which of two specific incidents over the last two years
to lead with in the audit response. There is no combat here.
There is no antagonist. There is only paperwork and the politics
that lives inside it.

## At stake if ignored

The folder lands incomplete. Pell files it without comment. The
audit response goes out late. The vote fails 3-2. The Dropline
loses recognition and what that *actually means* unfolds across
the campaign — Compact-frequency calls go elsewhere, the
humanitarian fueling rate evaporates, Sable's ledger has to do
more work to keep the Dropline fueled, and Sable's audit lands
worse.

## How it surfaces

The clock is *already* set, *already* public, and the players see
it on their first turn. The hook activates the moment Rusk asks
someone to read a section of the folder. Or the moment Pell's
arbitrator-visit date lands on the Yeoward schedule. Or the moment
a player asks why Dern is rereading the founding-history page in
the workshop.

## What I'm not pre-deciding

- The folder's two audit-response questions. They are determined
  by which incidents from the prior two years are documented in
  ways that invite clarification. I will pick the two from the
  party's prior arc work; if no arc has run yet, I will pick from
  the prior two years' implied operations the Dropline ran before
  the campaign opens.
- Whether the recognition vote passes. Genuinely open. The clock
  is public, the procedure is procedural, and the outcome is the
  product of player engagement plus the world's normal noise.
