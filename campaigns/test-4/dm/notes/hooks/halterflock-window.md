# The Halterflock Reading Window

The Halterflock surfaces every nine days at a pocket in the Hollow's
debris field. A six-minute argument in pre-Glassfall Sitharian
between three speakers about a containment failure at a hab they
know personally. The Dropline could record it cleanly with no
Conclave political cost — or could disclose to the Conclave and
trade autonomy for legitimacy.

## Situation

The flock has been surfacing on the same nine-day cycle for at
least the last three years, possibly longer — Vez logged it before
she left and named it. The Conclave does not know it exists. *Lush
Catch* can reach the surfacing pocket in three hours from Yeoward.
The argument runs roughly six minutes from the moment the flock
breaks the debris-field surface.

The argument is the same containment failure the Hollow's weekly
transmission is *also* about, from a different side — three
different speakers, slightly different inflection, different sub-
beats of the same disaster. The Dropline does not know they are
about the same event. Stillness has read both and not yet said.

## Touches

- **The Halterflock** — the recurring drifter flock.
- **The Hollow** — the pocket is in its debris field; the
  transmission is the other side of the same event.
- **The Held Stillness** — has read both the flock and the
  transmission and is the only person at the Dropline who could
  connect them.
- **Echo Ledger Conclave** — would value this reading enormously
  and is in a position to extract favours from anyone who brings
  it to them.
- **Vez Lush** — named the flock. Did not tell the Conclave. The
  pattern she set is the pattern Dern has continued.

## At stake if engaged (privately)

The Dropline gains a clean nine-day cycle of access to a
pre-Glassfall Sitharian primary source about a containment failure
at a hab the speakers know personally. Stillness can begin to read
it seriously — possibly identify the hab, possibly not. The
Dropline retains autonomy. The Conclave does not learn the flock
exists.

Risk: the Dropline becomes the *only* keeper of this reading. The
weight of that decision compounds across the campaign.

## At stake if engaged (publicly, via Conclave)

The Dropline trades the flock to the Conclave for political
legitimacy — a Conclave referral could materially help the
recognition review, soften the Stillrun audit's Conclave-archivist
contact, or pull resources for the Halsworn cordon. The Conclave
gets a primary-source reading they have wanted for a decade. The
Dropline becomes, in some functional sense, the Conclave's
contracted listener for the Hollow.

Risk: the Conclave does not let go of resources. Once they know
the flock exists, they will want the transmission. Once they want
the transmission, they will pressure the Dropline for it. The
politics of *Drift* shifts onto the Dropline's table.

## At stake if ignored

The flock surfaces and resurfaces on its cycle. Vez's pattern
holds. The reading is preserved in the Yeoward recordings but not
acted on. If the Conclave finds out by another route — Drosk's
buyer chain leading them to the duskgrain trade and from there to
the Hollow's resonance signature — they arrive without the
Dropline as intermediary, and the Dropline's ability to refuse
collapses.

## How it surfaces

Through Stillness, when the Dropline is in a position to ask. She
has not volunteered it. She would, if asked directly. She would
also, if asked indirectly, tell the asker she's been reading the
transmission and the flock both for two years and hasn't quite
named what she's reading.

## What I'm not pre-deciding

- The hab the speakers are at. (See secrets 1, 2, 10.)
- The decision-point of the Sitharian argument. The argument has
  a turn — the speakers reach a moment of deciding something — and
  whether they decide *the same thing every nine days* or whether
  the decision drifts is a play-to-find-out.
- Whether the Conclave already knows about the flock and is
  waiting for the Dropline to bring it forward. I have a slight
  preference toward *no* but am not committing.
