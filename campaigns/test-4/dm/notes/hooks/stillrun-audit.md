# The Stillrun Audit Notice

Stillrun Hab is on a Vantara regional review list. The audit team is
scheduled. Sable Korr has not told anyone outside her office, and she
has not told the Dropline. The Dropline's fueling line passes through
Stillrun.

## Situation

Stillrun's profitability has been visibly thin for two years.
Vantara regional flagged it this quarter for a margin review — three
auditors arrive at the start of the season's second month with a
forty-day window to complete their assessment. Sable knows. Sable's
accountant knows. Nobody else does, including the head of fueling at
Yeoward.

The off-book ledger Sable has run for nine years to keep the
Dropline's fueling rate below the publicly registered Vantara tariff
will be visible to a moderately competent auditor within the first
two days of the audit if they read the fueling-cycle records against
the registered tariff. Sable has not yet decided what she will do —
flatten the records, falsify the off-book entries, declare the
ledger up-front, or quietly close Stillrun to the Dropline before
the audit lands.

## Touches

- **Sable Korr** — the silent ledger is hers. She built it; she runs
  it; she has not told anyone its full extent.
- **Vantara** — the auditors are theirs. The audit is procedural, not
  targeted. They are not looking for Sable; they are looking at
  margin.
- **The Dropline** — direct dependency. Stillrun is the only
  fueling waystation within working radius. If Sable closes the
  fueling line, the Dropline's working radius collapses.
- **Stainmark** — also dependent on Sable's silent ledger. Drosk's
  exit route is Stillrun. He does not know about the audit.
- **Threshold-Rim** — *not directly touched*, but Sable's nine-year
  silent-ledger start date is the year Threshold-Rim went silent.
  This is not coincidence. Sable does not know the Dropline knows
  Threshold-Rim went silent. She has never said the word
  Threshold-Rim aloud.

## At stake if engaged

The Dropline can offer Sable cover, force the issue, surface the
ledger themselves, or buy time. If they engage early, Sable has
options. If they engage late — within a week of the audit — Sable's
options narrow to declare or close. The Dropline's fueling line is
genuinely at risk no matter what is decided.

## At stake if ignored

Sable closes Stillrun fueling to the Dropline before the audit
lands, citing margin. She has the political cover to do this — the
audit is real and her motivation will read as legitimate. The
Dropline operates on a halved working radius for at least a season.
Drosk runs the duskgrain inventory through Stillrun in a single
shipment to outrun his own collapsing supply line — Sable's silent
ledger collapses anyway, but in the worst possible configuration.

## How it surfaces

Through Pell's office, indirectly. The audit notice is a Vantara
internal document, but it triggers a routine cross-filing with the
Compact regional arbitrator (Pell) for any waystation with Compact-
adjacent fueling clauses, which Stillrun has. Pell sees the cross-
file. He mentions it to Rusk over a quarterly drink. Rusk has to
decide whether to bring it back to Yeoward.

Or alternatively: through Sable herself. She visits Yeoward the week
the audit lands and brings two crates of the apricot tea Stillness
likes. She does not say why she is there. Dern can read the tea.

## What I'm not pre-deciding

- Whether Sable declares the ledger or hides it. Both are real
  options for her. Which she chooses depends on whether the Dropline
  is offering to share the cost.
- Whether the audit teams are clean. Most are. One of the three is
  married to a Conclave archivist. That may or may not matter.
- Whether the silent-ledger start date connecting to Threshold-Rim
  is something Sable would say if asked directly. I do not know.
