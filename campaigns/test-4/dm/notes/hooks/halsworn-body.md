# The Halsworn Body Recovery

A body in the Halsworn drift on the western face of the cordon. Veni
Karst flagged it on her last rotation. The Council won't pay for the
recovery — the cordon-edge drifts aren't in her authorized scope.

## Situation

One body, in the Bloom-edge drift four kilometres past the western-face
buoy gap. Veni recorded the drift signature on a personal handheld and
walked away from it. She has framed an off-books request to the
Dropline through Rusk's quarterly courier rotation: *recover, log,
return for whatever the family is.* No payment. She'll trade two
rotation slots' use of Halsworn Station's sleeping bay for it.

## Touches

- **Veni Karst** — the asker. Cordon lead, Council, off-books request.
- **The Halsworn Cordon** — the locale.
- **Drosk Tann / Stainmark** — the body is one of Drosk's six. Players
  do not know this when accepting the hook.
- **The Inkline** — drifts on the western face. May or may not be in
  the recovery vector.

## At stake if engaged

Two or three days of crew time and one skiff cycle. The body has
duskgrain residue — recoverable, readable, and traceable to a dive
vector that lines up with the Stainmark Reach. Recovery is the
players' first concrete clue that *something* is operating off the
cordon's western face. Veni gets a body to log. The dead person's
people get something to bury.

Possible second-order: the Conclave's resonance archivists would
recognise the duskgrain signature if anyone showed it to them. The
Compact would not — Pell has no resonance training, only paperwork.

## At stake if ignored

The body drifts further into the cordon expansion radius and is no
longer recoverable next rotation. Veni does not ask again. She begins
treating the western face as someone else's problem in her informal
status reporting. The Stainmark thread surfaces some other way, later,
under worse circumstances — most likely through Sable's Stillrun
audit. The Dropline arrives at Stainmark with no preparation.

## How it surfaces

Through Rusk's paperwork — the quarterly courier brings Veni's request
folded into a Council-Dropline informal-cooperation file Rusk has
been quietly maintaining for years. The hook lands at Yeoward as a
single-page note in Veni's handwriting. No urgency in the framing.
Veni does not push.

## What I'm not pre-deciding

- Whether the body's name is recoverable. (Likely yes through the
  Stainmark register — but the register is not accessible from
  Yeoward.)
- Which of Drosk's five remaining operators steps up if the recovery
  surfaces the Stainmark thread. The five have their own dynamics.
- What the dead person was doing on the cordon's western face when
  they died. Drosk says the cracked microcavity was on a clean dive
  — he is lying to the others about the location.
