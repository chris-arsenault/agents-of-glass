# The Dim Buoy on the Western Face

A Halsworn damping buoy is failing on a routine crawl-by. The Council
can't replace it within their cycle — every replacement buoy is
stillwater the Conclave is not preserving as archive. Whoever
replaces this buoy is making a small decision with a long shape
behind it.

## Situation

Buoy K-9, on Halsworn's western face, is at thirty percent damping
output. Veni's last instrument reading shows a clean degrade curve —
not damaged, just used up. The Council's replacement cycle for
Halsworn is ninety days. K-9 will fail within forty.

The two small habs inside the western expansion radius are within
K-9's coverage cone. When K-9 fails, the cone collapses and the
habs are inside the Bloom Zone's revised growth curve. The Council
has not told the second hab.

The Dropline could:

- Source a replacement buoy through Pell, who would file an
  emergency Compact requisition that would land at the Conclave's
  stillwater desk in roughly twenty days.
- Source a replacement buoy through Stillness, who has Conclave
  contacts she has not used in eleven years and could probably
  extract one in five to seven days at a cost she would not name
  to Dern in advance.
- Build a substandard damping unit out of a salvaged microcavity
  rack and a stillwater fragment they already have at Yeoward.
  Stillness could tune it. It would hold for thirty to fifty days
  before failing differently than K-9 is failing now.
- Tell the second hab the cordon is failing and let the hab decide
  whether to relocate. The hab has approximately forty residents,
  no kite, and a Vantara fueling debt that prevents independent
  evacuation.

## Touches

- **The Halsworn Cordon** — the locale.
- **Veni Karst** — would be relieved by any of these decisions
  and could tell the Council she had taken cordon-stabilising
  action under the Dropline's auspices.
- **The Held Stillness** — her Conclave contacts are a card the
  Dropline has not played.
- **Echo Ledger Conclave** — every replacement buoy is stillwater
  that isn't preserving history. Lira's Wall logic. The Conclave
  resents the cordon's draw on stillwater and politely never says
  so.
- **The two small habs** — Talvet and Holdfast-Two. Talvet's
  council Veni has informally briefed. Holdfast-Two she has not.

## At stake if engaged

The cordon's western face holds for another season. The hidden
`halsworn-coverage` clock does not advance. One small hab does not
learn it was almost evacuated; the other does. Stillness's Conclave
relationship gets used up by some specific amount the Dropline
will not be told. The recognition review folder gains a
documentable cordon-stabilisation incident.

## At stake if ignored

K-9 fails. The cordon's western face becomes the visible breach
the Council cannot ignore politically. The Council formally closes
the cordon (the `halsworn-coverage` clock runs out). The two small
habs scramble. The Dropline's working radius becomes the Halsworn
cordon by default — they are the nearest. The Inkline drift
expands. The body Veni asked to recover earlier is now inside a
Bloom Zone.

## How it surfaces

Through any flyby of Halsworn — the buoy's degrade is visible on
*Lush Catch*'s resonance display. Or through Veni, who will show
the reading to anyone with a Tuner certification on board. Or
through Stillness, who reads the buoy's tone the way another
Tuner might read a heart rate.

## What I'm not pre-deciding

- Which of the four engagement options is "right." All four are
  legitimate. The third (substandard build) and the fourth (tell
  the hab) are the ones the players are most likely to choose
  *and* are most likely to surface knock-on consequences worth
  playing.
- What Stillness's Conclave contact specifically costs. She knows.
  I do not. I will find out when she has to pay it.
- Whether Holdfast-Two would actually relocate if told. The hab
  council exists; the residents have feelings; this is its own
  scene if the players go there.
