# The Vez Plate

A kinetic-tuned ringglass plate, roughly thirty centimetres square,
mounted on the workshop bench at Yeoward. It is what the kettle sits
on. The kettle is always on, because the plate maintains a steady
low-amplitude kinetic draw against ambient resonance and the kettle's
base, which is sized to the plate, converts that draw to a continuous
low heat.

It is named the Vez Plate because Vez Lush salvaged it. The Dropline
calls it the plate, the kettle plate, or just the plate. The full
name is reserved for the kind of formal moment that almost never
comes up.

## Description

A ringglass plate, slightly off-square, with the corner-rounded edges
that mark its origin as ring-era infrastructure rather than modern
salvage cut. Crews who handle it will note that it is *cool* to
touch on its underside even when the kettle is steaming on top. The
heat goes one direction. This is not a property the Dropline has
ever reproduced with another plate.

## Provenance

Salvaged by Vez Lush from the same Hollow fragment that yielded
Bonecord. Vez tested both on Stillrun Hab with **The Held Stillness**
before she mounted them at Yeoward. The plate predates the antenna
damper in the workshop by two days.

## Current status

In daily use. Workshop bench, Yeoward. The kettle is on it.

## What it does

- Maintains a continuous kinetic-band draw from ambient resonance,
  converted to low heat through the kettle's base.
- Heat is one-directional: top warm, underside cool. This is what
  lets the workshop run the plate continuously without bench damage.
- Functionally, the kettle is always boiling. The water level
  refills from a workshop tap on a slow drip.
- Folk-Tuner read: *the plate is listening for the kettle.* This is
  Stillness's reading, not anyone else's. She has not explained what
  she means. She has said this once, to Vez, before Vez left.

## Hook

Crews pulled out by the Dropline remember the kettle in the workshop
more clearly than they remember the rescue. The plate is what makes
the kettle possible. The plate is also a piece of ring-era ringglass
that does *something* for which the Dropline does not have a full
explanation. It is functioning correctly. It has been functioning
correctly for fourteen years. Anyone who looks closely at it is
asking the question Vez was asking the day she found it.

If Stillness is right, the plate is more than a heating element. The
plate's significance is held back from `shared/lore/`; in the
player-facing version it is "the kettle plate." In this DM-only
entry, it is the thing the players will eventually have to ask about.
