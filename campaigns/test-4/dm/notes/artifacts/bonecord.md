# Bonecord

The Hollow receiver's antenna damper. A thirty-centimetre length of
braided ringglass strands wound around a kinetic-tuned core, mounted
in a refurbished Span-quality housing that was salvaged with the rest
of the Dropline's antenna kit. Vez Lush named it Bonecord the day she
mounted it: *because if it goes, the rest goes too.*

## Description

A damper, in this context, is what keeps the receiver's intake
bandwidth narrow enough that the Hollow's transmission registers
cleanly against the surrounding Echo River bleed. Without the damper
the signal is noise. With a *correctly tuned* damper, the
transmission is clear in pre-Glassfall Sitharian.

Bonecord looks like a pale braid in a polished mount. Crews who do
not know what it is have walked past it. Crews who do know touch the
mount when they leave the workshop, the way crews everywhere touch
something for luck.

## Provenance

Salvaged by Vez Lush from the same fragment that became the Hollow.
She tuned it herself, tested it on Stillrun Hab with **The Held
Stillness** (then a younger fae, recently emigrated from a
high-bandwidth hab in the Sable Crescent), and mounted it at Yeoward
the week the Dropline opened. It has been in continuous use for
fourteen years.

## Current status

Failing. Has been failing for the last eight months. The drift in its
tuning is slow, regular, and not consistent with age-related decay.

**Held Stillness** has been retuning it on Dern's maintenance windows,
quietly, *for the Dropline.* Someone *else* has been retuning it on
*different* maintenance windows, the *other way.* Stillness only
realised this last month and has not told Dern. Dern suspects the
Conclave but cannot prove it.

## What it does

- Holds the receiver's intake bandwidth narrow enough to read the
  Hollow's transmission clean against Echo River bleed.
- Filters out a class of resonance interference that would otherwise
  make the Yeoward workshop unusable as a listening post.
- *Has, demonstrably, been intent-responsive on the receiver's
  behalf:* in the fourteen years since Bonecord was mounted, the
  Hollow's transmission has *not been heard outside Yeoward.* Other
  parties have tried. The transmission is in the air; the receivers
  do not catch it. Whether this is a property of Bonecord, of the
  Hollow, or of both together — nobody at Yeoward has tested.

If Bonecord fails entirely, the receiver becomes useless. If Bonecord
is *replaced*, the new damper does not necessarily inherit the
intent-responsiveness, because the property may be Vez's, not the
component's.

## Hook

The damper is failing. The Dropline knows. They do not know
*everything* about why. Replacing it is a near-term goal in the
org-level notes (`dm/notes/organization.md`); the question of how —
and *whether* — to replace it is an open one. If the players are the
ones to engage with this, Vez Lush's absence comes back into focus
fast.
