# The Dropline

The party's home org. A small Shear-edge response and salvage cooperative.
What they do is take calls — distress, salvage, courier, the occasional
cordon-edge contract — that the official chains have either dropped or
can't reach in time. They are not heroes. They are people with one good
kite-hauler, a refurbished Span-quality antenna, and a kettle that is
always on.

## Identity

**Founded:** roughly 14 years before campaign start, in the wake of the
Coriolis Breach refit work.

**Founding moment:** not the Breach itself — the *second* incident, the
one nobody outside the small Shear circuits remembers. A kite-hauler crew
out near the Yantis Drift called for help on a Compact emergency frequency
that the Compact had quietly allowed to go silent the previous quarter
(budget consolidation, the same kind of paperwork that closed three other
relays that year). Vez Lush — folk Tuner, ex-refit hand — happened to be
listening on a refurbished antenna pulled from a downed ring fragment.
She heard the whole call. By the time anyone official could have
responded the crew was dead.

A handful of Coriolis veterans pooled severance, Vez supplied the antenna,
Dern Morr signed the Compact paperwork, and the Dropline opened that
month. It has been operating continuously since.

**Where they sit:**

- *Legally:* a Compact-recognized salvage cooperative with humanitarian
  registration through the Displacement Council. Recognition is reviewed
  every two years. Review is due this season.
- *Politically:* nobody's first call. Tolerated by the Compact. Trusted
  by the small habs in their stretch of Shear. Mostly invisible to
  Sithari and the Accord. The Council has used them once for a job they
  couldn't justify staffing themselves; that contact has not been renewed.
- *Geographically:* operating out of **Yeoward**, a small station built
  into a stable Shear-edge debris cluster. About a day's burn from a
  fallen-but-not-dead ring fragment the crew calls **the Hollow**, which
  still glows on certain frequencies and occasionally transmits.

**The detail that makes it *this* org and not a generic version:** their
workshop has a kettle on a kinetic-tuned ringglass plate that was Vez
Lush's first salvage. The kettle is always on. They do not talk about
why. People who have been pulled out of bad situations by the Dropline
remember the kettle in the workshop more clearly than they remember the
faces.

## Goals

**Near-term (this season):**

- Keep the Hollow station receiver running.
- Renew the Compact recognition review (due this season).
- Replace the antenna damper that has been failing on the receiver.

**Long-term:**

- Remain solvent.
- Take calls others won't.

That second one isn't strategy. It's purpose. Strategy is everything they
do to stay alive long enough to keep doing it.

## Constraints

- Cannot operate inside Council cordons without explicit Council contract.
- Cannot legally salvage from intact ring habs (Compact rules; everyone's
  rules).
- Owe a standing, unwritten debt to **Sable Korr**, manager of a Vantara
  waystation that covered them through a thin year three winters ago.
  The debt is not what it appears (see Secrets).
- Cannot afford a full medic. They contract Shei (the Held Stillness),
  the fae folk Tuner from the nearest fueling hab, on a per-call basis.
- Cannot afford to lose the *Lush Catch* (their kite-hauler). She is
  named after Vez. There is no second hauler.

**Resources at start:**

- One working kite-hauler, *Lush Catch*. Mid-bandwidth kinetic array,
  a generation old, well-maintained. Crew capacity 6 plus pilot.
- Two skiffs (one structural-tuned, one signal-tuned for relay work).
- The relay station at Yeoward.
- The Hollow receiver and its failing antenna damper.
- A small inventory of refined ringglass for repairs (~3 weeks of normal
  operations).
- Compact recognition (pending review).
- Council humanitarian registration (intact).

## Existing members the players inherit

**Dern Morr** — Hab-Worlder, late forties. Leader. Former Coriolis Breach
refit lead. Founded the Dropline with Vez. Quiet to a fault, decides
slowly, will not raise her voice. Has a daughter on a shuttered hab
called Threshold-Rim that has not answered comms in eight years.

**Rusk Talt** — Hab-Worlder, early thirties. Fixer. Compact arbitrator's
nephew. Handles paperwork, recognition reviews, courier permits, all the
administrative drag the Dropline requires to stay legal. He is the
reason recognition keeps getting renewed. He knows it. He does not lord
it. He is also genuinely good at the job.

**Vez Lush** — Hab-Worlder. Absent founder. Folk Tuner. Heard the
original kite-hauler call. Left the Dropline four years ago after a job
at a Bloom-edge cordon went badly. Lives now on a hab three days' burn
from Yeoward. Has not responded to messages in eighteen months. Dern has
stopped sending them. Rusk hasn't.

**Shei (the Held Stillness)** — fae, folk Tuner-medic, on call. Lives on
the hab that hosts the Dropline's nearest fueling depot. Sometimes the
kettle is for her.

The four of them are the org's continuous staff. PCs join as the active
operating crew — the people who fly the *Lush Catch* and answer the
calls Dern and Rusk decide to take.

## Capabilities the org typically needs

(*Not* a role chart. Loose list of leverage the org operates by. Players
will invent characters that come at these from unexpected angles.)

- Someone who can read a Shear forecast better than the Compact does.
- Someone the Council will actually return calls from. (Currently
  nobody. This is a real and visible gap.)
- Someone who can keep the antenna running and the kinetic array tuned.
- Someone who can sit in a room with a desperate person and make them
  feel heard.
- Someone who can say no to a job, in a way that holds.

That last is currently entirely Dern. If anything happens to her, the
Dropline will say yes to something it shouldn't. This is structural.

## Advancement track

Four stages. Bonuses get resolved at the moment of advance based on what
the table cares about — no pre-baked numbers.

**1. Stringed (current).** Recognized by Compact, tolerated by Council,
occasional Vantara contracts. Solvent month to month. Three of the four
small habs in their stretch of Shear consider the Dropline a first call.

**2. Trusted.** Council issues a standing contract. Vantara routes calls
to them by default for their region. Fewer cash-flow crises. The
recognition review becomes a formality.

*Trigger:* complete a Council contract cleanly + pass the Compact
recognition review.

**3. Hub.** The de facto first-response node for their stretch of Shear.
Other small crews start asking to affiliate. The Dropline has to decide
who to bring in. Capacity grows. So does visibility.

*Trigger:* take a call no other crew could have taken, in a way that
becomes a story people tell — i.e., the players solve a problem at a
scale that gets remembered outside their immediate circuit.

**4. Pressed.** Visibility brings attention they didn't want. A rival
faction (likely Conclave; see Secrets) notices what the Dropline sits on
top of. The cost of being the answer becomes visible. Decisions get
harder.

*Trigger:* the Dropline either deliberately engages or fends off a
rival's interest in the Hollow.

I'll create durable public clocks at trigger-time, not in advance — but
I'll make one now for the Compact recognition review, since it's a
visible existing pressure.

## Public pressure clock

`recognition-review` — 4 segments. Where it ticks:

1. Submit paperwork (Rusk's job; can be done immediately, or delayed).
2. Compact arbitrator visit (mid-season).
3. Audit response.
4. Renewal vote.

This is a public clock. The players can see it. The Compact's vote can
go either way and will respond to evidence. The clock is in
`shared/clocks.md` once it exists.

## Secrets and agendas (DM-only)

The Dropline can keep things from the party. These belong here, not in
the player-facing entry. They surface through play.

- **The Hollow's transmission.** The receiver in the Yeoward workshop
  catches a weekly transmission from a hab that has been officially
  silent since the Glassfall. The transmission is in pre-Glassfall
  Sitharian and says the same thing every time, with minor variation in
  exactly two words. Dern has it cataloged. She has not told the
  Council. She has not told the Conclave. She has, in fourteen years,
  not replied.
- **Dern's daughter.** Is on Threshold-Rim, a shuttered hab three orbits
  out. The hab has not answered contact in eight years. The Dropline's
  founding grant from Coriolis-era severance was Dern's portion of a
  hardship payout for *her daughter's presumed death.* Dern does not
  presume.
- **The Vantara debt.** The waystation Sable Korr manages takes silent
  fueling contracts from a Coremark remnant operating out of a hidden
  Shear station. Sable knows. Dern knows. The "thin-year coverage" was
  Sable trading silence for silence. Neither has said it out loud.
  Rusk does not know.
- **The damper.** The antenna damper that's failing is not failing from
  age. Someone has been retuning it during scheduled maintenance
  windows. Dern suspects the Conclave, who would prefer the Hollow's
  transmissions reach their custodianship rather than the Dropline's
  cabinet. She cannot prove it. She has not told anyone.

## Constraint check

The org has secrets, agendas, internal politics, and long-term goals the
players don't know. It is *not* outright hostile to the party. Dern's
care is real. Rusk's competence is real. The kettle is real. Friction
yes; predator no. The party is the operating crew, not disposable
labor. They will be told things slowly, as the trust accrues.

## Hooks for character creation (for me, not for them yet)

- The Dropline needs someone the Council will return calls from. PC
  could be a former Council hand who left, or a Bloom-displaced
  survivor with Council contacts.
- The Dropline needs someone who can read the Shear. PC could be ex-
  Compact, ex-Coremark, ex-pirate, ex-Ratter.
- The Dropline needs someone the small habs trust. PC could be a folk
  Tuner from one of the four habs in their circuit.
- The Dropline needs someone who can hold a hard room. PC could be
  anything — a former hospice worker, a Bloom Coalition veteran, a
  Sithari cosmopolitan who left for reasons. The skill is the
  qualification.

These are *not* roles to fill. They are leverage the org needs. PCs
will come at them sideways. That is the point.
