# Threshold-Rim

A shuttered hab, three orbits out from Yeoward. Has not answered comms
in eight years. Officially listed as *presumed total population
loss* in the Council's hardship-payout records. Dern Morr's daughter
was on Threshold-Rim when contact was lost. Dern does not presume.

This locale is *not* on the Dropline's working route. It is the
locale that *exists for the campaign* — the one the players will
hear about in passing for many turns before any decision about
visiting becomes a real decision.

## Sensory (for when it matters)

If anyone reaches Threshold-Rim before a player decision warrants it:
the hab still glows on emergency-amber low-power frequencies.
Air-mix systems read nominal at extreme low. The dock has been
unused for eight years; the dust in the dock interior is millimetre-
deep. Whether anyone is alive inside the hab is a question the
Dropline does not yet have an answer to.

## Feature that does something

This locale's *narrative* feature is that it is *unanswered*. Dern
sends a comm to Threshold-Rim once every six months. There has been
no reply for eight years. She has continued to send them anyway.
Rusk knows. The rest of the Dropline does not.

## Who is usually there

Officially: nobody. The hab is shuttered.

## Hooks

- The Halterflock's argument. The hab the three speakers are talking
  about *might* be Threshold-Rim. The DM has not yet committed. This
  is play-to-find-out territory.
- The Hollow's weekly transmission. Same possibility. Same decision
  not yet made.
- Dern's daughter. The hardest hook in the campaign. Will surface
  when someone decides.
