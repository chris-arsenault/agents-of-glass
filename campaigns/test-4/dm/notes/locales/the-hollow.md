# The Hollow

A fallen-but-not-dead ring fragment one day's burn from Yeoward. The
Dropline's listening axis. The fragment is large enough that crews
who land on it call the various sections by name (the *long deck*,
the *plate room*, the *south antennae*). The Hollow itself, as a
locale, is the *intact transmitting volume* — the part of the
fragment that still glows on certain frequencies and emits a weekly
transmission that the Yeoward receiver catches through Bonecord.

The Hollow is not a Bloom Zone. It is not haunted in any conventional
sense. It is a piece of ring-era infrastructure that has *partial
power*. What is powering it is not known. The Dropline has a working
hypothesis: the same intent-responsive logic that lives in the Span
and in Bonecord lives in the Hollow's transmitter and is the reason
neither the Conclave nor the Compact has been able to read the
transmission from outside Yeoward's antenna line.

## Sensory

Cold. The fragment's interior, where the Dropline's crews land for
maintenance, runs at ambient cold storage temperatures regardless of
external conditions. The interior surfaces — corridors, deck panels,
the plate room walls — *hum*. The hum is below audible. Tuners
describe it as *a held tone, sustained, polite.* Non-Tuners describe
it as a feeling that they should walk softly.

Visually: pale-green emergency illumination on the active corridors,
no illumination at all on the inactive ones. The Dropline's
walk-throughs follow the active corridors only.

## Feature that does something

The transmitter. Located in what the Dropline calls the *south
antennae* deck. Active. Transmitting weekly. Pre-Glassfall Sitharian.
The same content with minor variation in two specific words. The
Dropline has fourteen years of recordings.

The Hollow is also, geographically, the source of:
- The kettle plate (Vez salvaged it from the long deck).
- Bonecord (Vez salvaged it from a shielded mount in the south
  antennae deck).
- The Halterflock surfacing pocket, which is in the debris field
  *around* the Hollow, not on it.

## Who is usually there

Nobody. The Dropline maintains a quarterly inspection schedule. The
Conclave has tried to send teams three times in the last decade; on
all three occasions their navigation routed them around the Hollow
the way it routes around the Span. Whether this is coincidence or
intent-responsiveness is an open question Dern has not asked the
Conclave about. Drosk Tann's Stainmark Reach is two days' burn from
the Hollow in a different direction; he has not come here.

## Hooks

- The weekly transmission. (Secret. The hab the speakers are at.)
- The Halterflock. (Same hab, different speakers, different argument.)
- Whatever else is in the Hollow that nobody has asked about. The
  Dropline's inspection schedule has covered roughly nine percent of
  the fragment.
