# The Halsworn Cordon

A Council Bloom-edge cordon four hours' burn from Yeoward. Formally
in operation. Functionally at quarter-coverage. Two small habs sit
inside its current expansion radius. Veni Karst is the cordon lead.
Halsworn is where the Dropline keeps getting pulled.

## Sensory

The cordon's outer ring of damping buoys is visible as a faint pale-
blue arc on a kite's resonance display, stretching across roughly
forty kilometres of orbital volume. Six buoys are missing or dim. The
pattern of the gaps — clustered toward the western face — is not
random. The Bloom Zone interior is invisible to ordinary instruments
but reads as a *pressure* on a Tuner's filter: a sense that the
resonance environment is being *pulled* toward the zone, slowly, the
way a dropped pen rolls across a tilted desk.

Halsworn Station itself is a single rectangular structure on a
tethered debris platform at the cordon's eastern face. Three room
interiors, two-person rotation, supply skiff cycle every twelve days.

## Feature that does something

The buoy line. Each buoy is a stillwater-loaded damping unit that
the Conclave provides through the Council. Buoys fail. Failed buoys
get replaced when the Council can. Currently, six are unreplaced. The
gap on the western face is large enough that the Bloom Zone's growth
curve is registering an asymmetric pull — the zone is expanding
faster on the side the cordon is failing on.

## Who is usually there

Veni Karst. Her partner on rotation, who is currently a junior
Council Tuner named *Den Halverk* (introduced if relevant; not
otherwise central). The Dropline, occasionally, off-books. The
Inkline, in its drift on the western face. Drosk Tann, *near* the
cordon, *not on it*, on his Stainmark dive cycle.

## Hooks

- The body in the drift. Veni's off-books request is to recover one.
  It is the body of one of Drosk's six.
- The asymmetric expansion. Has a cause. Probably.
- The cordon's quiet defunding. The Council has *not told the two
  small habs* inside the expansion radius. Veni has, informally, told
  one of the habs' councils. Not the other.
