# Yeoward

The Dropline's home station. A small Shear-edge station built into a
stable debris cluster, day's burn from the Hollow, four hours' burn
from the Halsworn cordon, four hours' burn from Stillrun Hab in the
opposite direction. Population at any given time: between four and
twelve, depending on which crews are out and which are at home.

## Sensory

The workshop floor tilts a quarter-degree to port. Nobody has ever
levelled it because levelling it would mean shutting the kettle plate
down, and the kettle plate has been on for fourteen years. Crews
walking through the workshop unconsciously compensate. New arrivals
limp for a day until they stop noticing.

## Feature that does something

The workshop bench: kettle plate, kettle, the receiver tied to the
Hollow antenna, **Bonecord** in its mount above the bench. The bench
is the Dropline's listening post and its kitchen and its decision-
making table. Most of the things that get decided at Yeoward are
decided at this bench, with the kettle on, by Dern saying something
quiet and waiting.

## Who is usually there

Dern Morr, when she is not on a call. Rusk Talt, almost always —
Rusk does not like flying. Whichever crew is between calls. The Held
Stillness, when the workshop has summoned her or when she has chosen
to come.

## Hooks

- The receiver. Catches the Hollow's weekly transmission. Catches
  whatever else the antenna pulls.
- Bonecord. Failing.
- The kettle plate. Listening for the kettle, per Stillness.
- The dispatch ledger. Open on the bench. Always.
