# Arc Plan: Halsworn Edge

DM-only working document. The opening arc. Pulls the Dropline toward
the cordon's western face — the body recovery, the dim buoy, and the
asymmetric Bloom expansion. Stainmark surfaces underneath, not in
front. Drosk does not enter the arc as a face until the second or
third scene at earliest.

## 1. Stakes question

**Who keeps the Halsworn cordon's western face — and what do they
spend to keep it?**

I do not know the answer. The Council formally cannot. The Dropline
might, at a real cost. Stillness's Conclave contacts might be spent
on it. The two small habs inside the expansion radius might
self-organise. Sable Korr's audit might trade resources sideways for
it. Drosk Tann might collapse it without ever appearing in a scene.
The answer surfaces from play.

## 2. Threats

### Threat A — The Halsworn Cordon (failing)

- A failing system, not a person. The cordon itself, doing what
  failing systems do.
- **Impulse:** *to expand asymmetrically along the western face.*
  Each tick, more drift, more Bloom-edge instability, more dim
  buoys.
- **Between scenes:** the `halsworn-coverage` clock drains. Buoys
  fail in their natural cadence. The Inkline's drift extends.
  Veni's two-person rotation keeps showing up alone.

### Threat B — Drosk Tann / Stainmark Holdings

- Antagonist (`dm/notes/npcs/drosk-tann.md`,
  `dm/notes/factions/stainmark-holdings.md`).
- **Impulse:** *to get one more dive into the Stainmark seam, and
  one more, and one more.* The duskgrain habit is the engine.
- **Between scenes:** the `stainmark-stir` clock fills. Drosk's
  next dive is on a roughly thirty-day cadence. Each dive bleeds a
  little more into the cordon's western face.

### Threat C — The Council's institutional silence

- The Council's regional leadership *has decided* — not
  formally — that closing Halsworn is the move. The decision is
  not yet on paper. Veni Karst is operating without being told.
- **Impulse:** *to retreat without saying.* The Council closes by
  attrition, not by announcement.
- **Between scenes:** the cordon's defunding accelerates. Veni's
  rotation is shortened. Replacement buoy requisitions are deferred.

### Threat D — The Inkline

- Bloom-corrupted apex predator (`dm/notes/creatures/the-inkline.md`).
- **Impulse:** *to feed on awareness in the western drift.* The
  failing buoys are extending its drift radius without it doing
  anything; that is enough.
- **Between scenes:** drifts further west; eventually overlaps the
  body recovery vector and the K-9 buoy maintenance crawl.

These four threats *share the western face.* They are not loose;
they are the same forty-kilometre arc seen from four different
angles. Pressure on any one of them moves the others.

## 3. Clocks

### `halsworn-coverage` (drain, 3 segments, hidden)

Already set. From `dm/notes/factions/displacement-council.md`. This
is Threat A's clock and Threat C's clock simultaneously.

- 3/3 → 2/3 → 1/3 → 0/3.
- Currently 3/3. A buoy K-9 failure (Threat A) advances it. Council
  silence (Threat C) advances it from a different vector — a
  Council non-decision lands as a tick.

### `stainmark-stir` (fill, 5 segments, hidden)

Already set. From `dm/notes/factions/stainmark-holdings.md`. This
arc *does not require* this clock to fill — it is a campaign-level
clock that runs underneath. The arc may or may not advance it. If
the Dropline never investigates the body, the clock fills on its
ordinary cadence. If they do, they may *temporarily slow it*
through pressure on Sable's fueling line, or *accelerate it* by
spooking Drosk.

### `inkline-drift` (fill, 3 segments, arc-local, hidden)

New for this arc. The Inkline's drift expansion as a function of
buoy failure. To be set when the arc activates.

- 0/3 — current drift radius (manageable).
- 1/3 — drift overlaps the K-9 coverage cone after K-9 fails.
- 2/3 — drift reaches the body recovery vector.
- 3/3 — drift reaches the Talvet hab approach lane.

## 4. Possible end-states

Five plausible silhouettes. Not commitments — just the shape of
what the arc could end as.

1. **The Dropline holds the western face for one season.** They
   sub-build a damping unit out of salvage, pay Stillness's
   Conclave-contact cost, and recover the body. The Council
   declines to formally close Halsworn; the cordon limps. Drosk's
   next dive is harder for him because the body recovery surfaced
   the duskgrain residue. The recognition review benefits from a
   documented cordon-stabilisation incident. Stainmark thread
   carries forward.
2. **The Dropline does not hold; the Council closes Halsworn.**
   The cordon authority lapses formally. The two small habs
   scramble. The Bloom Zone expands across the cordon's old
   footprint. The Dropline becomes the de-facto answer for the
   Halsworn region, without the Council's coverage. Drosk's
   pressure is reduced (the Stainmark Reach is now in
   un-cordoned space).
3. **The body recovery surfaces Stainmark explicitly.** Players
   read the duskgrain residue, follow the dive vector, and arrive
   at Pin Tracery before the campaign expects them to. The
   antagonist arc compresses; Drosk surfaces this arc rather than
   later. The cordon may or may not hold; the campaign question
   shifts to "what to do about Stainmark."
4. **Sable's audit collapses the fueling line first.** The
   Stillrun audit hook activates inside this arc; the Dropline's
   working radius halves; they cannot maintain Halsworn coverage
   from a halved radius and the cordon closes by default. The
   campaign moves to a different scarcity question — fueling
   rather than cordon.
5. **The arc is *displaced* — the players do not engage with
   Halsworn at all.** The recognition review consumes their
   attention; the body drifts; the cordon closes without them;
   the Stainmark thread surfaces through the Stillrun audit
   downstream. This is a legitimate ending and I will not punish
   it.

I will not pick. The arc resolves into one of these or into
something I haven't listed.

## 5. Strong start

**Yeoward, Rusk's quarterly courier rotation. Mid-morning. Kettle on.**

Rusk is at the workshop bench with the Compact recognition folder
open and Veni's note paper-clipped to the inside cover. The note is
in Veni's handwriting, on a Halsworn Station letterhead that looks
older than the Station. *One body, western drift, K-9 coverage,
sleeping bay slot in trade.* The note also says, near the bottom,
*saw K-9 at thirty percent on Tuesday.*

Dern is in the workshop. The kettle is on. Stillness has the
Bonecord drift signature on a handheld and is waiting for someone
to ask. The recognition folder is two-thirds prepared; Rusk needs
the audit-response section closed in ten days.

The hook the party sees: the note, the folder, the kettle. The
question the party will ask: *do we go.* The answer is theirs.

## 6. Nodes

### Node 1 — Yeoward workshop (the hook moment)

- Rusk, Dern, Stillness. The note. The folder. The kettle.
- **Learn:** the body exists. K-9 is failing. The recognition
  folder is due. Veni asked through Rusk, not directly.
- **Clues out:** to Halsworn (the body, the buoy); to Pell's
  office (the recognition folder, an indirect mention of the
  Stillrun audit cross-file); to Stillness's contacts (her
  Conclave card she has not played).

### Node 2 — Halsworn Station (Veni's place)

- Veni Karst, her one Tuner partner Den Halverk, the cordon
  outer ring of buoys.
- **Learn:** the western face's asymmetric expansion pattern. The
  K-9 degrade curve. The Council has not told Holdfast-Two. Veni's
  formal reporting language and her informal language differ in a
  specific way. Two of Drosk's six are flagged in Veni's
  handwritten margin notes by drift signature, not by name.
- **Clues out:** to the body recovery vector (drift readings);
  to the Holdfast-Two hab (which Veni has not informed); to
  Stillness's Conclave-contact path (Veni mentions, off-handed,
  that the buoy line is the Conclave's grief).

### Node 3 — The body in the drift

- Western drift, four kilometres past the K-9 buoy gap.
- **Learn:** duskgrain residue on the body's outer suit. A drift
  vector consistent with a Stainmark-direction approach. Personal
  effects: a microcavity-rack key, a hand-folded note in clipped
  Hab-Worlder shorthand referencing *the cold bay,* a small
  resonance focus tuned to a band the Conclave would recognise.
- **Clues out:** to Pell's office (the microcavity-rack key
  matches a Stainmark-registered model in the Compact archive,
  if anyone cross-references); to Stillness (the resonance focus
  reads as duskgrain-adjacent); to the Stainmark Reach itself
  (the drift vector traces back).

### Node 4 — Pell's office (the recognition folder + Stainmark cross-file)

- Pell Andrik, three piles of paperwork.
- **Learn:** the recognition folder's two audit-response questions.
  Pell will mention, if asked, that the Stainmark annual nominal-
  fee renewal is "still on the third pile, like clockwork." If
  asked again, that there is a Sithari archive cross-reference
  request open on a 1996 filing he has not had time to look at.
- **Clues out:** to the Stainmark grandmother's filing (clerical
  irregularity, secret 4); to the Stillrun audit (Pell saw the
  Vantara cross-file).

### Node 5 — Stillness's quiet (her Conclave card, her Bonecord reading)

- Stillness, her handheld with the Bonecord drift signature, her
  Conclave-side relationships she has not used in eleven years.
- **Learn:** Bonecord is being retuned by two hands (secret 3).
  The Conclave wants to read the Hollow's transmission and cannot
  (secret 7). She has felt Drosk at the cordon edge twice without
  knowing his name. A buoy-replacement requisition through her
  Conclave contact would cost her something specific she would
  not name to Dern in advance.
- **Clues out:** to the Conclave (politics of the buoy supply);
  to Drosk (Stillness has felt him); to the Hollow / Halterflock
  thread (secret 1, 2 — the same containment failure from two
  sides).

The graph: Yeoward fans out to Halsworn, Pell, and Stillness.
Halsworn fans out to the body. The body fans back to Pell and to
Stillness. Pell fans to Stillness's territory through the Stainmark
filing. The arc forms a five-node cluster with no required order.

### Three-clue-rule audit for arc-required reveals

- **The body is one of Drosk's six (secret 8) — required.**
  Clues at Halsworn (drift vector), the body itself (residue), and
  Pell's office (microcavity-rack key cross-reference). Three.
- **The cordon is being closed by attrition (Threat C).**
  Clues at Halsworn (Veni's two languages), Pell (no replacement
  requisitions in the Compact cross-file), and the Council's
  institutional non-response trail. Three.

## 8. Arc-specific secrets

Three to seven atomic facts, players do not know yet, deliberately
unattached to scenes.

1. **Veni Karst has been writing two reports.** A formal one to
   Council regional leadership, in clean handwriting, that says
   the cordon holds. An informal one in margin notes, on the
   reverse of the same paper, that names buoys, drift vectors,
   and people. The informal one is the real one. *DM-knows;
   surfaces if the players sit with her at Halsworn for a quiet
   half-hour.*

2. **The body has a hand-folded note in Hab-Worlder shorthand
   referencing *the cold bay.*** The note is operational — a
   shift handover, undated. The shorthand is from the Sable
   Crescent fueling line; Sable Korr would recognise the
   handwriting. *Play-to-find-out whether anyone shows it to
   Sable.*

3. **The dead person's name is Hess Korel.** Hab-Worlder, late
   thirties. Drosk's recruit from the year the Stainmark Reach
   yields doubled. *DM-knows; recoverable from the Stainmark
   register if the players ever reach it, or from Sable
   recognising the shorthand if they show her the note.*

4. **The Council's regional leadership has tabled a draft
   Halsworn closure document.** It is on the desk of a Council
   regional named Tarrick Voss. The document is not signed. Tarrick
   is waiting on either a buoy-resupply commitment from the
   Conclave (which will not come) or a formal Dropline cooperative
   agreement (which would require a recognition vote). *DM-knows;
   becomes public-pressure if the players force the question via
   Pell's office.*

5. **Stillness's Conclave contact is named Halloran Vace.**
   Stillwater archivist, mid-bandwidth. Owes Stillness for an
   eleven-year-old debt that involved a duskgrain-laced sample
   nobody has talked about since. *DM-knows; surfaces only if the
   players ask Stillness directly to use the contact.*

6. **The K-9 buoy's stillwater is the last of a batch the
   Conclave allocated to Halsworn five years ago.** Replacement
   from the Conclave is technically possible but politically
   improbable — the Conclave would have to acknowledge the cordon
   as worth more stillwater than archive. *World-knows-but-the-
   Dropline-doesn't, until they ask.*

## 9. Done criteria

The arc resolves when **the cordon's western face is either held,
formally closed, or the question of who keeps it has shifted to a
different threat.**

Concretely: the arc ends when (a) the Council issues either a
formal closure or a formal recognition of Dropline cooperative
coverage; or (b) the body recovery surfaces the Stainmark thread
with enough force that the campaign's antagonist arc takes
priority; or (c) the players actively decline the western face
and the world resolves the question without them (Council closes
by default, the two habs scramble, the cordon authority lapses).

I would consider abandoning this arc if the players' attention
moves wholly to the recognition review or to Sable's audit, the
cordon's clock runs out off-screen, and the arc becomes a
historical fact rather than a live pressure. That is a closure
failure I have to be willing to accept rather than drag the
players back to it.
