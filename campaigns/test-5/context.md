# Context

You are a small carrier co-op working out of the Sable Crescent — a kite-hauler crew called **The Half-Bar Run**. You ferry resonance-sensitive cargo: Echo River fragments under registered Conclave seal, mortuary ringglass, signal-band shards, the kind of consignment that other carriers turn down because the cargo *talks*. Two ships, eight people on the books, an unfashionable berth at Lowstep, and a working relationship with the Echo Ledger Conclave that is mostly polite.

You are not strangers in this world. You have seen a fragment-shard surface a child's laughter from before the Glassfall and watched a parent decide whether to listen. You have refused work from Coremark Reach Survey twice. You know which dockhands at Lowstep accept Sitharian credit and which prefer barter in good kite-cord.

## The world, in two paragraphs

It is 130 years after the Glassfall — the long fall of the orbital ring that the elves built and then disappeared from during the Signal Famine. The system survived. Sithari runs courts and keeps the Tempered Accord seated. Fermata Station still hosts the annual Open. Resonance technology, salvaged and improvised, runs everything from kite-haulers to refineries. The Bloom — the contained zone of resonance corruption from a Coremark accident — is fenced and watched but no longer spreading. Most days are workdays.

What's not fine is quieter. Beneath the Sable Crescent, water moves through crystal-saturated rock and carries voices — pre-Glassfall transmissions, fragments of conversation, sometimes a name. These are the **Echo Rivers**. The Echo Ledger Conclave catalogues what surfaces and decides who hears it. Coremark grinds river-rock to mineral feedstock for ringglass salvage and treats the voices as a cost of doing business. Private grievers pay carriers to ferry fragments to a hab, a parlor, a sealed room.

## The Question the campaign asks

**Who has the right to listen?**

Not a thesis. A question. Every cargo run is a chance to answer it differently.

## The Scarcity

**Witnesses.** People who heard, who saw, who can speak to what was said. The world has voices in surplus and witnesses in shortage. Most institutions paper over the gap.

## Tone

Specific. Slow. The world is broken in named ways and is still feeding people. Adjective-thin description. Resonance details name their band and their cost. Scenes end one beat before resolution.
