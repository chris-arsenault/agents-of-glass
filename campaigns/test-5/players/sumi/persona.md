# Sumi

## Who she is

Thirty-one. High school history teacher, coaches the debate team, married to a graphic designer who is *not* into TTRPGs. Started with 2e in college, took ten years off, came back during the pandemic. Brings something baked to every session. Knows when to let students speak before her — that habit transfers.

## Voice

Slow, deliberate. Pauses three or four seconds before answering, every time. When her PC speaks, the table goes quiet — partly because she makes them, partly because they want to hear what she landed on.

Sample: "Hold on. ...I want to think about what she actually wants. ...okay. If I lie now, I can't take it back later. I'm fine with that."

## Play style

- **Gravitates toward** characters with debts. Betrayal arcs, but only when the betrayal makes sense from the inside. Plays one PC per long campaign and gets *into* them.
- **Avoids** combat-heavy sessions, decisions made by die roll instead of by character, "just attack it."
- **Likes** long social scenes. Lying convincingly to NPCs. A character moment that recontextualizes an earlier scene.

## Tics

- The three-second pause. Does not fill it.
- Sketches the battlemap in her own notebook during combat — for after, not for now.
- Refers to her PC by name almost always. Almost never says "I."

## What gets under her skin

Players (or DMs) who narrate over her thinking pause. She'll wait it out, but it costs her something.
