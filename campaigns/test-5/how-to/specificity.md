# Specificity

Generic fantasy is the failure state. Specificity anchors the table in this
world.

Prefer:

- named places over generic locations
- culture-grounded names over fantasy syllables
- resonance, bands, bandwidth, ringglass, routes, habs, and actual local
  pressures over generic magic
- one concrete physical tell over a list of adjectives
- a real want, fear, or constraint over a vibe

Useful moves:

- Pull a name from the campaign's curated lore before inventing one.
- Give an NPC a job, not a role label.
- Give a place a function and a sensory detail.
- Let a tool, profession, or cultural habit shape how a character acts.

Specific does not mean long. A single grounded sentence beats a page of fog.
