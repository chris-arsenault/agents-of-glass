# Turn 1 — campaign planning, foundation laid

The mug is on the table, soft set. We're not done. This is the start.

## The shape

The campaign asks **Who has the right to listen?** Three questions stacked: who, by what claim, to take in what was said when nobody was meant to be in the room. I won't answer any of the three. The play answers them.

The scarcity is **witnesses**. The Echo Rivers carry voices in surplus; what's short is people who heard, who saw, who can speak to what was said. Every institution in the campaign tries to fill that gap and every fill is political.

## What got authored

- **`dm/foundation.md`** and **`context.md`** (player-facing). Question, scarcity, stance, what this is and isn't, the texture pulls I drew on.
- **The Half-Bar Run** — the party's home. A small carrier co-op based at Lowstep on the Sable Crescent, ferrying Echo River fragments and other resonance-sensitive cargo. Two ships (*Wickrun* active, *Halfsole* in dock), eight people, a Conclave-issued license that's up for ten-year audit in fourteen weeks. Public entry at `shared/lore/organizations/half-bar-run.md`. DM-only working state at `dm/notes/organization.md`.
- **Three factions**: the Echo Ledger Conclave (campaign layer over the world-bible entry), Coremark Reach Survey (subsidiary working the Sable river-rock), the Recursive Weave (new — pirate distribution net for grieving families). Each with goal, tier, hold, and a clock that ticks if nobody opposes.
- **Five NPCs**: Marn Vesh (Run leader, ex-Conclave), Auren Talindra (Sable Office archivist, Marn's old mentor), Pell (Weave handler), Yorek (recurring grieving client), Tev Korrish (Lowstep dockmaster). Plus one antagonist with a clock and a stat block — **Vendrik Solvaen**, Coremark's site lead, who is competent and has a sick daughter.
- **Two creatures**: drift-eels (everyday hazard, soft-mouthed parasite of resonance crystal) and **the Antiphon** (singular, recurring, a Bloom-corrupted resonance entity that wears the voices of the dead).
- **Three named things**: the *Wickrun* itself, **the Mereth Shard** (a pre-Glassfall fragment with an elven voice speaking a Sitharian name), **the Null Bell** (a tuned brass hand-bell that produces five seconds of mid-band silence on a strike).
- **Four locales**: Lowstep, Stillsink (the largest active surfacing in the Crescent), the Conclave Sable Office, and **the Holdcells** — a defunct enforcement house whose old jail cells hold a wooden crate of unclassified pre-Glassfall shards. The Holdcells came out of a web pull about an oral-history archive recovered from a jail cell in the Navajo Nation Library; the shape of *witnesses kept in a place built to hold prisoners* was too good to pass up.
- **Ten secrets** at `dm/notes/secrets.md`, mixing world-knows, DM-knows, and play-to-find-out blanks. Three Clue Rule applied to the load-bearing ones.
- **Five hooks** at `dm/notes/hooks/` — Yorek's fourth fragment, Pell's carriage offer, Tev's berth-consolidation rumor, the Mereth Shard transfer order, the Antiphon at the Holdcells.
- **Two philosophy entries** at `dm/notes/philosophy/` — listening as the moral atom of every scene, and hopecore-with-cost.
- **Opening arc**: ***Cord and Mandate***. Six to ten scenes. The audit, the Mandate, the Cut, and the Weave's offer all on the same six-week clock. Stakes question: *Will the Half-Bar Run survive the audit, and what will it have become to do so?* I do not know the answer. Plan at `arcs/cord-and-mandate/plan.md`, player-facing context at `arcs/cord-and-mandate/context.md`. Arc is active.

## Foundation check

- *Could the party side with Coremark?* Yes.
- *Could the party destroy a fragment?* Yes.
- *Could the party break the Conclave's monopoly?* Yes.
- *Could the party do nothing and just keep running cargo?* Yes. The Run is a livelihood. They don't owe the campaign a heroism arc.

The four-question test passes. Foundation holds.

## What I did not do

- Author the prelude. That's after character creation — it should respond to the actual PCs and party chemistry, not the reverse.
- Pre-decide who the Antiphon is, what's in the Coremark crate, whether Eshaia is gone or just diffuse, or whether Tev's brother is in the *Wickrun* tuner array. Those are play-to-find-out.
- Pre-decide who wins. The end-states for the opening arc are brainstormed at five plausible shapes; I committed to none.
