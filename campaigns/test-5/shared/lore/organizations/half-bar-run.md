# The Half-Bar Run

A registered carrier co-op working out of **Lowstep** on the western edge of the Sable Crescent. Two ships, eight people, one Conclave-issued license that's been current for nineteen years. Specializes in resonance-sensitive cargo — Echo River fragments under Conclave seal, mortuary ringglass, signal-band shards, anything other carriers refuse because the cargo *talks*.

Founded in 2117 by **Marn Vesh**, a former Conclave field-archivist. The name comes from a routing trick — slipping into a half-band silence between standard signal-monitoring frequencies long enough to clear a checkpoint with a delicate consignment. Carriers who run dirty cargo through Conclave-watched lanes still call it "running a half-bar."

## Public reputation

Among grievers and small Conclave field-offices: trusted, slow, will refuse a job before they take a bad one. Among Coremark Reach Survey and the larger industrial carriers: *small*, *unfashionable*, *politically irritating*, *lucky to have a license*. Among Lowstep dockhands: paid up, polite, pays in real marks.

## Named members the party knows about

- **Marn Vesh** — runs the co-op. Hab-Worlder, mid-forties, talks less than she reads. You will recognize her by the chipped enamel mug she carries everywhere.
- **Sela Pruth** — Lowstep fixer. Cargo bonding, customs, sealing protocols. Does not fly. Knows everyone who matters at the dock.
- **Joro** — engineer for the second-crew ship *Halfsole*. Orcish mononym. Has tuned the *Halfsole* engine three times and would tune it again rather than replace it.

There used to be a fourth founding member. Marn does not say the name. The crew has learned not to ask.

## Current standing

- Carrier license active; Conclave ten-year audit due in **14 weeks**.
- Two ships: ***Wickrun*** (small kite-hauler, current primary) and ***Halfsole*** (older, in dock for engine work, expected back in two months).
- Co-op fund: enough for one major refit and one month of crew pay. Not enough for both.
- Owes a quarterly mortuary-cargo report to the Tempered Accord's death-registrar.

## How the party fits

Each PC is on the books — a flying member of the second crew, a specialist, a fixer-in-training, a co-op apprentice, a recent hire, an owed favor working off a debt, whatever shape the player invents. The Run is your **home base**, not your employer in the strict sense. You have a vote at the co-op meeting. You also have a stake in the license surviving the audit.

What the Run needs is *kinds of leverage* — not a role chart. It needs people who can sit at a Conclave audit without flinching. People who can carry a sealed fragment past Coremark surveyors. People who can talk to a grieving family and not make it worse. People who read what nobody reads. People who can fly a kite-hauler in weather the others won't. Bring a character. The leverage will fit.

## What it isn't

It's not a heroic outfit. The Run is a livelihood. It is also, occasionally, a quiet line of resistance against an institution that decides who gets to hear their own dead. Both things are true at once.
