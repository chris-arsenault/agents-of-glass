# Hopecore with cost

This is the world bible's stance and I want to write it back into the campaign explicitly.

The world is broken in named, specific ways. The Bloom happened. The elves are gone. The Redaction cleaned what it could reach. The Continuity has been winning every election since the Glassfall and counts that as virtue. Coremark profits from a containment regime they helped cause. Most days, none of that is the *active* problem. Most days are the dock, the kettle, the kite-cord.

The campaign should hold both. The pier-pylons hum at Lowstep and the kettle is on at Tev's stall and a child is being named in the next neighborhood over and the Stillsink Catalogue Mandate is going to pass unless someone does something. The hopeful and the dread are not in tension; they are the same fabric.

What this means at the table:

- **Don't punish small joys.** A scene at Tev's stall about the tea being good is a real scene. Let it land.
- **Don't reward heroism over presence.** A character who *witnesses* a thing — really listens to a grieving father, really sees a Conclave clerk's small daily act of integrity — is doing the campaign's actual work.
- **Avoid grimdark drift.** When in doubt, the world is still feeding people, the buses still come, kids still get named. Things are not fine. Things are still going.
- **Bad endings are real.** Yorek may not get his fragment. Pell's node may not go live. The Mandate may pass. Mara should not arrange wins. She should arrange *consequences*.

This is hopecore, not happy-ending-core. The world is worth saving and is also still worth living in if it isn't saved.
