# The Mereth Shard

A pre-Glassfall ringglass communication shard, palm-sized, with a tuned crack running diagonally across its face. Holds a single sealed fragment — a recording of an elven voice speaking the name *Mereth Korvanis* and one full sentence after it. The sentence is not in any modern language. The Conclave's central archive holds the master record; the field copy is a Sable Office responsibility.

## Description

Cold to the touch. Hums at 0.4 kHz when active — almost subsonic, felt in the chest more than heard. The crack is *deliberate* — old elven engineering used controlled fractures to lock specific frequency profiles. The shard cannot be re-tuned without destroying it.

## Provenance

Surfaced in 2098 at Stillsink. Catalogued by an Auren Talindra-led field team in 2099. Auren personally maintained the field copy until 2114. Currently in the Sable Office's tertiary vault, accessible to Auren and one other archivist.

## Current status

Field copy in vault. Master in Sithari's deep Conclave archive. The shard has been requested four times by Conclave central since 2120; the Sable Office has stalled four times. The Stillsink Catalogue Mandate, if passed, requires the field copy be transferred to Sithari within 30 days of the vote.

## What it does

- Played in a properly tuned listening room, the shard speaks the name and the sentence. Eight seconds of audio.
- Mereth Korvanis was a Sitharian — a name the Continuity recognizes — but the *recording is elven*. An elf, two centuries ago, said a Sitharian name with the kind of intimacy that suggests they knew her. This is the load-bearing fact. The Conclave has not published it.
- Played in an *improperly* tuned room: drift-risk significantly elevated.

## Hook

If the Sable Office secedes, the shard goes with it. If it stays in Sithari, it gets buried. If it ends up in private hands, the Recursive Weave can copy it and the Conclave will burn the world to recover it. The party will be the ones moving it, regardless of who wins the political fight.
