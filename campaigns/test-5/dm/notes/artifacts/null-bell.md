# The Null Bell

A small hand-bell, brass, with a ringglass clapper. Tuned to *cancel* — when struck, produces a 5-second window of mid-band silence in a 4m radius. The Half-Bar Run's specialty equipment, used to seal a fragment-shard in transit if it begins to drift.

## Description

Looks ordinary. Brass shows wear from forty years of use. The clapper is grey-blue ringglass with a chip taken out of the bottom. Strikes with a sound like *un-ringing* — a sudden absence rather than a tone.

## Provenance

Made in 2093 by a Sithari instrument-maker named **Velin Doraleth** for the Conclave's mobile carriage program. One of twelve. Six are still tracked; six are unaccounted for. The Half-Bar Run owns *number 9*, purchased from a retiring Conclave field-archivist in 2118 — the bell was the archivist's farewell gift to herself.

## Current status

Held in the *Wickrun*'s mortuary bay seal-locker. Used 4-6 times a year. Never replaced; cannot be replaced without commissioning a new one from a current instrument-maker, and Velin Doraleth's tuning notes were lost in 2129.

## What it does

- Striking the bell produces 5 seconds of mid-band cancellation in a 4m radius. During those five seconds, a drifting fragment-shard re-stabilizes; an Echo River pool stops humming; an active drift-eel detaches. The bell does not work on the Antiphon (untested but assumed; nobody has been close enough to try).
- Cost: each strike scratches the clapper microscopically. After ~200 strikes, the clapper fractures. Number 9 has, by Marn's records, been struck 168 times.

## Hook

Coremark would like to know how many strikes are left on Bell 9. The Conclave would like to inventory all 12. The Recursive Weave would *significantly* like to have one of the unaccounted-for six.
