# The Recursive Weave

A distributed, leaderless network of grieving families, rogue archivists, ex-Conclave field workers, and a small number of fae — distinguished by the fact that nobody at the top knows everyone at the bottom. Operates the only reliable underground distribution channel for Echo River fragments outside Conclave control.

## One-sentence identity

The pirate library that gives families their dead back, sometimes recklessly.

## Goal — current

Place a working Echo River cataloguing-and-distribution node at **Stillsink** before the Coremark Cut destroys the surfacing point. They are six weeks out from operational. They need a carrier they can trust to ferry their starter fragment-archive to a hidden cache. They have approached Marn Vesh once. She did not say no.

## Tier

Regional, small. Disproportionate political reach because every Sable Crescent grieving family has heard of them.

## Hold

- A loose web of caches in the Sable lowlands and one at Fermata Station.
- Trusted private buyers in three Sithari neighborhoods.
- A working catalogue, kept on rotating ringglass shards, of fragments the Conclave has refused or delayed.
- The trust of about 200 families across the Crescent.
- Three Coremark dockhands at Lowstep who pass them early extraction schedules.

## Clock — Stillsink Node Operational (5 segments)

1. **Site identified.** Done. The node will sit in the lower channels of the abandoned Holdcells, where pre-Glassfall fragment-shards were stashed two generations ago and never retrieved.
2. **Carrier secured.** Currently negotiating. The Half-Bar Run is the candidate.
3. **Starter archive moved.** A single high-priority shipment, four crates, mortuary-shaped to deflect inspection.
4. **Antenna array bonded.** Tuner work on a buried river-channel. Specialist required.
5. **Node live.** Public *if you know who to ask*. Shifts the regional fragment economy permanently.

If Coremark breaks ground at Springreed before the node is live, the Stillsink surfacing degrades and the node becomes a museum of dead fragments.

## Relationships

- **Echo Ledger Conclave:** mutual non-acknowledgment. The Conclave will not name them. The Weave will not formally challenge them. Both sides know this is a fragile equilibrium.
- **Coremark Reach Survey:** opposed. The Weave's three Coremark contacts are double-edged — the contacts feed Weave intelligence, but Coremark security has identified them and is currently using them.
- **Tempered Accord:** the Accord *unofficially* prefers the Weave exists. Officially, the Accord prosecutes when caught doing so.
- **The Half-Bar Run:** approached, courted, partially trusted. Marn Vesh and a Weave handler called **Pell** have a long phone-tag relationship.

## Texture

The Weave is not virtuous. They have given a fragment to the wrong family — twice, that anyone knows. A 2131 incident at Fermata returned a child's voice to a grandfather who had never met the child and had no claim on her; he listened to the fragment for eleven straight days and then sealed himself in a stillwater room for six. The Weave does not talk about this. The Conclave does, when it serves them.
