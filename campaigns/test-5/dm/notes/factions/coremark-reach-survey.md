# Coremark Reach Survey — Campaign Layer

The world-bible entry covers Coremark — system-wide industrial extractor, complicit in the Bloom origin, still legitimate, still profitable. This file is the **Reach Survey** subsidiary doing river-rock work in the Sable Crescent right now.

## One-sentence identity

The Sable Crescent's largest single employer outside Sithari, harvesting crystal-saturated geology at industrial scale and treating the voices coming up with the rock as a regulatory cost.

## Goal — current

Open the **Springreed Cut** — a new extraction site upstream of Stillsink that would expand operations by an estimated 40%. The Cut requires a Tempered Accord environmental review and a Conclave fragment-recovery agreement. They're maneuvering both right now.

The Cut is the engine of the season. Whatever the party does, Coremark is pushing on this.

## Tier

System-wide, large. Wealthy. Local political access. Real lawyers.

## Hold

- The **Reach Survey** processing facility at the eastern edge of the Sable Crescent, including the only large-scale crystal-water separation works on the surface.
- Permits to extract from twelve registered sites. Three of those sites are within audible range of named Echo River surfacings.
- A long-term feedstock contract with a major Sithari ringglass manufacturer. The manufacturer doesn't ask where the rock comes from.
- A "community engagement office" in Lowstep that holds quarterly public consultations, scheduled at 14:00 on weekdays in Sithari (six hours' travel each way from Lowstep).
- Real, expensive, under-the-table relationships with two members of the Conclave central council — both of whom are voting in favor of the Stillsink Catalogue Mandate.

## Clock — Springreed Cut (5 segments)

1. **Submit Accord environmental review.** Filed. Currently in 60-day comment period.
2. **Sign Conclave fragment-recovery agreement.** In negotiation. Coremark gets cataloguing rights over fragments their extraction surfaces; Conclave gets first publication rights and a 6% material fee.
3. **Break ground.** Earthmoving teams stage at Springreed. Local population at Lowstep is informed via a posted notice.
4. **First extraction.** River-rock cores begin processing. Stillsink's surfacing point is permanently degraded — voices that were audible there for forty years simply stop coming up. Nobody catalogues this.
5. **Operations stable.** Reach Survey adds a second shift. Five-year extraction projection met inside two years.

The party can intervene at multiple segments. They cannot stop Coremark *as an organization*. They can stop, slow, or redirect this specific Cut.

## Relationships

- **Echo Ledger Conclave:** institutionally opposed, transactionally cooperative. The Mandate that hurts the Half-Bar Run is, ultimately, easier for Coremark to negotiate against than the Sable Office's autonomy.
- **Tempered Accord:** Accord audits Coremark every five years. Coremark pays the fines and continues.
- **The Recursive Weave:** Coremark's security has identified three Weave-affiliated dockhands at Lowstep and is letting them keep operating in exchange for early intelligence.
- **The Half-Bar Run:** non-priority. Coremark Reach Survey would prefer they not exist but does not consider them a strategic threat. That assessment can change.

## Texture (web pull)

Coremark's "community engagement" is structurally inaccessible. Quarterly consultations at 14:00 on weekdays in Sithari, with two-week registration windows that close at 09:00 the morning of the meeting. Real, on-paper, legal. The information asymmetry is the point — they'd rather satisfy the form than the substance. Lowstep has not had a single resident attend a Coremark consultation in three years. The Tempered Accord knows. The Tempered Accord has filed eleven complaints. Each complaint resulted in a written response. None of the responses changed the schedule.
