# Secrets and Clues

Ten short facts the players don't know yet. Unattached to specific scenes. I'll deploy whichever fits whichever moment. Three Clue Rule: for the truly required ones, three independent surfaces.

---

1. **Marn Vesh was fired, not "washed out."** She listened to a fragment in 2114 that she was not authorized to listen to. The Conclave knows. They have not used it. The audit in 14 weeks is not — *yet* — a pretext, but it could become one. **Three clues required if this becomes load-bearing.** Sources: Auren Talindra's notebook on her shelf; Stillsink sign-in book entry for 2114-03-19; a former Sable Office janitor at Tev's tea stall.

2. **Eshaia, the missing fae founding member of the Run, calibrated *Wickrun*'s tuner array in a way nobody can replicate.** The array still rings to her hand. She is intermittently present somewhere. Whether she *left* the Run or *became diffuse and never came back* is undetermined. (Play-to-find-out.)

3. **Sela Pruth is skimming co-op funds.** She uses the money to pay the Recursive Weave for fragment-access for her own family. Marn suspects. The audit will find this if anyone goes looking. Three clues if needed: the off-books ledger in Sela's drawer; Pell mentioning a "regular customer" by accident; a Lowstep dockhand who saw Sela hand off marks at Berth 11.

4. **The Mereth Shard's recording is an elf speaking a Sitharian name with intimate knowledge.** The Conclave has not published this. If the master in Sithari is destroyed and the field copy intact, the Sable Office can dictate publication terms. (DM-knows.)

5. **The Recursive Weave's 2131 Fermata incident — the wrong grandfather — was not as straightforward as either side claims.** The Conclave has the full story; they got it from a Tempered Accord investigator. The grandfather was not the *wrong* grandfather. The fragment was deliberately mis-routed by a Conclave informant inside the Weave to discredit the network. Pell does not know this. (DM-knows; reveal carefully.)

6. **The Coremark wooden crate in the Holdcells.** Inventoried by no one alive. Contents include at least one shard with an elven voice on it that does *not* match any known fragment. (Play-to-find-out what it actually says.)

7. **Tev Korrish believes his brother is partly inside *Wickrun*'s tuner array.** Whether this is true is genuinely undecided — there *is* an unidentified harmonic in the array, and the brother *did* die on a Run job, and Eshaia's calibration *does* hold residue. Three different things. They might be the same thing. (Play-to-find-out.)

8. **Joren Pell, Tev's niece, is feeding intelligence to all three sides.** She thinks she is in control. Coremark security has identified her as a Weave contact. The Weave does not know she is feeding Coremark. The dock thinks she is just a dockhand.

9. **The Stillsink Catalogue Mandate is being pushed by two Conclave central council members in Coremark's pocket.** Auren Talindra has noticed the voting drift but has not acted. If she gets evidence and the Half-Bar Run helps her surface it, the Mandate vote can be turned. Three clues if needed: voting records visible in published council minutes; a leaked correspondence accessible via the Recursive Weave; Auren's own private notes once she trusts the party.

10. **The Antiphon has been seen at the Holdcells, in Cell 4, with its hand on the Coremark wooden crate.** The teenager who saw it told nobody. The teenager is, in 2135, working at Tev's tea stall on weekends. (Play-to-find-out: what does the Antiphon want with what's in the crate?)
