# The Half-Bar Run

A registered carrier co-op working out of Lowstep, on the western edge of the Sable Crescent. Two ships, eight people on the books, a Conclave carrier license that's been current for nineteen years.

## Identity

Founded by **Marn Vesh** in 2117 (eighteen years before campaign start) as a single-ship operation hauling mortuary ringglass between hab cremation chambers and surface burial parlors — the cargo nobody else wanted because the dead sometimes still spoke through the resonance-bonded ash. Marn was a former Conclave field-archivist who washed out after a drift incident at Stillsink. She knew the routing and she knew people would pay.

The name comes from a routing trick. Carriers running into the Crescent's Conclave-monitored corridors used to "drop a half-bar" — slip into a half-band silence between standard signal-monitoring frequencies for the duration of one breath, just enough to clear a checkpoint without flagging cargo. Marn flew it as a joke; she also flew it as a livelihood. The trick still works on lazy operators. It does not work on a focused one.

## Goals

**Near-term (this season):** keep the carrier license intact through the Conclave's ten-year audit (current cycle ends in 14 weeks). Pay off *Wickrun*'s kite-cord refit. Land a stable second hauler so the second crew can stop borrowing.

**Long-term:** Marn wants the Run to outlive her — a real institution, not a personality. She is forty-four, tired, and has bad lungs from drift exposure. She does not say this out loud.

## Constraints

- **Carrier license.** Conclave-issued. Audited every ten years; lighter inspections quarterly. Pulled licenses don't get reissued.
- **Cannot cross the Conclave openly.** They can carry private commissions and they can refuse a Conclave job, but they cannot be *caught* moving sealed Conclave material to a non-Conclave buyer. That ends the co-op.
- **Cannot enter the Bloom Zone.** Boundary stillwater buoys flag any unregistered crossing.
- **Resources at start:** *Wickrun* (small kite-hauler, currently airworthy with a 30%-worn cord set), *Halfsole* (older, slower; second-crew berth, currently in dock for engine work — out two months), shared fund of about 4,800 marks (enough for one major refit and one month of crew pay), a strong reputation at Lowstep dock and a passable one at the Conclave's Sable Office.
- **Obligations:** owes the Tempered Accord's death-registrar a quarterly report on mortuary cargo. Owes a small recurring favor to **Tev Korrish** (a Lowstep dockmaster) whose brother went to drift on a Run job in 2129; the obligation is symbolic but real.

## Existing members the players inherit

- **Marn Vesh** — leader. NPC. (See `dm/notes/npcs/marn-vesh.md`.) Hab-Worlder. Two-part clipped name. Reads more than she talks.
- **Sela Pruth** — fixer / dockhand at Lowstep. NPC. Cargo bonding, customs paperwork, signal-band sealing. Does not fly.
- **Joro** (orcish mononym) — engineer, second crew, currently with *Halfsole* in dock. NPC. Tuned the *Halfsole* engine three times and would tune it again rather than replace it.
- *(Open slot)* — a former founding member who left the co-op eight years ago, last known address Fermata. Marn doesn't talk about her. The crew knows the name was struck off the founding roll. The name was **Eshaia** (fae true name). She's why the Run lost its Fermata berth.

## Capabilities the org needs

These are *kinds of leverage*, not roles. Players will come at them sideways.

- Someone who can sit at a Conclave audit and not flinch.
- Someone who can carry a sealed fragment through Coremark territory without it being noticed.
- Someone who reads what nobody else reads — half-band signal, redacted river-text, the kind of paperwork that isn't paperwork.
- Someone who can talk to a grieving family and not make it worse.
- Someone the Recursive Weave will return calls from.
- Someone who can run a kite-hauler in weather the others won't.

The Half-Bar Run does not have all of these. That's why character creation matters.

## Advancement track

Five stages. Each unlocks broadly; numbers get set when they advance.

1. **Unfashionable but solvent.** (Start.) Lowstep berth, small fund, two ships (one in dock), one license, regional reputation among grievers and small Conclave field-offices.
2. **Trusted.** Conclave audit cleared with commendation. New berth at a second dock — possibly Fermata if the Eshaia thing settles, possibly an outer hab. *Halfsole* fully back online. Crew pay buffer extends to three months.
3. **Established.** Recognized routing rights through Conclave-monitored corridors without per-trip authorization. A small permanent office at one settlement. Apprentice carriers showing up to hire on. Marn semi-retires; the co-op survives that.
4. **Influential.** A seat in a small Conclave advisory committee on private-commission carriage. Multiple ships. Small fleet recognition. Coremark notices and starts trying to buy out or undercut.
5. **Institutional.** The Half-Bar Run is *the* private carrier of resonance-sensitive cargo on the Sable Crescent. Mara should not let this happen quickly. If the table reaches Stage 5, the next campaign-shaped pressure is what an institution becomes when it forgets it was a co-op.

## Trigger conditions

- Stage 2 → 3: clear a Conclave audit *and* either land a major outside contract or settle the Eshaia obligation.
- Stage 3 → 4: complete a Major Threat clock against a faction-tier antagonist (Coremark or Conclave or other).
- Each stage advance happens at the moment the table actually does the thing. Mara names the unlock at advance.

## Public pressure (clock)

Create durable clock: **Wickrun: Cord Wear** at 5 segments. Long Shear runs and weather-on-the-line runs tick a segment. At 5/5, *Wickrun* is grounded for major refit (≈3,200 marks, six weeks).

## Secrets and agendas

- Marn was *fired* from the Conclave, not "washed out." She caused a Stillsink drift incident in 2114 by listening to a fragment she was supposed to seal. The fragment was someone's recovered last words to a sister who was already dead. Marn re-sealed it, walked out, and started the Run. The Conclave knows. They do not bring it up. They will if the Run becomes a problem.
- The Half-Bar Run has, twice, knowingly carried Conclave-sealed material to a non-Conclave buyer. Both buyers were grieving relatives whose claim the Conclave had refused for procedural reasons. Marn calls these "compassionate breaches." If audit-discovered, license revoked.
- Eshaia (the fae former founding member) was diffuse-state intermittent and tuned the *Wickrun*'s tuner array in a way that nobody else has been able to retune since. The *Wickrun* still hears Eshaia's calibration when it runs cold. Joro thinks this is structural; Marn knows it isn't.
- The fixer, Sela Pruth, has been quietly skimming a small percentage off mortuary commissions for three years. She uses the money to pay a private buyer at the Recursive Weave for fragment-access for her own family. Marn suspects but has not confronted.

The org keeps things from the party. The org is not their enemy. The org will protect them in front of an auditor and lie to them about Eshaia.
