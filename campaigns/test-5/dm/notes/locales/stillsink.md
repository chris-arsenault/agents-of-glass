# Stillsink

The largest active Echo River surfacing point in the Sable Crescent. A cold spring that opens onto a rock pool ringed by exposed crystal-bearing stone, with a small boardwalk built by the Conclave in 2110 to manage listener access.

## What it feels like

The water is cold enough to hurt your hand inside ten seconds. The pool is calm; the air around it carries voices in layers — most unintelligible, some heartbreakingly clear. Conclave protocol is *no listener stays more than thirty minutes*. Most experienced visitors stay twenty.

## What's worth writing

- The **boardwalk** has a sign-in book. Auren Talindra signed it 412 times between 2110 and 2114.
- The pool's **eastern lip** is fractured ringglass that someone, in 2089, tried to seal with a tuned brass plate. The plate is still there. The seal partially failed in 2127. Voices from before the seal occasionally surface clearer than voices from after.
- The boardwalk railings have small notches carved into them. Each notch is a listener's count of fragments they've personally heard. Auren's section runs 248 notches.

## Who's usually there

- One Conclave field monitor on rotation.
- Visiting researchers, by appointment.
- Grieving families with active permits (rare; bureaucratic).
- A small population of drift-eels in the lower channels.

## What does something

- The Stillsink pool is the load-bearing surfacing point for the regional fragment economy. Coremark's Springreed Cut, four segments away on the faction clock, will permanently degrade it.
- Marn Vesh listened to a fragment here in 2114 that she was not authorized to listen to. The fragment has not surfaced since. Auren's notebook (the one she keeps in her office) records the fragment's frequency profile but not its content.
