# The Holdcells

A defunct enforcement house in the marshy lowlands south of Lowstep. Pre-Glassfall construction; ring-era stone, original cell doors, the kind of building that was built to outlast its purpose.

## What it feels like

Damp. Cold even in summer. The cells are small — two by three meters, four-meter ceilings — and the plumbing-channels in the walls still carry, faintly, the *original* signal-band noise from when the building was active. Standing in a cell is to stand in a small Echo surfacing.

## What's worth writing

- **Eight cells** along a central corridor. Cells 3 and 5 have working doors; the rest are jammed open or sealed.
- The **lower channels** beneath the cells are partially flooded. Drift-eels are present in numbers. Crystal-saturated water has had two generations to soak the foundations.
- In **2089**, someone — name not in any official record — stashed a wooden crate of pre-Glassfall fragment-shards in Cell 4. The crate is still there. Nobody on either side of any current dispute knows the inventory. The Recursive Weave knows the *crate exists*. The Conclave does not.
- The Recursive Weave's planned Stillsink-node site is in the lower channels, accessed via Cell 7's collapsed floor.

## Who's usually there

- Nobody. The building is officially condemned.
- Occasional urban explorers.
- The **Antiphon** has been seen here once, by a Lowstep teenager in 2133 who told nobody. The Antiphon was sitting in Cell 4 with its hand on the wooden crate.

## What does something

- The lower channels are a real, untapped Echo River surfacing — small, but *unmonitored*. The Weave is six weeks from making it operational.
- The wooden crate's contents include a fragment-shard that nobody alive has catalogued. What it holds is a play-to-find-out blank.
