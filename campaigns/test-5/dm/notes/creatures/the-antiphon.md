# The Antiphon

Singular, named, recurring. Bloom-corrupted resonance entity that wears the voices of the dead. Uncategorized by the Conclave; informally documented by the Bloom Containment Office as Anomaly SC-11.

There may be others like it. The Antiphon is the only one that has shown a pattern.

## Where encountered

Sable Crescent surface, primarily at degraded Echo River surfacings — places where Coremark extraction or natural geology change has *partially* destroyed a surfacing point. The Antiphon prefers ruined acoustics. It avoids healthy pools and avoids the deep Bloom Zone equally.

## Behavior

Approaches alone. Speaks first. Says something the listener has heard before — sometimes a real fragment from a real river, sometimes a phrase the listener said yesterday and assumed unheard. Asks a question. Listens to the answer. Repeats it.

If engaged: extends. The voice multiplies. The listener experiences what the Conclave calls *deep drift* — uncertainty about which voice is theirs.

If left alone: walks away. Has been seen leaving a surfacing point with what looks like a small ringglass shard in its hand. Has not been seen using one.

## Sense

Looks like a person at a distance. Up close, the proportions are wrong by about ten percent — slightly taller, slightly thinner, hands slightly long. Speaks in *stitches* of borrowed voice — a syllable in one cadence, the next syllable in another. PCs who have heard a relevant fragment will sometimes recognize a voice in the stitching. Smells like wet rock and the inside of a sealed container.

## Mechanical hooks

- HP 28. Resonance-band entity; physical attacks deal half damage unless the attacker is using a *tuned* weapon.
- Attack profile: a Voice attack at signal-band, range 6m, target makes a Composure save. On failure: target loses one piece of speech they remember saying — gone, not retrievable. The Antiphon now has it.
- Mid-band aversion: a sustained mid-band tone (instrument, properly tuned tuner-array) deals 2/round and the Antiphon retreats at 50% HP.
- Cannot be killed in the conventional sense. Reduced to 0 HP, it disperses and reforms in a different surfacing within 7-30 days. The campaign's question of *whether killing it is the right answer* is a real one.

## Clock — The Antiphon Speaks (for the campaign)

A 4-segment soft clock. Each segment ticks when the Antiphon successfully takes a piece of borrowed speech from a named PC or NPC.

1. The Antiphon begins repeating PC speech in unrelated scenes (heard by an NPC; the PC does not know).
2. A Conclave field-archivist catalogues the repetition. Word gets back.
3. The Antiphon shows up at the Half-Bar Run berth speaking in Marn's voice.
4. The Antiphon has enough pattern to *predict* — to say a sentence the PC was about to say before they say it.

This clock isn't about defeating the Antiphon. It's about whether the campaign decides what the Antiphon *is*. The DM does not pre-decide.
