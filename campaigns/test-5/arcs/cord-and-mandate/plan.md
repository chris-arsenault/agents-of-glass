# Cord and Mandate — opening arc plan

The opening arc. Six to ten scenes. The pressure is the audit and the Mandate, both arriving on the same six-week clock. The party will be asked, by all three factions, to do incompatible things.

## 1. The stakes question

**Will the Half-Bar Run survive the audit, and what will it have become to do so?**

I do not know the answer. The two parts of the question are linked — the survival path and the integrity path are not the same path, and "survive" can mean *license intact and crew bought*, or *license intact and crew the same people they were*, or *license lost and the Run becomes something it wasn't*.

## 2. The threats

### Threat A — Vendrik Solvaen / Coremark Reach Survey

Site lead pushing the Springreed Cut. Has personal incentive (his daughter's medical care) and institutional reach (he can grief the Run's berth via the Tempered Accord faster than the Run can react).

- **Impulse:** *To close out the procedural sequence, sign by sign, until the Cut is operational.*
- **Between scenes:** advances the Coremark Reach Survey faction clock by one segment per scene that the party doesn't directly oppose. Files paperwork. Gives interviews. Does not personally engage.

### Threat B — The Stillsink Catalogue Mandate (institutional pressure)

Push from Conclave central in Sithari. Two council members in Coremark's pocket. The vote is mid-arc material.

- **Impulse:** *To centralize. To absorb the Sable Office. To withdraw private-commission carriage rights at next renewal.*
- **Between scenes:** the council circulates drafts, holds quiet hearings, schedules the vote. Auren Talindra writes letters and does not send them. The Mandate moves whether or not the party engages.

### Threat C — The audit pretext

The Conclave has Marn's 2114 firing on file. The audit is, technically, routine. It will become a pretext if the Run becomes politically inconvenient.

- **Impulse:** *To remain dormant unless triggered. Then to surface.*
- **Between scenes:** Auren preps the audit folder. The Conclave central liaison arrives at Lowstep mid-arc.

### Threat D — Pell's offer / the Stillsink-node opportunity

The Recursive Weave wants the Run to ferry the starter archive to the Holdcells. Six-week timeline before Coremark breaks ground at Springreed.

- **Impulse:** *To go live before the surfacing degrades. To give grieving families a route the Conclave has refused them.*
- **Between scenes:** Pell looks for alternate carriers if the Run delays past three weeks. She finds one in week four. The carrier she finds is bad.

The four threats share scarcity: **time** (six weeks), **trust** (everyone is asking the Run to keep secrets from someone), and **license** (Coremark can grief the berth, Conclave can pull the license, Weave can cost the Run both).

## 3. Clocks

### Public clocks (already set, surfacing in this arc)

- **Wickrun: Cord Wear** — 5 segments, currently 1/5. Long Shear runs and weather-on-the-line runs tick.
- **Conclave 10-year audit** — 7 segments, currently 0/7. Audit-relevant scenes that go badly tick.

### Arc-specific clocks (set via `glass clock --scope arc`)

- **Springreed Cut** — 5 segments, currently 1/5 (Accord review filed). Mirrors faction clock; arc-anchored.
- **Stillsink Catalogue Mandate** — 4 segments, currently 1/4 (Sable Office objection raised).
- **Stillsink Node Operational** — 5 segments, currently 1/5 (site identified). Hidden public — known to the Weave; party knows when Pell tells them.

The arc resolves no later than when *Springreed Cut* hits 4/5 (first extraction begins) or *Conclave audit* hits 6/6 (license review under cause), whichever first.

## 4. Possible end-states

(Brainstorm only. Not picking.)

- **Audit cleared, Mandate passes, Cut breaks ground.** The Run survives but loses private-commission rights at next renewal. The Stillsink-node never goes live. Yorek does not get his fragment. The campaign continues, slower, sadder. (Quiet failure.)
- **Audit cleared, Mandate fails (party surfaces the Coremark voting drift).** Cut still breaks ground. Sable Office stays autonomous. Auren Talindra retires early; her replacement is worse. (Mixed.)
- **Audit clears, Mandate passes, Stillsink-node goes live.** Run is now operationally aligned with the Weave. Audit-clear was on the strict letter; future audits will be hostile. The party is now part of an underground. (A win that becomes a different campaign.)
- **Audit fails (a breach surfaces).** License revoked. Run dissolves; some crew go to the Weave, some go elsewhere. The campaign continues without the Run as institution — the Run becomes a memory of a thing the campaign was about. (Reshape.)
- **The Mereth Shard becomes the lever.** The party uses it (delivered, copied, suppressed, leaked) to settle the Mandate one way or the other. The arc becomes about the shard. (Likely if the party engages with Auren personally.)
- **Coremark's voting-drift evidence surfaces, the council members are removed, Vendrik is recalled to Sithari.** Reach Survey continues under a less-known lead. The Cut delays one season. The party has made an enemy of Coremark central. (A win that opens a future arc.)

## 5. The strong start

**Lowstep, Berth 4, mid-morning. *Wickrun* just back from a Stillsink fragment-run. Conclave audit-prep notice arrives at Marn's office (from Auren personally, hand-delivered with a coffee for Marn). At the same moment, Pell appears at the head of the dock, watching from a polite distance.**

Yorek is at Tev's stall.

The party arrives at the berth to unload. Marn reads the notice. Tev sees Pell. Marn sees Tev see Pell. Yorek waves at one of the PCs.

What do you do?

## 6. Nodes (5)

### Node A — Berth 4 / *Wickrun*'s berth at Lowstep

The party's home base. Information available: dock gossip (Coremark consolidation rumor — Tev has not told Marn but might tell a PC); the audit notice on Marn's desk; *Wickrun*'s tuner-array harmonic anomaly (PCs with Tuner training will hear it).

- **Outbound clues:** to Conclave Sable Office (audit notice → Auren); to Stillsink (Yorek's request → fragment route); to the Holdcells (Pell mentions the Weave's site once if approached).

### Node B — Conclave Sable Office, Lowstep

Auren's office, the tertiary vault, the third floor. Information: the Mandate's draft text (Auren has it); Marn's apprenticeship notebook on the shelf (touchable, but only if Auren turns her back); the Mereth Shard's transfer order language (in a drawer, requires social engineering).

- **Outbound clues:** to Berth 4 (a copy of the audit prep folder); to Sithari (council voting records — the drift is in the public minutes if you read carefully); to the Holdcells (Auren has *not* told the Conclave about the unmonitored surfacing in the lower channels but knows it exists).

### Node C — Stillsink

The surfacing. Information: a fragment Yorek wants is in the catalogue but procedurally refused; the boardwalk sign-in book contains entries that contradict each other; the eastern-lip seal is failing slowly. Drift-eels in the lower channels.

- **Outbound clues:** to Conclave Sable Office (the catalogue index); to Stillsink (a junior Conclave monitor who is sympathetic to Yorek and shouldn't be); to the Holdcells (the surfacing geologically connects to the Holdcells lower channels).

### Node D — The Holdcells

Defunct enforcement house. Information: the wooden crate in Cell 4; the lower channels' unmonitored surfacing; Antiphon sighting history. Cold. Wet. No casual visitors.

- **Outbound clues:** to the Mereth Shard (one of the crate's contents references a Sitharian name); to Stillsink (the channels connect); to Pell (the Weave's intended node site).

### Node E — Coremark Reach Survey HQ, eastern Crescent

Vendrik's office. Information: Vendrik's pendant; the Cut's Springreed survey maps; the Conclave-pocket council member correspondence (in Vendrik's locked desk; Three-Clue-Rule item — also surfaceable from the Conclave Office and from the Recursive Weave's contacts).

- **Outbound clues:** to Conclave Sable Office (correspondence); to the Holdcells (Coremark security knows about the unmonitored surfacing too); to a future arc (Sithari council politics).

**Three-Clue check:** the council voting drift evidence — required for the Mandate-fails end-state — has three independent sources: the published council minutes (Conclave Sable Office), the leaked correspondence (Recursive Weave), Vendrik's locked desk (Coremark HQ). Three nodes. Three independent paths. Holds.

## 8. Arc-specific secrets

(In addition to campaign-level `dm/notes/secrets.md`.)

- The audit notice Auren hand-delivered includes a small scrap of paper Marn will find slipped between page 4 and 5. The note is in Auren's hand. It says: *I have the notebook still. — A.* (Auren has not made up her mind whether to send this. Once delivered, can't be unsent.)
- Vendrik's daughter Lyse has, twice in the last year, left voicemails on her father's pendant from the school resonance lab. Both messages are stored, locally, in the pendant's small buffer. He has not deleted them. The party will not know about this unless they get *very close* to him.
- The wooden crate in the Holdcells contains, among other shards, *one* with an elven voice speaking what sounds like "Mereth" — a pre-Mereth-Shard fragment that the Conclave never catalogued. (DM-knows. If recovered, dramatically up-shifts the Mandate political calculus.)
- Joren Pell (Tev's niece) has, this week, recorded a conversation between Vendrik and a Sithari council member that names both pocket members on the Mandate vote. She is sitting on it. She has not decided who to give it to.
- A Conclave field monitor at Stillsink, **Sevaril**, has been quietly logging her own catalogue of fragments she thinks the Conclave is wrongly sealing. She has 22 entries. She does not know what to do with them.

## 9. Done criteria

The arc resolves when **the audit cycle closes (one way or another)** AND **the Springreed Cut is either operational, delayed by at least one season, or dead**.

Whichever comes second is the arc's ending beat.

I will abandon the arc if the party drifts away — declines the audit prep, refuses Pell's offer, doesn't engage Coremark — and three months pass off-screen. In that case, all three clocks resolve to their default end-states (audit clears narrowly; Mandate passes; Cut breaks ground), the Run loses private-commission rights at next renewal, and the campaign opens with a different shape on the table. That is also a real ending.
