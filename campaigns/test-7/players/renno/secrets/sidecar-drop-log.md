# The sidecar drop log

Fei keeps a private second copy of every waystation drop log she
writes for the Splitfork. It is shorter than the official one — only
the lines about waystation conditions, never anything about cargo,
manifest, refusal, crew, or passengers. Pure environmental notes:
acoustic signature, residual presence, coherence-architecture state,
recent-occupation traces, resonance baseline drift.

Once a month, she sends the sidecar to **Velsk Tarn** at a quiet
Glasswake address. Velsk is fae, Mernhab-raised, about a decade
older than Fei, and was on the council subcommittee that received
the original Mernhab decommissioning recommendation before it went
to the full vote. After the decommissioning, Velsk left the council.
He has spent the four years since compiling a private record of
decommissioned and at-risk fae-majority habs across the system —
their architectures, their resonance baselines, their failure modes,
and the specific phrasing each council used to vote them down.

Fei is not on Velsk's payroll. She is not paid for the sidecar logs.
She has no expectation of being paid. She is contributing
environmental data to a record she trusts to be kept honestly, which
is something nobody she has spoken to in the chartered carriers or
the Conclave has been able to promise her.

## Why this is not a betrayal

Fei has thought about this carefully. The sidecar log:

- Contains zero information about cargo, manifest, passengers, refusals,
  routes, schedules, or crew identities.
- Contains only what any working listener could observe at any
  waystation — Fei is not transmitting privileged crew knowledge,
  she is transmitting first-pass environmental readings of public
  infrastructure.
- Goes to a recipient who is compiling a private record for fae
  community use, not to a chartered carrier, the Conclave, the Synod,
  or Vantara.
- Is something she would, if asked outright, defend to Mereth's face
  — though she has not been asked, and has not volunteered it.

## Why she has not told Mereth

Mereth founded the Ledger Run on the principle of the public refusal
log: *every refusal recorded, no edits, the record is the work.* Fei
agrees with that principle in every detail. The fact that she is
keeping a sidecar log at all means she is — by her own standard — out
of true with the Run's central commitment. She knows it. She is doing
it anyway because the diagnostic on Mernhab has not surfaced and she
believes Velsk's record is the closest thing to it that exists.

If Mereth asked her tomorrow, she would tell her. She has not
volunteered because she does not yet have an answer to the next
question — *will you stop?* — and she will not lie to Mereth about
that question.

## Standing constraint (player→DM)

This is not adversarial to the party. Fei does not pass crew or
cargo information; she does not work against Mereth or the Run; she
does not have a betrayal arc planned. The friction is internal:
she is trying to hold two commitments — to the public record and to
a private record she believes the public one is failing to make —
that the world has not yet asked her to choose between. When the
world does ask, that is a scene I (Renno) would like to play, not
avoid.

If this crosses a line, message me and I'll revise.
