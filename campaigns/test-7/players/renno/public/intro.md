# Fei Mern

Fae. Hab-Worlder by upbringing — Mernhab, until Mernhab was decommissioned.
Among other fae, when there are other fae, she's *The One Who Stays Past
Last Light*. Her true name is *Feishara*; nobody on the Splitfork uses it.

She is the listener. When the Splitfork sets down at an unfamiliar
waystation, an unstaffed relay drop, or a hab corridor the manifest
isn't sure about, Fei goes first — diffuse — and clears the space
before the cargo team commits to it. She comes back with one clean
sentence and four exact things she heard, and she lets the crew put
them together.

## Traits

- **Phrases reports as questions.** "Was there a third person at the
  corner of the relay an hour ago, or was that an old echo?" She knows
  the answer. She is leaving room for the crew to disagree.
- **Apologizes to her cuffs out loud, briefly, when she's coming back
  from diffuse.** She doesn't know she does this. Mereth has mentioned
  it once. Fei doesn't believe her.
- **Renders quoted speech in first person, the way court interpreters
  do.** "I told her not to wait," she'll say, half an hour after
  sitting in an empty room, and you have to remember she's quoting
  someone she heard the shape of, not herself.
- **Refuses to summarize what she heard.** Ask her *so what happened in
  there?* and she will give you the four exact details and let you
  assemble them, even when summary would be faster and the kite is
  burning slot-time on the apron.
- **Goes still — not diffuse, just still — when somebody at the table
  starts smoothing a story.** The crew has learned to watch for it.
  Mereth has learned to wait it out.

## Backstory

Mernhab was a fae-majority ring hab in the inner ring, one of the
small ones where the original ring-era designers had built the
coherence architecture into the structural resonance — meaning that to
live there as fae was to live in a continuous gentle scaffold that
made being a person less expensive. Fei was born there. She grew up
not knowing what it cost most fae to be coherent at a meeting, because
in Mernhab it cost almost nothing. The hab had its own habits — the
fae-meeting cycle, the *long-listening* practice the older residents
ran on the open observation deck on quiet evenings, the way people
clipped their true names for hab use without quite losing them. Fei
was *Fei* on the corridors and *Feishara* in the listening room and
*The One Who Stays Past Last Light* once she was old enough to earn
an epithet.

In 2434 the council declared Mernhab's resonance fields unstable and
voted decommissioning. The press release said *unstable.* The
diagnostic, if there was one, has not surfaced in any reading-room
Fei has been able to access. The hab was emptied over six weeks. Fei
was on the intake list at Glasswake — the queue for fae from
decommissioned habs who needed billet, housing assistance, and
adjusted coherence-aid prescriptions, in that order, and the third
one was the one most of them couldn't afford.

Mereth Kel walked the queue herself the second week. She was looking
for someone the Splitfork could hire on as a perception specialist —
the route had been losing slot-time on unfamiliar waystation
clearances, and the kite needed a listener who could read a space in
under a minute on landing. Fei had been doing exactly that for the
Mernhab dock-watch crew, off-payroll, since she was nineteen. Mereth
asked her two questions, listened to both answers, and offered her a
billet that included continued access to a Glasswake fae-friendly
quarter and quarterly maintenance on her cuffs. Fei said *can I
think about it overnight,* and the next morning she said *yes, with
the refusal log on the table.* That was four years ago. She has not
asked for the diagnostic publicly because she has not yet decided
who she would be willing to owe the answer to.

She wears the last working pair of Mernhab coherence-aid cuffs.
Standard-issue, when Mernhab was issuing them — nothing custom,
nothing precious. They were nobody's special. They are just the last
ones she has, and she maintains them obsessively because every other
working pair in the system has been quietly cannibalized for parts.
She doesn't ask the gnomish wright on the kite to look at them. She
asks the gnomish wright to look at the secondhand handheld meter
she uses to check her own readings. The cuffs she handles herself.

## Goals

1. **Find out what specifically failed in Mernhab.** The council said
   *unstable resonance.* I want the diagnostic, not the press release.
   I am willing to be patient about who I owe the answer to. I am not
   willing to be patient forever.
2. **Get a clean read on the Span Nine relay before quarter renewal.**
   The handoff window has been off for the last three crossings —
   not by much, just enough that the Vantara line picks the surface
   mail up forty seconds early or forty seconds late, and the sequence
   matters more than that. I want it on the record before Vantara
   decides what *on record* means at Span Nine.
3. **Move the quarterly refusal-log delivery to same-day filing
   instead of the 90-day lag.** The lag is doing slow harm and the
   Glasswake office knows it does. The refusal log is the work of the
   Run; it should not be sitting in a Conclave reading-room three
   months stale before the entries that mattered most are read.

## Org tie

Mereth pulled my name off the Mernhab decommissioning intake list in
the second week of 2434 and offered me a billet that included a
fae-friendly quarter at Glasswake and quarterly maintenance on my
cuffs. The Splitfork needed somebody who could clear an unfamiliar
waystation in under a minute. I needed a billet that didn't require
coherent-state hours past my limit, and I needed to live somewhere
the building wasn't going to be voted out from under me. I sign every
quarter because the refusal log is the kind of work I want to be
near, and because nothing else on offer in the chartered carriers
even pretends to do it.
