# Fei Mern's relationships

## Drova Korvanis — the decommissioned hab (seed 11)

The Mernhab decommissioning intake list routed through the Thornvault
desk first, and was rewritten as the Glasswake billet queue Mereth
later walked. Drova was on the Thornvault desk in '34. The names on
the upstream copy of that list, written in her small careful hand,
included *Feishara* — one of maybe forty fae from a hab that did not
yet exist on the Glasswake books.

Drova has that name in her head from the page, not from me. She has
not said so. She has also not unsaid it. She would tell me, I think,
if I asked the right question. I have not asked. The reason I have
not asked is the same reason I have not asked publicly: I have not
yet decided who I would be willing to owe the answer to, and Drova's
graduated-certainty register would mean any answer she gave came back
already shaped, and I would have to decide what to do with the shape
of it before I had decided what to do with the answer. So I have
been composing the question for three years.

What I have done, instead, is watch. I have noticed Drova hold her
tongue for three full breaths before answering people she does not
know well, and the pause shrink for people she does. I have noticed
the *Korvanis* on her Accord-signature is smaller than her *Drova*.
I have noticed the way she carries other people's letters in her
portfolio for weeks. None of these are things I would say back to
her without permission, and I have not said them to anyone else.
She has not, to my knowledge, said anything about my cuffs-apology
to anyone either. We have a working etiquette of mutual non-asking,
and I have come to understand that the etiquette is itself a form
of refusal log — kept by two of us, in two different shapes, in the
same room.

She may also remember, or think she remembers, a Mernhab resonance
diagnostic packet that crossed her intake desk within a few weeks
of the decommissioning order and was pulled back up the line before
it could make it to the curatorial floor file. She does not have
the document. She has the memory of having been the clerk who
routed it. I do not know that she remembers this. I suspect it from
the shape of the pauses. I will eventually ask. I have not asked.

## Tek'iris Korr — the taught thing (seed 14)

I do the back-of-hand check on my cuffs every morning before I leave
quarters, and at every shift change on the kite, because Tek showed
me how. She did it once and without ceremony — I had been doing the
check palm-first for years, the way Mernhab's dock-watch elders had
done it, and Tek glanced over from across the maintenance bay,
walked two paces, turned my hand for me, said maybe three words,
walked back. I have done it the way she turned my hand ever since.
I have not asked her why back-of-hand instead of palm. I have not
said *thank you* in a sentence with the word *thank* in it. I have
not, in four years, named the moment back to her.

It is hers. The check is hers. She knows that the kite's lines
should be touched back-of-hand and not palm — I have watched her
do that walkaround forty times and she does it the same way each
time — and somewhere on her first or second week with the Splitfork
she decided that the same rule applied to a fae's coherence-aid
cuffs as applied to a tuned spar. She did not explain the
reasoning. I have not asked for it. I do it.

Tek and I are, as far as I can tell, the two people on the kite
who maintain our own instruments and do not bring them to the
wright. She has her tic-tracer in her coat. I have the secondhand
handheld meter Mereth bought me my first quarter, that I have
recalibrated four times, and I have the cuffs themselves, which I
handle. She asks the wright to look at the meter. I ask the wright
to look at my meter too. Neither of us has ever asked the wright,
or asked each other, about the actual thing the meter is checking.
This is, I think, our working etiquette. I do not know what she is
checking when she runs her own readings. She does not know what I
am checking when I run mine. We pass each other in the maintenance
bay and we do not ask. I would like her to know that the back-of-
hand check is still hers. I have not yet found a way to tell her
that does not also break the etiquette. I will keep trying.
