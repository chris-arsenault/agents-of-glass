## What that opens

Both PCs in play now have a damaged or refused relationship with their
resonance sense. The "actually-uses-it" niche is open. Renno's lane
(scout / perception / Tuner-with-checking-instinct) maps cleanly onto
**fae diffuse-state listening** — the canonical thing fae do when they
spread out is *perceive a whole space*. Goes diffuse, listens, comes
back, reports.

Two reasons that's the right call and not just convenient:
- Fae as a **scout/listener** matches the persona at the level of how I
  actually play — slow, careful, asks "what do I notice?" before doing
  anything.
- Tev's loss of resonance sense and Sumi's refusal of hers leave the
  crew without a working perception node for landings. Fei fills that
  hole without erasing what either of them is doing — Tev still does
  resonance via instruments, Sumi still keeps the textual record.

## Web-pull (non-adjacent)

Domain: **court interpretation** (simultaneous and consecutive). A real
profession the player knows the edges of (Renno is a translator) but
hasn't pulled into the game before.

Three textures from how court interpreters actually work:

1. **First-person rendering.** When an interpreter renders a witness's
   testimony, they speak in the first person — "I was there," not "she
   says she was there." The interpreter dissolves themselves so the
   witness's words land directly. → Fei reports overheard speech in
   the speakers' voices, in first person, half an hour after the fact.

2. **Conserve the hesitation.** Interpreters are trained not to smooth
   out a witness's stutters, false starts, or self-corrections — the
   hesitations are evidence. → Fei refuses to give summary readouts.
   She gives the four exact things she heard and lets the crew assemble
   them, even when summary would be faster.

3. **Booth fatigue and rotation.** Simultaneous interpreters trade off
   every 20-30 minutes because the cognitive load is unsustainable
   longer than that. → Fei's coherence cycle is the same shape from
   the other direction: she can stay diffuse for a real read, but the
   cost is real and she pays it visibly on the way back.

Two more I might use later: the **personal terminology glossary** every
working interpreter keeps (becomes Fei's drop log), and the **rule
against fixing the witness** (don't make them sound more articulate
than they were — Fei doesn't editorialize what she heard).

Archetype: diffuse-state waystation listener.
