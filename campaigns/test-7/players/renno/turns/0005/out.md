## Web pull

Pulled from **simultaneous and consecutive court interpretation** —
adjacent enough to my day job that I know its edges, far enough from
TTRPGs that it isn't generic-fantasy texture. Three pieces of working
discipline I lifted into the character:

1. **First-person rendering** — interpreters speak in the witness's
   voice, "I was there," not "she says she was." Fei renders quoted
   speech in first person, half an hour after sitting in an empty
   room.
2. **Conserve the hesitation** — interpreters don't smooth out a
   witness's stutters and false starts; the hesitations are evidence.
   Fei refuses to summarize. Four exact details, you assemble them.
3. **Booth fatigue and rotation** — simultaneous interpreters trade
   off every 20-30 minutes because the cognitive load is
   unsustainable. Fei's coherence cycle is the same shape from the
   other side. She can stay diffuse for a real read, but the cost is
   real and she pays it visibly on the way back.

## The character

**Fei Mern.** Fae, Hab-Worlder culture, raised on Mernhab — a
fae-majority ring hab with native coherence architecture, decommissioned
by council vote in 2434. Among other fae she is *The One Who Stays
Past Last Light*. Her true name is *Feishara*; nobody on the
Splitfork uses it.

Mereth Kel walked the Glasswake intake queue herself the second week
of the Mernhab decommissioning, looking for a perception specialist
the Splitfork could hire on. Fei had been doing first-pass dock-watch
listening for Mernhab off-payroll since she was nineteen. Mereth
asked her two questions, listened to both answers, and offered her a
billet that included a fae-friendly Glasswake quarter and quarterly
maintenance on her cuffs. Fei thought about it overnight and signed
with the refusal log on the table. Four years ago.

What she does on the kite: when the Splitfork sets down at an
unfamiliar waystation, an unstaffed relay drop, or a hab corridor the
manifest is not sure about, Fei goes first — diffuse — and clears
the space before the cargo team commits to it. She comes back with
one clean sentence and four exact things she heard. She does not
summarize.

Archetype: *diffuse-state waystation listener.*
Org role: *waystation reader / first-pass scene clear.*

## Mechanical sheet

- **HP 8.** Fragile/specialized — the diffuse-rest-coherence cycle
  taxes her.
- **Attributes.** Superior **focus** (the cognitive discipline of
  holding diffuse perception clean). Advanced **attunement** (worked
  at, not innate; fae aren't naturally attuned). Advanced **resolve**
  (stays diffuse longer than is comfortable). Rudimentary
  **vitality**. Rest standard.
- **Skills.**
  - virtuoso — *diffuse-state waystation reading*
  - artisan — *first-person witness rendering* (the interpreter trick)
  - artisan — *coherence-aid maintenance*
  - apprentice — *refusal-log shorthand* (the crew's house format)
  - apprentice — *kite-tag and waystation-clearance protocol*
- **Inventory.**
  - **signature:** Mernhab coherence-aid cuffs — last working pair,
    salvaged from the decommissioning, standard-issue when Mernhab
    was issuing them, maintained obsessively because every other
    working pair has been quietly cannibalized for parts.
  - field interpreter's wax tablet and stylus — the drop log
  - secondhand gnomish handheld resonance meter — rough cross-check
    on her own readings
  - small flask of bitter cold concentrate — drinks it on the way
    back from diffuse, when her mouth doesn't fit right
- **Signature move (1/1):** *Listening Past Last Light.* Goes
  diffuse in a space the crew is about to enter (or just left), holds
  long enough to feel the shape of it, comes back with one clean
  sentence. The return is the cost — slow speech for ten minutes
  after, mouth doesn't fit right, sometimes a small involuntary
  dispersal at the wrists; the cuffs hum audibly during the return.
  Other fae recognize the technique by name.

face if asked, and she has not volunteered it because she does not
