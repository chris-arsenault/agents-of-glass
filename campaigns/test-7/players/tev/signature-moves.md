# Signature Moves

Start with one simple move at level 1. Add another slot at levels 3, 5, 7, and
9, for five total slots by level 9. These are recurring things your character
can actively do under pressure: spells, combat maneuvers, search techniques,
social tactics, chase tricks, rituals, or any other repeatable move.

Signature moves are narrative consistency tools, not powers with guaranteed
mechanics. They help the table remember what you tend to do, and they give the
DM something the world can learn about and react to. Do not use this file for
ordinary tics, traits, possessions, or backstory facts. Spell-like resonance techniques are valid signature moves.

Use `glass character signature-status <character-id>` before adding a move, and
`glass character signature-add <character-id> <name>` when repetition makes a
move identity-defining. Do not hand-rewrite this file around the slot limit.

## Moves

### The walkaround

- **Look:** Slow loop around the kite — eyes up, back of the hand trailing the rigging at shoulder height. Doesn't speak until she's all the way around. Forty paces, give or take. Sometimes stops at a spot for half a minute without saying why.
- **Usual use:** First-look on any kite job, rigging problem, or 'is this safe to fly' question. Buys three independent readings — eyes, hand, and the tic-tracer in her coat pocket.
- **Tells/costs:** She always does it. The crew has learned to wait through it. If she skips the walkaround, something is wrong with her, not the kite.
