# Tek'iris Korr's relationships

## With Bren Mosk — the Shear (seed 2)

Bren was in the cargo hatch of the run two years ago in the Shear. Drop-pilot,
contracted in for the slot window when my contractor's regular jumper couldn't
make the call. We didn't know each other before the apron brief. I remember her
face from it — counting under her breath at the wind board, *nine, eight,
seven*, and the call was made before she got to one. Right before the lift-rig
moved I walked the haul line with the back of my hand. Then I walked it again.
Two passes, not one. I had never done the second pass before and I have not
done it since. Something had flickered, and the second pass was me trying to
get the flicker to come back so I could place it. It didn't. The rig moved. The
cascade went. That second pass on the line was the last time my old sense was
fully working, and I did not yet know that.

Bren saw both passes. She has never said. She watches my hands when I touch the
spar before flight — just for a count, and then she looks away — and I know
that she knows the second pass meant something, even though she does not know
what it meant. She also saw the date go in wrong on the Compact filing. The
cascade was on a Wednesday; the filing says Tuesday. Bren clocked it and did
not speak up, and I clocked it and did not speak up, and we have been crewing
together at the Splitfork ever since like two people who have been holding the
same secret separately because neither of us has yet figured out how to set it
down. I think we will, eventually. Not soon. The day she asks me about my
hands, I will have to choose between the answer about my aunt and the other
answer, and right now I am not sure which one will come out.

## With Drova Korvanis — the diagnostic I shouldn't have read (seed 7)

About four months after the Shear, before I came to the Splitfork, I went
looking for the Echo River transcript of my own cascade. I told myself I was
confirming what I already knew. I was actually looking for a single document —
any document — that named the right date, so I could stop walking around as
the only person who had it. The Glasswake Conclave annex reading-room is where
unsealed Conclave correspondence is meant to land if it ever lands anywhere. My
transcript wasn't there. It had been requested back up the line to Thornvault
eighteen months earlier and not returned. The only paper in the folder was the
request receipt. The clerk who had signed for it at Thornvault was *D.
Korvanis, intake desk* — small careful hand, the *Korvanis* tighter than the
*D.* I did not know the name. I copied it into the back of my private refusal
log and left.

Drova joined the *Splitfork* about a year later. I watched her sign *Drova* on
Mereth's wage book the first morning — small careful Korvanis on the line
below where Accord wanted her full name — and matched the hand against the
receipt in my book that night. I have not said anything. I do not yet know
whether she pulled the transcript because she had noticed the date or because
someone above her had asked her to put it under glass, and I have not asked,
because asking means telling her what I was doing reading the Compact filing on
my own cascade. She has read my crew name on enough refusal-log entries by now
to know I'm here without Conclave clearance, and she has not asked me about it
either. I take this for a kind of agreement. I do not yet know what I am
agreeing to. I am not sure I trust her. I am sure she is the only person at
the Splitfork who would recognize the shape of the thing I am keeping quiet,
and that is its own kind of weight to carry, separately from whether I trust
her, and I notice that I have started leaving the tic-tracer in my coat
pocket instead of my workbox on days she is on the manifest.
