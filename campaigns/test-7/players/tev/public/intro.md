# Tek'iris Korr

Gnome. Hab-Worlder, raised on Hab Meridian. Kite-wright on the Splitfork.
Tek to crewmates; the apostrophe is for gnomes and her own people only.
Glimmers a little in direct sunlight. Hums in her sleep, when she sleeps.

## Traits

- Touches every line on the Splitfork with the **back** of her hand before flight — not the palm. The palm she stopped trusting two years ago. She tells anyone who asks that it's how her aunt taught her. It is, but it isn't only that.
- Won't sign the pre-flight inspection log until she's been on the deck a full count of forty. If you press her she says "walkaround," in the tone of someone whose half-answer is the whole answer she's willing to give.
- Hums under her breath when she's concentrating. She doesn't know she does it. Other gnomes find it tone-perfect. Humans find it eerie. Ev Sken once said it made the office sound "structurally honest" and then drifted away mid-sentence and didn't come back to the thought.
- Apologizes to the kite when she has to leave it grounded. Quietly, out loud, hand on the spar. Has done this since she was an apprentice and never grew out of it.
- Goes very still when the words "attunement test" come up in conversation. Comes back two beats later as though the conversation hadn't paused.

## Backstory

She grew up on Hab Meridian, in the lower-pressure ring where her aunt Mere Korr kept the conduit and the kid alike. Mere wasn't a Tuner — she was a hab-electrician, the kind of folk practitioner who never said the word *resonance* in her life and could feel a misaligned ringglass array from across a corridor anyway. Mere taught Tek the lineworker's check the way other aunts teach prayers: *eyes first, hand second, instrument third, and if you can't see it grounded, it's not grounded.* Tek was eleven. She thought it was a song.

She was a working kite-wright by twenty, on a small inner-ring contractor that did Span Nine maintenance and didn't ask Conclave questions of its hands. Then a Shear run, two years ago. She doesn't talk about what happened. The Compact filing is on the public record — a lift-rig harmonic cascade, no fatalities, no findings against the crew. What isn't on the record is that she came off that run with her lattice running a half-octave wrong and didn't tell anyone, because telling anyone would have meant a sense test, and a sense test would have meant the end of the only thing she'd ever been good at. She put in for the Conclave-adjacent jobs that didn't require clearance, and when those dried up she put in for the Ledger Run, and Mereth Kel read her work record and read her face and signed her on without asking for the certification she didn't have.

The lattice loss is real and it is slow. She still feels the resonance landscape — the temperature of a room, the harmonic-or-not of a tuned spar. She just feels it through a softer cloth than she used to, like listening through a wall she didn't ask to be built. Most days she compensates. On the bad days she has the tic-tracer in her coat pocket, scratch-built from a salvaged ringglass shard and a pearwood casing, which beeps soft when the line is good and harder when it isn't. She doesn't trust the meter. She uses it anyway. Mere would have approved of the principle and disapproved of the secrecy, and Tek knows both.

She joined the Splitfork because she could. She stayed because of the refusal log. She had been keeping a private version of it for six years before she ever saw the public one in the Glasswake office, and seeing the public one made her cry, briefly, in a way she has never fully explained.

## Goals

- Keep the Splitfork in the air without anyone — not Mereth, not Ev, not the crew — clocking how much of her reading goes through the meter instead of her hand. She is willing to work harder to maintain the appearance than she is to maintain the kite itself, which she knows is the wrong order, and she is working on it.
- Save enough off-the-books pay to commission a private lattice-realignment from a folk practitioner she trusts in Glasswake. The practitioner is Sava Lensk, who doesn't advertise and doesn't give discounts and asks exactly the questions Tek doesn't want to answer. Tek estimates it at eighteen more months of saving at current rates. The work is degrading faster than that.
- Find out what actually went wrong on that Shear run. The Compact cause-of-incident filing dates the cascade to a Tuesday. It happened on a Wednesday. She has not raised this with anyone in two years because raising it would mean explaining how she knows, and she does not yet have a story for that part.

## Org Tie

Mereth Kel hired her without asking for Conclave certification — which Tek would have failed — and without asking why a kite-wright with her record was looking for work that didn't require it. The refusal log is what makes her stay. She likes working for a crew that publishes its no, because she has been keeping her own private list of those for six years and the public version felt, the first time she read it, like permission to keep writing the private one. The role on the manifest reads *kite-wright, Splitfork*. The Halfsign being grounded means there is more work than she should be doing alone, which is a problem she is also not raising.
