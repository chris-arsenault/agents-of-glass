# Turn 4 — Sumi

*[Sumi sets down a thermos of tea on the table, takes the lid off slowly,
and waits until everyone's looking before she speaks. She has a half-page
of notes in her own hand, in pencil, and she's already half-erased one
line and rewritten it.]*

**Sumi (OOC):** Okay. ...Hold on.

...

I want this to be one I can stay with. So I went looking for a
non-adjacent texture before I started, and I landed on **forensic
document examination** — specifically the ESDA technique, the way
examiners read indented writing on the page underneath the page that's
actually missing. The discipline is built around the idea that *what's
gone left a trace*, and that you describe the trace in graduated
certainty rather than concluding from it. *Consistent with. Cannot be
eliminated as a source. No evidence to support.* I want to play someone
whose grammar got built that way and never quite came back.

...

Her name is **Drova Korvanis**. Sithari orc, mid-thirties. Mononym
plus a Sithari family name she keeps trying not to use. Trained at the
Echo Ledger Conclave's Thornvault intake desk under an archivist named
Senna Talindra, who taught her to *describe rather than conclude* and
then died while Drova was on her way out the door. Drova filed three
internal observations naming a senior archivist's redaction patterns,
got reassigned to a basement, signed a binder of confidentiality clauses,
and then walked out of Thornvault with Senna's last sealed letters to
Senna's daughter in her portfolio. She carried them all the way to
Glasswake. She didn't deliver them. They're in a box at a pawn shop on
the dock road. She has not opened the box. She visits it.

She fills the **refusal-log clerk** chair on the *Splitfork* and at
the Glasswake office. Mereth Kel pulled her off the dock pub three
years ago after watching her spot three forged Accord seals on a manifest
at the next table. Drova is not a tuner — her *attunement* is
**rudimentary**, on purpose; her aunt steered her into records work
because resonance never came easily and orc cognition hits a wall on the
abstract end of it. So she built **handheld instruments** instead. Three
ringglass-scrap loupes she ground herself at the Glasswake hostel. The
signature move is just *holding the page low and letting the light rake
across it*. She calls it **The Angle**. Anyone who's watched a Clarisant
examiner work recognises it on sight.

Mechanically she's **focus superior, finesse and resolve advanced,
attunement rudimentary**. Skills are **virtuoso reading what's been
redacted from a paper record**, artisans in **Conclave intake protocol**
and **stillwriting** *(writing without leaving an indentation trace —
turns out this is also a craft)*, apprentices in **Sithari formal
register** and **kite passenger-manifest paperwork**. HP 10. Inventory:
the loupe set, a leather portfolio with hidden compartments, a pen set
including one inkless reading-pen, and a small notebook of Senna's
handwriting samples she's been measuring other writing against for ten
years.

I sent the DM a secret about the letters. The shape of it is *quiet
friction, not adversarial*. Drova will be evasive about Glasswake
layovers and unusually serious about anyone else's
undelivered-letter problem. If the campaign brings Senna's daughter
Lira onto the deck of the *Splitfork*, Drova will recognise her on sight
and her face will give her away. Whoever's paying attention will see it.

I noted Tev's table-talk on the Tek'iris build — a gnome Hab-Worlder
kite-wright who lost most of her resonance sense in the Shear and hides
it with handmade meters. No species or culture overlap with Drova,
different shape of secret *(mechanical compensation versus archival
withholding)*, and they share the trait of *not trusting the resonance
sense the world expects them to have*. That's a Round-2 conversation,
not a Round-1 one — saving it.

...

**[IC, when Drova first speaks at Mereth's wage-table.]**

*[Drova sets her loupe set on the corner of the table at exactly
right-angles to the edge. The case clicks. She apologises to it under
her breath, in Sithari formal register, then looks up at Mereth.]*

**Drova:** ...Three breaths. Then I'll answer.

I have read your refusal log. The entries from the second quarter of
two years ago are *consistent with* a cataloguer's hand and *cannot
be eliminated* as Sithari-trained. I would describe rather than
conclude. ...The phrasing in the November entry on the resealed
Vantara packet is the phrasing my mentor at Thornvault used when she
was angry but couldn't say so on the page. I would not have caught it
if I hadn't been her apprentice.

...

I am not asking who wrote it. ...I am telling you I noticed. I am
telling you I will keep noticing.

*[She slides the wage book back across the table without signing the
family name. *Drova*, only. The K of *Korvanis* is on the form upstairs,
in the Accord clerk's binder, and that is where it stays.]*
