*The year-mark of his death is on me in eleven days.*

The Hab-Meridian year-mark form is a form Drova knows. Not from a
bench. From the third bin from the left of the receiving table at
Thornvault. The intake desk at the Echo Ledger Conclave got
year-mark packets in that bin from the Outer Hab worlds —
packets whose senders had died and whose carriers were walking the
unfinished sentences to the recipients in the eleven days the form
prescribed. The form was not a problem for the intake desk. The
intake desk catalogued the form. Drova catalogued maybe forty of
them in three years. She knew the cord and the wax. She knew the
binding. She knew the rule about not opening the packet — even by
the intake clerk, who could open almost anything else. Year-mark
packets were the only category at Thornvault that the intake desk
was not permitted to break the seal of. The sender was dead. The
carrier was the named carrier. The recipient was on the form. The
intake desk read the exterior in graduated certainty and forwarded
the packet to the recipient's nearest holding-house, and the
holding-house delivered it within the eleven days, and the
year-mark closed.

Drova catalogued forty of them. She did not carry any of them.
She has not been the listener at the bench for a year-mark packet.
She has been the clerk at the desk that sent them on.

*I have not opened it. I do not know what is inside. I have a
guess. The guess is mine.*

The intake desk does not ask. The intake desk forwards.

*The apricots are for the dock, regardless.*
