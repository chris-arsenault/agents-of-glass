# What the party doesn't know

Drova still has Senna Talindra's letters.

They are in a steel-bound box on the third shelf behind the counter at
**Mer Vasht's pawn shop** on the dock road in Glasswake. The box is
listed in Mer's ledger under Drova's name, paid up two years in advance.
Mer has not asked what's in it. Mer has not opened it. Mer would not.

The letters are still sealed. Drova has not read them. She has, on
roughly thirty separate Glasswake layovers, taken the box down off the
shelf, set it on Mer's back-room table, and sat with it for an hour
without opening it. She tells herself this is restraint. It is closer to
a habit she cannot stop performing.

## What Drova actually wants

She wants to find a way to know what the letters say without breaking
the seals, and to convey their contents to Senna's daughter Lira without
identifying herself as the courier. Her own training is the problem.
*Indented writing*, the foundational technique of the intake desk, is
what she is afraid of: she could lay each sealed letter on a clean sheet
and rake a graphite shadow over the inside of the envelope to see what
Senna pressed hardest into the page. She has not done this. Doing it
once would mean she has done it, and the binder she signed makes that a
specific kind of theft from a specific dead woman who trusted her.

So she sits with the box.

## What's in play for the campaign

- **A person who knew Senna Talindra well** — a former Conclave
  colleague, an old Sithari friend, a sympathetic Thornvault archivist
  — could legitimately offer to read the letters and pass the contents
  to Lira *as themselves, not as Drova's proxy*. Drova would surrender
  the packet to such a person without a struggle. She has not yet met
  one.
- **Lira herself** — *Lira Talindra*, mid-twenties, lives somewhere in
  Glasswake. Drova does not know where, has not looked, has been
  carefully not looking. If the campaign brings them into contact,
  Drova will recognise her on sight from Senna's hand-drawings; Lira
  will not recognise Drova at all.
- **The Conclave's confidentiality binder.** It is a signed instrument
  with specific clauses about Senna's estate and posthumous
  correspondence. Drova believes — correctly, mostly — that breaking it
  would be cited. The binder is at Thornvault, not on her. Locating
  the original copy and what it actually says (vs. what she has been
  remembering it says for three years) is a thread the DM can pull.

## Constraints

This is not adversarial. Drova is not planning to lie to or work
against the party. The secret produces friction in three shapes:

1. She will be *evasive* about Glasswake layovers — disappears for an
   hour, comes back tense, won't say where she went.
2. She will be *unusually serious* about anyone else's
   undelivered-letter problem. The party will read this as trauma
   without knowing the source.
3. She will *recognise Senna's hand* in the wild if the campaign
   surfaces it, and her face will give her away to anyone who's paying
   attention.

If the DM wants to bring the letters into the campaign earlier or later
than Drova's own schedule, that's welcome. The box is a thread with a
clear handle.
