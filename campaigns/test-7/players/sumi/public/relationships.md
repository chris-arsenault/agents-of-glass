# Drova Korvanis's relationships

## With Tek'iris Korr — the diagnostic I shouldn't have read (seed 7, mirror)

Drova does not remember pulling Tek's Echo River transcript. She does not
remember the receipt. The Korvanis on the line below *D.* is in her own
hand because she signed thousands of them in nine years at the Thornvault
intake desk — small careful, the family name tighter than the initial,
the way Drova always wrote her own family name when an Accord form
required her full one. End of shift, queue of upstream-pull requests,
each one a slip from a different department, each one signed off and
routed back up the line to Thornvault Curatorial without a clerk's
question. The pull on Tek's transcript was one of perhaps forty that
week. It would not have stayed in her head.

What Drova *does* know, and has not said: somebody above her ordered
that pull. Intake clerks do not request transcripts back to Thornvault
on their own initiative. The request slip would have come down with
others — Sable Crescent items, station-frequency-band items, the same
shape of thing Drova was, around then, beginning to file her own
graduated-certainty observations about. Whether the senior archivist
she would later name in the third observation was the one who put
Tek's transcript on the pull list, or whether it was routine
annex-to-curatorial upstreaming on a different chain of authority,
Drova cannot reconstruct. She is afraid the answer might be either,
which is a different sentence from being afraid of one specific answer.

She has been at the Splitfork three years. In three years she has
noticed that Tek goes very still when the words *attunement test* come
up in a conversation, and comes back two beats later as though there
had been no pause. She has noticed that Tek touches the lines with the
back of her hand and not the palm. She has noticed the tic-tracer in
Tek's coat pocket on the days Tek thinks no one is watching for it.
And she has noticed that she herself has the refusal-log archive at
her elbow, which would, with thirty minutes' work, tell her what was
in Tek's Compact filing and whether the date on it was wrong.

She has not looked. That is the load-bearing piece of this whole
arrangement from Drova's side. She has the tools to know and she has
chosen not to know, and she does not yet have a clean story for why.
*Consistent with* respecting another crewmate's right to her own
silence. *Cannot be eliminated as a source* she is afraid of what she
might be the trace of. *No evidence to support* — yet — that Tek would
forgive her for having signed the receipt. Drova does not know Tek
knows. Drova does not know what Tek would do with the knowing. Drova
does the same thing on Tek's silence that she does on Senna's letters
in the box at Mer Vasht's: sets it down on the table, sits with it,
puts it back on the shelf without opening it.

## With Fei Mern — the decommissioned hab (seed 11)

Drova logged Mernhab's decommissioning paperwork on the intake desk at
Thornvault in '34. The upstream copy. Before Mereth ever walked the
queue at Glasswake, the same intake list had crossed Drova's desk
upstream, and Drova had logged each name on the routed list in her
small careful hand with no comment, the way she logged everything.
*Feishara* was one of perhaps forty fae names on that page. Drova
remembers it because it was a true name, not a hab name — most fae on
the routed list had given the short form, and *Feishara* was the only
one that quarter spelled out. The clerk's eye picks up that kind of
thing without permission. The name sat in Drova's head from a page
and stayed there.

She did not connect *Feishara* to *Fei* the first three months they
crewed together. Fei goes by *Fei* on the Splitfork. The wage book and
the apron manifest both read *Fei Mern* and that is what Drova logged
on the refusal-log header the first time Fei's name needed to be in
one. The connection landed in Glasswake, in the office, on a quarterly
review afternoon: Drova was reading down the receipts file for the
Mernhab quarter she could not afford to remember in detail, and she
saw the true-name field on a coherence-aid prescription transfer, and
the name on the field was *Feishara*, and Drova put the page down very
carefully and walked out to the dock for ten minutes and came back and
did not finish the file that afternoon. She has not told Fei.

She also has — or thinks she has, and has not yet allowed herself to
verify against her own memory or against any archive she still has
access to — the recollection of a Mernhab resonance diagnostic packet
passing through Thornvault intake within a few weeks of the
decommissioning order. The packet was routed back upstream before it
reached the curatorial floor file. Drova would have logged the routing
slip. She does not have the document. She has the memory of having
been the clerk who handled the document and signed the routing. She
has the memory and she does not, today, fully trust it, because she
knows what she would want her own memory to give her in the absence
of the page.

She has not volunteered any of this to Fei. Fei has the listener's
training and asks her reports as questions; she leaves the room for
the answer to disagree with her. Drova has, twice, almost set the
shape of an answer down on Fei's table — *was there a Mernhab
diagnostic that came through intake at Thornvault in '34* — and both
times she has not. Once because Fei was already on her way to a
landing brief. Once because she did not know what she would do if Fei
turned and asked her what it had said. She is going to. She does not
know yet how.

(She has been quietly relieved that the cuffs-apology thing exists
and that Bren has noticed it. Drova apologises to objects she breaks,
never to people. When she has finally said something to Fei about
Mernhab, she is going to apologise to her portfolio first, and Fei
will hear it from across the bay, and that will probably be the way
the conversation starts.)
