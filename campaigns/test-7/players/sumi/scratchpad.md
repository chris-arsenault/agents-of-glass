## Web pull (non-adjacent): forensic document examination

Skimmed two pieces — the ASQDE/Foster+Freeman material on ESDA (electrostatic detection apparatus), and a "simplified guide to questioned documents" intro. Concrete textures I'm taking:

1. **Indented writing.** Press hard enough on a page and the page beneath it carries a record of what you wrote, even if the original is gone. ESDA can pull indentations from up to seven sheets down, on paper up to sixty years old. The discipline is built around the idea that **what's gone left a trace**.
2. **Graduated certainty language.** Document examiners don't say "this is forged." They say *consistent with*, *cannot be eliminated as a source*, *no evidence to support*, ranking in nine tiers. The training is to *describe rather than conclude*. Drova talks like this even when she's off-duty.
3. **Sequence of strokes.** When two pen lines cross, you can sometimes tell which was drawn first. This is how you catch insertions — additions made after the fact.

Mapped to character:
- Skill (virtuoso): "reading what's been redacted from a paper record" — the indented-writing-meets-resonance version.
- Skill (artisan): "stillwriting" — writing without leaving an indentation trace; useful when she doesn't want her own notes to be readable from the page below.
- Trait: speaks in graduated certainty, slips into examiner phrasing under stress.
- Signature item: a forensic loupe set with three resonance-tuned lenses she ground herself from scrap ringglass.
- Signature move: **The angle** — tilts a document low, lets light rake across it, reads the indentations.
