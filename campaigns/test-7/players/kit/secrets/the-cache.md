# The cache at her mother's

Bren has been quietly running unmanifested ringfall fragments off the route. Not paid cargo. Not anyone else's pickups. Pieces she finds during her own drops — small enough to disappear into the coil of her let-down line, marked with her own chalk-pattern, and offloaded at her mother's room in Glasswake at the end of every loop.

She has been doing this since her first quarterly review on the Splitfork. She is on her seventeenth piece. They are kept in a wax-paper-lined kindling crate under the bed.

## What she tells herself

She isn't selling them. She isn't lying on the manifest, because the manifest is for cargo the org takes responsibility for and these were never offered to the org. She isn't refusing them either, because the refusal log is for things the crew was asked to carry and she was asked to carry none of these. She found them. They are hers. The Conclave can argue otherwise; she'll listen if they do.

The plan, when she lets herself have one, is to clean and broker the cache through a private buyer once it's worth enough to retire her mother's Tarrest relocation debt in a single payment. Not Vantara — Vantara would record it. A small Hab-Meridian dealer she met on a passenger drop two loops back. She has not contacted him yet. She will when the cache is ready, and not before.

## The piece she will not broker

The fourth one she pulled — a wedge of dark-banded ringglass with a hairline groove along one face that she initially read as impact damage. She has since looked at it under chalk-light a dozen times. The groove is too regular. There is a second mark on the back that is almost certainly the *ve* of a chain patronymic, partially scoured. She does not know what to do with it. She has not told her mother it exists. It is wrapped separately, in oilcloth, behind the kindling crate against the wall.

She is not planning to do anything with this piece in the immediate route. She does not believe an elven artifact in a Glasswake bedroom is the start of any campaign she wants to be in. She would prefer for it to remain a thing she has and is not yet thinking about.

## What this is and isn't

This is friction, not sabotage. She isn't planning to hurt the crew. She isn't planning to lie to them about a cargo. She is keeping a private side of her work private the way most working scavengers do, and she would lose the seat if Mereth saw the cache.

If the DM wants this to surface — through a routine inspection, a Conclave inquiry, a Vantara competitor with a grudge, or because the elven-marked piece starts doing something — that's an arc the player would enjoy. Bren losing the cache is on the table. Bren *betraying* the crew over the cache is not. She'd take the loss.
