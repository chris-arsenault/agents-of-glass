# Bren Mosk

Drop-pilot of the Splitfork. Human, Hab-Worlder. Twenty-nine. She/her.

The one who throws a paper streamer out the cargo hatch before she lets the kite commit to anything.

## Traits

- Counts under her breath when she's reading wind. *Nine, eight, seven.* You can lip-read it from across the bay. By the time she gets to one, the call is already made.
- Won't eat seated. Stands at counters, finishes other people's plates, says sitting to eat is for people who think the day is over. The galley table on the Splitfork has never been used by her in three years of crewing.
- Marks her gear in chalk, never paint. Every rope, every hook, every crate latch. Trusts paint less than chalk because chalk wears off honestly — when the mark's gone, the rope is past trust. She has reasons.
- Won't say *we should*. Says *I'm going to* or *I'm not.* Other people can come along.
- Apologizes after a hard drop, never before. Never says she's sorry on the way out the hatch. Always finds you the next morning to say it then.

## Backstory

She was born on Hab Tarrest, an inner-ring station with a population that started thinning when she was six and was formally drawn down through her childhood. Her father stayed behind on the decommissioning crew when the rest of the family relocated; he died in the ring-side closure work in '23. Her mother took surface mining at Glasswake. Bren was eleven when they came down. She spent two years in the Glasswake school and then walked out of it at thirteen for a dust-camp on the ringfall plain west of Span Nine, and never came back to the school.

Ringfall scavenging is what she did from thirteen to twenty-six. Walking grid lines on the salt flats with a small crew, eyes on the ground, watching for fusion-crust and the half-buried glint of a fragment fresh from descent. She wasn't a Tuner — she's human, never trained, has no ear for the resonance landscape and never wanted one. What she had was a good eye for impact patterns and a steadier hand with rope than most of the camp. By eighteen she was the one they'd send down a sinkhole on a single line. By twenty-two she was reading wind off the ridge with weighted paper streamers and dropping spotters into the difficult pickups. She owns a thumb-sized shard of edge-polished ringglass from her first find at thirteen. She still carries it in the streamer pouch, against the ribbons, where the cold of it matches her hand by the end of a drop.

She got onto the Splitfork in '34. She was bidding for the recovery of a Sable Crescent fragment — small piece, low-grade, but the impact pattern said a clean inner core, and she'd already roped off a section of ground that wasn't hers to rope off. The Splitfork came in low to bring an Accord clerk to the impact site. Mereth Kel didn't ask why Bren was there. She asked what the wind was doing. Bren told her. Mereth offered her a seat at the dock pub that evening and a probationary slot on the Splitfork the next morning — drop-pilot, the seat that had been open since the kite's previous jumper died on a Hab Meridian descent in '32.

She didn't sign that night. She signed two weeks later, after she'd read a full quarterly refusal log front to back in the Glasswake office. Not because it was clean — because nothing in it was hidden. The year of the Sable Crescent embargo, two of the entries were in Mereth's hand, refused-with-reason, and one of them was a letter Mereth had written herself and handed back unsent. That was the thing that made Bren put her name on the Article 14 paperwork. She has kept it on the paperwork ever since.

## Goals

- **Get the Halfsign back in the air.** The Splitfork is running tight every loop and a backup kite means a backup drop seat, and a backup drop seat means I am not the only thing between this crew and a missed deadline. I want to be replaceable.
- **Find out what happened to the drop-pilot before me.** The crew talks around her. Mereth says *Hab Meridian descent, '32* and nothing else, and the refusal log doesn't have an entry for that month at all — not a refusal, not a cargo, not a passenger. The log is supposed to be unedited. I want to know why that month is.
- **Pay off my mother's Tarrest relocation debt without the Glasswake office knowing it's me.** The amount isn't enormous. The provenance of the money is the part I want kept off the manifest.

## Org Tie

Mereth has needed a drop-pilot since '32. I have needed work that didn't require me to lie about being resonance-blind. The refusal log is what made me sign; the rest is route work. The seat fits me and I fit the seat, and the day either of those stops being true I will say so out loud before anyone else has to.
