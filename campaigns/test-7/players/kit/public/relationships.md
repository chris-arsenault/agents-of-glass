# Bren Mosk's relationships

## Fei Mern — the cultural slip

Fei is from Mernhab. Bren is from Tarrest. Different habs, different decades of closure — but both decom-hab kids, and Bren hears it.

She hears it in Fei's clipped consonants on the common nouns, the ones Fei flattens when she has been diffuse a long time. *Co-ord, comp-act, app-ron* — the Mernhab long-listening register, the one the older residents on the observation deck used in the evenings to keep coherence at low cost. Bren has never set foot on Mernhab. She knows the register because her father came off the Tarrest decommissioning crew clipping his consonants the same way, and her mother used to mimic him across the supper table when she was annoyed and forgave him for it ten minutes later. Her father clipped until the last year of his life. Bren grew up reading it as *home voice.* Hearing it from Fei was the second time it had ever surprised her on a kite.

There is also the cuffs-apology thing — the one Mereth has mentioned to Fei once and Fei does not believe. Bren has seen it twice on the bay floor. It is a long-listening habit, the kind the observation-deck practice would have grown. The cuffs are a thing you are *with*, not a thing you wear, and you do not end a long stay-past-last-light without acknowledging the thing you were with. Bren did not know the Mernhab practice by name until the second time she saw Fei do it, when she went to the Glasswake library on her next layover and read about it on the dock-watch shelf where the decommissioning-era materials sit unsorted in three crates and a dustcover.

She has not named any of this to Fei. The naming would cost Fei something Fei has not yet decided to spend, and Bren is resonance-blind and does not get to charge admission to the listening work. She is going to keep not naming it.

(She has stolen the questions-as-reports habit, twice, on dock-approach calls. She'll admit that one if Fei asks.)

## Tek'iris Korr — the Shear run

We were both on the Shear run. The Compact filing reads *lift-rig harmonic cascade, no fatalities, no findings against the crew.* I have read it twice. Both times the air went out of me at the same line.

I was in the cargo hatch when it started. Tek was on the spar, checking the harmonic on the lift-rig — the lineworker's check her aunt taught her, *eyes first, hand second.* She did it once, the way she always does. Then she did it again. Tek does not do anything twice in a row when she trusts the read. I saw her face change between the two passes. I did not see what she had been reaching for on the second pass and did not catch. Then the rig moved and I was busy and then we were busy for an hour and then the cascade was done and we were on the apron and nobody was dead.

I have watched Tek's hands at every walkaround since. A count of four, then I look away. I do not know what the second pass meant. I am sure that it meant something, and I am sure that the thing it meant was not nothing, and I am not the one to ask. What I am is the one who is in the room when she does it. If the second pass ever comes back and Tek needs somebody to have been there for it without making her say so, that is a job I can do without a streamer in the air.

I read the filing the next morning at the Glasswake office. The cause-of-incident date was a Tuesday. The cascade ran on a Wednesday. I know that because I had thrown my own streamer that morning and the wind from the streamer call was a Wednesday wind, and you do not get the Wednesday cross-pull on Tuesday in that lee of the Shear in that season, and I had told Mereth so before we lifted. The filing has Tuesday. I did not say so to anyone, because Tek did not say so to anyone, and Tek had not slept in three days and her hand had moved twice in a row, and I was not going to be the one. I have not said so since.

Tek does not talk about the Shear run. I do not either. We work next to each other every loop and there is the thing neither of us names. *Apologizes after a hard drop, never before.* I have not yet had the morning where I find Tek to say sorry for the Shear run. I am going to. I do not know yet what for.

If she goes first, I will be relieved. If I have to go first, I will.
