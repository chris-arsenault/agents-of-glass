# Campaign Summary

Running campaign-level summary. The DM keeps this current as arcs
resolve, facts become durable, and the party's situation changes.

## So far

**Campaign-planning, turns 1–2.** Foundation drafted (turn 1).
Encyclopedia populated, opening arc scaffolded, public clock created
(turn 2). No play yet. No characters yet.

The campaign asks: *Who decides what the system remembers?* The world
is short of *witnesses*. The party belongs to **the Ledger Run** — a
chartered kite-courier crew working a recurring route between
Glasswake, Hab Meridian, and the Sable Crescent.

## Established facts

- The Ledger Run was founded in 2419 by Mereth Kel, a former Vantara
  route officer. She still captains.
- The crew flies the **Splitfork** (primary) and the **Halfsign**
  (secondary, currently grounded for repairs the official cause
  document calls *Shear damage* and a closer look does not).
- The crew keeps a public refusal log — the only such log of any
  chartered carrier in the system. Filed quarterly to the Accord
  clerks and to the Echo Ledger Conclave.
- The Ledger Run's office crew at Glasswake includes Mereth (captain),
  Ev Sken (fixer), and Halvi Tern (dispatcher / bookkeeper).
- The opening arc is **Caulden Rack**: the current outbound loop
  carries a microcavity rack from Caulden Refinery to Hab Meridian
  receiving dock, and one cell in the rack is duskgrain in microcavity
  housing. Whose decision it was to misdeclare the cargo, and what the
  crew does with that fact, is the arc's stakes question.
- Vantara has filed an Article 14 amendment that would strip tariff
  exemption from independent carriers' bulk mail. Public durable
  clock active: `article-14-amendment` (0/5).

## Established factions (campaign layer)

- **Echo Ledger Conclave** — quietly working a non-renewal of the
  research-mail contract via a night-shift dossier on the Ledger
  Run's irregularities. Antagonist clock runs through Veth Karran.
- **Vantara** — pushing the Article 14 amendment via Risca Donn
  (route officer, has private contradictions she has not raised).
- **The Accord clerks** — distinct from the Accord institution. Tal
  Vorenis (Author-caucus, indexes the refusal log) is the party's
  most useful procedural ally if they ever realize he exists.

## Open questions

- Who the PCs are. Character creation is the next mode.
- *Whose decision* substituted the Caulden Rack's twelfth cell. Open
  across at least five plausible authors. Mara has not chosen.
- What the Halfsign actually got hit with. Mara has options on the
  board; the answer is play-to-find-out.
- Whether the Drag is alive. Genuinely undecided.
