# Where We Are

It is 2438 CE. The system is reconnected — habs and the surface and the
outer worlds talk again — but the sixty years of the Signal Famine left
every community with its own version of those decades, and the
reconnection hasn't reconciled the versions. The Tempered Accord holds.
The Bloom Zones haven't shrunk. Everyone has a story about the Bitter
Reach, and no two of them match.

You belong to **the Ledger Run** — a chartered kite-courier crew working
a recurring route between several reconnected settlements. You carry
mail. You carry small cargo. You carry passengers when there's room.
And sometimes — sometimes — you carry news, which is the part of the job
nobody licensed and everybody needs.

## The Question

**Who decides what the system remembers?**

This campaign is asked to answer it. Not me, and not the world-bible —
the table, scene by scene, by what you choose to carry.

## What This World Is Short Of

**Witnesses.** Trusted carriers of what happened. The Conclave hoards
proof. The Synod won't talk in plain language. The Accord prefers
procedural silence to public reckoning. Vantara will carry your cargo
and refuse to carry your story. The honest gossip in a Glasswake pub is
more reliable than the official briefing, and everyone knows it.

You are not the answer to that shortage. You are people who keep
running into it.

## Tone

Hopecore, played straight. The world is rebuilding without blueprints.
The unexpected is normal. Strange things are written into the building
codes. The environment is the louder antagonist; sentient opposition is
real but rarely the whole shape of a scene.

The rules stay open. The dice stay where you can see them. Scenes end
before they should. What do you do?
