# Intermission-01: Player Requests → Arc Prep

Four players, four passes. All requests received. Notes below for integrating into scene design.

## Committed scenes — build these explicitly

**A. Bren learns about her corridor (turn 1 of arc, or pre-scene)**
- Mereth reports back. Bren needs one beat where she finds out whether the corridor held.
- Small scene or even a line. Kit: "it just needs to land."
- Good place: before cargo is loaded, or in the same moment as the strong-start dock scene.

**B. Tic-tracer scene: Drova asks Tek**
- Drova asks because the instrument doesn't fit a category she knows. Not a confrontation.
- Fei is present. She noticed before the question was asked. She hasn't filed it.
- Scene: two people with adjacent skill sets (Drova = compliance/documents; Tek = engineering/meter) figuring out what they're looking at. Tek decides what to say.
- Build the room: small space, Tek doing maintenance, Drova passing through, Fei nearby.
- Happens before the cell becomes a problem.

**C. Fei renders the four glyphs**
- She recorded the contact in listener shorthand during the prelude shear crisis.
- The crew knows she logged something. They don't know what.
- Scene: Fei renders the shorthand into readable language. What comes out is load-bearing for the arc — something that matters.
- She decides what to translate fully and what stays in shorthand only she can read.
- She's trained as a first-pass reader. This is the first time she has to be the one deciding what gets surfaced.

**D. The misdeclared cell — four entry points**
- Fei reads the rack: "this one reads different from the others." She sets down the concentrate flask. Found it before it's a problem.
- Drova reads the manifest: she finds it because she reads manifests. The question is what she does — file, sit on it, or use it. It costs something either way. No obvious answer.
- Tek's tic-tracer beeps hard when it shouldn't. The crew starts to understand why she built it. Her prep pays off in a way she can't explain without admitting what she is. Not a redemption beat — just prep paying off.
- Bren files the correct parts list without matching it to the official cause document. Waits to see if anyone notices. That's the kind of thing she'd do.
- These are separate beats that land in the same crew-level scene.

## Threads to hold live — one scene each, not resolution

**Tek: Shear run date discrepancy**

- Not the answer — just the shape of the question. Tev: "close enough to the right archive that she knows the shape of what she's looking for."
- Possibly Node E (waystation) or adjacent to the Halfsign damage investigation.

**Drova: Senna Talindra letters**

**Drova: Conclave attention on the listener-window supplement**
- She filed it in the supplement (Section 4, subsection 8). It's on paper, with her name.
- Scene: someone in the Conclave is following the refusal log and noticed it. Not hostile, maybe. Drova can't tell if it's an ally or a problem.
- Possible route: Tal Vorenis (Accord clerks, Node F) has read Risca's procedural note — could he be the one following the log? Or Veth, from the other direction.

**Fei: Mernhab cross-reference**

**Bren: Previous drop-pilot gap**

**Bren: Halfsign true cause**

**Bren: One thing technically wrong by the manifest but right by the drop**
