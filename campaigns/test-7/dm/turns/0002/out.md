# Turn 2 — Mara

The mug is on the table.

Campaign-planning, second invocation. Foundation built. The
encyclopedia is populated, the opening arc is scaffolded, the public
clock is ticking, and there is a duskgrain cell sitting in a
microcavity housing in the *Splitfork*'s cargo bay that doesn't know
yet whose decision put it there. I don't know either. That's the
point.

## What I built this turn

**Three factions, in `dm/notes/factions/`:**

- **Echo Ledger Conclave (campaign layer)** — the world-bible entry
  is already imported; this is what's true about them *this season*.
  Their goal: neutralize the Ledger Run's refusal log without being
  seen to do so. Mechanism: a quiet contracts-office dossier on
  delivery irregularities, compiled at night. 5-segment clock.
- **Vantara (campaign layer)** — same pattern over the world-bible
  entry. Goal: pass the Article 14 amendment. Their internal split
  (route officers vs. legal team) is a leverage point for the players
  if they find it. 5-segment clock = the public clock.
- **The Accord clerks** — campaign-original, distinct from the
  Accord-as-institution. The clerks are the party's actual
  procedural interface with the framework. 3-segment *clerks defer*
  clock, plus an Author/Defaulter internal split.

**Six NPCs, in `dm/notes/npcs/`:**

- **Mereth Kel** — captain. Carries the refusal log. Has refused two
  Conclave research letters in the past three years that named
  living Bloom survivors and has *not* logged either. The third
  unloggable refusal will break something in her.
- **Ev Sken** — Hab-Worlder fae fixer. Sometimes a person, sometimes
  the warmth in the corner. Was at Fermata during the failed Sit-In
  and has not told anyone.
- **Halvi Tern** — Glasswake-side dispatcher. Keeps a *clean ledger*
  parallel to Mereth's *refusal log*. Two ledgers, two sides of the
  same accounting.
- **Veth Karran** *(antagonist, flagged)* — Sithari Conclave
  archivist on the night-shift seal-setter rotation. The web pull
  is her: the auditor who closes the day's books at 0200, alone,
  and decides the totals are what they are. Has a 4-segment personal
  clock. Is also the clerk who, on detail rotation, took both of
  Mereth's unlogged refusals — neither of them knows the other knows.
- **Risca Donn** — Vantara's Sable Crescent route officer. Mereth's
  ex-colleague. Has had two opportunities to walk off her Vantara
  job over a sealed letter and twice did not. Both decisions are
  alive in her.
- **Tal Vorenis** — Sithari Accord clerk who has been indexing the
  Ledger Run's quarterly refusal log against Vantara filings for
  nineteen consecutive quarters. Author-caucus minority. The party's
  most useful procedural ally if they ever realize he exists.

**Two creatures, in `dm/notes/creatures/`:**

- **Threshold Blooms** — Bloom-touched flock at the south end of the
  Sable Crescent, attracted to full-spectrum hum. Mechanically: the
  rack thickens the flock if it's full-spectrum. Sensory tell rather
  than combat threat.
- **The Drag** — Span Nine resonance phenomenon. Some couriers say
  it's alive. The Conclave officially says it isn't. *I am not
  resolving this.* It is genuinely play-to-find-out.

**Five named things, in `dm/notes/ships/` and `dm/notes/artifacts/`:**

- **The *Splitfork*** — primary kite. Mereth's eccentric cargo-cradle
  repair is the reason she holds line in a resonance-tide.
- **The *Halfsign*** — grounded secondary. The official cause of
  damage is *Shear*; the cradle's stress patterns say otherwise. I
  have four candidates on the board for what actually happened. Will
  pick at the table.
- **The Caulden Rack** — opening arc cargo. Twelve cells, declared
  standard, one cell duskgrain in microcavity housing. Hums right.
  Reads right on a casual scan. Threshold Blooms can hear it.
- **The Refusal Log** — the hand-bound book. Twenty years. Four
  rebindings. The campaign's central recurring artifact.
- **The Withdrawn Letter** — the founding envelope. Daughter on Hab
  Meridian still alive; Halvi's sibling works the same dock. Two
  threads not currently connected, in the players' awareness.

**Five locations, in `dm/notes/locales/`:**

- The Glasswake reclaimed dock (home port).
- Span Nine handoff ledge (Vantara handoff; the Drag latent here).
- Thornvault Stoneward courtyard (Conclave receiving — where the
  party will meet Veth).
- Hab Meridian receiving dock (containment-rated to *kinetic-band*,
  not full-spectrum — the Caulden Rack's worst-case lands here).
- Threshold Station courtyard drop (Synod silence-drop, latent —

**Ten atomic secrets, in `dm/notes/secrets.md`.** Three-Clue check
done on the Caulden Rack misdeclaration: the flock thickening, a
Tuner's active hum-check, and Halvi's clean-ledger cross-check.
Three independent paths, three different access modes (in transit,
sensory, paperwork). The players can reach it through any of them.

**Four hooks, in `dm/notes/hooks/`.** Article 14, the Halfsign repair,
the refusal-log-cited investigation, and the Caulden Rack itself
(held back from character creation; surfaces as the strong-start

**One philosophy entry: `dm/notes/philosophy/witness-and-record.md`.**
A *record* is what was filed; a *witness* is what someone saw. The
campaign's Question lives in the gap between them. The Refusal Log is
deliberately a strange object — a record of witness work. Holding
this so the prep stays honest.

**One opening arc, scaffolded with `glass arc create`:**
**`arcs/caulden-rack/`.** Stakes question — *whose decision was it
that the Caulden Rack would be misdeclared, and what does the crew
do with that fact once they know it?* Four threats (Veth, Vantara
through Risca, the Rack itself, the route's own weather). Three
clocks (Conclave dossier, Article 14 amendment public, the Rack's
own three-segment arc clock). Five end-state shapes — none of them
mine. Strong start scene at the Glasswake dock with a Caulden
courier named Per Vask whose dispatch slip Halvi reads twice. Seven
nodes graphed for investigation, with clue-paths between them. Five
arc-secrets. Done criteria.

**One public durable clock:** `article-14-amendment` (0/5, public,
anchored to the Caulden Rack arc). Players can see this from turn
one of play; it ticks on the calendar and on whose comment record
carries weight.

## The Question and the Scarcity, restated

> **Question:** *Who decides what the system remembers?*
>
> **Scarcity:** *Witnesses.*

Both of them got their first piece of concrete material this turn:

- The duskgrain cell in the Caulden Rack is *something the world
  does not know yet*, sitting inside *something that has been
  declared as something else*. It is the Question made into cargo.
- Veth Karran, alone at 0200, deciding which seals close — she is
  the institutional version of the same act. The decision happens
  whether anyone witnesses it or not. The campaign's load-bearing
  opposition is exactly this: not villains, but unobserved
  procedure.

## Pulls used this turn

- **Lore pull:** `player/concepts/duskgrain.md` — off-gradient (I
  was not going to deal with material substances). The fact: a
  crystallized resonance that releases across the full spectrum
  simultaneously, with a hum Tuners describe as unsettling. That
  fact landed in the Caulden Rack's twelfth cell, in the Threshold
  Blooms' attraction behavior, and in the opening arc's whole
  material substance. One pull, three downstream uses.
- **Web pull:** night auditor work at hotels. The texture: the
  auditor closes the day's books between midnight and 0200, alone,
  and decides the totals are what they are. That texture became
  Veth Karran's entire shape — her *deeper* want isn't winning the
  Conclave's argument; it's clearing the night-shift seal queue by
  0200. She is a person, not an institutional archetype. The
  rewrite was needed; the first two drafts of the antagonist were
  generic.

Cumulative across both planning turns: 2 lore pulls, 2 web pulls.
That's the lower bound of the methodology's 4-6 lore + 3-4 web. I'm
holding at the lower bound because the texture density per pull was
high — each one cascaded into multiple downstream pieces — rather
than spreading thinly across more pulls.

## What I deliberately did NOT prep

Standing list, restated for honesty:

- **A planned climax.** I do not know how the Question gets answered.
- **Whose decision** substituted the Caulden Rack's twelfth cell. Five
  plausible authors on the board. The players' pull selects.
- **What actually damaged the Halfsign.** Four candidates. Same
  pattern.
- **Whether the Drag is alive.** Genuinely undecided. The campaign
  may answer it. The campaign may not.
- **A reveal arc on the elves, the Adversary, the Communication
  Shard.** World-bible DM truths. Not what this campaign is for.
- **NPC voices.** I do not do voices. Affect through word choice,
  silence, the mug.
- **The PCs' internal motivations.** That is character creation's
  jurisdiction, which is the next mode.

## Coherence check

- Factions have reasons to act: Conclave wants the contract lapsed;
  Vantara wants the amendment passed; the clerks want the queue
  cleared without authoring blame. Each has its own clock.
- NPCs have wants and fears tied to factions: Veth to the Conclave,
  Risca to Vantara, Tal to the clerks, Mereth and Ev and Halvi to
  the Ledger Run. The wants pull the clocks; the fears are where
  the players find leverage.
- Hooks tie back: Article 14 → Vantara + clerks. Refusal-log-cited
  → Conclave + Tal. Halfsign repair → finances + a deferred
  mystery. Caulden Rack → opening arc, ties everything.
- The named things sit on stories worth pulling on: the
  *Splitfork*'s reflection-corridor naming, the Withdrawn Letter's
  daughter, the Refusal Log's twenty-year continuity, the Caulden
  Rack's substituted cell.
- The imported lore is load-bearing: Conclave / Vantara / Accord
  for factional grounding, Glasswake / Span Nine for routing,
  duskgrain / kite-sail / tuners for material texture, the species
  and cultures for naming the people who do the work.
- The philosophy holds: every NPC and every clock can be read
  through *witness vs. record*, and that frame keeps me honest in
  scene adjudication.
