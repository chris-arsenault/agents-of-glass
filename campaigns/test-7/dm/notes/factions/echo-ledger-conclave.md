# Echo Ledger Conclave (campaign-specific)

World-bible entry already imported at `shared/lore/npcs/factions/echo-ledger-conclave.md`. That file is the public, system-wide picture: archivists, gatekeepers, headquartered at Thornvault. This file is *what's true about them in this campaign, this season*.

## One-sentence identity (this campaign)

The Conclave is the institution most threatened by the Ledger Run's refusal log, and the institution most useful to it. They will not say either thing out loud.

## Goal — right now

**Neutralize the refusal log without being seen to do so.** The Conclave's senior archivists have decided, quietly, that the Ledger Run's quarterly refusal-log filing has begun to be cited in Accord proceedings — twice in the last year, both times to the Conclave's procedural disadvantage. They want this to stop. They want it to stop without acknowledging that the log matters.

The mechanism they've chosen: an internal seal-review of the standing Ledger Run research-mail contract, conducted by the Conclave's night-shift archivists, with the goal of producing a clean recommendation that the contract should not be renewed at the next quarter. The recommendation will cite delivery irregularities — *real ones,* the Conclave is patient — and the contract will lapse without theatre.

If the contract lapses, the Ledger Run loses one of three standing contracts and a major load-bearing client. They become smaller. The refusal log shrinks in apparent significance. Nobody had to attack it.

## Tier

System-wide reach, mid-tier influence. They cannot order the Ledger Run shut down. They can starve it.

## Hold

- **Thornvault** itself — the receiving courtyard the Splitfork docks at every loop.
- **The seals.** Who can read what, when, under what authority. The Conclave's night-shift archivists set the seals. By morning, a sealed entry is a sealed entry. There is no appeal.
- **The standing research-mail contract** with the Ledger Run, currently up for renewal this quarter.

## Clock — *Conclave quietly closes the loop*

5 segments. This is the antagonist clock that drives the opening arc's pressure.

1. **Night-shift seal review opens.** A Conclave archivist (Veth Karran — see `dm/notes/npcs/veth-karran.md`) is assigned to compile a delivery-irregularity dossier on the Ledger Run's last eight quarters. *(Already started, before play begins.)*
2. **The dossier finds its first real entry.** A genuinely irregular handoff — something the crew did or that happened on the route — gets formally logged with the Conclave's procedural office.
3. **The dossier is shared with Vantara.** The two factions are not allies. They share an interest. Karran's dossier becomes a footnote in a Vantara filing on the Article 14 amendment.
4. **The contract review is escalated.** The Conclave's contracts office issues a notice of intent to non-renew. The Ledger Run is informed by formal letter. They have one quarter to respond.
5. **The contract lapses.** Quietly. The Ledger Run loses the research-mail line. The refusal log loses its load-bearing citation.

The clock advances on a tick of *the Conclave is not interrupted in this work*. Players who interrupt — by surfacing the dossier, by cleaning up an irregularity before it lands, by giving Karran a different problem to solve — slow it. Players who do nothing watch it tick.

## Relationships — this campaign

- **Vantara:** parallel interests, no formal alliance. Both want the Ledger Run smaller. Vantara has the Article 14 amendment; the Conclave has the seal review. They share the dossier the second time it gets written, not the first.
- **The Ledger Run:** structurally resented, individually used. Several Conclave archivists rely on the crew personally — including one who pays for off-the-books authentication couriering through the black-route list. Internal contradiction the Conclave has not resolved.
- **The Accord clerks:** neutral. The clerks file what the Conclave gives them. The clerks read more of it than the Conclave assumes.
- **The Synod:** silent. The Conclave has tried for forty years to get the Synod to confirm or deny readings of pre-Glassfall echoes. The Synod neither confirms nor denies. The Conclave has stopped asking and started filing it as policy.

## Internal split (DM-only)

The Conclave is not monolithic on the Ledger Run question. A faction inside Thornvault — call it the **Open-Seal caucus** — believes the refusal log is *useful infrastructure* and that the Conclave's interests are better served by partnering with it than starving it. They are a minority. They are losing the argument internally. One of them is the off-the-books client on Mereth's black-route list. Another is a Tuner-archivist in the satellite Sithari office who has not yet acted.

This is reachable for play if the party finds it. Not deployed as a hook by default.
