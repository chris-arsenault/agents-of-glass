# Vantara (campaign-specific)

World-bible entry already imported at `shared/lore/npcs/factions/vantara.md`. That file is the system-wide picture: largest civilian kite operator, hospitality network, route infrastructure, armed escorts. This file is the local pressure that touches the Ledger Run.

## One-sentence identity (this campaign)

The corporation that Mereth used to work for, that needs the Article 14 carriers' margins, and that has filed the amendment that would take those margins away.

## Goal — right now

**Pass the Article 14 amendment** at Accord clerks' level and restore margin on bulk mail. The amendment is drafted, filed, sitting in the queue. It strips tariff exemption from independent civil carriers (Article 14, the Ledger Run's charter) on bulk mail above a certain weight threshold. Vantara's margin on bulk mail has been compressed by independents undercutting them on routes Vantara already maintains the infrastructure for. The amendment closes the loophole.

Vantara's pitch to the clerks is reasonable on its face: *we maintain the beacons, we run the rescue coverage, we pay for the waystations the independents use; the tariff exemption was for when the independents were doing public-good work the corporations weren't, and the corporations now are.* The pitch is partly true.

## Tier

System-wide. Larger than any single Accord institution. Does not control the clerks but pays a great deal of attention to them.

## Hold

- **The route infrastructure** itself — every waystation between Glasswake and the Sable Crescent is Vantara-operated or Vantara-affiliated. The Ledger Run pays Vantara waystation fees on every loop.
- **The legal team** drafting and lobbying the amendment.
- **The Sable Crescent route officer**, Risca Donn (see `dm/notes/npcs/risca-donn.md`), who handles independent-carrier liaison for the entire region. She is the face the Ledger Run sees.
- **A subcontracting relationship** — Vantara occasionally hires the Ledger Run for routes Vantara can't justify staffing. The subcontract is small and load-bearing for the crew's secondary income.

## Clock — *Article 14 amendment becomes law*

This is the **public durable clock** the players can see and track. 5 segments. I will create it via `glass clock` this turn with `--public`.

1. **Amendment filed at clerks' level.** *(Already done, before play begins.)*
2. **Amendment is heard.** A clerks' committee reviews and either advances or returns. Vantara pushes for advance.
3. **Public comment period.** Independent carriers, hab municipalities, and affected industries file responses. The Ledger Run's quarterly refusal log is, predictably, on the comment record.
4. **Clerks return a recommendation.** Either to ratify, modify, or reject.
5. **The Accord ratifies or doesn't.** If ratified, the Ledger Run loses tariff exemption on bulk mail at the next quarterly filing. The org's margin shrinks; the *Halfsign* repair budget evaporates; one of the standing contracts becomes uneconomic to keep.

The clock advances on the calendar — and on whose comment record carries the most weight. Players can intervene by lobbying clerks (see Tal Vorenis), by surfacing facts about Vantara's infrastructure subsidy claims, by negotiating directly with Risca Donn, or by changing the public's perception of the refusal log.

## Relationships — this campaign

- **Echo Ledger Conclave:** parallel interests, occasional information-sharing. See the Conclave entry's clock.
- **Accord clerks:** Vantara's lobbyists know the clerks by face. Some clerks resent this. Tal Vorenis specifically.
- **The Ledger Run:** Mereth used to work for them. Risca Donn replaced her on the Sable Crescent route in 2419. Risca has not forgiven Mereth for walking off the job — and has, in private, considered her own version of the same walk-off twice in the past five years.
- **The Synod:** Vantara delivers contracted goods to Threshold Station's standing-courtyard drop. They do not understand the drop. They consider not-understanding-the-drop to be Vantara's competitive advantage.
- **Glasswake locals:** mixed. Vantara's hospitality is reliable and dull. The dock pubs prefer the Ledger Run.

## Internal split (DM-only)

Vantara's **route-officer corps** (the working liaisons like Risca) and Vantara's **legal team** (the amendment lobbyists) disagree about the amendment. The route officers know the independents' refusal logs and quirks save them money — the independents do work Vantara doesn't want and absorb risk Vantara won't insure. The legal team has metrics. Risca has not raised her objection to the amendment internally because she doesn't think it would change anything and because raising it would mark her as soft.

If the players surface this split — by giving Risca a reason to raise the objection out loud — the amendment slows or stalls. This is play-to-find-out territory.
