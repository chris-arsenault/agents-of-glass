# The Glasswake Reclaimed Dock

The Splitfork's home port. A reclaimed warehouse and its dock fragment, on the surface side of Glasswake, in what used to be an old hab-debris staging yard before Glasswake reclaimed it.

## Sensory grounding

The floor tilts a quarter-degree to the south, where the original hab fragment is still settling into the sand. The ringglass on the south wall hums in response to dock cranes — a slow rising pitch as a crane lifts, a slow falling pitch as it sets down. The dock crews use the wall as an unofficial lift counter.

## What's here

- **The office.** Reclaimed warehouse. Refusal log archive (twenty years of quarterly transcriptions, in cabinets). A small bunkroom for the crew when the kite is grounded. Halvi's desk by the open window. The framed envelope of the Withdrawn Letter behind the desk.
- **The Tuner-rated cargo cradle.** A standing fixture in the dock's loading bay where resonance-classified cargo is checked before loading. Mereth's repair to the Splitfork's cradle was made here.
- **The Splitfork's berth.** Currently the kite is in the berth or on the loop, depending on the scene.
- **The Halfsign's berth.** Tarped. Visible from the office window. The crew refers to her as *the grounded sister* without affect.
- **The dock pub.** Two doors down. Run by the previous Ledger Run bookkeeper. Not on the office's books. Where everything actually gets discussed.

## Who's usually there

- Halvi, in office hours, by the window.
- Mereth, when the Splitfork is in port, generally in the bunkroom corner.
- Ev, sometimes coherent at the desk, sometimes the warmth in the doorway.
- Two or three dock workers on standing call for kite handling.
- One or two regular clients dropping off bonded packages.
- Occasionally: a Conclave courier from the Glasswake satellite, in for a research-mail handoff.

## Hooks

- The Halfsign — visible from every scene set here.
- The framed envelope — visible from every scene set here.
- The dock pub — alternate scene venue, harder for institutional NPCs to set foot in, easier for gossip to land.
