# Threshold Station Standing-Courtyard Drop

The Lattice Proxy Synod's accepted point of contact with the Ledger Run — and with everyone else who sends them anything. An open courtyard on Threshold Station where deliveries are left, signed for by no one, and collected by mechanism nobody currently alive has watched in real time.

The Synod does not engage in person. The courtyard exists so that not-engaging-in-person can happen procedurally.

## Sensory grounding

A wide stone-floored courtyard, open to the station's atrium above, no walls — just pillars marking the perimeter. The atrium's resonance environment is unusually still — the Synod has the courtyard tuned to a baseline silence that swallows ambient hum. Tuners who walk into the courtyard report the absence as a physical pressure. Couriers who walk in report nothing and notice nothing and walk out again.

On certain frequency windows, the silence *responds* to a delivery — a tonal shift in the station's ambient resonance, audible only to Tuners who actively listen for it. Mereth has noticed. She has not told the crew. She does not understand it.

## What's here

- A drop platform in the courtyard's center. A delivery is placed on the platform; the courier signs a delivery form left on a plinth at the courtyard's edge; the courier leaves; the delivery is gone the next time anyone looks.
- A logbook on the plinth (Synod-maintained, in a script nobody else reads) where deliveries are logged in some way.
- No Synod presence visible to the courier. Ever.

## Who's usually there

- The courier (in this case the Splitfork's crew, on Synod-routed loops).
- Nobody else. Visibly.

## Hooks

- The tonal-shift response — discoverable for a Tuner PC.
- The logbook — readable for someone who has cracked the Synod's script (extremely rare, possibly campaign-shifting).
- Mereth's quiet observation that the silence *answers* — surface if a player asks her about the Synod and she trusts them.

## Note

Latent locale. The Splitfork's standing route does not normally include Threshold Station. Synod-routed deliveries are sub-contracts, accepted irregularly. This locale is on the board for whenever the campaign reaches it. Mara is not steering toward it.
