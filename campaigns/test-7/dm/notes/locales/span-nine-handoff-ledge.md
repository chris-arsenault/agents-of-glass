# Span Nine Hand-Off Ledge

A specific point on the Span Nine relay where the Ledger Run hands off Sithari-bound surface mail to a Vantara line. Not the whole relay — one ledge, one bay, one Vantara dock master. The campaign-specific layer of the world-bible Span Nine entry (already imported at `shared/lore/locations/landmarks/span-nine.md`).

## Sensory grounding

The relay is a pre-Glassfall conduit ridge — open structure, no atmosphere, kite-tag operations only. The hand-off ledge is a docking armature on the spine of the ridge, with the Sithari-side glasslight reflecting up from below at a steep angle. The ringglass under the dock hums on a different note than Glasswake's — a higher, drier pitch. Couriers say it sounds like the relay is *thinking about* something.

## What's here

- The Vantara hand-off bay. One docking armature, one bay-master, one set of cradles.
- A Ledger Run cargo locker (rented quarterly from Vantara) where overflow surface mail can be staged for the next loop.
- A small Compact monitoring station, automated, with a human inspector who shows up for one shift per Earth-week and rubber-stamps everything else.
- The reflection corridor where Mereth's documented Splitfork run was made. Visible from the docking armature on certain windows. Not signposted.

## Who's usually there

- The Vantara dock master (a different one each week — Vantara rotates the position; the rotation is part of the corporate consistency).
- An automated cargo manifest scanner.
- Occasionally a passing Compact inspector.
- The Drag, sometimes, a kilometer or so off the ridge, on the right resonance windows.

## Hooks

- The Drag — visible if a player asks the right Tuner-aware question.
- The reflection corridor — discoverable if a player asks Mereth about the Splitfork's name and gets the second sentence of the answer.
- The Vantara dock master rotation — useful if the players want to track when a specific Vantara face will be present.
