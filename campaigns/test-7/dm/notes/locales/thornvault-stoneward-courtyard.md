# Thornvault Stoneward Courtyard

The Echo Ledger Conclave's receiving courtyard at Thornvault. Where the Splitfork docks for the standing research-mail contract handoff. Where the players will meet Veth Karran the first time.

## Sensory grounding

A walled courtyard built into the side of the Thornvault canyon-bridge complex, open to the sky, faces the glasslight at dusk. The ringglass-laced buttresses overhead hum in a structured chord — the architecture is *tuned* for it; the building is its own slow instrument. The courtyard floor is paved in flat stone slabs that absorb sound; footsteps are muffled, conversations carry only a few meters.

The Conclave's archivists prefer this courtyard for handoffs because the architecture makes private conversations possible without screening.

## What's here

- A docking armature for kites of the Splitfork's class.
- A receiving table where manifests are read and signed.
- A small Conclave sub-office (locked, normally) where contracts work happens.
- Two stone benches against the south wall, for waiting.
- A side passage to the satellite Sithari office's courier track — used by Conclave archivists going between Thornvault and Sithari without going through the main hall.

## Who's usually there

- One Conclave archivist on duty for handoffs (rotating schedule; Veth Karran is the night-shift archivist for contracts).
- One or two Thornvault dock crew (Conclave-employed, hab-worker culture, not archivists themselves).
- Occasionally a Conclave senior coming through to or from the side passage.

## Hooks

- The architecture's tuned chord — Tuner PCs will read it as a deliberate configuration. They are correct. The Conclave does not tell visitors why.
- Veth Karran's first appearance.
- The side passage to Sithari — useful if a player wants to follow a Conclave archivist out without going through the main hall.
