# The Halfsign

The Ledger Run's secondary kite. Currently grounded at the Glasswake dock fragment, awaiting repairs the crew cannot fully fund without a margin the Splitfork's loops aren't quite producing.

## Provenance

Bought used in 2429 from a Sable-side independent who couldn't keep her in the air. Lighter than the Splitfork — two-crew rated, smaller cargo bay, faster turning. Mereth named her *Halfsign* because her kite-tag was registered under a clipped form of the previous owner's signature, which the Compact accepted as filed and which Mereth chose not to correct. *Half a sign is what we got and half a sign is what we'll fly.*

## Current status

Grounded at the Glasswake dock fragment. Tarped. Visible from the office window. Mereth visits her at the end of every loop and adds to the repair list.

The official cause of grounding is **Shear damage on a deep-route detour, last quarter.** This is what the manifest filing says. This is what the crew has been told.

## What actually happened (DM-only)

She did not take Shear damage. She was damaged by *something the crew currently aboard could not name what they saw.* The Splitfork's crew were not aboard for the incident — the Halfsign was on a sub-contract from Vantara, with a temporary two-crew Mereth doesn't usually use. That two-crew filed the Shear-damage report. They have not contradicted it. They have also not signed onto another Halfsign loop.

What happened is open. Mara has not decided. Possibilities on the board:

- A Drag encounter that escalated past ordinary distortion.
- A Bloom-touched thing the cordon authority does not list.
- A salvage-faction skirmish nobody wants on the record.
- Something the elves left.

The damage is consistent with several of these. The repair list reads as Shear-damage on the surface and as something-else if you know what to look for. Mereth has not looked closely. She does not want to know yet.

## What she does, when she's flying

Same as the Splitfork at smaller scale. Faster, lighter, less cargo. Better in the Sable-side cordon corridors. Worse in the deep Span Nine reflection windows.

## Hook

The Halfsign's repair is operational pressure on the org's finances and is the campaign's slow-burn mystery. If the players investigate the *real* damage, they will reach one of the open possibilities above. If they don't investigate, the Halfsign sits at the dock and the financial pressure tightens.

A second pressure: if the Article 14 amendment passes, the repair budget evaporates entirely and the Halfsign is sold for parts. Mara has flagged this as a stakes-bearing consequence in the opening arc.
