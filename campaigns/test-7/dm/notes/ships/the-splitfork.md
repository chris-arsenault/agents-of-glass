# The Splitfork

The Ledger Run's primary kite-hauler. The kite the players are crew on. Mid-class, four-crew, cargo-bay rated for bonded packages and resonance-classified materials, Shear-rated but route-preferring.

## Provenance

Built in 2417 at the Glasswake yards as a Vantara-spec courier. Sold off after a docking incident that bent the cargo cradle. Mereth bought her in 2420 for the Ledger Run, repaired the cradle herself with a Tuner-grade alignment that nobody at the yards has reproduced, and named her in 2421 for a documented run she made along a *split Span-Nine reflection corridor* — a configuration the relay only briefly held during a resonance-tide event, which she navigated by dead reckoning and a working knowledge of where a reflection would and would not bend.

The story is in the Compact's archives. The crew has heard it once. Mereth does not retell it.

## Current status

In the air. Loop-in-progress for the standing route. Currently between Glasswake and Span Nine on the outbound leg, or about to depart, depending on the scene.

## What she does

- **Cargo bay:** rated for two standard microcavity racks, four bonded-package pallets, and one Tuner-cradle for resonance-classified items.
- **Crew rest:** four bunks. Cramped. The captain's bunk is a curtained corner with the refusal log on a shelf above it.
- **Kite-cord:** standard four-line configuration. Mereth's repaired cargo cradle reads three lines as primary instead of the standard two; this is widely considered eccentric and is, in fact, why the Splitfork holds its line in a resonance-tide better than its sister hulls.
- **Tuner instrument bay:** a recess in the kite's central spine for resonance instruments. Two bays. Currently one is in use; the other is the cradle Mereth uses for kite-cord checks.
- **Speed:** route speed standard. Can push to deep-Shear-rated speeds for short bursts; the cord-cradle does not love this.

## Hook

The Splitfork is the campaign's *vehicle* in every sense. Scenes happen on her. Decisions get made on her. Cargo gets loaded onto her and refused at her dock. Mereth has flown her for sixteen years and considers her not-quite-a-character but close.

If she is damaged in play, the campaign is constrained until she's repaired. If she is destroyed, the campaign reshapes around the loss — Mereth will not buy another. The Halfsign would have to be made airworthy. (And the Halfsign's grounded for reasons the crew has not surfaced.)
