# Veth Karran

*Sithari human, late thirties, Thornvault.* Echo Ledger Conclave archivist, night-shift seal-setter. The campaign's primary recurring antagonist. Has a clock. Has a job that nobody else wants. Has reasons.

## Tell

Comes to the Splitfork's docking bay at Thornvault wearing a charcoal coat too thick for the courtyard's microclimate, a clipboard with one page on it, and a small enamel cup of something hot that she does not offer to share. She introduces herself by title only — *Conclave archivist, contracts office* — and does not give her personal name unless asked twice. When asked the second time, she gives it without resentment, like checking a box on the form.

She listens better than she speaks. She nods once, slowly, when she understands something. She nods twice, slowly, when she has decided what to do about it.

## Wants

- **The Ledger Run's research-mail contract not to be renewed at the next quarter.** This is her assignment from the Conclave's contracts office. She did not ask for it. She is going to do it well.
- **The night-shift seal queue cleared by 0200 every night.** This is her *personal* want, and the deeper one. The shift exists because someone has to close the day's seals before the morning archivists arrive. She is the person who decides, alone, that the day is done. She takes pride in this in a way she would not put words to.

## Fears

- **A sealed entry being publicly contested in a way she cannot procedurally answer.** Specifically: a refusal log entry that names a Conclave seal she set, with cause, that other Conclave archivists then have to defend. The refusal log puts her work in the open. She has not yet been named in it. She is afraid of the day she is.
- **Being the one whose name is on the Ledger Run termination.** The recommendation will go up the chain with her signature on the dossier. She knows it. She has not told her supervisor that the signing is going to bother her.

## Bond / hook

- Has been a night-shift archivist for nine years. Recommended for promotion to day shift twice. Declined both times without explanation.
- Has her own off-the-books practice — not on Mereth's black-route list, separate — of authenticating personal letters from Bloom survivors to family on other habs, on her own time, without seal. She does not know that Mereth's black-route list includes another Conclave archivist doing similar work. She would not believe it if told.
- Is procedurally polite to Mereth. Has refused, twice, to log the refusals Mereth requested be left out of the public log. (Mereth has not realized that the *Conclave* clerk who took the unlogged refusals was Veth, on detail rotation. Veth knows. Veth has said nothing.)

## Next move (if the players never appear)

The dossier on the Ledger Run continues to grow. The next genuinely irregular handoff (and one *will* happen — the route has weather) gets logged. The dossier's first real entry lands. Veth files a copy with a Vantara legal contact (see Conclave clock segment 3). The contract review is scheduled. She drafts the recommendation by hand. She signs it. She does not sleep that night.

## What players will see in the first round

A polite, tired, unornamented woman in a coat too thick for the weather. She has their manifest before they hand it to her. She returns it with one small note on the back — *please clarify line 7* — and waits.

## Mechanics

She does not fight. If physically threatened, she goes to a procedural office and files a complaint. The complaint has weight; the Conclave will respond to threats against archivists with sanctions that compound across the system. She is not a combat opponent. She is a slow-clocked institutional one.

For the rare scene where she is unavoidably present in a fight: HP low-mortal, unarmed, will not run because her clipboard is the witness she is not willing to surrender.

## Antagonist clock

Veth's personal advancement clock — distinct from the Conclave faction clock she serves. 4 segments.

1. **First real irregularity logged.** A handoff goes wrong on the route. Veth logs it, formally.
2. **Dossier shared with Vantara contact.** The two adversary clocks (Conclave + Vantara) start synchronizing.
3. **Recommendation drafted.** Veth signs the non-renewal. Sends it up the chain.
4. **Contract terminated.** The Ledger Run loses the research-mail line.

She advances this clock on her own schedule. Players can interrupt by giving her a different problem to solve (see her off-the-books practice — a player could surface that work in a way that *protects* her, building leverage), by surfacing the dossier inside the Conclave (the Open-Seal caucus would use it), or by doing something genuinely irregular enough that the dossier writes itself and she is forced to take her name off it.

## Open hooks tied to her

- Her two refusals of Mereth's unlogged refusals — *she remembers*. If Mereth and Veth are in the same room and the refusal-log topic comes up, this surfaces.
- Her own off-the-books authentication practice — discoverable through Bloom-survivor family contacts.
- The supervisor who assigned her the dossier — not yet named. May surface later.
