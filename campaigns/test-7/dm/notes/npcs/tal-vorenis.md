# Tal Vorenis

*Sithari human, early forties, Sithari.* Mid-ranking Accord clerk. Reads the Ledger Run's quarterly refusal log. Indexes it. The party's most useful procedural ally, if they ever realize he exists.

## Tell

Wears the standard Accord clerk's mid-grey, no insignia, two pens clipped to the collar — one black, one red — and a third pen in his sleeve that nobody is supposed to see. Files his own paperwork rather than handing it to a runner, which marks him as either fastidious or distrusting; both readings are correct.

He has indexed the Ledger Run's refusal log for nineteen consecutive quarters. He has shown the index to no one. He uses it to cross-reference Vantara filings against independent-carrier comment records. He has not told the Authors' caucus that he does this. He has not told the Defaulters' caucus, either.

## Wants

- **A procedural cover story for drafting the Article 14 amendment recommendation himself.** Without one, he will let the default — the Vantara-friendly framing — go through. He is an Author by temperament, but he is not a martyr. He needs the paper trail.
- **The refusal log to keep being filed.** It is a load-bearing document for his entire indexing project, even though he cannot use the index publicly.

## Fears

- **Being named.** If his cross-reference index becomes attributable to him, his career is over; the Defaulters will see to it. He is not afraid of the political cost so much as of the loss of the indexing project.

## Bond / hook

- Knows Mereth by face. Has handed her receipts for nineteen quarters. Has never spoken with her about the log's contents. Considers this professional.
- Has noticed the Conclave dossier footnote in the Vantara filing. He has not yet decided what to do with the noticing.
- Has no relationship with Veth Karran beyond filing her contracts office submissions. He could not pick her out of a crowd.

## Next move (if the players never appear)

He drafts the Article 14 amendment recommendation in the Vantara-friendly framing because he has no procedural cover to do otherwise. Files it. Files it cleanly. Goes home. Indexes the next quarterly refusal log. Sees that the Ledger Run is now footnoted *by* the Conclave. Crosses the reference. Does not act.

## What players will see in the first round

If they ever look — a clerk who hands Mereth her quarterly receipt with a small nod and a double-check of the manifest signatures. They will not notice him unless someone (Mereth, Risca's note, an investigator) tells them to look.

## Mechanics

Not a combat character. If physically threatened, he files a procedural complaint that takes effect within forty-eight hours and that compounds into long-term institutional pressure. HP low-mortal. Unarmed. Patient.

## What he can be paid in

Procedural cover. Specifically: an external party — a hab municipality, a Sithari political contact, a Conclave Open-Seal caucus member — formally requests an Author-drafted framing on Article 14, *citing the refusal log as material evidence*. That request gives Tal the paper trail. He will draft.

The party can manufacture this request. They will need to know Tal exists, what he wants, and which external party will sign the request. None of this is on a silver platter. All of it is reachable through play.

## Open hooks tied to him

- The cross-reference index — discoverable through observing his drop-off ritual or asking the right clerks the wrong question.
- The Author/Defaulter split inside the clerks — accessible through him.
- The Sithari political contact who *would* sign the procedural cover request — not yet named. Mara has left this open.
