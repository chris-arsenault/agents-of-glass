# Halvi Tern

*Hab-Worlder human, early thirties, Glasswake-born.* The Glasswake-side dispatcher and bookkeeper who runs the Ledger Run office when the Splitfork is out. Not a PC. Newest of the standing crew — six years on the books, hired after the previous bookkeeper retired to run a dock pub.

## Tell

Sits with the dock-side window open in all weathers. Keeps the day's manifest tablet on the desk and the *previous* day's manifest tablet on a shelf, never the same shelf twice. Knows the names of every kite-cord supplier within a fortnight's reach by face and the prices of each by memory. Speaks fast. Does not waste the second sentence if the first one was enough.

## Wants

- **The Halfsign back in the air.** Not for Mereth's reasons — for the books. Two kites means two revenue streams and the ability to absorb a missed loop. One kite means the office runs hand-to-mouth.
- **A clean ledger.** Halvi *also* keeps a record — separate from the refusal log — of every cargo that cleared. The clean ledger is theirs. They are quiet about it.

## Fears

- **A package arriving at Glasswake with a manifest that doesn't match what the crew handed off.** This has happened twice. Both times Halvi caught it. Both times Halvi did not tell Mereth. They told Ev. Ev told nobody.

## Bond / hook

- Reports to Mereth. Reports *separately* to Ev — same information, different framing. Has accepted that the two reporting lines will sometimes contradict.
- Knows every kite-cord supplier the Halfsign repair will need, and which one will give a Ledger Run discount and which will not. This is operational power that has not yet been used in play.
- Has a younger sibling on Hab Meridian who works the receiving dock there. The sibling does not know Halvi keeps the clean ledger separately.

## Next move (if the players never appear)

Halvi runs the office. Files Halfsign repair quotes that Mereth hasn't approved. Catches a third manifest mismatch, this time on a microcavity rack. Tells Ev. Ev diffuses.

## What players will see in the first round

The dispatcher who hands them their first paid loop's accounting at the end of it, line-by-line, before they ask. Asks them to sign for receipt. Files it.

## Mechanics

Not a combat character. Knows where the Splitfork's emergency cord-cache is kept. Knows the dock pub well enough to talk a brawl down. HP solid-mortal, no weapon training.

## Open hooks tied to them

- The clean ledger (parallel to the refusal log).
- The two unsurfaced manifest mismatches — load-bearing for the opening arc.
- The Hab Meridian sibling — opens a Hab Meridian-side investigation route.
