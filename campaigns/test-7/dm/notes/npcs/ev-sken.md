# Ev Sken

*Hab-Worlder fae, indeterminate age, route-resident.* Fixer for the Ledger Run. Books waystation slots, negotiates with Compact clerks, knows every dock master between Glasswake and Sable. Not a PC.

## Tell

Sometimes Ev is in the office in Glasswake holding a paper cup. Sometimes Ev is the warmth in the corner near the door and a pile of finished paperwork on the desk. Crewmates have learned to greet the corner the same way they greet the cup. Ev considers this acceptable. The dock masters have learned that *Ev will get back to you* means an answer in three hours, or forty hours, depending on which Ev they spoke to.

The epithet they go by professionally is **Soft Landing**. The clipped Hab-Worlder hab-name they use on dock paperwork is **Sken**. The true name is for nobody currently in this campaign.

## Wants

- **The route to keep running.** Their reason is not Mereth's reason. They will not say what their reason is. (DM-only: see Fears.)
- **The waystation contracts to stay routine.** Surprises in the paperwork are surprises in their day.

## Fears

- **Fermata Station appearing on the route.** They were at Fermata during the failed Sit-In thirty years ago. The dock master at Fermata has told nobody. The Ledger Run does not stop at Fermata. Ev has not asked for it to. If Mereth ever proposes adding Fermata to the route, Ev will diffuse for forty-eight hours and not coherently object. The route will not be added. Mereth will not know why.

## Bond / hook

- Has been with the Ledger Run since 2421 — second member after Mereth. They have not signed a charter document. They have not been asked to. The trust is older than the paperwork.
- Knows every Compact clerk between Glasswake and Sable by face. Several by their personal preferences in dockyard tea.
- Will not deal with Conclave seal officers personally. Hands those handoffs to someone else on the crew. This is unspoken policy.

## Next move (if the players never appear)

Ev fixes the waystation slots. Files the Compact paperwork. Diffuses on schedule. Is not present for two of the next four standing-courtyard drops at Threshold Station, with no explanation.

## What players will see in the first round

A presence in a corner of the Glasswake office who hands them their first manifest signed and stamped without having visibly written anything down. A tone of warmth. A quiet welcome. A request, on the way out, that they handle the Conclave handoff at Thornvault themselves, please, this loop.

## Mechanics

Ev does not fight. Ev cannot be reliably located in a fight. Ev's diffuse state is treated as off-screen for combat purposes; their coherent state has the standard fae intermittent-presence properties (see `shared/lore/species/fae.md`) and a non-combat utility: they can stand near a sealed manifest and tell you, with hours of inaccuracy, when the seal was set.

## Open hooks tied to them

- Fermata, latent. Will not surface unless someone makes the route question come up.
- Their refusal to handle Conclave seal officers — surface if a player asks why.
- The paperwork-without-writing tell — surface as a strange detail; let players read it however they like.
