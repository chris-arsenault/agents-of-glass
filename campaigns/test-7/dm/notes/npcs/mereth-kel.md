# Mereth Kel

*Hab-Worlder human, late forties, Glasswake by choice.* Captain and founder of the Ledger Run. The leader the players inherit. Not a PC.

## Tell

Carries a hand-bound copy of the refusal log everywhere. Sets it down on a flat surface before she sits. When she's about to refuse a package, she opens the log first — *before* she's said no — and turns it to face the would-be sender. The opening of the book is the answer. The conversation is the courtesy.

She speaks in short sentences. She does not raise her voice. She has gone two years without sleeping more than five hours a night and it shows in the skin under her eyes.

## Wants

- **The route runs.** All of it. Glasswake to Sable and back. Every fortnight. On the day it's supposed to.
- **The Halfsign back in the air.** The grounded kite in her dock fragment is a personal grievance. She visits it at the end of every loop and adds to its repair list.

## Fears

- **Being forced to choose between two refusals she cannot log.** She has refused two Conclave research letters in the past three years without entering them in the public log, because logging them would have surfaced living Bloom survivors' names. She has not told anyone. The third such refusal will break something. She knows this.
- **The route ending under her watch.** Not from any specific threat. From accumulated entropy. From letting it.

## Bond / hook

- Founded the Ledger Run in 2419 after walking off a Vantara route officer post during the Sable Crescent embargo. The founding moment is documented (she will tell it once if asked, briefly) and is the org's only mythologized story.
- Personally knows Risca Donn (Vantara, Sable Crescent route officer). They were colleagues. They have not spoken in seven years. They will see each other again this campaign and Mereth will not start the conversation.
- Personally knows one Conclave archivist on the off-the-books client list. The archivist pays cash for hand-to-hand authentications the Conclave wouldn't approve.

## Next move (if the players never appear)

She continues the route. She keeps the log. She loses ground slowly. She refuses a third unloggable letter, alone, and does not record the refusal. She does not tell the crew. The Halfsign stays grounded another quarter.

The route ends, eventually, the way most things end — not from a single blow, but from the day she has to choose between filing a fourth unloggable refusal and letting the request go through. She does not know which way she will choose. The campaign's Question — who decides what the system remembers — goes through her hands first.

## What players will see in the first round

A woman who sets a hand-bound book down before she shakes their hand. Asks one question — *why this route?* — and listens to the full answer without interrupting. Hires them on a one-loop trial. Does not promise a permanent berth.

## Mechanics

She does not fight. If forced to fight, she runs. If she cannot run, she negotiates. If she cannot negotiate, the *Splitfork* is in the air on time.

For the rare scene where she is in danger: HP middle-of-mortal, no combat skill above competent, has a small Tuner-grade resonance instrument she carries for kite-cord checks that can be improvised as a localized resonance disruption (one use, one round, then needs to recharge).

## Open hooks tied to her

- The Halfsign repair (financial pressure, mechanical mystery — see `dm/notes/ships/the-halfsign.md`).
- The two unlogged refusals (slow-burn revelation; surfaces if a third is requested).
- The black-route list (one Conclave archivist; one unidentified Coremark-affiliated payer through a Glasswake intermediary).
