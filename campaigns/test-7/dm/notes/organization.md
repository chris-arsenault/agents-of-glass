# The Ledger Run

Formal name on the Accord paperwork: **Long-Carry Circuit, Article 14
courier crew.** Nobody calls it that. *Ledger Run* is the working name
the crew picked up in the Conclave reading-rooms two decades ago, and
it stuck because the Conclave hated it. They still hate it.

## Identity

A small kite-courier crew flying a single primary hauler on a recurring
multi-stop route between the surface and the inner ring habs. They are
chartered under the Tempered Accord's Article 14 (independent civil
carriers), which gives them tariff exemption on official mail and a
legal duty of non-discrimination — they cannot refuse a package on
political grounds alone. Their charter is *real*, not aspirational.
Every six months they file a manifest log and a route report with the
Accord clerks. Every six months the clerks file the reports without
reading them.

**Founded** in 2419, twenty years after the Bitter Reach, by **Mereth
Kel** — a former Vantara route officer who walked off the job during
the Sable Crescent embargo and refused to come back. The founding
moment was specific: a sealed Conclave letter from a Bloom survivor in
Glasswake, addressed to a daughter on Hab Meridian, that Vantara
declined to carry because of an unresolved diplomatic block. Mereth
flew it herself. She lost her job. She kept the route.

The org is named for what the Conclave does — keep ledgers — and for
what the crew does, which is keep *their own*. Every package gets
entered. Every payment gets entered. Every refusal-to-carry gets
entered, with reasons. The ledger is public to clients on request.
This is the thing Mereth built that nobody else does.

## Where it sits in the world

- **Legally:** Accord-chartered, surface-incorporated in Glasswake.
  Files with the Shear Compact for kite-tags. Pays Vantara waystation
  fees like everyone else.
- **Politically:** trusted by nobody, used by everybody. The Conclave
  resents the name and uses the route. The Accord ignores them and
  needs them. Vantara views them as a margin-eater and occasionally
  sub-contracts them for routes Vantara can't justify staffing. The
  Synod will not engage but accepts deliveries at Threshold Station
  through the standing courtyard drop. Coremark remnants try to bribe
  them periodically.
- **Geographically:** Home port is **Glasswake**, surface side, at a
  reclaimed dock in the old hab fragment. The standing route, in
  order: Glasswake → Span Nine relay → Hab Meridian → Sable Crescent
  (Thornvault and Ledgerfall) → home. Roughly a fortnight's loop.

## The detail that makes it *this* org rather than a generic version

**They publish a refusal log.** The Ledger Run's standing charter
includes a clause Mereth wrote herself: every package the crew refuses
to carry is recorded — date, sender, recipient, weight, reason — and
the refusal log is public on request at the Glasswake office. Every
quarter, an updated copy goes to the Accord clerks (who file it) and
to the Conclave (who refuses to acknowledge receipt). This is the only
publicly-readable refusal log of any chartered carrier in the system.
Vantara's PR team has been told to never speak about it.

The refusal log is the engine. It is why the crew is trusted. It is
why the crew is hated. It is why the players' decisions about what to
carry matter — every refusal goes in, with reasons, and the reasons
are read.

## Goals

**Near-term (this season):**

- Keep the route running. Two of the standing contracts (Conclave
  research-mail and a Glasswake-Hab Meridian municipal contract) come
  up for renewal this quarter.
- Replace the secondary kite (the *Halfsign*), which took Shear damage
  on a deep-route detour last quarter and is grounded.
- Survive a Vantara pricing move that's currently being negotiated at
  Accord level — Vantara wants Article 14 carriers to lose tariff
  exemption on bulk mail. This would gut the Ledger Run's margins.

**Long-term:** The Ledger Run is *for* the route — for keeping it
running, expanding it when habs reconnect, and keeping the refusal log
intact when factions push back on it. Mereth has no plan to grow into
a corporate competitor to Vantara; she's not interested. What she
wants, if pressed: a route that reaches a hab that doesn't trust
anyone yet, with a kite the hab will let dock. *Witness work,* she'd
call it. She hasn't said this out loud to the crew.

## Constraints

- **Charter limits.** Article 14 carriers cannot carry weapons-grade
  cargo, contraband, or sealed personnel transports. They *can* carry
  unsealed passengers and resonance-classified materials under
  manifest. The line is real and the crew knows it.
- **Can't cross:** Vantara as an institution, the Conclave as an
  institution, the Accord clerks as a class. Individual employees of
  each, yes; the institution itself would break the Ledger Run.
- **Resources at start:**
  - The **Splitfork**, a mid-class kite-hauler, four-crew, cargo bay,
    Shear-rated but route-preferring. Named for a documented run
    Mereth made in 2421 along a split Span-Nine reflection corridor.
  - The **Halfsign**, secondary kite, currently grounded for repairs.
  - The **Glasswake office**, a reclaimed warehouse on the dock
    fragment, with the refusal log archive, a small bunkroom, and a
    Tuner-rated cargo cradle.
  - **Three standing contracts**, two up for renewal.
  - **A list of names** that pay extra for direct hand-to-hand
    delivery and don't appear in the manifest log under their real
    names. Mereth keeps this list in her head.
- **Obligations:** The Accord charter requires quarterly filings and
  route compliance. The Conclave research contract requires Tuner-
  certified handling. The Glasswake municipal contract requires a
  certain on-time rate.

## Existing members the players inherit

- **Mereth Kel** *(Hab-Worlder human, late 40s, Glasswake)* — Captain.
  Founder. The leader. Not a PC. Visibly tired. Carries a hand-bound
  copy of the refusal log everywhere — she has not yet been asked
  whether she sleeps with it, but she does. She does not romanticize
  the job. She will fly into Shear weather she shouldn't if a letter
  matters. Full entry pending in `dm/notes/npcs/`.
- **Ev Sken** *(Hab-Worlder fae, indeterminate age, route-resident)* —
  Fixer. Books the waystation slots, negotiates with the Compact
  clerks, knows every dock master between Glasswake and Sable. They
  are intermittently present — sometimes diffuse, sometimes coherent
  — and the dock masters have learned that "Ev will get back to you"
  means an answer in three to forty hours. Reliable on every scale
  but the one where you wanted to feel reliable. Full entry pending.
- **The absent founder.** Mereth founded alone. There is no other
  founder figure. This is by design — the org has no mythologized
  past that the players have to live up to. The Ledger Run is
  twenty-six years old. Mereth is still in the chair. The players are
  joining a working concern, not a legend.
- (Pending NPC) A Glasswake-side bookkeeper / dispatcher who runs the
  office when the kite is out. Will name and flesh out next turn.

## Capabilities the org typically needs

Not a role chart. The *kinds of leverage* the Ledger Run operates by:

- **Someone who can move a kite through Shear-edge and Bloom-cordoned
  air** without losing cargo or hours. This is technical work.
- **Someone who can read a sealed manifest** — Tuner-grade resonance
  literacy, or Conclave training, or hard-won field experience — and
  know whether what they're being asked to carry matches the
  paperwork.
- **Someone the Conclave will return a kite-bell call from.** Not
  trust; just *return the call*. This is rare and load-bearing.
- **Someone who knows what gossip from which hab is worth what.**
  Witness work, by another name.
- **Someone the Accord clerks know by face** — not by influence, but
  by *paperwork*. Filing things correctly is a skill, and one the org
  cannot afford to outsource.

Players will invent characters who come at these from angles I haven't
listed. That's fine. The point is the *kinds* of leverage, not the
slots.

## Advancement track

Five stages. Each names what advancing *broadly* unlocks. I will
resolve mechanical bonuses at the moment of advance based on what the
table cares about.

1. **Working crew.** Where the org starts. One kite. Three contracts.
   One reputation, mostly local.
2. **Carrier of record.** A Bloom-cordon hab or a previously
   isolationist hab opens to the route. The refusal log starts being
   cited in Accord proceedings. New contracts arrive unsolicited.
3. **The route is the institution.** A second kite running a parallel
   sub-route. Two named affiliates in different cities. The crew is
   invited to mediate, not just deliver. Vantara starts a quiet PR
   campaign against them.
4. **Witness corps.** The crew is called to witness — formally — at
   an Accord proceeding or a hab integration ceremony. Their
   testimony is *load-bearing* on something the Conclave wanted
   buried.
5. **The crew becomes the precedent.** The refusal log is codified
   into Accord article precedent. Article 14 is rewritten around the
   Ledger Run's standing practice. Mereth — or whoever — gets asked
   to leave the route and run the codification. They will say no.

Each stage advance is triggered by an arc resolution or a named
threat neutralized. I will not pre-specify the trigger for each; the
arc's resolution shape will tell me.

## Public pressure (durable clock)

There is one visible long-running pressure I want as a durable public
clock. I will create it via `glass clock` next turn once I've named
the arc; the segments will probably read something like:

- *Vantara files the Article 14 amendment proposal* (already pending)
- *the proposal is heard by the Accord clerks*
- *the clerks return a ruling*
- *the ruling stands or falls*
- *the Ledger Run keeps tariff exemption — or doesn't*

This is the Vantara pricing pressure. It is the visible pressure that
shapes early scenes. It is not the campaign.

## Secrets and agendas (DM-only)

- **Mereth's refusal-log clauses.** Two refusals in the past three
  years are *not* in the public log. Both were Conclave research
  letters that named living Bloom survivors. Mereth refused to carry
  them and refused to log the refusal, because the log would surface
  the survivors' names. She has not told anyone. She does not know
  what to do with this. The first time she has to refuse a third, the
  decision becomes a scene.
- **The standing-courtyard drop at Threshold Station.** The Synod
  receives Ledger Run deliveries but does not engage in person. The
  drop is in an open courtyard where, on certain frequency windows,
  the Synod's silence *responds* to the delivery — a tonal shift in
  the station's ambient resonance, audible to Tuners. Mereth has
  noticed. She has not told the crew. She does not understand it.
- **Ev Sken's quiet.** Ev was at Fermata Station during the failed
  Sit-In thirty years ago. They have not told the crew. The dock
  master at Fermata has told nobody. The Ledger Run does not have a
  Fermata stop. Ev has not asked for one.
- **The black-route list.** The list of names that pay for direct
  hand-to-hand delivery includes one Conclave archivist (off-the-
  books authentication work) and one person Mereth herself does not
  know the identity of — the payments come through a Glasswake
  intermediary. She suspects but has never confirmed it is a Coremark
  remnant. She keeps carrying because the payments are clean and the
  packages are small.
- **The Halfsign's damage.** The secondary kite did not take Shear
  damage. It was damaged by something the crew currently aboard *has
  not reported,* because they could not name what they saw. This is
  for later; it surfaces if the players go looking.

## Constraint check

The Ledger Run is the *party's home*. It has secrets, agendas, and
internal politics, but:

- It is not actively trying to harm the party. Mereth wants the crew
  alive. The crew is small enough that losses matter to her
  personally.
- It is not asking the players to be disposable. The refusal log
  applies to *their* refusals too.
- It is not in competition with itself. One org, one kite, one route.
  The friction is external.

Friction yes; predator no. The org is a frame the players can choose
how to be part of.
