# The Caulden Rack

A standard-sized microcavity rack — a tray of thumbnail-sized sealed ringglass cells, each one a contained store of resonance, racked together for shipment. Identical, on the surface, to the racks Vantara moves by the dozen every week. Declared in the Splitfork's manifest as **standard kinetic-band microcavities, twelve cells, refinery-sealed, destination Hab Meridian receiving dock, sender Caulden Refinery (Sable Crescent).**

This is the cargo at the center of the opening arc.

## Provenance

Caulden is a small ringglass refinery in the Sable Crescent, downslope of Thornvault. Real refinery. Real microcavities, most of the time. Has had a standing supplier relationship with the Ledger Run for nine years. Their racks have come through the Splitfork's cargo bay roughly once a quarter without incident.

This rack was loaded at the Caulden dock on the previous loop, signed for by Mereth, sealed by the refinery's foreman, and entered into the manifest as twelve standard cells.

## Current status

In the Splitfork's cargo bay, in the Tuner-cradle, sealed. To be delivered to Hab Meridian receiving dock at the end of the current outbound leg.

## What it does (declared)

Standard microcavities. Each cell is a thumbnail-sized sealed ringglass chamber holding a standing resonance wave at high density. Cracking the seal releases the stored energy in a controlled burst — kinetic-band output, used for industrial processes and emergency thrust. Twelve cells. Standard rack.

## What it actually does (DM-only)

**One of the cells is not a microcavity.** It is duskgrain in microcavity housing. *Probably.* The rack hums at the standard low frequency of a charged microcavity rack, and the duskgrain cell hums *also* at that frequency — but the duskgrain cell's hum is not narrow-band kinetic; it is full-spectrum, flattened by the housing into something that reads, to a casual sensor, as ordinary stored resonance. Sensor scans require deliberate spectrum analysis to detect the deception. A Tuner aboard who actively listens for it can hear the difference. The Threshold Blooms can hear the difference and *will follow the rack* on the Sable-Crescent leg.

**Who substituted the cell, when, and why** — Mara has not decided. The opening arc's stakes question is exactly this. Possibilities open on the board:

- **Caulden Refinery insider** — someone at the refinery is using the Ledger Run's reliability as cover for a duskgrain export. Reasons unknown.
- **Hab Meridian receiver** — the rack's destination knows what's in it. They ordered it. They paid for it through legitimate channels to avoid drawing attention.
- **Substitution after sealing** — the rack was tampered with at a waystation, by someone with refinery-grade equipment.
- **Conclave provocation** — Veth's dossier needs an irregularity. One was provided.
- **Vantara setup** — the amendment fight needs a public Article 14 carrier scandal. One was arranged.

Mara will choose the answer at the table, in response to which thread the players pull.

## What happens if it gets through clean

Hab Meridian receives the rack. Cracks the seals. Eleven cells release controlled kinetic bursts as expected. The twelfth cell releases full-spectrum resonance into a Hab Meridian receiving bay. *Outcomes depend on the bay's containment.* Best case: a localized resonance flare, no casualties, an investigation, the Ledger Run's Hab Meridian municipal contract terminates. Worst case: Tuners injured. Bloom-attractor signature now in a clean ring hab.

## What happens if the crew detects it in transit

Their move. The refusal log clause activates: *we can refuse to carry this if we know what it is.* Mereth will follow the crew's lead — she has decided this in advance and has not told them. The decision becomes the scene.

## Hook

The Caulden Rack is the opening arc's strong-start cargo. It is the campaign's first concrete instance of *something the party is being asked to carry that is not what it has been declared to be.* It is also the test of the refusal log as a *move* rather than a *record.*
