# The Refusal Log

A hand-bound book. The Ledger Run's defining document. Kept by Mereth Kel; quarterly copies filed with the Accord clerks and the Echo Ledger Conclave; readable on request at the Glasswake office.

## Provenance

Started in 2419 by Mereth Kel, the morning after the founding letter run. The first entry is a refusal: a Vantara-routed package she declined to carry on her first independent loop, with reasons. The book is the same book — she has had it rebound four times. The original cover sits in a drawer in her bunk on the Splitfork, soft-leather and faded.

There is exactly one master copy. The quarterly filings go out as transcribed copies, in Mereth's hand, sometimes Halvi's. The original never leaves Mereth's possession.

## Current status

On the shelf above Mereth's bunk on the Splitfork when she is in the air; on the desk in the Glasswake office when the Splitfork is docked; in her travel-bag when she is moving between the two. Locked, not secured. She trusts that nobody on her crew would move it.

The most recent entry is from sixteen days ago — a Sable-side merchant's package refused for a manifest discrepancy.

## What it does — concretely

It records every package the Ledger Run has declined to carry. Date, sender, recipient (where known), weight, reason, signature. The reason field is the load-bearing one. *Manifest discrepancy* is the most common. *Charter violation* is the second. *Captain's discretion* appears five times across twenty years.

Each quarterly transcription goes to the Accord clerks' filing room and to the Conclave's contracts office. Both are public on request. The Accord copy is filed without redaction. The Conclave copy has, on three occasions, been requested by Conclave archivists for cross-reference work.

The cost: the log makes the Ledger Run trustworthy and the Ledger Run hated. It is the reason their Hab Meridian municipal contract exists. It is the reason Vantara wants the Article 14 amendment passed.

## Hook

The Refusal Log is the campaign's central recurring artifact. Every refusal the crew makes is an entry. Every entry is a move. Citations of the log in Accord proceedings, in Conclave dossiers, in Vantara filings — these are the campaign's slow-tempo political weather.

If the master copy is lost or destroyed, the org loses something irrecoverable. The quarterly transcriptions exist; the originals do not. The Conclave keeps theirs in a sealed cabinet at Thornvault. Mereth would not retrieve them.

## DM note

The two unlogged refusals (see Mereth's NPC entry) are *not* in this book. The book's integrity is the campaign's central truth-claim. When the third unloggable refusal comes, what Mereth does with the book — and what the players do with what she does — is a scene the campaign is built to reach.
