# Atomic Secrets

Sly Flourish's atomic-secrets technique. Ten short facts the players don't know yet, *unattached* to specific scenes. Mara deploys whichever fits whichever moment.

Mix codes:
- **WK** — World-knows truth waiting to be discovered (an investigation can find it).
- **DK** — DM-knows truth held back (Mara controls the timing).
- **PTF** — Play-to-find-out blank — Mara doesn't know either.

---

1. **(DK)** Mereth has refused two Conclave research letters in the past three years that named living Bloom survivors, and has *not* logged either refusal in the public log. The clerk who took both refusals on the Conclave side, on detail rotation, was Veth Karran. Neither has acknowledged this to the other.

2. **(WK)** The Halfsign's "Shear damage" report is wrong. The damage is consistent with several things; *Shear* is not what the cradle's stress patterns indicate. Discoverable by any Tuner-aware character who looks at the kite's repair log with intent.

3. **(WK)** The Caulden Rack on the current Splitfork loop contains one duskgrain-laced cell, sealed to read as a standard microcavity. Threshold Blooms can detect it on the Sable-side leg. A Tuner aboard who actively listens can detect it. A casual sensor scan cannot.

4. **(PTF)** *Who* substituted the duskgrain cell in the Caulden Rack, and *why*, is not yet decided. Mara will choose at the table based on which thread the players pull.

5. **(DK)** Mereth's black-route list includes one Conclave archivist (off-the-books authentication work — pays for personal letter authentications the Conclave wouldn't approve) and one person whose identity Mereth does not know. Payments on the second name come through a Glasswake intermediary Mereth suspects but cannot confirm is a Coremark remnant.

6. **(WK)** Ev Sken was at Fermata Station during the failed Sit-In thirty years ago. The Fermata dock master has told nobody. The Ledger Run does not have a Fermata stop. If the route ever gets proposed to add Fermata, Ev will diffuse and quietly make it not happen. Discoverable through Fermata-side investigation.

7. **(DK)** The Conclave's contracts office has assigned Veth Karran to compile a delivery-irregularity dossier on the Ledger Run with the goal of non-renewing the standing research-mail contract. The dossier already exists. It needs one real entry to become actionable.

8. **(WK)** The Accord clerk Tal Vorenis has been indexing the Ledger Run's quarterly refusal log for nineteen consecutive quarters and cross-referencing it against Vantara filings. He has shown the index to no one. Discoverable by observing his drop-off ritual or asking the right clerks the wrong questions.

9. **(DK)** Risca Donn, Vantara's Sable Crescent route officer, considered walking off her job over a sealed letter Vantara refused to carry, twice in the past five years. Both times she did not. She has not told her supervisor. She has not told Mereth, with whom she has not spoken since 2419.

10. **(PTF)** The Drag's nature — is it a creature, a recurring resonance pattern, something the elves left, or something else — is genuinely undecided. Mara will not pre-resolve this. The campaign may answer it. The campaign may not.

## Three Clue Rule check

The opening arc requires the players to discover that the Caulden Rack is misdeclared (secret #3). Per the Alexandrian, three independent clues:

- **Clue A:** the Threshold Blooms thicken on the Sable-side leg if the rack is aboard.
- **Clue B:** a Tuner PC who listens to the rack's hum can hear the difference.
- **Clue C:** Halvi's manifest cross-check at the Glasswake office's clean ledger — *if* the players bring her the rack's manifest before the Sable-side leg, she will catch the discrepancy with the Caulden refinery's standard rack pattern.

Three clues, three different access paths (in transit, sensory, paperwork). Players can reach the secret through any of them.
