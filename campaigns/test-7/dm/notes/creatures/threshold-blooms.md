# Threshold Blooms

Small, plural, near-Bloom-cordon — the route hazard the Ledger Run actually meets.

## What they are

A flock of small Bloom-touched fliers, roughly the size of a hand, that nest along the cordon-edge cliffs at the south end of the Sable Crescent. Probably descended from a pre-Bloom seabird species. The Bloom changed something in their resonance signature that nobody at Thornvault has been allowed to study formally — the cordon authority denied the Conclave's research request twice.

They are *plural* always. There is no Threshold Bloom. There is only a flock of them.

## Where encountered

- Sable Crescent route: between Thornvault courtyard and the Ledgerfall turning point.
- Specifically: the cordon-edge cliff face the Splitfork passes within a kilometer of on every loop.
- Time of day: dusk and just-after. They roost at noon and at full dark.

## Behavior (one sentence)

They follow kites for short stretches, harmlessly, and then peel away — *unless* the kite is carrying duskgrain, microcavities at high charge, or anything else with a full-spectrum hum, in which case they will follow at close range and the flock thickens.

They do not attack. They do not need to. A flock thick enough to obscure forward visibility is a navigation hazard at kite speed.

## Sensory detail (one sentence)

Their wings make a sound like a long exhalation; you don't hear it as wing-beat, you hear it as breath, and Tuners report a quarter-octave drift in ambient resonance when the flock is within range.

## Mechanical hooks

- **HP per individual:** trivial — a kite-cord whip kills one. The hazard is volume.
- **Flock dynamics:** a flock thickens by 1 per round if the kite is carrying full-spectrum-resonance cargo. At 5+ thickness, navigation rolls take disadvantage. At 8+, the kite must reduce speed to stay safe. They do not engage in combat directly.
- **Resonance interactions:** they amplify the cargo's hum back at the kite, which means a Tuner aboard can read the cargo's resonance signature *off the flock* even if the cargo is sealed. This is one of the few ways to detect a duskgrain-laced microcavity in transit without breaking seal.

## What they want

To be near the hum. The thing the Bloom changed in them is an attraction to full-spectrum resonance release. They are not aggressive. They are interested.

## Open hooks

- A Tuner PC reading a flock thickening around the Splitfork on the Sable Crescent leg = the duskgrain in the Caulden Rack is detectable without breaking seal.
- The cordon authority's denial of the Conclave's research request = an investigation thread for a player who pulls on it.
