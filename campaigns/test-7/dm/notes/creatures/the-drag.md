# The Drag

A Shear-resonance phenomenon that some couriers swear is alive. The Conclave's official position is that it isn't. The Tuners' guild has not taken a position. The dock pubs in Glasswake have several positions, all incompatible.

## What it is

Between Span Nine and Hab Meridian, on certain frequency windows, the ambient Shear resonance forms a slow-moving distortion that *behaves* like a creature: it follows kites, it picks favorites, it shows preferences for certain cargoes and courses, and it reacts to attempts to scan it by going still in a way that scans read as absence rather than concealment.

It does not have a body. It moves as a region of distorted ambient resonance, roughly fifteen to forty meters across, traveling at kite speed or just under.

## Where encountered

- Span Nine to Hab Meridian leg. Always.
- Resonance-tide conditions: when the Shear's ambient distortion is in a slow rising phase. The Compact's forecasting service publishes the windows; experienced couriers know when to watch for it.

## Behavior (one sentence)

It follows. It does not attack. It will sometimes overtake the kite and sit in the kite's forward arc until the kite's pilot deviates course, at which point it deviates with the kite for several minutes before peeling off.

## Sensory detail (one sentence)

Tuners report it as a low pressure in the inner ear and a faint warmth at the base of the throat; non-Tuners report nothing at all, which is part of why some couriers say it isn't real.

## Mechanical hooks

- **Not engaged in combat.** The Drag does not attack and cannot be attacked. Resonance weapons used against it are simply absorbed.
- **Navigation effect:** when the Drag is in the kite's forward arc, navigation rolls take a small penalty *and* the kite's path becomes harder to predict for anyone tracking from outside. The Drag is incidentally a stealth advantage.
- **Cargo interaction:** the Drag has shown a documented preference for kites carrying personal letters with high emotional charge — a fact that Vantara has been told, will not act on, and considers proprietary research.

## What it wants

Mara has not decided. The Drag is genuinely play-to-find-out — a creature whose nature the campaign will or will not investigate, depending. It may be alive. It may be a recurring resonance pattern. It may be something the elves left behind. The campaign does not need to answer this.

## Open hooks

- A PC who tries to communicate with the Drag — open the door, see what happens.
- A scene where the Drag is in the forward arc and the players have a sealed package with a high emotional charge in the cargo bay — the Drag is staying for a reason.
- The Conclave's official no-creature ruling — discoverable as a contested filing, hooks back into the Conclave faction.
