# Constructive Secrets

Secrets are good when they create future scenes other players will enjoy being
inside. They are bad when they steal agency, invalidate another player's
choices, or turn the party into a trap.

Useful secret shapes:

- a debt the character has not mentioned
- a prior relationship with an NPC or place
- an old name, old mistake, or old affiliation
- a thing the character recognizes but cannot explain publicly yet
- a goal that creates tension without requiring betrayal

Risky secret shapes:

- planning to sell out the party
- secretly working for the main opposition
- invalidating another PC's backstory
- hoarding required information so the table cannot act

The useful test: when the secret surfaces, will the other players have more
interesting choices than they had before?
