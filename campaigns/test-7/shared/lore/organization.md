# The Ledger Run

A chartered kite-courier crew working a recurring route between the
surface and the inner ring habs. You are members.

Formal name on the paperwork: **Long-Carry Circuit**, registered as an
Article 14 independent civil carrier under the Tempered Accord.
Nobody calls it that. The crew picked up *Ledger Run* twenty years
ago in the Conclave reading-rooms, and the name stuck because the
Conclave hated it.

## What the route is

A roughly fortnight-long loop, in this order:

1. **Glasswake** — home port, surface side, a reclaimed dock built
   against the old hab fragment.
2. **Span Nine relay** — the pre-Glassfall conduit ridge between
   Glasswake and Sithari. The crew doesn't stop at Sithari; they hand
   surface mail off to a Vantara line at the relay.
3. **Hab Meridian** — the ring hab that first reconnected with
   Glasswake after the Famine. Standing municipal contract.
4. **Thornvault, in the Sable Crescent** — the Echo Ledger Conclave's
   headquarters castle. Standing research-mail contract.
5. **Ledgerfall** — a small settlement deeper in the Crescent, where
   the route turns and heads back.

Two kites: **the Splitfork**, the primary hauler — four-crew, cargo
bay, Shear-rated, route-preferring; and **the Halfsign**, secondary,
currently grounded for repairs.

## What the crew does

- Carries mail under official Accord seal.
- Carries small cargo and bonded packages.
- Carries passengers when there's room and the manifest allows.
- Quietly: carries *news*. The crew does not advertise this. They
  also do not deny it. The Conclave hates this. The Accord pretends
  not to know. Vantara has been told to never speak of it. Everyone
  else uses it.

## The thing everyone knows

The Ledger Run keeps a **public refusal log.** Every package the crew
declines to carry is recorded — date, sender, recipient, weight,
reason — and the log is readable at the Glasswake office on request.
Every quarter, an updated copy goes to the Accord clerks and to the
Conclave.

No other chartered carrier in the system does this. It is why the
crew is trusted. It is why the crew is hated.

## Who runs it

- **Mereth Kel** — Captain. Founder. Late forties, Hab-Worlder by
  upbringing, Glasswake by choice. Former Vantara route officer. She
  walked off Vantara during the Sable Crescent embargo of 2419 over
  a single sealed letter Vantara refused to carry, and she has not
  gone back. She founded the Ledger Run that year. She is still in
  the chair.
- **Ev Sken** — Fixer. Hab-Worlder fae, intermittently present,
  manages waystation slots and Compact paperwork. Sometimes diffuse,
  sometimes coherent. Reliable on every scale but the one you
  expected.
- **(office staff, to be named)** — the Glasswake-side dispatcher.

## Public reputation

Mixed.

- Among working couriers and dock crews: respected. The refusal log
  is taken seriously. The route is on time more often than the
  weather should allow.
- Among the **Echo Ledger Conclave**: resented. The name is an
  insult. The crew is a structural critique. They are used anyway.
- Among **Vantara**: tolerated as a regulatory nuisance. Subcontracted
  on routes Vantara can't staff. Targeted in quiet Article 14
  pricing negotiations.
- Among the **Tempered Accord** clerks: ignored, on paper. The
  filings get filed. The refusal log gets received and shelved. Some
  clerks read it.
- Among the **Lattice Proxy Synod**: not acknowledged in person.
  Deliveries are accepted at the standing courtyard drop on Threshold
  Station.
- Among the **Shear Compact**: kite-tags up to date, no complaints.
- Among Glasswake locals: ours. Drinks bought at the dock pub.

## What it stands for

Not advertised; observable from practice.

- The route runs even when politics say it shouldn't.
- A package gets through or it gets refused on the record.
- The refusal log is not edited.
- The crew is small enough that losing one of you matters.

## What's stressed about it right now

- Vantara is pushing an Article 14 amendment that would strip tariff
  exemption from independent carriers' bulk mail. If it passes, the
  Ledger Run loses its margin.
- The Halfsign is grounded. The crew is running everything off the
  Splitfork, which means tighter scheduling and less slack.
- Two of the three standing contracts come up for renewal this
  quarter.

## What you joined

You are crew on the Splitfork. You have your own reasons for being
here. You invented them in character creation. The job is what
brought you to Mereth's dock, and the refusal log is what made you
sign instead of walking away.
