# Campaign Framing

**Year:** 2438 CE. Twenty years past the Bitter Reach. The Tempered
Accord holds. The Bloom Zones have not shrunk.

**The Question the campaign asks:** *Who decides what the system
remembers?*

**The Scarcity:** *Witnesses.* Trusted carriers of what happened
between the habs, between the surface and the ring, between the
versions of the last sixty years that different communities are now
forced to reconcile.

**The party:** crew on **the Ledger Run** — a chartered Article 14
kite-courier crew running a recurring loop between Glasswake (surface),
Span Nine relay, Hab Meridian, and the Sable Crescent (Thornvault and
Ledgerfall). They carry mail. They carry small cargo. They quietly
carry news. They keep a public refusal log, which is what distinguishes
them from every other courier in the system.

**Where they are right now:** at home dock in Glasswake, between
routes. The Halfsign — secondary kite — is grounded for repairs. Two
of three standing contracts come up for renewal this quarter. Vantara
has filed an Accord amendment that would strip Article 14 tariff
exemption from independent carriers' bulk mail; the proposal is
pending at the clerks' level.

**Active threads (campaign-level):**

- *Article 14 amendment.* Vantara's pricing amendment. Public,
  named, slow-burning. Tracked as the public durable clock
  `article-14-amendment` (0/5). Visible to everyone.
- *The refusal log.* Building campaign-long stakes. Each entry the
  crew makes is observable, citable, and politically charged. The
  Conclave watches; one Accord clerk indexes it.
- *Witness work.* Implicit, unspoken. The crew has not been formally
  asked to witness for anyone yet. They will be.
- *The Caulden Rack.* The current outbound loop's cargo. The opening
  arc's pressure. (Held back from public framing until the strong
  start fires.)

**Tone:** hopecore played straight. The unexpected is normal. The
building codes account for the eighth dimension. Combat happens; it
is not the texture. The load-bearing opposition is institutional
silence — the things people will not say.

**What is NOT on the table:** the elves' fate, the Adversary, the
Communication Shard. These are world-bible DM truths. They are not
what this campaign is *for*. If a scene reaches them, fine; the
campaign is not steered toward them.

This document gets updated when a thread advances a beat, a faction's
stance changes, or the party arrives in a new region. The first such
update will come when the strong start fires and the Caulden Rack
becomes player-visible.
