# Arc Plan: caulden-rack

The opening arc. Pressure: *something the party is being asked to carry is not what it has been declared to be.*

## 1. Stakes question

**Whose decision was it that the Caulden Rack would be misdeclared, and what does the crew do with that fact once they know it?**

Mara does not know the answer to either half. The first half is open across at least five plausible authors (see Caulden Rack secret). The second half is what the players will choose under pressure — and the choice will set the tone for what kind of crew the Ledger Run becomes.

## 2. Threats

Four threats with their own impulses. Linked through the rack: each wants the rack to land somewhere different.

- **Veth Karran** *(Conclave archivist, antagonist — see `dm/notes/npcs/veth-karran.md`)*. Identity in this arc: the institution's quiet hand on the dossier scale. Impulse: *to file the Ledger Run's irregularities cleanly.* Between scenes: she works the night shift. The dossier grows. If a real irregularity surfaces, she logs it — and the contract review begins.
- **Vantara — through Risca Donn** *(see `dm/notes/factions/vantara.md` and `dm/notes/npcs/risca-donn.md`)*. Identity in this arc: the corporation that wants Article 14 amended, whose route officer has private contradictions she has not raised. Impulse: *to advance the amendment without embarrassment.* Between scenes: the amendment moves through the clerks' queue. Risca's procedural note becomes a comment-record entry — or doesn't, depending on what the players give her reason to do.
- **The Caulden Rack** *(named thing, see `dm/notes/artifacts/the-caulden-rack.md`)*. Identity in this arc: the cargo at the center, declared and not what it has been declared as. Impulse: *to reach Hab Meridian's containment-rated bay and crack.* Between scenes: it sits in the Splitfork's Tuner-cradle, humming at the standard low frequency, waiting for the seal to break.
- **The route's own weather** *(condition, no NPC)*. Identity in this arc: the loop's ordinary hazards — Threshold Blooms thickening on the Sable leg, the Drag on the Span Nine leg, the standard waystation politics. Impulse: *to provide an irregularity for the dossier on its own.* Between scenes: weather happens. Even with no antagonist intervention, weeks of running the route generate enough variance that the dossier finds its first entry eventually.

## 3. Clocks

Three clocks. Two run in parallel; one is shared infrastructure.

- **The Conclave dossier clock** (see `dm/notes/factions/echo-ledger-conclave.md` for full segment list). 5 segments. Tick when the dossier finds an irregularity it can log. Players slow it by clean handling, by surfacing the dossier inside the Conclave, or by giving Veth a different problem to solve.
- **The Article 14 amendment clock** (public, `glass clock` to be created this turn — see `shared/clocks.md`). 5 segments. Tick on the calendar (one segment roughly every two loops) and on whose comment record carries weight. Players intervene by lobbying clerks (Tal Vorenis), by surfacing facts about Vantara's infrastructure subsidy claims, by giving Risca reason to raise her objection.
- **The Caulden Rack clock** (arc-only, not public). 3 segments. Specific to this rack and this loop:
  1. *Loaded.* Already done — the rack is in the Splitfork's cargo bay at scene start.
  2. *Sable Crescent leg.* Threshold Blooms detect the cell. Tuner sensory window opens.
  3. *Hab Meridian dock.* Rack delivered or refused. Cracking or not cracking.

The Caulden Rack clock resolves in this arc. The other two clocks survive past it.

## 4. Possible end-states

3-5 shapes the arc could resolve into. *Not* a script. Mara picks none of these — they're the silhouette.

- **The crew detects in transit and refuses delivery on the record.** The refusal log gains its most consequential entry. Veth's dossier closes — *she cannot log a clean irregularity if the crew themselves logged it first.* Caulden Refinery is investigated, and one of the open *who-substituted* options surfaces. The Conclave's contract may still lapse on other grounds — but not this one. Article 14 amendment gets a public-comment data point.
- **The crew detects in transit and delivers anyway, with a quiet warning to Hab Meridian.** Hab Meridian receives the warning and takes its own decision. The rack may or may not be cracked. The crew has chosen to be a witness without being a refuser. Implications across the org's reputation are mixed — some clients will respect this, some will not.
- **The crew does not detect, the rack is delivered, the twelfth cell cracks.** Best case: localized resonance flare, contract terminates. Worst case: Tuner injuries, Bloom-attractor signature in a clean ring hab, Hab Meridian municipal contract terminates with public blame on the Ledger Run, Veth's dossier writes itself, the Article 14 amendment passes on the back of the scandal. The campaign continues, smaller, harder.
- **The crew detects, refuses, and surfaces *who* — landing on Caulden Refinery, Hab Meridian, or one of the other options.** Investigation arc opens for Mara to author next. The Question gets asked of the *receiver* or the *sender* this time, not the carrier.
- **The crew detects, makes Veth the offer to keep the irregularity off her dossier in exchange for a different favor, and turns the antagonist clock against itself.** Risky. Possible. Mara will not foreclose it.

## 5. Strong start

**Scene at the Glasswake reclaimed dock, the morning the Splitfork is to depart on the current loop.** The cargo is loaded. The manifest is signed. Halvi is at her desk with the day's documents. Mereth is in the bunkroom corner with the refusal log open on her knee. Ev is somewhere in the warmth near the door.

A Caulden Refinery courier arrives with the Caulden Rack on a hand-cart, late by twenty minutes — apologetic, tired-looking, a small Hab-Worlder named *Per Vask* whose dispatch slip says he caught a delayed kite from Sable two nights ago. He hands the rack over with the standard manifest and the standard refinery foreman's seal. He waits, nervously, for the receiving signature. He is unusually attentive to Halvi's clean-ledger cross-check, which Halvi performs in front of him because he is watching.

Halvi catches that the rack's delivery slip says *Per Vask* and the previous Caulden Rack from three quarters ago was delivered by *Per Vask* and the rack from six quarters ago was delivered by *someone else who looked like Per Vask but signed a different name*. She doesn't say anything. She files it.

The hook: Per is leaving. The rack is in the cradle. The Splitfork is loading the rest of the manifest. Departure is in two hours. *What do you do?*

(If Halvi raises the Per question: the cross-check goes deeper. If a Tuner PC checks the rack's hum: the difference is detectable. If nobody does anything: the rack ships. The arc proceeds either way.)

## 6. Nodes

5 nodes, navigable by the players. Each has clues to others.

### Node A — the Glasswake reclaimed dock (cargo origin point)
- *What's there:* the Caulden Rack, Halvi's clean ledger, Per Vask's delivery slip, Mereth and Ev in the office.
- *What can be learned:* Per's repeat-courier pattern (Halvi catches it, will say if asked), the rack's standard hum (Tuner sensory check possible), the foreman's seal (legitimate; Caulden Refinery is real).
- *Clues to other nodes:* Per Vask → node E (the dispatch slip names a Sable-side waystation). The clean-ledger discrepancy → node B (the Caulden Refinery itself). The Tuner hum check → node D (Threshold Blooms can confirm).

### Node B — Caulden Refinery (Sable Crescent, downslope of Thornvault)
- *What's there:* the refinery foreman; refinery production records; the actual rack-sealing bay.
- *What can be learned:* whether the substitution happened pre- or post-sealing. Whether the foreman knew. (Mara will choose at the table.) The refinery's own pressure: Caulden has been struggling to compete with Vantara-affiliated refineries; *someone* is paying them to look the other way, or *someone* is doing the substitution without the foreman's knowledge.
- *Clues to other nodes:* the foreman's records → node A (a discrepancy in the refinery's outgoing log vs. Halvi's clean ledger). The refinery's pressure → node C (Vantara is squeezing them). The substitution's actual mechanism → node E (post-seal tampering happens at a waystation).

### Node C — Vantara's Sable-side liaison office (Risca Donn)
- *What's there:* Risca, her route-officer's bunk, her two not-walking-off decisions in the back of her mind.
- *What can be learned:* whether Vantara has anything to do with the substitution (mostly not — but the corporation's pressure on Caulden is part of the texture). Risca's procedural note on the Article 14 comment record (if the players ask). What Risca knows about Conclave-Vantara dossier-sharing (she's met Veth, didn't like her, was not asked).
- *Clues to other nodes:* Risca's procedural note → node F (Tal Vorenis will read it). Risca's account of the Conclave dossier → node G (Veth is the source).

### Node D — the Sable-side route, Threshold Blooms zone
- *What's there:* the cliff face at the south end of the Crescent. The flock. The Splitfork in transit.
- *What can be learned:* the Caulden Rack is full-spectrum — observable through the flock thickening. *If* the players are paying attention. *If* a Tuner PC actively listens. (Two of the three Three-Clue paths land here.)
- *Clues to other nodes:* the rack's confirmed misdeclaration → node A or node B (the substitution is real; the question is who). The flock's behavior → the Conclave's denied research request (a side investigation, leads back to node G).

### Node E — the Sable-side waystation (where Per Vask transferred kites)
- *What's there:* the waystation logs, the dock master, Per's previous-night arrival record, the bay where post-seal tampering would have happened.
- *What can be learned:* whether Per spent unusual time near the rack between kite handoffs. Who else was at the waystation that night.
- *Clues to other nodes:* the waystation logs → node B (the rack arrived sealed; the question is whether it left tampered). Whoever else was at the waystation → potential additional NPCs Mara will name as needed.

### Node F — the Accord clerks' filing room (Tal Vorenis)
- *What's there:* the comment record on Article 14, Tal's index of refusal-log citations, the procedural cover question.
- *What can be learned:* Tal exists. The clerks are split. Risca's procedural note has been read. The Conclave dossier has been noticed.
- *Clues to other nodes:* Tal's index → node G (Veth is named in Conclave footnotes). The comment record's openings → node C (a Vantara route officer's note carries weight if anyone files an external request citing it).

### Node G — Thornvault Stoneward Courtyard (Veth Karran)
- *What's there:* Veth, the contracts office, the dossier (in a cabinet she has the key to).
- *What can be learned:* the dossier exists. Its current state. Veth's two refusals of Mereth's unlogged refusals. The Open-Seal caucus, accessible if the players push.
- *Clues to other nodes:* Veth → node A (she remembers Mereth's two unlogged refusals; if surfaced, this changes the Mereth/Veth scene shape). The Open-Seal caucus → potential expansion node Mara will author if reached.

## 8. Arc-specific secrets

5 atomic facts the players don't know yet, specific to this arc.

1. **(WK)** Per Vask is the Caulden Refinery courier on three of the last four rack deliveries. Halvi can spot the pattern. The fourth delivery (six quarters ago) was someone else with a similar name — *probably* a substitute Per made informal arrangements for. The pattern matters because it limits who could have tampered with the rack.
2. **(PTF)** Whether Per knows about the duskgrain cell. Mara will decide based on what the players bring to the conversation. He may be the substituter; he may be the unwitting mule.
3. **(DK)** Caulden Refinery's foreman, Sten Korr (Hab-Worlder, mid-fifties, will be drafted as needed), is in financial pressure from a Vantara-affiliated supplier squeeze. He has been approached, twice in the past year, by a buyer who pays in untraceable Coremark scrip for "off-spec" microcavities. He has refused both times. He has not refused the third. The third was the Caulden Rack.
4. **(WK)** The Hab Meridian receiving dock's containment in bay 4 is rated for kinetic-band release, not full-spectrum. This is in the dock's posted specifications. Anyone who looks will see it.
5. **(PTF)** Whether the buyer at the receiving end is the Conclave (provocation), Vantara (setup), Coremark (genuine duskgrain market), Hab Meridian black market (legitimate-illegitimate buyer), or none of these. Mara will choose at the table.

## 9. Done criteria

The arc resolves when the Caulden Rack reaches Hab Meridian, is refused before Hab Meridian, or fails to reach Hab Meridian (lost en route, etc.) — *and* the crew has had to decide what to do with the fact of the misdeclaration. The stakes question — *whose decision was it, and what does the crew do with that fact* — gets answered in a specific way, even if the answer is *we don't know whose decision and we shipped it anyway*.

The arc may *seed* further arcs: the Conclave dossier work continues, the Article 14 fight continues, the question of the buyer at the receiving end may become its own arc, the Halfsign repair remains unresolved. None of these are required for the Caulden Rack arc itself to close.

The arc is **abandoned** if the players obviate it — e.g., they refuse the rack at Glasswake before loading because something at the Per Vask conversation tipped them off, never make the delivery decision, and the question of *whose decision was it* gets answered as a cold investigation rather than a hot one. That is fine. The Question gets answered either way.
