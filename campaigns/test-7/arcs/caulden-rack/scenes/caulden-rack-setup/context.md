# Scene: caulden-rack-setup

The Glasswake reclaimed dock, departure morning.

The Splitfork is in her berth. Most of the manifest is loaded — crates, bonded packages, the standard outbound mix. The ringglass on the south wall hums as the dock crane settles the last ordinary crate, the pitch rising and falling with the lift.

Mereth came in from the berth side a few minutes ago. She set the refusal log on the desk corner. She said one thing to Bren before she did anything else.

Halvi is at the window desk with the manifest tablet. The window is open. Ev is near the door, a stack of stamped paperwork already on the desk beside them.

The last item on the manifest arrived twenty minutes late: a microcavity rack from Caulden Refinery, delivered by a courier named Per Vask, small and Hab-Worlder, carrying a dispatch slip that says he caught a delayed kite from the Sable side two nights ago. He handed it to Halvi with the standard foreman's seal and the delivery form. He has been waiting, near the door, while Halvi runs the clean-ledger cross-check.

The rack is in the Tuner-cradle now, sealed.

Departure in two hours.
