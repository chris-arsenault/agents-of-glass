# Scene Prep: caulden-rack-setup

**Scene type:** `scene-play`

DM-only. Short. Re-readable during play.

## 1. Recap

Two prelude scenes closed. The crew made the first loop under the new charter: Den Vell's packet for Eshil Caur is in transit, year-mark form live for eleven days, Splitfork the carrier of record. Mid-loop a shear signal forced a heading shift — Mereth took the kite fifteen degrees west of Pane Seven, threading Bren's half-named corridor. Whether it held is unconfirmed. Drova filed the listener-window supplement in Section 4, subsection 8 on the shelf-box question. Fei logged four glyphs from a contact during the crisis; the crew knows she logged something, nobody has asked. Tek's tic-tracer came out during the shear crisis. The Splitfork is back in Glasswake, and departure on the current outbound loop is this morning.

## 2. Strong start

Glasswake reclaimed dock, loading morning. Most of the manifest is already in the Splitfork's bay. The ringglass on the south wall hums as the dock crane sets down a crate — slow rising pitch, then falling. Halvi is at the window desk with the manifest tablet. Mereth comes in from the berth side, sets the refusal log on the desk corner without sitting, and says one thing to Bren about the corridor before she does anything else.

Per Vask, a small Hab-Worlder courier from Caulden Refinery, arrived twenty minutes late with the last cargo piece: a standard microcavity rack on a hand-cart, foreman-sealed, dispatch slip from the Sable-side kite connection. He has handed it to Halvi. He has not been asked to stay. He is still here, standing near the door, watching Halvi run the clean-ledger cross-check. Halvi is running it in front of him. Because he is watching.

The rack is in the Tuner-cradle. Departure in two hours.

**Lore pull used:** Echo Rivers — the Sable Crescent's marshy lowlands are where surfacing points are densest. Signal-band resonance in quantity causes "drift" — listener loses track of which voice is theirs. Land this texture on the Sable leg, not here. Also informs why the duskgrain cell's full-spectrum hum has headache-adjacent properties when the rack is unsealed near the Threshold flock.

## 3. Possible directions

**A — Bren's corridor report**
- *What:* Mereth gives Bren one line: corridor held, or it didn't.
- *Who:* Mereth. Bren.
- *Stakes:* Whether Bren's chart knowledge is validated or burned. What she decides to file (or not file). If it held, she could name it. She doesn't have to.
- *Hook:* Mereth sets down the refusal log before she says anything. The opening of the book is the answer.

**B — Per Vask at the door**
- *What:* Someone asks why Per is still here. He answers or he doesn't.
- *Who:* Per Vask. Halvi (cross-check in progress). Whoever asks.
- *Stakes:* Whether the crew probes him now or lets him walk. He is not a trained liar. His dispatch slip names the Sable-side waystation (Node E lead). If he walks, the arc carries the question forward.
- *Hook:* Per's cross-check attention is visible. He's watching Halvi's clean-ledger check like he knows what she might find.

**C — The rack reads different**
- *What:* Fei does a Tuner check during loading, or walks past the cradle. The rack hums at the standard low frequency — but full-spectrum, not narrow-band. She can hear the difference if she listens. Or: Tek's tic-tracer fires hard near the cradle. The crew starts to understand what the instrument is for.
- *Who:* Fei. Tek. Whoever is near the cradle.
- *Stakes:* The misdeclaration surfaces before departure. Refusal-log clause activates now. Or the rack ships and the arc carries the question to transit.
- *Hook:* "This one reads different." Fei sets down her concentrate flask.

**D — Drova-Tek tic-tracer scene**
- *What:* Drova asks Tek about the instrument. Small space — maintenance bay or corridor. Fei nearby, already noticed, hasn't filed. Two people with adjacent skill sets figuring out what they're looking at. Tek decides what to say.
- *Who:* Drova, Tek, Fei (present, silent).
- *Stakes:* The crew's shared picture of what the instrument measures — and whether that picture includes what's in the cradle.
- *Hook:* Fei's silence. She has already heard the rack.

**E — Fei renders the glyphs**
- *What:* She has the four-glyph shorthand from the shear contact. Pre-departure, alone (or not), she decides what to render into readable language and what stays in shorthand only she can read.
- *Who:* Fei. Possibly whoever asks.
- *Stakes:* What the crew knows about the contact. Whether it connects to anything in the Caulden Rack's orbit.
- *Hook:* The crew knows she logged something. Nobody has asked.
