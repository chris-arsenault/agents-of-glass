# Scene: caulden-rack-setup

The Glasswake reclaimed dock. Departure morning.

The Splitfork is in her berth. Most of the manifest is already loaded. The ringglass on the south wall hums as the dock crane settles a crate — pitch rising as the crane lifts, falling as it sets down. The dock crews use the wall as an informal lift counter. Today it's counted three.

Mereth came in from the berth side a few minutes ago. She set the refusal log on the desk corner before she said anything. Then she said one thing to Bren.

Halvi is at the window desk with the manifest tablet — window open, previous manifest tablet on the shelf behind them. Ev is near the door, a stack of stamped paperwork already on the desk.

The last cargo item arrived twenty minutes late: a standard microcavity rack from Caulden Refinery in the Sable Crescent, delivered by a small Hab-Worlder named Per Vask. His dispatch slip says he caught a delayed kite two nights ago. He handed the rack to Halvi with the foreman's seal and the delivery form. He has not been asked to stay.

He is still here, near the door, watching Halvi run the clean-ledger cross-check.

The rack is in the Tuner-cradle now. Sealed.

Departure in two hours.

*What do you do?*
