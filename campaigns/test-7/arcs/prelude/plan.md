# Arc Plan: prelude

The first on-screen incident for this party. Two scenes only. A
walk-up sender at the apron asks the Splitfork to carry a packet to a
recipient already on the refusal log. The crew has to decide before
Mereth comes out of the office. Then the lift window closes and they
have to commit.

Per `methodologies/prelude-arc.md`. The prelude shape is deliberately
light here — this is not the opening arc.

## Prelude promise

What this should reveal in play:

- How the four of them make a decision together when the captain is
  not in the room.
- Whether *the refusal log* is a document Drova maintains or a
  document the *crew* maintains.
- What each PC's withholding looks like when somebody asks them to
  speak.
- One concrete thing — a packet kept, refused, conditioned, or filed
  — that survives the time jump into the Caulden Rack arc.

The prelude is *not* the place to:

- Pull the Wednesday/Tuesday Shear thread.
- Surface Veth Karran or Tek's Conclave-pulled transcript.
- Resolve the Mernhab diagnostic question.
- Burn the Caulden Rack arc by adjacency. The packet here is a
  different cargo, a different sender, a different recipient.

## Normal scene — `prelude-opening` (scene-play)

- **Locale.** Glasswake reclaimed dock, the Splitfork's apron berth,
  pre-dawn. Dock light low. Splitfork loaded for the outbound loop
  except for the last bag. Apron quiet — Mereth and Halvi in the
  office finishing the manifest, Ev somewhere warm and mostly inside
  himself. The four PCs are at their usual stations: Tek on the spar
  side starting her walkaround, Drova at the apron lectern with the
  refusal-log day-page open, Fei coming back from the dock corridor
  where she went to listen for first-light, Bren on the cargo hatch
  with a streamer pouch and chalk on her sleeves.
- **Starting pressure.** A walk-up sender — *Inka Vell*, Hab-Worlder
  widow, mid-fifties, came down from Hab Meridian on the night
  passenger-kite — has been waiting in the dock corner since before
  the PCs got there. She has a small sealed packet in waxed paper and
  a basket of dried apricots for whoever decides. The packet is for
  her late husband's brother in Sable Crescent — recipient name on the
  outside: *Eshil Caur*. The apricots are not a bribe. She has
  brought them because nobody arrives at someone's dock empty-handed
  in her hab.
- **The hook.** Drova will recognise *Eshil Caur* from the refusal log
  by the time she opens the day-page (or before — she has the archive
  at her elbow). Two years and four months ago, the office refused to
  carry a Conclave-marked summons to that recipient at the request of
  a Crescent advocate. Mereth signed the refusal. The summons went
  back. Eshil Caur has been on the log ever since. The packet in
  front of them now is small, unmarked, from a different sender, in
  waxed paper. It is plausibly nothing. It is plausibly not nothing.
  Mereth is in the office. The four of them have a window — fifteen,
  twenty minutes — to handle this themselves before the captain comes
  out and decides for them. The decision will tell the table what
  kind of crew this is.
- **What choice the party can make now.** Carry, refuse, condition,
  defer to Mereth. *How* they decide is the scene. Whether they look
  at the packet (Drova's signature move would do it without breaking
  the seal). Whether Fei reads Inka in the corridor. Whether Bren
  commits or asks. Whether Tek finds something on the walkaround that
  changes the arithmetic. Each PC has a reason to weigh in.

## Action scene — `prelude-action` (action)

- **Pressure source.** A Shear-edge front rolls in faster than the
  morning forecast called for. The Glasswake dock master, *Han
  Pellow*, comes onto the apron at first light with a hard call:
  lift in fifteen minutes or scrub for the day. The crew has to
  commit to the packet decision *and* execute a kite-out under wind
  that is closing the window. The Splitfork is the only kite the
  Ledger Run has running — the Halfsign is grounded — so a scrub day
  costs the loop its margin.
- **Public endpoint.** Lift clean, lift dirty, or scrub. Three
  shapes; none of them is a campaign-defining win or loss. Each
  shape carries a small concrete consequence into the time jump.
- **Tracker.** **Lift window** — 4 segments, public, scene-only.
  Starts at 0/4. Ticks toward 4 each round on the dock master's
  call (or on a clock-tick from a fumbled walkaround, an unsecured
  load, a delayed log entry). At 4/4, the dock master scrubs the
  lift; the kite is grounded for the day; the loop runs short.
- **Resistance / honest pressure.** Three things have to be true
  before the Splitfork can lift:
  1. **Walkaround signed** (Tek). Her signature move — the back-of-
     hand walkaround — takes a count of forty. Cannot be skipped
     without cost.
  2. **Cargo manifest closed** (Drova). The day-page has to read
     either *carried* or *refused* on the Vell packet, and the
     refusal-log entry, if any, has to be filed. No blank line.
  3. **Approach line read** (Fei + Bren). Fei listens the corridor
     and the apron lee for the front's edge; Bren throws a streamer
     and counts down for the dock master.
- **What failure looks like.** *Scrub* is not a failure — it is a
  decision under pressure. The cost is one full day off the loop,
  which means the next standing contract (Hab Meridian municipal,
  due at the receiving dock on a hard window) gets a late note. A
  *dirty lift* — lift with one of the three things not done —
  costs each in a small specific way: a missed walkaround means Tek
  flies out unsigned and her hand will be on the spar all loop;
  an open log entry means Drova carries the open page and Mereth
  finds out before lunch; a missed listen means Fei has nothing for
  the apron-lee record and the Vantara handoff at Span Nine will
  read it as inattention. None of these end the campaign. All of
  them carry into the Caulden Rack arc as friction.

## Character spotlights

- **Tek.** Walkaround under pressure. The signature move surfaces in
  the action scene with real time pressure on it. No need to surface
  the lattice loss yet.
- **Drova.** The graduated-certainty register lands publicly for the
  first time at the apron lectern. The packet decision puts the
  refusal log itself onto the table.
- **Fei.** The listener does what the listener does — corridor first,
  then apron lee. *Listening Past Last Light* is available; using it
  here costs ten minutes of slow speech, which is a real cost when
  the window is closing.
- **Bren.** *I'm going to / I'm not.* The drop-pilot's posture about
  the lift call is the one that carries the action. She also has
  permission to say *streamer up* on the packet decision, not just on
  the kite.

## Time jump

After the action scene resolves, jump *two days* — long enough for the
loop to begin in earnest. The campaign reactivates the **Caulden Rack**
arc as the main opening arc. The morning of the Caulden Rack delivery
(the strong start of that arc) is two mornings after the prelude
action.

What can be skipped at the jump:

- the rest of the day's ordinary apron work
- the first leg out of Glasswake (uneventful by design — if the
  prelude scrubbed the lift, the next morning's lift is what
  reactivates caulden-rack)
- any Inka Vell follow-up — Inka's resolution is what scene 1
  delivered

What must survive the jump:

- whatever the crew did with the Vell packet, named on the refusal
  log or named in the bonded mail manifest
- whatever the crew did about the lift window — clean, dirty,
  scrubbed
- any fallout from a *dirty lift*: open log line, unsigned
  walkaround, missed apron-lee read

```bash
glass arc activate caulden-rack
```

## Exit criteria

End `prelude` mode only when:

- both scenes have ended with `glass scene end`
- both scene modes have ended with `glass mode end`
- the prelude arc summary is written
- `summary.md` or `shared/quest-log.md` carries the Vell packet
  outcome and the lift outcome
- `dm/scratchpad.md` records party chemistry, friction, hooks, and
  carry-forward
- the final prelude turn names the time jump
