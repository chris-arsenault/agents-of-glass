**Mode:** scene-play. **Scene:** `prelude-opening`. **Arc:** `prelude`.

Pre-dawn, Glasswake reclaimed dock, Splitfork apron berth. About
eight minutes to first light.

## What is on the apron

- **Splitfork** — loaded for the outbound loop except the last bag.
  Walkaround complete; haul line and rigging read clean.
- **Refusal-log day-page** — open at the apron lectern. Two lines
  on it now, both in graduated-certainty register:
  - Cross-reference to prior refusal-log entry *2434-Q1-007*
    (recipient consistent with the bench-sitter's packet, sender's
    hand not the bench-sitter's, decision pending consultation
    with crew and manifest officer).
  - Negative-comparison clause: *Packet binding consistent with
    Hab-form domestic register, sender not claiming private status;
    not consistent with the binding category of the prior
    refusal-log entry.*
  The original reason from the prior entry is still not on the
  day-page.
- **Archive shelf-box** — at Drova's ankle, lid down. Internal
  note on the prior entry still not surfaced.
- **Inka Vell** — on the forwarding bench, basket on her lap,
  packet between two hands, thumb across the apricot-oil print on
  the wax. Has answered Fei's second question in widow's register:
  no word from her to carry with the packet — *he knew what he
  wanted to leave; I'm not in it.* Has been carrying this since the
  fourth morning after Den died; has not put it down. Her guess
  about the packet contents remains hers and unspoken. She has not
  stood. She has not asked anything in return.
- **Mereth & Halvi** — in the office, finishing the manifest.
  Faint stamp audible through the doorframe — Halvi closing one
  page of the manifest, opening the next. Not the door. The
  shadow of the office doorframe on the deck has not moved.
- **Ev Sken** — somewhere warm, mostly inside himself.
- **Dock lamp at the spar-end corner** — dipped three times now.
  The shape the same shape each time. First light on the next dip
  or the one after. The wright's count is *seven*.

## Where each PC is

- **Tek** — at the apron lectern, back of right hand on the
  spar-side rim of the page at Fei's earlier height. Has spent
  one sentence this morning to Drova in dock-pub register —
  *cord and wax. Between form. Domestic, not claimed-private.* —
  and one word for the wright's clock — *nine.* Tic-tracer in
  the coat pocket. Walkaround closed. Hand on the rim.
- **Drova** — at the lectern, hand on the rim of the page. Has
  written the cross-reference and the negative-comparison line.
  Has lifted her head once and spent two sentences in dock-pub
  register to Fei (*I do not know what she thinks it is. The
  binding is not what was refused.*) and given the smallest nod
  the Hab form has toward the bench. Wet pen across the inkwell.
  Inkless pen beside it.
- **Fei** — at the forwarding bench, three paces from Inka, the
  polite Hab distance. Has spent the courtesy-register
  self-introduction in Drova's form (*good morning. I am Fei. I
  listen at landings for the kite.*) and the trimmed
  bench-version of the lectern question (*has anyone told you
  what you are carrying.*) Cuffs quiet. Hands at her sides.
  Waiting after Inka's answer.
- **Bren** — at the cargo edge of the apron, two paces from the
  hatch, back of her right hand on the cargo seat at hip height.
  Standing-beside (not-yet). Cargo board on the hatch clean,
  chalk on left sleeve, streamer pouch closed. Has the wind read
  (*yellow lift, possibly white if the south corner holds;
  cross-pull at twelve past light*) and the hab-knot read
  (*cord is Inka's own; tied on the passenger-kite around what
  she was given*) — neither yet spoken.

## What the scene is asking

Carry, refuse, condition, defer. The page has three lines. The
hab-knot read, the wind read, the year-mark form — all in the room.
Inka has answered every question the apron has asked: no word from
her to carry; she has been carrying this four months and has not
put it down. The decision has not been made. First light is the
next dip or the one after. Mereth has not come out.
