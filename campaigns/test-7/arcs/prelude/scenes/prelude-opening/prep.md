# Prep: prelude-opening

## 1. Recap

End of character-creation. Round 2 closed. Four PCs authored, four
relationships files written, every PC pointed at by at least one
other. No prior in-fiction scenes. The campaign opens here.

## 2. Strong start

Pre-dawn at the Glasswake reclaimed dock. Splitfork berthed and
loaded for the outbound loop except the last bag (which Halvi is
still papering inside). All four PCs at their usual stations: Tek on
the spar, Drova at the apron lectern, Fei coming back from the dock
corridor, Bren at the cargo hatch. Mereth and Halvi inside finishing
the manifest. Ev mostly absent.

In the dock corner, on the forwarding bench, **Inka Vell** —
Hab-Worlder widow, mid-fifties, came down on the night
passenger-kite. Small sealed packet in waxed paper, one cord, no
seal. Recipient name written on the outside: *Eshil Caur*, Sable
Crescent. Inka has a basket of dried apricots for whoever decides.
She has been waiting on the bench since before the PCs got in. She
has not interrupted.

The hook *Eshil Caur is already on the refusal log* lands as soon as
Drova opens the day-page (or any PC reads the name and asks). Two
years four months ago, the office refused to carry a Conclave-marked
summons to that recipient at the request of a Crescent advocate.
Mereth signed the refusal. The packet now is small, unmarked, from
a different sender. It may be nothing. It may not.

The four PCs have a fifteen-to-twenty-minute window before Mereth
comes out and decides. The scene is asking what kind of crew this
is when she is not in the room.

## 3. Possible directions

- **Drova opens the archive.** Pulls the original refusal-log entry
  on Eshil Caur. Reads the conditions Mereth recorded. Decides
  whether the new packet meets the same shape or a different one.
  Stake: the refusal log gets cited *by the crew* in the crew's own
  hands for the first time on screen. Hook: she walks her hand to the
  shelf-box without breaking eye contact with Inka.
- **Tek does the walkaround and finds something.** The signature move
  surfaces on screen. She may notice that the apron-lee humidity is
  already shifting — the Shear front is closer than the morning
  forecast, which sets up scene 2's tracker. Or she may notice
  nothing, which is also true to her practice. Stake: Tek's
  walkaround becomes the table's anchor for *the kite is what is in
  this scene too*. Hook: the back-of-hand check on the haul line.
- **Fei reads Inka.** Or she reads the corridor for the front. Or
  both — but *Listening Past Last Light* costs ten minutes of slow
  speech and the lift window is closing. Stake: whether Fei spends
  the move now or saves it for the action scene. Hook: she does the
  cuffs-apology when she comes back.
- **Bren talks to Inka.** Drop-pilot's posture is *I'm going to / I'm
  not*. She may simply ask the woman the question nobody else has
  yet asked. Stake: the conversation lands with Inka before the
  others have decided in committee. Hook: she steps off the cargo
  hatch toward the bench.
- **The packet itself surfaces.** Drova's *Angle* signature move can
  read indentations through waxed paper without breaking the cord —
  the page above this packet may have left ghosts of pen-pauses, a
  crease, a return-address erasure. Stake: the table learns whether
  *Inka* wrote the recipient or whether somebody else did. Hook:
  Drova tilts the packet flat against the lectern and lets the dock
  lamp rake across it.

## 4. NPCs in play

- **Inka Vell** *(new — stub here)*. Hab-Worlder widow, fifty-five,
  Hab Meridian. Late husband Den Vell, brother to Eshil Caur in
  Sable Crescent. Came down on the night passenger-kite specifically
  to walk the packet to the dock; would not entrust it to a Hab
  Meridian forwarder. Wants this delivered before the year-mark of
  her husband's death (which is in eleven days). She does not know
  the recipient is on the Splitfork's refusal log. She knows that
  Eshil and Den had not spoken in nineteen years. The packet is what
  Den left Eshil; she has not opened it. She believes she is doing
  what her husband would have asked her to do if he had asked. She
  wants this off her hands. She is patient and does not interrupt.
  *Promote to dm/notes/npcs/inka-vell.md after the scene.*
- **Mereth Kel** *(off-screen for the window of this scene; in the
  office with Halvi)*. Wants the manifest closed cleanly. Will come
  out at first light and ask whether it is closed. Will respect a
  decision the crew has made; will not respect a question handed to

  `dm/notes/npcs/mereth-kel.md` if it exists; else the org file.)

## 7. Secrets that might surface

Drawing from the wider list — none of the Caulden Rack arc-secrets.
Atomic-secrets technique; pick from this short list as appropriate:

1. **(About Inka.)** Inka has not opened the packet. She believes
   it contains a single object her husband Den left for his brother.
   She does not know what the object is and is afraid that if she
   opened it she would not be able to close it again with the same
   intention. *Surfaces if Bren or Fei asks the question directly.*
2. **(About the original refusal.)** Mereth's two-year-four-month-old
   refusal of the Conclave-marked summons to Eshil Caur was filed
   without a stated reason in the public log — *refused, no reason
   given* — but the office's internal note (which Drova can find in
   the archive shelf-box if she looks) says: *advocate's request
   inconsistent with subject's standing; no harm visible in carry,
   no good visible either*. *Surfaces if Drova pulls the archive.*
3. **(About Eshil Caur.)** Eshil Caur is alive, in Sable Crescent,
   and is a former Conclave reading-room steward who left the
   service shortly before Drova arrived at Thornvault. He is not on
   any other office's refusal log. He is not the senior archivist
   from Drova's third observation. He is not anyone Drova
   recognises. *Surfaces only if directly asked, and only as
   *consistent with* and *no evidence to suggest otherwise*.*
