# Scene: the apron before light

Glasswake reclaimed dock, the Splitfork's apron berth. Pre-dawn. The
dock lamps are still on; you can see your breath. The Splitfork is
loaded for the outbound loop except for the last bag, which Halvi is
walking the paperwork on inside.

Where you are:

- **Tek** — spar side of the apron, coat on, just beginning the
  walkaround.
- **Drova** — at the apron lectern, refusal-log day-page open, the
  archive shelf-box on the floor by her ankle.
- **Fei** — coming back from the dock corridor, where she went out to
  listen for first-light. Cuffs steady. Slow-speech window almost
  closed.
- **Bren** — at the cargo hatch, streamer pouch hanging at her chest,
  chalk on her left sleeve.

Mereth is in the office with **Halvi Tern** finishing the manifest.
Ev is somewhere warm, mostly inside himself. The apron is otherwise
yours.

## Who else is here

In the dock corner — has been here since before any of you got in — a
Hab-Worlder woman in her mid-fifties is sitting on the forwarding
bench with a basket on her lap and a small parcel of waxed paper
between her hands. She has not interrupted. She has been waiting.

- **Inka Vell**, Hab-Worlder, widow, mid-fifties, came down from Hab
  Meridian on last night's passenger-kite. She has a small sealed
  packet — waxed paper, one cord, no seal — that she wants the
  Splitfork to carry up the line. The recipient name on the outside
  is *Eshil Caur*, Sable Crescent. She has a basket of dried apricots
  she will give to whoever decides — not as a bribe, she will explain
  if anyone asks; in her hab nobody arrives at a dock empty-handed.

## What is open

You have somewhere between fifteen and twenty minutes before Mereth
comes out of the office. The lift window is at first light. The day
is starting.

You may have noticed (Drova will have, by the time she opens the
day-page; the rest of you may pick it up from her or from the name
itself) — *Eshil Caur* is already on the refusal log. Two years and
four months ago, the office refused to carry a Conclave-marked
summons to that recipient at the request of a Crescent advocate.
Mereth signed the refusal. It has been on the log since.

This packet is small. It is unmarked. It is from a different sender.
It may be nothing. It may not be nothing. Inka is waiting for
somebody to look at her and say something.

## What is not on the table

- Mereth's call. She will make one if the four of you don't. The
  scene is asking what kind of crew you are when she isn't in the
  room.
- The Caulden Rack. The outbound loop's signature cargo is not loaded
  yet — the refinery courier is not due until tomorrow morning.
