# Scene: the apron before light

Glasswake reclaimed dock. Splitfork apron berth. Pre-dawn — dock
lamps still on, breath visible. The kite is loaded for the outbound
loop except the last bag, which Halvi is walking the paperwork on
inside the office. Mereth is in there with her, finishing the
manifest. Ev is somewhere warm and mostly inside himself.

You four are on the apron.

- **Tek** is on the spar side, coat on, just beginning her walkaround.
- **Drova** is at the apron lectern, refusal-log day-page open, the
  archive shelf-box on the floor at her ankle.
- **Fei** is coming back from the dock corridor where she went out to
  listen for first-light. Cuffs steady. Slow-speech window almost
  closed.
- **Bren** is at the cargo hatch with the streamer pouch at her chest
  and chalk on her left sleeve.

In the dock corner, on the forwarding bench — has been there since
before any of you got in — a Hab-Worlder woman in her mid-fifties is
sitting with a basket on her lap and a small sealed parcel of waxed
paper between her hands. She has not interrupted. She has been
waiting for somebody to look at her and say something.

Her name is **Inka Vell**. She came down from Hab Meridian on last
night's passenger-kite. The packet is for her late husband's brother
in Sable Crescent — recipient name written on the outside: *Eshil
Caur*. The basket is dried apricots, for whoever decides; in her hab
nobody arrives at a dock empty-handed.

*Eshil Caur* is on the refusal log. Two years and four months ago,
the office refused to carry a Conclave-marked summons to him at the
request of a Crescent advocate. Mereth signed the refusal. The
packet on Inka's lap now is small, unmarked, from a different sender,
in waxed paper and one cord. It is plausibly nothing. It is
plausibly not nothing.

Mereth is in the office. You have somewhere between fifteen and
twenty minutes before she comes out and decides for you. The lift
window is at first light.

...what do you do?
