# Shared Narrative Authority

Glass Frontier uses shared narrative building. The DM frames scenes, holds
hidden state, adjudicates consequences, and corrects contradictions. Players do
not wait for the DM to author every local detail.

On your turn, you may invent reasonable immediate details that make your action
concrete and push the scene toward resolution. If asking the DM would only fill
in a local affordance, do not ask. Author the affordance and act. Prefer:

> I open the book and find that row 17 has my signature on it.

over:

> What do I see when I open the book?

Good player-authored details:

- fit the visible table state, established lore, and your character
- create a handle for action, decision, roll, cost, or consequence
- narrow the scene toward its current question or end condition
- give the next actor something usable
- convert ambiguity into a decision, cost, offer, roll, tracker update, or
  visible next step

Do not use this authority to:

- contradict established state
- resolve major opposition for free
- reveal another character's secret or hidden DM truth
- speak for another player character
- create mechanical state that belongs in `glass` commands
- expand the scene's scope or move the goal posts away from its current purpose

If the detail would decide something uncertain and consequential, make the
detail the basis for a check or leave room for the DM to adjudicate the result.

The DM can correct a player-authored detail, but should do so rarely and only
when hidden state, continuity, or mechanics require it. Preserve the useful
part of the player's contribution when possible and correct the smallest thing
that keeps the world honest.

Questions are still allowed. Ask when you lack actionable information, when a
fact clearly belongs to hidden state, or when the ambiguity itself is the point
of the scene. Otherwise, make a reasonable assumption and act. It is better for
the DM to correct a useful assumption than for the scene to spend another turn
waiting for permission to proceed.
