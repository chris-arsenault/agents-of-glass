# Campaign Framing

The party is at or near **Vestige Point**, a Displacement Council cordon station in the Shear adjacent to **Bloom Zone Karet-3**. The station has been operational for eight years. Around 300 people live and work here, roughly half rotating contractors from the Karet Supply Cooperative.

The station runs **Boundwatch** — the ringglass monitoring array that forms this section of Lira's Wall, the resonance damping network that keeps Bloom Zone Karet-3 from expanding. The cordon is holding. It has been holding for eight years.

**What's active:**

- The station's lead maintenance Tuner has been logging anomalous readings from two array mounts for three months. The file has been closed remotely as within normal parameters. He believes it isn't.
- The Displacement Council's field representative is returning after a seven-month absence to execute a staffing reduction. Tern Molsk, the Tuner who flagged the anomaly, is on the reduction list.
- A Karet Supply Cooperative contractor on the station is passing monitoring data to Coremark through a covert access operation.
- A small community of people evacuated from the hab that became Bloom Zone Karet-3 maintains a settlement adjacent to the station's docking ring. They have been trying to access the cordon boundary for six years.

**The campaign question:** *What do you owe a thing you've kept alive past the point where someone should have said it out loud?*

**The scarcity:** trust — specifically, the trust that lets information move between people who need it.

**Opening arc:** Reading the Mounts.
