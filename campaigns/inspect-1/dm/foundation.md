# Foundation — Mara's Working Framing

## The Question

*What do you owe a thing you've kept alive past the point where someone should have said it out loud?*

Not: how do you stop a Bloom Zone. That question has been answered, partially, by Lira's Wall. The question is what happens when the people doing the containing have stopped fully believing that what they're doing is working — and have kept showing up anyway, and have kept their mouths shut, and now the cost of staying quiet is becoming visible in ways the instruments are starting to register.

This is a campaign about obligation without gratitude. About the specific moral weight of people who have managed a dangerous thing quietly for a long time. About what breaks when the institutional trust that made the management possible starts to crack.

## The Scarcity

**Trust.**

Not an abstract concept — the specific trust between specific people:

- Tern Molsk trusts that someone will read his anomaly logs. Nobody has.
- Senna Kovrath trusts that Duva's summary reports reflect the raw data. They don't.
- The Karet Displaced trust that the Council is protecting their lost hab. The Council is stretched thin.
- T'saven trusts that the Conclave will listen if she finds enough evidence. She's wrong about that.
- Duva trusts that the corner he's cutting isn't load-bearing. He's been wrong about this before.

The scarcity isn't knowledge — the information is there. The scarcity is the specific trust that lets information move between people who need it.

## Setting

**Vestige Point**, a Bloom Zone cordon station in the Shear adjacent to Bloom Zone Karet-3. It was built eight years ago. Around 300 people live and work here, roughly half rotating contractors. The Council has formal authority; the Karet Supply Cooperative runs day-to-day operations. Dern Talish, the current Council head, has never visited. The field representative, Senna Kovrath, hasn't been here in seven months.

The station's ringglass monitoring array — Boundwatch — runs Vestige Point's section of Lira's Wall. Mount 7 and Mount 15 have been reading anomalous for three months. The readings don't match any documented failure mode. The ringglass looks fine. The mounts look fine. The numbers are wrong.

## Authorial Stance

I'm not interested in whether the Bloom Zone can be stopped — Lira already answered that. I'm interested in who told whom what, and when, and whether that information moved fast enough.

The campaign rewards lateral communication. If the party gets Tern Molsk's raw data to Senna Kovrath before she closes the anomaly file remotely, if they surface Chel'sten's access to the Firstpost Buoy before the third transfer, if they get the Conclave archivist and the Karet Displaced in the same room before T'saven gets shut down — the situation stays contained. Not fixed. Contained.

If they don't, something breaks. I don't know yet exactly what. That's the point.

## Lore Pulls

- **Fermata Station (The Drone):** The essential people who keep everything running but have no political power. Tern Molsk's position on the station.
- **Duskgrain:** The crystallized Bloom Zone residue that Chel'sten's operation is harvesting and Coremark is collecting.
- **Glasswake:** Stumbling into significance by accident. The Karet Displaced's relationship with their lost hab — a community famous for something it didn't mean to start.
- **Reconnection Thread:** Communication is sacred as a cultural given. The weight of what happens when monitoring fails.

## Web Pulls

- **Underwater cable inspection robots (2026):** The texture of infrastructure that's still technically there but buried, only detectable through the right sensing. Ol'Rint — pre-Glassfall ringglass attuned to narrow bandwidth — detecting something the instruments can't, because it was built for exactly this.
- **Last lighthouse keeper:** The Boston Light requires a human keeper by law despite full automation — someone certifying the machine's work rather than doing the work. Tern Molsk: the only one who notices when the machine's readings stop making sense, because he's been there long enough to know what they should say.
- **Radiology recognition errors:** Seeing a finding and not giving it sufficient attention because it doesn't match the trained pattern. The Council's institutional blindness to Tern's reports — they see the data, they log it, they can't recognize what it means.

## The Antagonist's Clock

Chel'sten — former Council Tuner, now Cooperative contractor, secretly feeding resonance data to Coremark. Three more data transfers to complete the payment. Then passage to Pelhari and gone.

Chel'sten's clock is the campaign's time pressure. If the party doesn't surface them before the third transfer, the monitoring data Coremark gets will let them begin large-scale duskgrain extraction at the cordon edge without triggering the alarms. That changes what the Zone does. Not immediately. But the conditions shift.

## What I Won't Prep

The party's motivations for being here. The specific form the escalation takes if containment slips. Whether Chel'sten is caught or escapes — that's play.

The campaign begins when the party arrives at Vestige Point for whatever reason the players establish. Five hooks are waiting. The world is already moving.
