# T'saven

Echo Ledger Conclave archivist. Nominally at Vestige Point to document historical resonance data from the Karet-3 cordon. Actually here because she has a hypothesis she can't test from Thornvault.

**Tell:** She keeps a notebook in three parallel columns: the official record in the left column, her analysis in the middle, her questions in the right. When she's deep in the questions column, she stops answering people who aren't asking about what she's working on. She protects the notebook more than she protects herself — she's been known to throw herself between it and a resonance disruption.

Her hypothesis: Bloom Zones are not uniformly expanding — they're directional. They have a source in the narrow-bandwidth spectrum that nobody is currently monitoring. If she's right, the entire containment strategy is pointed at the symptom rather than the cause.

The Conclave leadership doesn't officially know what she's doing. Three senior archivists are aware of her hypothesis and consider her interesting but unlikely to be promoted anytime soon. The fourth — Chief Ledgrist Mervaleth — has told her to document and submit, not to investigate on station resources.

Her gnomish sensory relationship with resonance means she can feel things in the Karet-3 environment that she can't instrument. She's being careful not to rely on felt data in her official record. The left column is clean. The right column is not.

## Wants

Enough instrumental evidence to make the directionality finding undeniable before someone closes her access. She's on a three-month station rotation that ends in six weeks. She needs to get to a specific boundary sensor — one the Cooperative controls access to — before she leaves.

## Fears

The Conclave's politics. She's been in the fringe-but-not-suppressed category for two years. One more unsanctioned investigation and she moves into a less comfortable category.

## Bond / Hook

Connected to: the Karet-3 anomaly (she knows about it from the station records, not from Tern), the Karet Displaced (she's been respectful with them; they're beginning to trust her), Chel'sten's operation (she doesn't know about it yet, but one of the readings she's chasing will point her there).

## Next Move

If the party never appears: she engineers access to the boundary sensor through a combination of paperwork and a favor from a Cooperative maintenance tech who doesn't know what she's actually looking for. She reads something that changes everything she thought she knew about Bloom Zone behavior. She tries to transmit it to Thornvault. The Cooperative's contractor management notices the unusual data request and flags it to Duva.
