# Tern Molsk

Lead maintenance Tuner at Vestige Point. Seven years on this station. The person who noticed the anomaly.

**Tell:** Before sitting down in any room, he checks the ringglass mount on the south wall — a habit from the station's first year when the mounts kept shifting during resonance fluctuations. The check takes about three seconds. He doesn't realize he does it.

He's not a Clarisant-certified Tuner — he trained under a folk practitioner on a now-defunct hab and picked up the rest on the job. His feel for Vestige Point's specific resonance landscape is better than anyone the Clarisant could send. That's the job. Being right isn't the same as being listened to.

He's on the staff reduction list. In six weeks, if nothing changes, he's off the station.

## Wants

Someone to look at the raw data from Mount 7 and Mount 15 and tell him he's wrong. He's spent three months trying to find the error in his own readings. He can't find it. He needs someone outside his own head to check.

A second thing, softer: for the work to mean something. Seven years. He keeps the Wall running. Nobody from the Council has been here in seven months.

## Fears

That he's wrong, and by raising the anomaly he's already spent whatever credibility he had. He's been careful. He's filed the reports in the proper channels. He hasn't made noise. But the reports have gone somewhere and produced nothing, and now he's starting to wonder if quiet was a mistake.

## Bond / Hook

Connected to: Boundwatch (he runs it), Ol'Rint (he has a theory about it), the Karet Displaced (he's been here as long as they have; they know each other), the anomaly.

## Next Move

If the party never appears: he starts a personal log. Not an official report — personal, written in a notebook, not filed anywhere. He documents everything he's seen, with timestamps, so that if something happens there's a record. He has no plan for what to do with the record. It's the only thing he can think of.
