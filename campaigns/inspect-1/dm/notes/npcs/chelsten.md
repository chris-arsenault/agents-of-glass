# Chel'sten

Former Displacement Council Tuner. Currently on the Cooperative's payroll. Running a covert Coremark data transfer operation via the Firstpost Buoy. Three more transfers and they leave.

**Tell:** They acknowledge every direct question before sliding past it. Not a deflection — a genuine acknowledgment of the question's validity, followed by an answer to a slightly different question. It sounds like engagement. It's one of the most effective social techniques on the station.

They know both sides of the Vestige Point operation better than anyone. They trained under Lira's containment methodology, worked Bloom Zone cordons for four years, understood the resonance environment at a level that takes most Tuners years to develop. The Council's staffing cut three years ago included them — budget line, not performance review. They landed at the Cooperative six months later through a recommendation from a former colleague who turned out to be connected to Coremark.

The work they're doing isn't unique in the system. There are former Council and Compact staff at Coremark-adjacent organizations across the ring. What makes Chel'sten's case specific: they know exactly what the data they're transmitting is being used for, and they know what it will do to the cordon.

They're not doing it because they believe in Coremark. They're doing it for passage money. The distinction feels important to them. It has stopped feeling important to anyone who would understand it.

## Wants

Enough to pay passage to Pelhari. Three transfers — they're counting. Leave before anyone connects the dots. Start over somewhere the name Chel'sten isn't in anyone's personnel records.

## Fears

A genuine breach. They know what the Bloom does to gnomes — the crystal-laced nervous system picks it up directly. They've felt it. They have calibrated exactly how much risk the current data transfers add to the cordon and concluded it's acceptable. They're starting to not be sure that calculation was right.

## Bond / Hook

Connected to: the Firstpost Buoy (their access point), Coremark (their client), the Cooperative (their cover), the boundary anomaly (they've noticed Tern's readings from the maintenance access logs, which they have access to, and they understand what the readings mean, and they've said nothing).

## Next Move (if party never appears)

Complete the third data transfer. Disappear from station roster via an unremarkable crew rotation. Be off the station before Senna Kovrath's review. Gone.

## Stat Block

**HP:** 18  
**Attack profile:** Tuner disruption — mid-bandwidth signal pulse (2d6, disrupts resonance perception for one round; Tuners and gnomes make a harder save). Can attune to a ringglass mount and blind it for 2d4 turns as a full action.  
**Escape:** Overload the nearest ringglass mount (creates an alarm and a burst of disorienting resonance), use the confusion to exit the area. They know every maintenance route on this station. If they decide to run, catching them requires either someone who also knows the maintenance routes or blocking the Deep Tap access point first.  
**Dangerous because:** They know what the party knows — if the party starts closing in, Chel'sten adapts. They're not aggressive; they're tactical about when to stop being present.
