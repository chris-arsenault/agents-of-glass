# Duva

Operations manager for the Karet Supply Cooperative at Vestige Point. He's not a villain. He's a person who has made a series of defensible decisions that have combined into something indefensible, and he's started to notice.

**Tell:** He doesn't lie. When he's giving you incomplete information, he answers a different question than the one you asked — with total specificity about the thing he chose to answer. It's genuinely hard to catch unless you know what you're looking for.

He was Coremark logistics before the Bloom — fourteen years. He wasn't near Pyre. He was in the deep Shear, running supply routes to extraction rigs, doing exactly what the job required. He went legitimate when Coremark went underground because the alternative was becoming what Coremark became. He considers himself to have made the right call. He's mostly right.

The Cooperative's connection to Coremark is not his doing. It was already in the structure when he took the operations manager role. He could have walked then. He didn't. That's the decision he can't explain without implicating himself.

## Wants

The contract extension. Genuinely — not just for Coremark's benefit but because losing this contract means his crew loses work, and his crew has been reliable, and he owes them. He'll betray the Cooperative if he concludes they're about to get him killed. He's not there yet.

## Fears

Coremark specifically. He knows one or two names in the organization's current structure. Those names know his. The relationship is polite and he understands the terms.

## Bond / Hook

Connected to: Senna Kovrath (she trusts his reports; he leverages this without feeling good about it), Chel'sten (he has tacit non-knowledge of the data transfer operation — he doesn't ask, which means he doesn't know, which means he maintains deniability), the Karet Supply Cooperative (his crew, his loyalty, his problem).

## Next Move

If the party never appears: he gets the contract extension by ensuring Senna Kovrath's station review goes smoothly. He does this through selective information management — showing her the right data, framing the anomaly reports as within range, being professionally thorough about everything except the things that matter. The extension is signed. He feels bad about this for approximately two days and then gets back to work.
