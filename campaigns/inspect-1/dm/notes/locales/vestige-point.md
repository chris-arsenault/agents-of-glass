# Vestige Point

The cordon station. It smells like processed ringglass and recycled air — a combination that people who've worked cordon stations describe as "the smell of controlled failure." Around 300 people; roughly half are rotating contractors. Eight years old.

The south wall's ringglass has a low-frequency hum. Tuners find it soothing on days when the Zone is stable. On days when Mount 7 or 15 spikes, the hum changes character in a way that's hard to name — it's the same frequency but it feels different, like the same word said in a different tone of voice.

**What does something:** The Council meeting room has a chair nobody sits in. The station's first director — whose name is in the Displaced Quarter's memorial wall as the one unverifiable entry — died in a minor Zone event two years ago. Nobody has formally claimed the director's role. The station runs on inertia and informal authority structures, which is part of why things have been able to slip through.

**Who's usually here:** Tern Molsk (maintenance bay, south side), Chel'sten (maintenance routing access, everywhere and nowhere), the Cooperative's logistics crew (docking ring, supply bays), a rotating Council monitoring team (three people currently, down to two when the reduction hits), T'saven (the cramped satellite office off the main corridor that used to be a storage closet).

**The maintenance bay:** Has a window that looks directly toward the Zone. The maintenance crew has learned to work with their backs to it. The distortion in the Zone's space is visible as a shimmer where the hab used to be, and the shimmer has a geometry that shifts. New crew members stare. Old crew members don't.
