# The Displaced Quarter

Twelve families — about forty people — on a surface platform adjacent to Vestige Point's docking ring. They've been here eight years. The platform wasn't designed for long-term habitation; the Karet Displaced adapted it. The adaptations are visible — a greenhouse module on the upper deck that shouldn't be there structurally but has been there long enough that nobody argues with it, cooking smells from what used to be a storage bay, a water reclamation unit installed by someone who knew what they were doing.

**The memorial wall:** The platform's main corridor has a wall covered in names — everyone who was on the Karet hab at evacuation, added by their families. The names are at different heights and in different hands, accumulated over years rather than placed all at once. The one unverifiable name — Resh Molant, the former director — is at eye level in the center, in the first-placed handwriting. All the other names were added around it.

**What does something:** One of the families has a child — twelve years old now, born in the Quarter — who has what they call "a sense for the Zone." The child has twice described seeing Karet's Echo before any adult noticed it. The description is consistent and specific: "a person standing at the boundary, pointing toward the gap." The family doesn't know what to make of this. T'saven, when she heard about it second-hand, asked very specific follow-up questions.

**Atmosphere:** The Quarter is not a sad place. It's a tense one. The people here are not grieving — or not only grieving. They're waiting, in the way that people wait when they're not sure what they're waiting for but they're sure it hasn't happened yet.
