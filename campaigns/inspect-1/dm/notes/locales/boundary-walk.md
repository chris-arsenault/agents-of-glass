# The Boundary Walk

The external inspection route around the cordon perimeter. Mag-grip suited, tethered, four hours at pace. Most crews do it in pairs. The station's operational protocols require a walk every 72 hours; the current schedule is running at 96 due to crew shortage.

**The view:** From the Walk, you can see Bloom Zone Karet-3 directly — no station walls, no filters, just the cordon boundary and the distortion behind it. The Zone doesn't look like nothing. It looks like space that has forgotten which direction it's supposed to be pointing. Depth becomes uncertain. Objects at the boundary's edge appear and don't appear in alternation.

The shimmer — what the distortion looks like from this distance — has a geometry. From the Walk's southern section (near Mount 7 and 15), the geometry has changed. The shimmer used to be roughly circular. It's now oval, extended approximately 200 meters toward the station. This is visible. It has been visible for eighteen months. It has not been formally documented because the Boundary Walk crews are Cooperative contractors, not Council monitoring staff, and they report informally to Duva.

**What does something:** The Threshold Pack is regularly visible from the Walk, moving along the boundary's outer edge. Their density is currently higher than usual. The Glass-Rind Drifter is visible from the Walk's northern section — a pale shape in the debris field, moving slowly, filaments catching the light.

**The Firstpost Buoy:** First buoy on the route, hence the name. Access panel. Standard maintenance port. Chel'sten's access is through this panel — there are marks on the panel's frame that a careful eye would recognize as repeated opening.
