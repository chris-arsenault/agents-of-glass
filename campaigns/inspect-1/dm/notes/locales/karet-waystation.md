# Karet-Adjacent Waystation

Vantara-managed, two hours from Vestige Point by kite along the Keel spur. The nearest regular traffic stop. Population: around 80 at any given time, mostly transient. Three docking arm clusters — inner arms (Vantara-administered, strict registration), middle arms (shared administration), and outer arms (minimal oversight).

**Operational:** It's a waystation. Fuel, supplies, resonance calibration services for kites, a medical bay staffed by one nurse. The station's permanent staff are six Vantara logistics technicians and a stationmaster who has been here eleven years and plans to stay until retirement.

**What does something:** The outer docking arms have seen a pattern of Coremark-adjacent vessels over the past six months — specifically, vessels with no cargo manifests, registrations from outer-system jurisdictions that the Vantara log system can't verify, docking for twelve to twenty hours and leaving with lighter hull resonance signatures than they arrived with (consistent with having offloaded cargo, not taken it on). The stationmaster has noticed but has not filed a formal report because the vessels' docking fees are paid on time and the Accord's station-neutrality provisions are ambiguous on this point.

**Where Saltmark is:** The kite-hauler is currently at the middle-arm loading dock, two days into what should have been a one-day stop. The captain, Dern Kask, is in the waystation's common area, drinking something warm, with the expression of someone who has been told to wait and hasn't been told for what.
