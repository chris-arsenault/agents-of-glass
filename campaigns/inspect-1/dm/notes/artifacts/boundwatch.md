# Boundwatch

The ringglass monitoring array that forms Vestige Point's section of Lira's Wall. Eighteen primary mounts and four secondary mounts arranged in a ring around the Karet-3 cordon perimeter. The heart of the station's containment function.

**Provenance:** Installed during Vestige Point's construction eight years ago, based on Lira Vashtenri's containment methodology. The array design is standard Council spec — the same configuration deployed at every cordon station. The specific ringglass in the mounts was sourced from Shear salvage at the time, selected by a Tuner who is no longer with the Council.

**Current status:** Operational. Mount 7 and Mount 15 are reading anomalous — three months. The readings don't correspond to any failure mode in the Council's documentation: the ringglass looks undamaged, the mounts are structurally sound, the calibration checks pass, and the numbers are wrong. The anomaly file has been reviewed remotely and closed as within normal parameters.

**What it does:** Monitors the resonance gradient along the Karet-3 cordon boundary and provides data to the stillwater damping buoys (Lira's Wall mechanism). When a mount reads correctly, it keeps the relevant buoy calibrated. When Mount 7 and 15 misread, two sections of the boundary are running on the damping buoys' last calibration — which is three months old. This is within operational tolerance. It is not within the tolerance Lira's design intended.

**The primary focus:** The array runs through Ol'Rint, the pre-Glassfall ringglass section installed as the primary focus. See separate entry.

**Hook:** Mount 7 and 15's anomalous readings are the first visible symptom. They're not the cause. The cause is what Ol'Rint is detecting.
