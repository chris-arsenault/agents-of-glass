# The Cartography Note

A water-warped field notebook, roughly A5 size. Green cover, originally. Now mostly brown. Came out of the Karet hab during the evacuation — one of six hundred personal objects recovered from the hab that were tagged as salvage and eventually sorted into storage at Vestige Point when the station was built.

**Provenance:** Belonged to Resh Molant, the Karet hab's director. Resh was a trained Tuner — specifically, an observer who'd spent twelve years mapping the hab's resonance environment and tracking its shifts over time. The Cartography Note is Resh's working survey of the Karet hab's resonance landscape, documented across three years of observation prior to the Bloom.

**Current status:** In a box in Vestige Point's storage room, on a shelf labeled KARET EVAC — MISC PERSONAL EFFECTS. The box hasn't been opened since it was shelved. Nobody has compared the notebook's resonance survey data to current Boundwatch readings.

**What it contains:** Survey notes, resonance readings by date, hand-drawn maps of the hab's crystal structure and resonance gradient distribution. The last six weeks of entries describe something Resh noticed in the hab's resonance environment — a gradual shift in the gradient's orientation that they couldn't explain. The final entry, dated three days before the evacuation order, reads: *"The gradient is pointing out. Not random — directional. SW of the transit ring, bearing consistent. Something beyond the hab is pulling at it. Filed a report with the Compact relay. Waiting."*

The Compact relay was offline at that point. Nobody received the report.

If the Cartography Note's survey data is compared to current Boundwatch readings: the Bloom Zone has expanded approximately 200 meters toward Vestige Point in the past 18 months. This matches the direction of the gradient shift Resh was observing before the Bloom.

**The narrow-bandwidth entries:** The last twelve pages of the notebook are written in a different hand — tighter, more abbreviated. Resh had narrow-bandwidth sensitivity (a restricted Clarisant credential, now expired). The entries in this section describe a signal in a frequency range standard instruments don't monitor. The entries are dated across the final six months before the Bloom. They describe the signal's bearing (SW of the transit ring), its apparent stability, and Resh's repeated attempts to calibrate against it. The final entry reads: *"The gradient is pointing out. Not random — directional. SW of the transit ring, bearing consistent. Something beyond the hab is pulling at it. Filed a report with the Compact relay. Waiting."*

Someone reading the notebook without narrow-bandwidth training will see the standard survey data and recognize the Zone expansion evidence. They will see the last section is denser and abbreviated and may not fully parse it. A Tuner with narrow-bandwidth sensitivity, or T'saven with her instrumentation, will see that the last section is describing the same signal Ol'Rint is currently detecting from Vestige Point — from eight years earlier, from inside the Bloom Zone before it was a Bloom Zone.

**Hook:** The Karet Displaced know this notebook exists — Resh's family knows it was on the evacuation manifest. They've been asking for its return for six years. The storage room is under Cooperative management.
