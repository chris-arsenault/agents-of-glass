# Firstpost Buoy

The oldest stillwater damping buoy still in active service at Vestige Point. One of the original buoys deployed during the Bloom response — installed two years before Vestige Point was built, as part of the emergency cordon established by the Bloom Coalition. When the station was built around the cordon, the buoy was incorporated into the array rather than replaced.

**Physical description:** Older design — a squat cylinder, about a meter tall, with a distinctive blue-grey casing that the current-model buoys don't use. The Vestige Point crew calls it Firstpost because it's the first one you reach on the external inspection walk.

**Provenance:** Lira Vashtenri's original containment deployment. The stillwater inside absorbed cascade frequencies during the early Bloom response — when the Zone was at its most active — and has been holding them for ten years. It's never been opened or replaced. Replacement was on three consecutive maintenance schedules and deferred each time for budget reasons.

**Current status:** Still operational, still holding. Chel'sten has been tapping it — using a narrow-bandwidth access technique that doesn't show up on standard monitoring — to extract resonance data that they're transmitting to Coremark. The tapping doesn't damage the buoy's primary function; it's siphoning the data layer, not the damping layer.

**What's inside:** The stillwater didn't store the cascade frequencies in the way that archival stillwater stores data — it absorbed them, the way a sponge absorbs water. The contents are still active, still present. Opening the buoy properly (rather than tapping it) would release the absorbed frequencies into the local resonance environment. The specific frequencies are what the Bloom Zone propagated along during the early cascade — mid-bandwidth, consistent, directional in a way that T'saven's hypothesis would predict and that nobody has formally documented.

**Hook:** Chel'sten's tapping has been amplifying the buoy's background resonance signature — the frequency bleed that's been drawing the Glass-Rind Drifter. If the tapping stops, the Drifter loses its gradient. If the buoy is opened, the consequences are not small.
