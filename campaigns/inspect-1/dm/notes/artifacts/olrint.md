# Ol'Rint

The primary ringglass focus for Vestige Point's Boundwatch array. An intact pre-Glassfall ringglass section, recovered from Shear salvage and installed at the station's founding.

**Physical description:** Roughly sixty centimeters across, asymmetric, shaped like a lopsided teardrop with three flat facets on the broad end. The color is a deep amber-brown that the standard grey of Shear-salvage ringglass almost never produces. In resonance-active conditions it produces a faint harmonic that people describe as feeling like a held breath.

**Provenance:** Salvage origin unknown. The recovery records show it came from a large structural section in the deep Shear, ring-era origin confirmed, but what function the original structure served is lost. It was selected for the Boundwatch array because of its size and unusually stable resonance alignment — the installing Tuner noted that it "calibrated clean without adjustment," which is rare for salvage material.

**What it does:** As a standard array focus, it concentrates the monitoring signal and distributes it across the perimeter mounts. It does this correctly and has for eight years.

What nobody has documented: Ol'Rint appears to be tuned to narrow-bandwidth resonance — a frequency range that the elves operated in and that no modern equipment monitors. Tern Molsk's hypothesis, developed over three months of anomaly investigation, is that Ol'Rint is detecting something that the standard monitoring array isn't designed to see — and that the anomalous readings on Mount 7 and 15 are not instrument error but signal bleed from Ol'Rint trying to route narrow-bandwidth data through mounts calibrated for mid-bandwidth work.

**Current status:** Still installed. Still functioning as the array focus. Still apparently detecting something that nothing else on the station can see.

**Hook:** If Tern's hypothesis is right, what Ol'Rint is detecting is whatever T'saven's directionality hypothesis is pointing toward. The source. The narrow-bandwidth signal that nobody is monitoring for. Getting the data out of Ol'Rint in readable form requires a Tuner with narrow-bandwidth sensitivity — extremely rare, Clarisant-prohibited for general practice. A folk Tuner who has never been trained to limit themselves might manage it.
