# Displacement Council

*The institutional face of Bloom containment. Holds authority over Vestige Point; exercises it mostly from a distance.*

**Identity:** The organization that maintains Lira's Wall. Neutral by charter. Trusted by everyone, controlled by no one, funded by voluntary contributions from factions that could stop at any time.

**Campaign-specific:** The Council's budget is under pressure. A recent Accord negotiation reduced their operational funding by 18%, and Dern Talish — the current Council head, fourth in the role — is managing the shortfall by cutting staffing at cordon stations. Vestige Point is on the reduction list: monitoring crew to drop by 30% in six weeks.

## Goal (right now)

Maintain the appearance of functional containment while absorbing a budget cut without triggering a formal incident report. The field representative, Senna Kovrath, is returning to Vestige Point after seven months away — her return is motivated by pressure to sign off on the staffing reduction, not by operational concern.

## Hold

Formal authority over Vestige Point and the Boundwatch array. Legal responsibility for Lira's Wall in the Karet-3 section. The Accord's humanitarian provisions — which nobody wants to test by defying them.

## Clock (4 segments)

1. Senna Kovrath returns and reviews the station (in progress — she arrives soon)
2. Staff reduction approved; Tern Molsk and two other senior monitoring crew are cut
3. Monitoring gaps open; Cooperative takes over vacated roles
4. Coremark extraction at the cordon edge begins without triggering alerts

## Relationships

At odds with: The Karet Displaced (they want access the Council won't grant). Frustrated by: The Echo Ledger Conclave (satellite archivist on station pursuing an unsanctioned investigation). Dependent on: The Karet Supply Cooperative (the only contractor capable of running station operations at the current budget level, which is exactly the problem).
