# Karet Displaced

*The people the Zone used to be a home. Eight years on and still here, which is its own kind of statement.*

**Identity:** Twelve families — around forty people — who were evacuated from the hab that became Bloom Zone Karet-3. They've built a small settlement in a surface platform adjacent to Vestige Point's docking ring. They're not leaving. The Accord's humanitarian provisions give them residency rights in the platform; the Council gives them nothing else.

They maintain a memorial wall listing the names of everyone who was on the hab at evacuation. The wall has one name that nobody from the original hab can verify — inscribed but unrecognized.

Their relationship to the Zone is not grief exactly. It's something more like guardianship. The Zone is where their home was. They watch it. Some of them believe things about what's in it that the Council doesn't take seriously and the Conclave finds professionally interesting.

## Goal (right now)

Retrieve a specific object from the cordon boundary — the former hab-director's field survey notebook, which they believe was left behind during the evacuation. The cordon's edge has recently become permeable enough in one section that retrieval might be possible without full Zone entry. They're looking for a way in — and someone to vouch for them with the Council.

## Hold

The memorial quarter. The accumulated knowledge of twelve families who lived inside what is now the Zone. An informal relationship with Tern Molsk, who has been at the station almost as long as they've been in the quarter.

## Clock (4 segments)

1. Identify that the permeable section of the boundary exists (they've already done this)
2. Negotiate access through either the Council or someone with authority at the station
3. Attempt retrieval — during which they discover that Karet's Echo is responsive to their presence
4. The notebook is found; it contains the former director's resonance survey notes from the week before the Bloom. What those notes say will determine whether the Zone can continue to be treated as a local problem.

## Relationships

Frustrated by: The Council (seven years of formal access requests, zero grants). Cautious toward: The Cooperative (they've seen Cooperative personnel at the cordon boundary at off-hours, which they haven't reported because they don't trust anyone official enough to report it to). Tentative trust: Tern Molsk. Professionally interesting to: T'saven, the Conclave archivist.
