# "Someone's been in the Deep Tap."

**Situation:** A maintenance log — accessible from the station's standard engineering terminals, not restricted — shows twelve access entries to the secondary shaft in the Deep Tap over the past two months. The Cooperative's official maintenance records for that period show one entry. The discrepancy is visible to anyone who compares both logs. The official records are the ones reported to the Council; the maintenance log is the station's own system.

The party can discover this through Tern Molsk (who uses the Deep Tap for Mount 7 and 15 inspections and has noticed the discrepancy but hasn't connected it to anything), through the station's engineering system access, or through someone mentioning that the Deep Tap has been feeling different lately — colder, more active in resonance terms.

**What's at stake if engaged:** The party goes to the Deep Tap, finds the secondary shaft, follows it to the exterior port, finds the marks on the Firstpost Buoy's access panel, and potentially catches Chel'sten mid-operation or finds the duskgrain cache. This is the fastest route to surfacing the Coremark data operation.

**What's at stake if ignored:** Chel'sten completes the third transfer and disappears during the next crew rotation. The evidence remains, but the person is gone. The Cooperative's contract extension goes through without anyone present who knows what the secondary shaft is for.

**Which NPCs/factions it touches:** Chel'sten, the Deep Tap, the Firstpost Buoy, the duskgrain cache, Duva/Cooperative.
