# "She was at my wall."

**Situation:** An elder of the Karet Displaced reports that Karet's Echo appeared on the Vestige Point side of the cordon boundary — the Displaced Quarter side — for the first time. Previous appearances have all been on the Zone boundary. This one was maybe twenty meters past the boundary line, standing at the section of the cordon that has become permeable. It was there for forty minutes. It pointed, twice, toward the permeable gap.

The elder who saw it knows what the Echo is — or believes she does — and she's not frightened. She's urgent. She's been trying to get formal access to the cordon boundary for six years and the Council keeps saying no and she is done with asking the Council.

**What's at stake if engaged:** The party has a reason to visit the Displaced Quarter. The Karet Displaced share what they know: the Echo's behavior pattern, the unverifiable name on the memorial wall, the Cooperative's presence at the boundary after-hours (which they've observed but not reported). The party gains the Displaced's trust, which opens access to the permeable gap and potentially to the director's notebook.

**What's at stake if ignored:** T'saven gets there first. She's been monitoring the Echo's appearance pattern and will reach the elder within the week. She gains the information, and the Displaced's relationship with the party starts from a lower baseline because the first person who showed up and listened was the Conclave archivist, not anyone the party sent.

**Which NPCs/factions it touches:** Karet Displaced, Karet's Echo, T'saven, the permeable gap, the Cartography Note.
