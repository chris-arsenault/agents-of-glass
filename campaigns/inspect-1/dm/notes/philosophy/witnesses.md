# Witnesses

The Bloom happened because nobody was looking at the right data in time. "That's what they said about Pyre" is losing its weight with people who weren't alive when Pyre burned.

The specific texture of this campaign: the people who built the cordon are tired, or reassigned, or gone. Tern Molsk has been here for seven years and is about to be let go. The Displacement Council's field representative hasn't visited in seven months. The Cooperative's operations manager knows something is wrong and has decided it's not his problem. The Conclave archivist is working an unsanctioned investigation on a three-month clock.

The scarcity isn't knowledge — all of the relevant information is physically present on or near the station. The scarcity is witnesses. People who have been paying attention long enough to recognize what a change looks like. People who will say, out loud, to someone with authority: *this is different from last year.* Not because they have proof — the proof is three months of anomaly logs that nobody in authority has read. Because they have been here, and they were here last year, and it's different.

The campaign doesn't require the party to be experts. It requires them to be present. To show up for Tern's hour of raw data. To sit with the Displaced Quarter's elder long enough for her to trust them with what she's seen. To be the person Dern Kask can tell what she saw at the waystation's outer arms without worrying that it'll disappear into a Cooperative report.

The party are witnesses in the making. The question is what they do with what they witness.
