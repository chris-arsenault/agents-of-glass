# Threshold Pack

Four to six canid-descended organisms that have adapted over generations to function at the cordon boundary of Bloom Zone Karet-3. Not Bloom-corrupted in any alarming sense — they've evolved to tolerate and exploit the resonance saturation that makes the cordon edge hostile to most life.

**Where encountered:** Boundary Walk, the cordon perimeter, occasionally on the exterior of the station's lower hull during peak resonance fluctuations. Never inside.

**Behavior:** They feed on duskgrain residue that crystallizes along the cordon edge — small deposits, but enough. Passive unless cornered. In the presence of a Tuner who asserts active resonance, they scatter; a sustained signal-band projection reads as a territorial challenge from something larger than they are. They hunt in loose coordination — one will draw attention while others feed.

**Physical / sensory:** Grey-brown, longer-legged than surface canids. Their eyes reflect resonance frequencies in ways their ancestors' didn't — in the dark, they glow faintly at a blue-green that Tuners recognize as mid-bandwidth emission. They're quiet. Their approach registers as a slight shift in ambient resonance before you hear anything.

**Mechanical:** HP 8-12 each. Attack: resonance bite (1d6 + mild disruption — save or Tuner ability reduced by one step for one scene). Pack tactics — advantage on attacks when flanking. A pack of three or more will mob rather than confront.

**Impulse:** They want the duskgrain. The fact that duskgrain is present in quantity along the boundary is why they're here in numbers. More pack members than usual means more duskgrain. Their presence density is a reliable indicator of how much extraction activity the Cooperative has been running at the boundary.
