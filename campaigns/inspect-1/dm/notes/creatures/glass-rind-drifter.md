# Glass-Rind Drifter

A deep Shear organism that has been drawn toward Vestige Point over the past two months. Nobody has seen one this close to an active cordon station before.

**Where encountered:** Currently maintaining position approximately 300 meters from the station's hull, in the Shear debris field between Vestige Point and the cordon boundary. Visible from the Boundary Walk.

**Behavior:** Passive. The Drifter moves slowly — roughly two meters per minute — along resonance gradients, the way a leaf follows a current. It has no aggressive impulse. Its danger is incidental: the crystalline filaments it trails are resonance-active, and when they make contact with ringglass structures, the harmonic response disrupts whatever that structure is doing. It's not trying to break anything. It's just very large and it follows resonance, and the station produces resonance.

**Physical / sensory:** Carapace-shelled, roughly the size of a small courier kite. The shell is opaque pale grey with irregular crystal inclusions that catch light in frequencies outside the visible range — Tuners see it differently than non-Tuners. The trailing filaments are hair-fine, dozens of them, extending ten to fifteen meters. They move with the debris field's slow currents. Up close they produce a faint harmonic that registers at the edge of hearing.

**Why it's here:** Ol'Rint — the station's primary ringglass focus — has been resonating in a frequency band that Drifters respond to. Specifically, the frequency that Chel'sten's tapping has been amplifying. The Drifter is following that signal the way it would follow any resonance gradient in the deep Shear. It's responding to something Chel'sten did.

**Mechanical:** HP 35. Not combatable in the conventional sense — it has no attack; trying to physically damage it causes filament contact, which disrupts the Boundwatch array for 1d4 hours per section of filament damaged. Repelling it requires sustained counter-resonance work — a skilled Tuner can redirect its gradient sense away from the station, but it takes time and the right equipment.

**Impulse:** It wants the gradient. It's following the signal. If the signal stops (i.e., if Chel'sten's operation is disrupted), the Drifter loses its orientation point and drifts back into the deep Shear within 24-48 hours.
