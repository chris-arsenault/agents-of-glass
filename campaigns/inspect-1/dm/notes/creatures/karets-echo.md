# Karet's Echo

A Bloom Zone phenomenon that has begun manifesting on the Vestige Point side of the cordon boundary — the Displacement Quarter side, near the memorial wall. Not a creature in the ordinary sense. The Karet Displaced consider it sacred. The Conclave archivist considers it the most significant data point in her investigation. The Cooperative's contractors avoid it and don't report it.

**Where encountered:** The section of the cordon boundary adjacent to the Displaced Quarter, during periods of elevated resonance activity — specifically, when Mount 7 or Mount 15 readings spike. It appears for between ten minutes and four hours. It has never appeared during a formal monitoring window.

**What it is:** A preserved resonance imprint. The former director of the Karet hab — Resh Molant, whose name is the unverifiable entry on the memorial wall — broadcast a final all-hab transmission during the evacuation. The transmission was absorbed into the Zone substrate. The Echo is a fragment of that transmission, expressed as presence rather than signal: a human-shaped resonance pattern that acts rather than speaks.

The Karet Displaced who've seen it recognize the director's posture, their way of standing. They've said nothing official about this.

**Behavior:** It doesn't communicate in language. It does things: points, responds to proximity, picks up objects and sets them down. It has twice pointed in the direction of the section of the cordon boundary that has recently become permeable. It appears to respond to specific people — those from the original hab specifically — with more complex behavior.

**What it knows:** The director's final transmission described what they'd seen in the hab's resonance environment in the week before the Bloom — specifically, the narrow-bandwidth signal Resh had been tracking for six months, its bearing, and the observation that the Karet hab's resonance gradient had reoriented to point toward it. The transmission also included the current state of the hab's ringglass structure and the fact that the Compact relay was offline and the report wasn't going through. This information is encoded in the Echo.

Getting it out requires: a folk Tuner or someone with narrow-bandwidth sensitivity (to read the resonance pattern directly — the Echo's signal band is narrow, not mid); or the director's field survey notebook (the Echo points toward the permeable section of the boundary where the notebook was left, not where the notebook currently is — it doesn't know the notebook was recovered during the evacuation and is in storage); or T'saven's stillwater instrumentation, which can capture fragments of the signal during a resonance spike, though she hasn't tried this yet because she hasn't understood what the Echo is encoding.

**Mechanical:** Cannot be attacked or physically interacted with — contact passes through it. Attempting to strike it disrupts the cordon boundary array in the adjacent section for 1d4 hours (the imprint is load-bearing on a micro-scale; disrupting it disrupts the nearby damping buoys). T'saven's instrumentation can detect it; standard monitoring equipment cannot.

**Impulse:** The Echo persists because the information it carries hasn't reached anyone who can act on it. That's not an intentional impulse — it's a resonance property. If the notebook is retrieved, or if the information is extracted by a skilled Tuner, the Echo dissipates.
