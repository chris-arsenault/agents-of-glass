# Secrets and Clues

Ten facts the players don't know yet. Unattached to specific scenes — deploy whichever fits the moment.

---

1. **The Zone has expanded.** Bloom Zone Karet-3 has grown approximately 200 meters toward Vestige Point in the past 18 months. The evidence is in the Cartography Note, sitting in a storage room box. Nobody has compared the notebook's data to current readings. Three clues: the geometry of the shimmer visible from the Boundary Walk; Tern Molsk's Mount 7 and 15 readings, which make sense as boundary-position drift; the Threshold Pack's increased density at what used to be the outer edge of their territory.

2. **Chel'sten is passing data to Coremark.** Three more transfers planned. The Firstpost Buoy access panel shows repeated-opening marks. The secondary shaft in the Deep Tap has twelve logged accesses against one official record. The Cooperative's supply inventory shows a resonance-contact line listed as "corrosion test equipment" that has never been used in a corrosion test.

3. **Ol'Rint is detecting narrow-bandwidth signal.** The anomalous readings on Mount 7 and 15 are Ol'Rint routing narrow-bandwidth data through mid-bandwidth mounts — which the mounts can't render correctly. Tern has the hypothesis; he lacks the confirmation. Confirmation requires either a Tuner with narrow-bandwidth sensitivity or comparison of the anomalous readings against a database of narrow-bandwidth frequency signatures (which the Conclave holds).

4. **Karet's Echo is the director's final transmission.** Resh Molant's evacuation broadcast was absorbed into the Zone substrate. The Echo is that broadcast expressed as resonance imprint. T'saven suspects this; she's been comparing the Echo's appearance pattern against Echo River research on historical resonance preservation. She's right, and she's about two weeks from having enough data to say so.

5. **The duskgrain cache is growing.** The Cooperative's extraction at the cordon boundary has been sporadic; the cache near the Deep Tap's exterior port has been growing because the extraction equipment's resonance bleed is *creating* more duskgrain, not just harvesting it. Chel'sten knows this and has included it in the Coremark data. What it means: the Cooperative's operation is changing the Zone boundary's chemistry in ways that accelerate duskgrain formation, which attracts the Threshold Pack, which draws the Glass-Rind Drifter.

6. **Senna Kovrath closed the anomaly file without seeing the raw data.** She reviewed Duva's summary. The summary described the Mount 7 and 15 readings as "within normal parameters for a station of Vestige Point's operational age." The raw data does not support that description. Tern Molsk filed the original reports through the correct channels, properly formatted. The summary is technically not false — it's accurate about what Duva believes, not about what the data says.

7. **Tern's readings match pre-expansion signatures from three other Bloom Zones.** The anomalous pattern on Mount 7 and 15 has appeared at three other cordon stations in the years before those stations reported Zone expansion. The correlation data is in the Council's archives. T'saven has access to those archives and has been cross-referencing without finding the connection yet — she's searching by frequency band, not by mount-reading pattern.

8. **The unverifiable name on the memorial wall belongs to a narrow-bandwidth Tuner.** Resh Molant maintained a Clarisant credential that the Conclave has in their records — specifically, a restricted credential for narrow-bandwidth observation, which the Clarisant issues rarely and tracks carefully. The credential is expired; it expired when Resh didn't renew it after the Bloom. T'saven could find this in the Conclave's records if she knew to look. The Conclave's Sithari office would have the physical credential file.

9. **The Bloom Zones are directional.** T'saven's hypothesis is correct. The narrow-bandwidth signal that Ol'Rint is detecting has a source — T'saven's readings point toward a specific region of the Shear, roughly in the direction Resh's Cartography Note described as "SW of the transit ring." Nobody has investigated what's in that region. It's outside the standard Keel traffic lanes.

10. **The Glass-Rind Drifter is a leading indicator.** Drifters don't approach cordon stations. The fact that this one is here — and approaching — means the Firstpost Buoy's resonance bleed is significant enough to register across several hundred meters of Shear. If Chel'sten's tapping stops, the Drifter's orientation point disappears and it drifts away within 48 hours. If it gets closer — if no one acts — its filament contact with the station hull will disrupt Boundwatch's primary array for long enough that the cordon's calibration falls out of spec across multiple sections simultaneously.
