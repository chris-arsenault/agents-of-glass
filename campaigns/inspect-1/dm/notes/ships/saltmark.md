# Saltmark

A mid-size kite-hauler on the Cooperative's supply contract, running the route between Vestige Point and the Karet-adjacent Keel waystation. Crew of six. Hull designation K-4481. She's been running this specific route for four years under the same captain.

**Physical description:** Standard hauler configuration — elongated hull, two kite-sail arrays for resonance-gradient propulsion, cargo bays rated for bulk aggregate and sensitive electronics. The hull has been patched twice where Shear debris caught the ventral plating. The patches are a slightly different color than the original hull and always will be.

**Provenance:** Originally a Vantara-contracted hauler; the Cooperative acquired her on lease three years ago when Vantara renegotiated their regional routes. The captain, Dern Kask, came with the ship.

**Captain — Dern Kask (hab-worlder, human):** Has run this route for four years. Knows the Shear segment between the waystation and Vestige Point the way you know a commute — every drift, every resonance pocket, every place where debris clusters. She's noticed Coremark-adjacent vessels at the waystation more frequently in the past six months, docked on the outer arms where Vantara's registration logs are less rigidly enforced. She hasn't said anything because she doesn't know who to say it to, and partly because the Cooperative is her current employer.

**Current status:** Three days overdue. Last transmission placed Saltmark at the waystation loading dock. No distress signal. The station's communication log shows a routine departure confirmation and then nothing.

**Hook:** Saltmark's delay is the most visible entry point for the Coremark presence at the waystation. Whether the ship is fine (delayed by debris, a hold issue) or not fine (intercepted, pressured, or used as cover) — either outcome reveals something.
