# Arc Plan — Reading the Mounts

## 1. The Stakes Question

Will the information about what Bloom Zone Karet-3 is doing reach someone with institutional authority before the monitoring apparatus passes into hands that profit from its silence?

## 2. The Threats

**Chel'sten** (antagonist, Cooperative contractor, former Council Tuner)
Impulse: *to finish and be gone.* Running three scheduled data transfers via the Firstpost Buoy, then disappearing via routine crew rotation before Kovrath's review concludes. Between scenes: completes transfer preparation, reviews departure documentation, monitors whether the party is asking about the buoy. Adapts when threatened. Does not initiate confrontation — becomes absent instead.

**Cooperative operational momentum** (faction-as-threat, embodied in Duva)
Impulse: *to control what the monitoring data says.* Not malice — institutional self-interest. Duva briefs Kovrath on summaries that Chel'sten wrote. The contract extension document circulates. The secondary shaft access gets absorbed into maintenance logs. Between scenes: Duva answers Kovrath's questions before she can ask Tern's; the contract paperwork moves; what's irregular gets normalized by proximity to normal.

**Bureaucratic closure** (institutional reflex, embodied in Senna Kovrath)
Impulse: *to finish the review and file it resolved.* Kovrath is not corrupt. She is operating exactly as she was trained to operate — review summaries, sign off, leave. The system that produced her review process built the latent weakness in. Between scenes: she reviews the anomaly file (Duva's summary; raw data not accessible without asking the right person), signs the reduction order, schedules departure. She closes things. That's what she does.

**The Glass-Rind Drifter** (environmental pressure, not a villain)
Impulse: *to orient toward the signal source.* It doesn't know the station is there. The Firstpost Buoy's resonance bleed is the strongest signal in its range. Between scenes: moves roughly 50 meters closer on a 48-hour cycle. At filament contact range, Boundwatch's primary array disrupts across four sections.

## 3. Clocks

**Chel'sten's Transfers**
1. Second transfer prepared. Chel'sten schedules a four-hour uninterrupted maintenance window during Kovrath's third day on station.
2. Second transfer complete. Firstpost Buoy resonance bleed increases. Threshold Pack density at the boundary rises. T'saven notices the increase; doesn't know the cause.
3. Third transfer complete. Coremark has full mapping data. Chel'sten begins departure documentation. Passage booked under a different name.

**Kovrath's Review**
1. Kovrath arrives. Duva briefs. The reduction list is already posted in the common area.
2. Kovrath reviews the anomaly file (Duva's summary). Files review complete. Signs off on the reduction. Does not speak to Tern before signing.
3. Reduction effective date set. Tern is off the station in six weeks. Monitoring responsibility transfers to Cooperative-aligned crew who won't inherit Tern's paper logs.

**The Drifter's Approach**
1. Drifter visible during resonance spikes from the station hull. Tern logs it. It's not in Boundwatch's standard detection range so there's no formal alert.
2. Drifter within 100 meters. Filament tendrils register on Boundwatch during spikes as static on the outer sections.
3. Filament contacts station hull. Boundwatch disrupts across four sections for 2d4 hours. Cordon calibration falls out of spec. Kovrath cannot leave — the disruption triggers a mandatory review hold.

## 4. Possible End-States

**Contained.** Party gets Tern's raw data to Kovrath before she closes the review. Kovrath sees that the summary and the raw data describe different things. She defers the reduction. Chel'sten's buoy access is surfaced — by the party, T'saven, or the Displaced (any path works). The contract extension collapses. Monitoring gaps don't open. The Zone expansion is on the official record. The narrow-bandwidth source question is open for arc two.

**Partial — institution closes, operation stopped.** Kovrath signs the reduction before the party reaches her with the raw data. But the party stops Chel'sten's third transfer — they get to the buoy, or surface the access logs, or catch Chel'sten in the Deep Tap. Coremark doesn't get the full mapping data. The monitoring situation is degraded but the immediate threat is paused. Arc two opens from a weaker institutional position.

**Partial — operation runs, information survives.** Chel'sten completes all three transfers. But the party gets the Cartography Note out of storage and into hands that matter — T'saven, the Conclave, Glasswake's memorial committee. The information lives outside official channels. Coremark has the mapping data; the Conclave is now investigating. Arc two is a conflict between those two nodes.

**Partial — Drifter forces the hand.** The Drifter reaches the hull. Boundwatch disrupts. The disruption triggers a mandatory review hold — Kovrath cannot leave. The crisis breaks the closure reflex. The party now has more time and more chaos. Kovrath is angry; so is Duva; Chel'sten is watching for an exit. The arc continues in a different register.

**Failed.** All three timers run. Monitoring is compromised, Coremark has the mapping data, Tern is gone, and whatever crew replaces him is reporting what the Cooperative wants reported. The next arc opens with the party knowing something nobody official will believe: the Zone expanded 200 meters and the instruments watching it are now speaking for the people extracting from it.

## 5. The Strong Start

Tern Molsk is in the monitoring bay. He got word Kovrath arrived two hours ago. He's sitting in front of Mount 7's raw feed with his paper log open on his lap, pen uncapped. The outer display shows the Firstpost Buoy's resonance output ticking upward — he can see it but hasn't reported it yet because he doesn't know whether it's connected to the anomaly or something else, and he is tired of filing reports nobody reads.

When the party enters, he doesn't look up. After a moment: "She closed the file. You know that? She got in this morning and Duva sent her a summary and she closed the file."

Mount 7 chirps. He turns back to the screen. The amber glow of Ol'Rint in the array housing fills the corner of the bay.

"...okay. What do you do?"

## 6. Nodes

**The Monitoring Bay** — Vestige Point, restricted-access, always staffed
Tern, the raw feed, three months of paper logs. Ol'Rint installed in the array housing — deep amber-brown, asymmetric, a held-breath hum when the resonance is up. The anomalous readings on 7 and 15 are visible in the raw feed; the official log shows them marked *within normal parameters* (Duva's note, not Tern's).
- Clue out: the Firstpost Buoy access logs show twelve entries vs. one official record — Tern noticed, can't explain it (→ The Deep Tap).
- Clue out: Tern mentions the Echo appearing twice in the past week near the boundary section adjacent to the Displaced Quarter (→ The Displaced Quarter).
- Clue out: for anyone with resonance training, the readings look like narrow-bandwidth signal routing through mounts calibrated for mid-bandwidth — Tern has the hypothesis, lacks the confirmation (→ T'saven's workspace).

**The Displaced Quarter** — docking ring, residential platform, no access restriction
A greenhouse that shouldn't exist structurally (but does, and has for four years). A memorial wall. One child who sees the Echo before the adults each time. The Echo appears during resonance spikes, in the section adjacent to where the boundary has become permeable. The Displaced recognize the director's posture; they've said nothing official.
- Clue out: they've been asking for the director's field notebook for six years — it's in storage, marked KARET EVAC — MISC PERSONAL EFFECTS (→ Vestige Point storage room, the Cartography Note).
- Clue out: the Echo started appearing more often roughly six weeks ago, about when the Firstpost Buoy's readings shifted (→ Chel'sten's timetable).
- Clue out: an elder says Resh was watching "the same corner of the sky" the last six months before the Bloom — a bearing SW of the transit ring (→ T'saven's directionality data).

**Kovrath's Review Room** — Council meeting room, south wall, the chair nobody sits in
Kovrath reviewing files from Duva. The anomaly file marked closed, the reduction order drafted, Duva's summary on the table. She's defensive but not dishonest — she reviewed what she was given. The summary says *within normal parameters for a station of Vestige Point's operational age*. This is technically accurate about what Duva believes. The latent weakness is in the process, not the person.
- Clue out: Duva's name is on the summary — the chain becomes visible (→ Duva's office, the Cooperative).
- Clue out: the reduction list has Tern's name and a note that his anomaly reports have been reviewed and closed (→ Tern, urgency).
- Clue out: Kovrath mentions Chel'sten with mild approval — "the Cooperative's Tuner has been very helpful with the technical questions" (→ Chel'sten).

**The Deep Tap** — maintenance access, restricted, Cooperative management
The secondary shaft the Cooperative added, not in the blueprints. Twelve logged accesses against one official record. On a shelf near the external port: a rack of microcavities — thumbnail-sized glass cells, each one humming faintly — listed in the supply log as "corrosion test equipment," never used in a corrosion test. These are Chel'sten's transmission rig. The Firstpost Buoy access panel shows repeated-opening marks. The duskgrain cache near the external port is larger than it should be.
- Clue out: the access log timestamps correspond to Chel'sten's shift rotations — someone who knows the station schedule can read it (→ Chel'sten).
- Clue out: the Firstpost Buoy's open-facing resonance bleed is directed toward the outer waystation arms (→ Karet-Adjacent Waystation, Saltmark, Coremark staging).
- Clue out: the duskgrain density is consistent with extraction-resonance bleed creating more duskgrain, not just harvesting it — boundary chemistry change (→ Tern's anomaly reading pattern, Zone expansion).

**T'saven's Workspace** — assigned research quarters, instrument-dense, clear table nobody uses
Seven weeks of data. Three-column notebook (she guards it more than herself). Instrumentation that can detect the Echo — she's been tracking it. The Zone is directional: she has the data to say this in two more weeks; she's not there yet. She has Conclave archive access, including Clarisant credential records. Her supervisor thinks she's chasing a pattern artifact.
- Clue out: her directionality data points SW of the transit ring — matches the bearing Resh Molant's final notebook entry describes (→ the Cartography Note).
- Clue out: she's been comparing the Echo's appearance pattern against Echo River research on resonance preservation of final transmissions — she's right, and she knows she's right (→ the Displaced Quarter, the Echo).
- Clue out: if shown Tern's anomaly readings, she can immediately confirm the narrow-bandwidth hypothesis — they match pre-expansion signatures from three other Bloom Zones in her archive data (→ the Monitoring Bay, urgency).

## 7. What's In Play

**Antagonist:** Chel'sten. Primary opposition. Patient, tactical, knows every maintenance route.

**Creatures:** Threshold Pack (density at the boundary indicates the extraction operation's scale — a player who knows what to look for reads it as a map). Glass-Rind Drifter (time pressure; Tern is the only one tracking its approach). Karet's Echo (information node; appears during resonance spikes; T'saven and folk-Tuner PCs can read it directly).

**Named things:** Boundwatch (the broken communication channel — everything flows through it incorrectly). Ol'Rint (the unexpected sensor — needs narrow-bandwidth sensitivity to read cleanly). The Cartography Note (the hidden proof — most likely found by someone looking for something else). The Firstpost Buoy (Chel'sten's access point — the rack of humming microcavities is on the shelf). Saltmark (thread to the Karet-Adjacent Waystation; captain has seen Coremark vessels at the outer arms).

**Locations:** Vestige Point (hub). The Boundary Walk (Zone expansion visible in the shimmer's changed geometry — oval, extended toward the station — to anyone who knows what the cordon is supposed to look like). The Displaced Quarter (Echo node; greenhouse; memorial wall). The Deep Tap (Chel'sten's operation; the incriminating shelf). Karet-Adjacent Waystation (Coremark staging; Saltmark).

## 8. Arc-Specific Secrets

1. The narrow-bandwidth signal Ol'Rint is detecting has a stable origin point: something pre-Glassfall, in the region Resh Molant's notebook describes as "SW of the transit ring." It has been active for at least three years — the full extent of Ol'Rint's anomalous readings. T'saven's directionality data is pointing at the same bearing from an independent instrument. The two data streams confirm each other, but no one has put them in the same room.

2. Duva did not falsify the summary. He wrote what he believed. He has never looked at Tern's raw data himself — he has Chel'sten's read on it, which Chel'sten provided specifically because Duva would rely on it without looking further.

3. Chel'sten has not yet completed the second transfer. The window is Kovrath's third day on station — a scheduled four-hour maintenance period. The transfer requires uninterrupted buoy access. Interrupting this window stops it; the data would have to be rebuilt from scratch.

4. Resh Molant's last transmission is not only preserved in the Echo. A fragment of it is in the Firstpost Buoy's absorbed cascade frequencies — the buoy was the closest active transmitter during the Karet evacuation. Chel'sten knows this. They have not included it in the Coremark transfers. They haven't explained this to themselves.

5. The permeable section of the cordon boundary — where the Echo points — isn't random failure. It corresponds to where the Zone expanded and where boundary damping buoys weren't repositioned to match. The cordon isn't failing there. It's just thin.

6. The Karet Displaced's greenhouse uses duskgrain residue as a soil amendment without knowing it. They're using "the grey soil from the boundary walk" because certain crops take to it. The duskgrain is being metabolized by the plants into something chemically inert. T'saven would consider this significant for Bloom Zone interaction research.

7. If the party stops the third transfer, Chel'sten doesn't leave. The passage money was contingent on full delivery. They're stuck on the station with no exit and no leverage. What they do then is genuinely unknown.

## 9. Done Criteria

The arc is resolved when the stakes question has been answered one way or another: did the information move in time?

**Resolved** when: the window has passed and the situation has settled into a new state — whether contained, partially failed, or completely failed. Practically: Kovrath has either reversed the reduction order or it's taken effect; Chel'sten has either been surfaced or has completed all transfers and departed; the Drifter has either been addressed or has made hull contact. Any combination qualifies. The arc is over when the pressure of the ticking clocks has discharged.

**Abandoned** when: the party pivots entirely to the directionality question, the waystation, or some other thread the arc didn't anticipate, without engaging the institutional failure and the three clocks. If that happens, the arc runs off-screen: Kovrath signs the reduction, Chel'sten finishes the transfers and leaves, the monitoring gap opens. The campaign continues from a changed landscape. Note the off-screen resolution in the arc's context.md; close the arc; move on.
