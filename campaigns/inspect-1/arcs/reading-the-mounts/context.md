# Reading the Mounts

The party is at Vestige Point. The station is running. The cordon is holding. Something has changed.

Mount 7 and Mount 15 on the Boundwatch array have been reading anomalous for three months. The station's lead maintenance Tuner believes this is significant. The Council's field representative, returning from a seven-month absence, has already closed the anomaly file remotely. A contractor on the Cooperative's payroll is quietly passing monitoring data to Coremark. The Bloom Zone Karet-3 has expanded 200 meters toward the station in the past 18 months. Nobody knows all of this at the same time.

**The arc's pressure question:** Will the party surface what's happening — and get it to someone with authority — before the staff reduction creates monitoring gaps, before Chel'sten completes the final data transfer, and before the cordon's calibration falls far enough out of spec that the next boundary event isn't containable?

The arc has no predetermined climax. It ends when the situation resolves or fails to resolve. If the party gets Tern Molsk's data to Senna Kovrath before she closes her review, if they surface Chel'sten's access before the third transfer, if they keep the contract extension from being signed on false pretenses — the situation stays contained. Not fixed. Contained.

If they don't, something changes. The monitoring gap opens. The Cooperative controls what gets reported. The Bloom Zone continues to expand toward the station, and the next person to notice will be working with worse instruments and fewer experienced colleagues.

**What the players know at the start:** They're at Vestige Point. Tern Molsk would like someone to look at some data. The supply ship is three days late.
