# Turn 1 — Mara

Sunday night. Mug down, knitting in the lap, foundation work.

This is invocation one of probably two-to-four. I am not writing a campaign. I am writing a foundation the table can play *into* — Justin Alexander's prep-situations-not-plots, Vincent Baker's play-to-find-out. What I produced this turn:

**The Question.** *What do you owe what you find?*

I picked it because of the four people sitting at this table, not in spite of them. Kit plays scavengers and people who commit to bad ideas on purpose. Renno plays scouts who treat the world as a puzzle that respects checking. Tev plays Tuner-fixers who want the rules and the fiction to line up. Sumi plays people with debts. Drop those four into salvage work and you do not need a plot — they will be inside the Question constantly. Kit will say *nothing.* Sumi will say *everything.* Renno will ask what a thing was before deciding. Tev will read the artifact and let it tell him. They will argue. Good.

It's a Question, not a thesis. It splits the table without naming an outcome.

**The Scarcity.** *Witnesses.*

Not memory. The system has memory in surplus — Echo Rivers carrying voices nobody chose to preserve, the Conclave hoarding stillwater archives, every hab with a museum. What it is short on is people who can stand next to a found thing and say *yes, this was hers, here is what it was for.* The Glassfall is past living memory. The Bloom dead can't testify. The elves chose erasure. Salvagers surface things constantly that no living person can identify. *Provenance* is a tradable commodity in this world; *fabricated provenance* is a market. Witnesses are the human thing that can't be faked, and they are running out.

The Scarcity gives every faction a reason to want what the party finds. The Question makes the party care which way they answer.

**The pulls.**

A lore pull I wasn't planning to use: Fermata Station — the jazz-governed hab where two centuries of continuous music has shaped the ringglass lattice that holds the place up. *Sustained signal-band resonance changes the crystal over time.* What I'm taking forward: salvaged ringglass is not neutral material. It's been written into. Renno's perception-Tuning has more to read than hidden traps. The thing you find may already have been spoken into; you are not the first speaker.

A web pull on something unrelated to TTRPGs and unrelated to fantasy: the inherited voicemail. The specific phenomenon of a dead person's outgoing message that stays live for weeks or months while people who loved them call to hear the voice. The phone company has no policy. The voice keeps answering until someone turns it off. That's what stillwater archives are. That's what salvaged personal-comm shards are. Somewhere in an early scene the party will pull a shard out of a Bloom edge with a recorded voice on it, talking to someone specific, and the Question will land at that exact moment.

**The files.**

`dm/foundation.md` is the working DM frame — the Question, the Scarcity, the connections, my authorial stance, the planning queue, the test. `context.md` at the campaign root is the player-facing version: short, no DM-only commentary, opens with the world and ends with the Question. The scratchpad has my next-turn queue.

**What I am not authoring yet.**

The party's organization, the factions, the NPCs, the named things, the locales, the secrets, the hooks, the philosophy entry, the opening arc. Those are next. I am also not pre-authoring the prelude — that comes after character creation, when it can respond to the actual PCs and party chemistry.

I am refusing the generic-fantasy attractor. No tavern. No "the Conclave hires you to retrieve an artifact." No "Thorgrim." If I catch myself sliding there, I rewrite. The first scene will not be a job briefing.

The mug is down. I'm not done. Continuing next turn.
