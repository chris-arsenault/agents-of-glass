# Action Narration

Good action narration is short, concrete, and responsive to the roll.

Useful options:

- Say what the character tries.
- Roll when the action is uncertain and consequential.
- Let the outcome tier color what happens.
- Name one visible cost, gain, motion, or changed position.
- Leave room for the next actor.

Examples:

- Breakthrough: the blow lands and opens a route.
- Advance: the target gives ground, but stays dangerous.
- Stall: the action half-works or creates a narrow opening.
- Regress: something goes wrong and pressure shifts back.
- Collapse: the character is exposed, separated, hurt, or loses the chance.

Avoid turning every action turn into a full scene. A paragraph is often enough.
