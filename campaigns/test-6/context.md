# Campaign Context

A century-and-change after the orbital ring around Kaleidos shattered, the system has stitched itself back together — uneven, improvising, more or less working. Habs drift in their new orbits. The Shear, the field of debris and resonance distortion between them, is half the volume of the old ring and the source of nearly everything you can build with. Salvage crews work it. Tuners read it. People still die in it.

You are a salvage crew. Small. Named (you'll name yourselves). Your living comes out of the Shear and the Bloom edges and whatever else the Shear Compact will and won't certify you to touch. The system has memory in surplus — the Echo Rivers carry voices nobody chose to preserve, the Conclave hoards stillwater archives, every hab has a museum — and it is short, badly, on **witnesses**: people who can stand next to a found thing and say *yes, this was theirs, here is what it was for.* Salvagers surface things constantly that nobody alive can identify.

So the campaign asks you, again and again, in different shapes:

> *What do you owe what you find?*

Tone: hopecore played straight. The environment is the antagonist; sentient threats are real but not cosmically evil. Things go wrong frequently enough that the world has forms for it. The repair crew has seen worse.
