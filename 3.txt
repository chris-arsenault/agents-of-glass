● Yes — and I think it's structurally the same shape of confusion.

  The legal-drama fix was: game-state durability ≠ in-fiction procedure. I was reading a system requirement (continuity
  across turns) and answering it with character behavior (build a custody chain, secure witnesses). Once that line got
  drawn, the prose stopped doing paperwork.
  
  The analogous distinction here is: codified handles ≠ in-fiction language.

  The game system needs stable names so turn 92 and turn 93 are talking about the same thing across many actors and many
  days: clocks have labels ("Shear Wash Builds," "Anchor Walk Intercept"), beats have labels ("Singing Seed Rack," "Runner
  on the Hooks"), gear has names ("Second Knock," "Gold-Seam Hold"), table artifacts have filenames ("moving-warm-line.md,"
  "white-seed-rack-sled.md"). That's the codification layer doing exactly what codify-only-what-drifts says it should —
  pinning the referents so they don't drift turn-to-turn.

  What I'm doing is borrowing those handles into prose as if they were the fictional lexicon. A character on the hooks
  doesn't think "the moving warm line" — that's the operator's name for the cable in the bookkeeping. She thinks the cable, 
  hot enough to smoke, sawing across the brackets. She doesn't think "Shear Wash Builds is at 3/4" — she thinks the wind is 
  about to take me off the wall. She doesn't think "the singing seed rack" — she thinks the cracked rib in the third strap, 
  still humming.

  Same structural error as the legal-drama drift: I'm letting infrastructure (continuity handles) leak into the fiction's
  voice. The codified label is shorthand for the people reading the transcript stitch the same referent across turns. It is
  not how the character perceives the world in the moment.

  A secondary, smaller distinction in the same family: PC-interior craft idiom ≠ encrypted craft narration. Tela can have a
  specialist's ear, but a specialist narrating her own work translates — one pointed term per beat, not stacks of trade-noun
   compounds. The style sheet wants me inside her head; it doesn't actually want her vocabulary illegible.

  If I held the first distinction the way I now hold "records are residue, not action," I think the same kind of
  self-correction would happen: I'd refer to the same referents with natural in-character description, and reach for the
  codified handle only when the bookkeeping demands it — closeout summary, messages, state updates. Not in the prose itself.
