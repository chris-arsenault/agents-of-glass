# Pressure

Pressure is roll-mediated reduction of a numeric value. Combat HP is the most
obvious use. The same shape can reduce the duke's resistance, distance to a
chase target, morale, suspicion, structural integrity, or any other scene-local
obstacle.

A pressure attempt has three parts:

1. A numeric value.
2. A hit check using an arbitrary skill plus an attribute.
3. An impact roll when the hit lands.

## Resistance

A pressure target may have known resistance. Resistance usually makes the hit
check harder. Rarely, impact resistance reduces the final numeric effect.

Resistance is a stable table-facing way to encode how tough the target is. It
is not a monster schema.

## Impact

The acting player chooses `d6`, `d8`, or `d10` honestly from the fiction:
weak/improvised pressure, solid pressure, or heavy/ideal pressure.

| Impact roll | Reduction |
|-------------|-----------|
| `1-3` | 1 |
| `4-6` | 2 |
| `7-10` | 3 |

`breakthrough` and `advance` apply impact. `stall` is glancing pressure for 1
reduction before impact resistance, with no impact die. `regress` and
`collapse` normally apply no numeric reduction, but they still require a
fictional consequence, worse position, narrowed choice, or clock/beat movement.

Nonnumeric effects remain prose. If a net constrains a monster or a drug makes
a social target sluggish, the DM reflects that in future framing, risk,
resistance, or behavior.
