# Action Scenes

Action scenes are quickfire rounds for contested moments: combat, chases,
social pressure, escapes, disasters, heists, duels, trials, and any scene where
every turn changes the situation.

`combat`, `chase`, and `social-pressure` are toolkit examples, not an
exhaustive list.

## What Changes

- Turn order is tight and usually rolled on scene entry.
- Fictional time is seconds or a few heartbeats.
- More actions are uncertain and consequential, so rolls are more common.
- The scene has a clear, player-visible endpoint, often numeric.

## Turn Menu

On an action-scene turn, the actor can:

- move
- take one meaningful action
- do housekeeping needed for that turn

Housekeeping includes messages, inventory checks, reading the table, reading
relevant lore/state, and asking necessary clarifying questions. It is not a
second action.

## Endpoints

The DM should declare what would end the action scene and track progress
honestly: enemy HP, morale, distance, suspicion, concession progress, hazard
pressure, survival rounds, or another concrete value.

When the endpoint resolves, end or shift the scene. Do not add one more twist
after the tracker says the action is over.
