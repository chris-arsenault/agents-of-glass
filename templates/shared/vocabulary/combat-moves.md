# Combat Moves

A small curated set of verbs for what a PC does on a combat turn. **Reference, not validation.** The DM reads your prose; this just gives the table a shared dialect for what kinds of moves exist.

Combat is atomic — declaration + roll + outcome narration in one turn (see [`/docs/design/turn-loop.md`](../../../docs/design/turn-loop.md#combat-specifically)). These verbs name the *declaration* shape.

## The verbs

### attack
Direct harm against a target. The default. Always grounded in a specific weapon or ability — a hammer swing, a kinetic burst from a tuned ringglass focus, a kite-cord garrote. Not "the wizard casts."
> *"Karrith swings the hammer at the leader's shoulder."*

### maneuver
Reposition, claim a positional advantage, set something up. No attack — a check that produces a state another move will exploit.
> *"I work my way around to get the gantry rail between us — maneuver, finesse?"*

### prepare
Set up a preparatory ability. The setup is now true; the trigger fires later. (Same verb as in [turn-verbs.md](turn-verbs.md); combat is where it shows up most.)
> *"I'm not attacking — I'm attuning a kinetic shield around Karrith. If anything swings on him, the shield fires."*

### hold
Wait for a specific trigger. Your turn passes; you fire on the condition. The DM tracks the trigger from your prose.
> *"I hold action: the moment she reaches for the rope, I cut it."*

### press
Commit harder on an existing advantage — a wounded foe, a momentum surge, a positional edge. Higher stakes, higher reward, higher risk if it goes wrong.
> *"She's bleeding and off-balance. I press — hammer to the same shoulder, this time with momentum."*

### disengage
Break contact. Retreat from melee, exit a kinetic engagement, get out of arrow-line. A check, but the goal is *leaving the fight cleanly* rather than landing a hit.
> *"I disengage from the leader — back two paces, kite-cord between us."*

### attune
Mid-combat retuning of your equipment to a different band or bandwidth. Costs your action. Useful when the fight reveals what you actually need.
> *"The whole patrol is signal-band — I attune my kite-fork for signal disruption, lose my structural reinforcement to do it."*

## Notes on tone

- Every verb above is grounded in this world's mechanics — resonance bands, ringglass, kite-tech, momentum. Generic-fantasy verbs ("cast," "smite," "channel power") don't exist here.
- The DM will rewrite or reject a turn that says "I cast fire" instead of "I push my kite-fork to a kinetic flare."
- A turn can include multiple of these as preamble + main verb (*"I maneuver, then attack"*). The DM resolves the actual mechanics; you narrate the shape.
