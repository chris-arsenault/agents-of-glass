# Social Moves

A small curated set of verbs for non-combat interpersonal action. **Reference, not validation.**

For the principle that social rolls aren't auto-resolved by dice — that the DM reads your prose and decides whether a check is even called — see [`/docs/design/turn-loop.md`](../../../docs/design/turn-loop.md#non-combat).

## The verbs

### persuade
Argue toward a position. Give reasons, appeal to interest or value, build a case. Often the DM will let it play through prose without a check; sometimes the situation is contested and the dice come out.
> *"I tell her: this isn't a Conclave matter, this is a Tempered Accord matter. She knows the difference."*

### provoke
Try to get a reaction — anger, fear, the betrayal of a tell. The DM sets the risk based on what you're poking. Can blow up badly.
> *"I bring up the Bloom Coalition. Watch his hands."*

### conceal
Hide an intent, a fact, or yourself. Lying counts; misdirection counts; physically tucking a shard into a pocket counts. The DM may or may not call a check depending on how scrutinized you are.
> *"I act like I haven't recognized her. I'm betting on her not wanting to be recognized either."*

### read
Observe and intuit a person's state. In this world, this often means catching their resonance signature — emotional attunement leaks. A check the DM calls when you commit to *paying attention* rather than acting.
> *"I read him — what's his attunement doing right now? Is he scared?"*

### offer
Make a proposal, trade, or deal. Has weight when the offer is concrete (a named thing for a named thing) and the other party stands to gain. The DM may call a persuade-shaped check on the back of it.
> *"I offer her the cord in exchange for the patrol's transit log. Nothing else, nothing more."*

### press
Push harder verbally — escalate pressure, commit to your line, refuse to back down. Mirrors `press` in combat: compounding on an advantage, with corresponding risk if it breaks.
> *"I press: she already gave me half the answer. I'm not leaving without the rest."*

## Notes on tone

- Per [`/docs/principles/resist-generic-drift.md`](../../../docs/principles/resist-generic-drift.md): a social check is grounded in *this conversation*. "Persuade" is the verb; the actual move is *"I appeal to her Tempered Accord oath in front of her own people."* Don't strip the specifics.
- The DM's adjudication of social moves is more discretionary than combat. Most won't need a check. The ones that will are the contested ones — when the other party has a real reason to refuse, or when failure costs something.
