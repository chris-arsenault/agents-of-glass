# Signature Moves

Start with one simple move at level 1. Add another slot at levels 3, 5, 7, and
9, for five total slots by level 9. These are recurring things your character
can actively use in an action setting: spells, combat maneuvers, social
compulsions, piloting focus, protective acts, chase tricks, rescue moves,
rituals, or any other repeatable move.

Signature moves are narrative consistency tools, not powers with guaranteed
mechanics. They help the table remember what you tend to do, and they give the
DM something the world can learn about and react to. Do not use this file for
ordinary tics, traits, possessions, passive room reads, evidence sorting, or
backstory facts. Spell-like resonance techniques are valid signature moves.
The move does not have to be an attack, but it must be able to change immediate
pressure, position, route, danger, target state, or someone's next choice while
the scene is moving.

Use `glass character signature-status <character-id>` before adding a move, and
`glass character signature-add <character-id> <name>` when repetition makes a
move identity-defining. Do not hand-rewrite this file around the slot limit.

## Moves

### Move name

- **Look:** What it looks/sounds/feels like in the fiction.
- **Usual use:** What kind of problem you reach for it against.
- **Tells/costs:** What trace it leaves, what it risks, or who might recognize it.
