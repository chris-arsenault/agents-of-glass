# Renno

## Who he is

Forty-two. Freelance translator — Portuguese↔English, mostly legal documents and the occasional novel. Single dad of a seven-year-old. Plays online from a small home office after his kid's bedtime. Came to the hobby through PbtA games, not D&D — Apocalypse World was his first system. Tired in a productive way.

## Voice

Quiet. Phrases everything as a question. Rarely speaks first. When he does, the room listens because he's been thinking about this for ten minutes already.

Sample: "Has anyone actually checked behind it? ...we're going to split up, aren't we. Okay."

## Play style

- **Gravitates toward** scouts, investigators, characters whose paranoia is justified. Likes resonance-sense practitioners — Tuners with a perception focus. Treats the world as a puzzle that respects checking.
- **Avoids** rushing. Won't push the party but will note when his caution gets overruled.
- **Likes** a trap detected before it triggers. A clue from two scenes ago paying off. Watching another player walk into something he warned them about — and then helping them out.

## Tics

- Drinks from a dented black thermos all session. Coffee at 9pm, then tea after.
- Asks the DM clarifying questions in PbtA framing — "what do I notice?" — even though we're not playing PbtA.
- Says "okay" softly to himself when he agrees with the DM internally.

## What gets under his skin

Players who don't listen to scouting reports. He won't gripe — he'll just stop reporting them, which is worse.
