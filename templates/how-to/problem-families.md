# Problem Families

A problem family is the kind of pressure the scene asks the party to solve. It
is not the scene mode, not a taxonomy the players must obey, and not the
solution path. It gives Mara a quick check against dressing the same extraction,
proof, or loading problem in new scenery.

Use one primary family per scene. A secondary pressure can exist, but the prep
should know what the scene is mainly about.

## Useful Families

**Environment / traversal.** The main obstacle is physical space: height,
weather, terrain, moving machinery, damaged architecture, unstable resonance,
crowds, routes, visibility, or access.

**Social pressure / coercive bargain.** Someone wants the party to agree,
submit, betray, trade, perform, concede, or choose under public or personal
pressure. The danger is not just embarrassment; someone can be hurt, seized,
exposed, displaced, or abandoned if the party loses leverage.

**Investigation / clue trail.** The party must find, test, connect, or interpret
clues through action. Put clue sources in people, places, objects, bodies,
damage patterns, creatures, sounds, routes, or tools. Documents can help; they
should not be the whole scene.

**Fight / monster pressure.** A person, creature, swarm, machine, place, or
hazard is actively trying to hurt, stop, seize, or drive off someone. The
interesting question is not only who wins; it is what the fight changes.

**Chase / escape / pursuit.** Position, routes, obstacles, shortcuts, and
mistakes matter. The party may be chasing, fleeing, escorting, intercepting, or
trying to reach something before the opposition.

**Extraction / rescue.** Someone or something must be removed from danger.
Complicate the removal with injury, unwilling targets, hostile terrain,
separated groups, unstable objects, rivals, or an exit that changes.

**Breach / infiltration.** The party must get into, through, or out of a hostile
or guarded place. Interesting breaches include locks, rituals, social covers,
vertical access, decoys, timing windows, and choices about what to leave behind.

**Disaster / containment.** A bad process is already in motion: fire, collapse,
contamination, riot, spreading resonance, flood, sickness, or machine runaway.
The party is buying time, redirecting harm, rescuing people, or choosing what
cannot be saved.

**Puzzle / weird mechanism.** The main obstacle is an actionable oddity:
machines, ruins, resonance behavior, creature habits, impossible architecture,
or ritual logic. It should be manipulable on screen, not only explained.

**Triage / impossible choice.** The party cannot fully solve everything at once.
Make the costs concrete and visible: people, places, assets, routes, faction
trust, time, or bodily safety.

## Checks

- Knowledge is not a problem family. Knowledge is an output. "Find out what
  happened" still needs a pressure shape: investigation, social pressure,
  environment, puzzle, chase, or another family.
- Repeating a family is fine only when the new scene pressures a different part
  of the party toolkit or changes the fictional stakes.
- Preserve player freedom. The family tells Mara what problem to throw at the
  table, not which move will solve it.
- A good scene prep can answer: why is this not the last scene again?
