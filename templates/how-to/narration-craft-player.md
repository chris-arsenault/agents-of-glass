# Narration Craft — Player

You write public prose every turn. This guidance exists to keep your character
legible through action: they say things, do things, want things, carry things,
risk things, and change the board.

Pair with [`how-to/specificity.md`](specificity.md),
[`how-to/shared-narrative.md`](shared-narrative.md), and
[`docs/principles/resist-generic-drift.md`](../../docs/principles/resist-generic-drift.md).

## The Slop Attractor

The dominant test-7 fingerprint was **negative-space narration**: prose that
gets weight from absent speech, unnamed shared knowledge, unfinished thoughts,
generalized forgetting, or gestures treated as if they replaced dialogue. It
feels literary on first pass because it creates pressure without committing to a
specific event. Across a transcript, it makes every character sound like the
same wry narrator.

Use this replacement rule:

**Write the positive event.** Put one of these on the page instead:

- the exact line your character says
- the exact action your character takes
- the named fact your character knows
- the concrete object, tool, debt, mark, document, route, or signal your
  character handles
- the state change the next actor must respond to

If a sentence gets its force from what is absent, translate it into the person,
fact, and consequence that are present.

## Commit. Advance. Resolve.

The player methodology says to choose one contribution that changes the scene: a
decision, action, offer, refusal, discovery attempt, cost accepted, concrete
question, or visible local detail your character can act on.

Three rules for public prose:

**1. Commit to the line.** Your character speaks or acts. Write the words or the
action. Let the table read significance from what lands on the page.

**2. Take authority.** When you can invent a useful visible detail within your
authority, do so. When your character makes a decision, make it. When your
character needs hidden information, ask once on the record and hand off with a
clear open question.

**3. Resolve to a next state.** Your character ends somewhere different:
physically, socially, emotionally, materially, or informationally. If you cannot
state the delta in `glass done --state`, the turn has not advanced enough.

## Player-Specific Craft Rules

**Lead with the play move.** OOC process notes are useful only when another actor
needs them. When possible, start with the visible character action.

**Keep your register yours.** Another PC's specialized diction belongs to that
PC. Build your own speech habits from your character's profession, culture,
tools, and temperament.

**Use one live branch at most.** If the next beat depends on another actor, leave
one clear conditional or one open question. Do not pre-resolve a full tree of
possible responses.

**Put the contribution on screen.** If you brought a form, tool, rumor, route,
or offer into play, show where it is, who can reach it, and what it changes.

## Positive Rewrites

Use one of these shapes when a paragraph starts drifting into negative-space
narration:

**Direct line**

> Vorda says, "We run V-9 before Coremark closes the corridor."

**Concrete action**

> Seld lays the probe kit on the table and turns the case latch toward Talene.

**Named knowledge**

> Kern recognizes Chel'sten's mark on the permit folio. He folds the page to
> hide the credential field before anyone else reads down.

**Character want**

> Talene wants Sabel Torm in the room before the crew answers the courier.

**Visible cost**

> Vorda signs the acceptance line with her registry name. The debt is now tied to
> her license record.

These rewrites are short because the event carries the weight.

## Self-Check Before `glass done`

Re-read what you wrote. For each paragraph, ask:

- Did my character do, say, want, reveal, take, spend, move, or decide something?
- Did I put speech, action, named knowledge, or a visible object on the page?
- Did I explain significance that the scene can carry through action?
- Did my close create a new state for the next actor?
- Can I state the turn delta in `glass done --state`?

If any check fails, revise before ending the turn.
