# Useful Inventory

Inventory should give the character something to do when the scene turns. A
keepsake can be excellent, but a whole inventory of keepsakes leaves the player
with no handles in combat, chase, rescue, infiltration, social pressure, or
hazard scenes.

Starting inventory is exactly **3 items**. One must be a **weapon or combat
implement**: a thing the character can plausibly reach for when violence,
pursuit, monsters, or immediate bodily danger enter the scene. It does not have
to be attack-only; it can block, cut, threaten, disable, bind, signal, deflect,
or control space. Mark that item's affordance with an effect tag beginning
`weapon:`.

Useful shapes:

- **Favored weapon:** dueling saber, ringglass baton, hook-axe, line knife,
  weighted chain, shield-cloth, conductor staff, collapsible spear, sidearm,
  fighting cane, war-bell, shock net.
- **Instrument:** brass horn, resonance fork, signal pipe, tuned bell, portable
  drum, field harmonica, glass-chime strip. If this is the required weapon,
  explain how it can defend, strike, disrupt, or control.
- **Special apparatus:** handheld meter, loupe set, smoke lens, cable gun,
  grapnel spool, field microscope, foldout barricade.
- **Restraint or control:** weighted net, chalked let-down line, shock cord,
  binding tape, drugged wine, snap manacles, bright flare.
- **Protective gear:** rigger harness, insulated gloves, mirror goggles,
  pressure hood, fire cloak, reinforced case.
- **Leverage token:** forged seal, signed warrant, debt marker, witness bell,
  guild badge, stamped manifest. Use at most one, and never instead of the
  required weapon.

Weak inventory by itself:

- old letter
- lucky coin
- family token
- notebook of memories
- keepsake shard

Those are good texture. They become better when paired with useful gear, or
when reframed as useful under pressure:

- old letter -> letter wrapped around a knife grip, naming who forged the blade
- lucky coin -> weighted coin used to test gravity shear or distract a guard
- family token -> ringglass charm set into a shield-cloth clasp
- notebook of memories -> field book with waterproof route pages and flare tabs
- keepsake shard -> polished ringglass edge in a knife hilt or tuning fork

The best items do double duty: practical enough to come up in play, specific
enough to reveal who owns them.
